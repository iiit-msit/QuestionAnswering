<i lang="fr">A\xc3\xa9roport de Beauvais-Till\xc3\xa9</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>BVA</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="German_use_during_World_War_II">German use during World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: German use during World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>This airport was built in the 1930s and seized by the Germans in June 1940 during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_France" title="Battle of France">Battle of France</a>. Beauvais was used as a <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> military airfield during the occupation. Known units assigned (all from Luftflotte 3, Fliegerkorps IV):<sup id="cite_ref-ren1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ren1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_76" title="Kampfgeschwader 76">Kampfgeschwader 76</a> (KG 76) June \xe2\x80\x93 24 October 1940 <a href="/wiki/Dornier_Do_17" title="Dornier Do 17">Dornier Do 17</a>Z-2 (Fuselage Code: F1+)</li>\n<li>Sturzkampfgeschwader 1 (SKG 1) July 1940 <a href="/wiki/Junkers_Ju_87" title="Junkers Ju 87">Junkers Ju 87</a>B Stuka</li></ul>\n<p>The initial German use of the airport was as a bomber base.&#160;kg 76 and SKG 1 both took part in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Britain" title="Battle of Britain">Battle of Britain</a>.&#160;kg 76 was reduced to 19 out of 29 serviceable machines by 18 August 1940.&#160;kg 76 raided London on 7 and 15 September 1940.\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_26" title="Kampfgeschwader 26">Kampfgeschwader 26</a> (KG 26) September 1940 \xe2\x80\x93 February 1941 <a href="/wiki/Heinkel_He_111" title="Heinkel He 111">Heinkel He 111</a>H (Fuselage Code: 1H+)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_77" title="Kampfgeschwader 77">Kampfgeschwader 77</a> (KG 77) 3\xe2\x80\x9322 March 1941 <a href="/wiki/Junkers_Ju_88" title="Junkers Ju 88">Junkers Ju 88</a>A-1 (Fuselage Code: 3A+)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_4" title="Kampfgeschwader 4">Kampfgeschwader 4</a> (KG 4) 30 June \xe2\x80\x93 19 July 1941 <a href="/wiki/Heinkel_He_111" title="Heinkel He 111">Heinkel He 111</a>H (Fuselage Code: 5J+)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_54" title="Kampfgeschwader 54">Kampfgeschwader 54</a> (KG 54) 9 July \xe2\x80\x93 16 August 1942 <a href="/wiki/Junkers_Ju_88" title="Junkers Ju 88">Junkers Ju 88</a>A-1 (Fuselage Code: B3+)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_6" title="Kampfgeschwader 6">Kampfgeschwader 6</a> (KG 6) December 1942 \xe2\x80\x93 February 1943 <a href="/wiki/Junkers_Ju_88" title="Junkers Ju 88">Junkers Ju 88</a>A-1 (Fuselage Code: K6+)</li></ul>\n<p>With the Luftwaffe switching to night attacks on England, the badly damaged units at Beauvais were replaced by a series of He 111 and Ju 88A units that carried out anti-shipping missions (KG 26, KG 77) and night bombing missions over England (KG 4, KG 54, KG 6).<sup id="cite_ref-ren1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ren1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Jagdgeschwader_26" title="Jagdgeschwader 26">Jagdgeschwader 26</a> (JG 26) 15 August \xe2\x80\x93 3 October 1943 <a href="/wiki/Focke-Wulf_Fw_190" title="Focke-Wulf Fw 190">Focke-Wulf Fw 190</a>A</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jagdgeschwader_1_(World_War_II)" title="Jagdgeschwader 1 (World War II)">Jagdgeschwader 1</a> (JG 1) 6\xe2\x80\x9330 June 1944 <a href="/wiki/Messerschmitt_Bf_109" title="Messerschmitt Bf 109">Messerschmitt Bf 109</a>G</li></ul>\n<p>The increasing number and frequency of USAAF Eighth Air Force <a href="/wiki/Boeing_B-17_Flying_Fortress" title="Boeing B-17 Flying Fortress">Boeing B-17 Flying Fortress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_B-24_Liberator" title="Consolidated B-24 Liberator">Consolidated B-24 Liberator</a> daylight heavy bomber raids over occupied Europe and Germany made the Luftwaffe move out the bomber units and assign day interceptor fighter units to attack the American bombers as part of the <a href="/wiki/Defense_of_the_Reich" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense of the Reich">Defense of the Reich</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Normandy" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasion of Normandy">invasion of Normandy</a>, elements of JG 1 were moved to France and were tasked with providing air support to the German army, along with their normal air defense role against Allied bombers.<sup id="cite_ref-ren1_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ren1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In response to the interceptor attacks, Beauvais was attacked by USAAF <a href="/wiki/Ninth_Air_Force" title="Ninth Air Force">Ninth Air Force</a> <a href="/wiki/Martin_B-26_Marauder" title="Martin B-26 Marauder">Martin B-26 Marauder</a> medium bombers and <a href="/wiki/Republic_P-47_Thunderbolt" title="Republic P-47 Thunderbolt">Republic P-47 Thunderbolt</a> fighter-bombers with 500-pound general-purpose bombs, unguided rockets and .50 caliber machine gun sweeps when <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Air_Force" title="Eighth Air Force">Eighth Air Force</a> heavy bombers were within interception range of the Luftwaffe aircraft assigned to the base. The attacks were timed to have the maximum effect possible to keep the interceptors pinned down on the ground and be unable to attack the heavy bombers. Also the <a href="/wiki/North_American_P-51_Mustang" title="North American P-51 Mustang">North American P-51 Mustang</a> fighter-escort groups of <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Air_Force" title="Eighth Air Force">Eighth Air Force</a> would drop down on their return to England and attack the base with a fighter sweep.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_use">American use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: American use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>It was liberated by Allied ground forces about 3 September 1944 during the Northern France Campaign. Almost immediately, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> IX Engineer Command 818th Engineer Aviation Battalion cleared the airport of mines and destroyed Luftwaffe aircraft. Little battle damage was sustained, and the airport became a USAAF Ninth Air Force combat airfield, designated as <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Landing_Ground" title="Advanced Landing Ground">Advanced Landing Ground</a> "A-61" about 15 September, also being known as "Beauvais/Tille Airfield".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From Beauvais, the Ninth Air Force <a href="/wiki/322d_Bombardment_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="322d Bombardment Group">322d Bombardment Group</a> flew B-26 Marauder medium bombers from mid-September until March 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-Maurer,_Maurer_1983_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maurer,_Maurer_1983-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Once the combat unit moved east, the airport was used by transport units, flying in supplies from England and evacuating combat casualties on the return trip. The Americans returned full control of the airport to French authorities on 17 August 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-Maurer,_Maurer_1983_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maurer,_Maurer_1983-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_1950s">Development since the 1950s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development since the 1950s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Beauvais was selected to become a NATO Emergency airfield (Beauvais\xe2\x80\x93Till\xc3\xa9 Air Base), controlled by the <a href="/wiki/French_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="French Air Force">French Air Force</a> and intended for use by all NATO air forces to disperse their aircraft in case of war.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Demolition crews arrived and removed the wartime wreckage and any unexploded munitions were removed from the site. Funding shortages did not allow the construction of an 8000\' jet runway, dispersal pads and other features found at a modern military airfield. Instead, in 1953 the NATO plans for Beauvais were discontinued and the airport was returned to private hands.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1956 (or possibly slightly earlier) Beauvais\xe2\x80\x93Till\xc3\xa9 was rebuilt as a civil airport and reopened for commercial use. It was the French end of the world\'s first low-cost Coach-Air service, linking London to Paris via <a href="/wiki/Lympne_Airport" title="Lympne Airport">Lympne</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/Skyways_Coach-Air_Limited" title="Skyways Coach-Air Limited">Skyways</a>. Redevelopment began in 2005. The airport then had three gates, housed within a <a href="/wiki/Marquee_(tent)" class="mw-redirect" title="Marquee (tent)">marquee tent</a> while the permanent facilities were being redeveloped.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Evidence of its wartime history is present around the threshold of runway 22, northeast of the airport, with about 2000 feet of the runway end being the unused surface of the wartime runway, complete with several bomb craters left by the Ninth Air Force bomber attacks and some single-lane concrete roads, being the remainders of wartime taxiways. In addition, ruins of the support technical site remain to the northeast of the airport, near the commune of Morlaine, with connecting taxiways and the foundations/rubble of what appears to be buildings or an aircraft hangar. Wartime dispersal revetments in a wooded area, also connected by taxiways, remain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Control_tower">Control tower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Control tower">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The new control tower is active since 22 January 2019. It is located on the southern side of the airport and is replacing the one of 1962, sitting between the two terminals.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The main runway has an <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">Instrument landing system</a> <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system#ILS_categories" title="Instrument landing system">CAT III</a> for runway 12 and CAT I for runway 30 plus a <a href="/wiki/Precision_Approach_Path_Indicator" class="mw-redirect" title="Precision Approach Path Indicator">Precision Approach Path Indicator</a> (PAPI) for runway 12. A PAPI will also be installed for runway 30 at the end of 2019. This enables aircraft to land at the airport in bad weather conditions, with visibility as low as 75 metres.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beauvais%E2%80%93Till%C3%A9_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>When the low-cost airline <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> chose Beauvais\xe2\x80\x93Till\xc3\xa9 in May 1997 for three daily connections with <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, the terminal of this regional airport consisted of a simple hangar built in 1979. Since then four additional stations for planes and in 2010 a second terminal of 6,000 square metres (65,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) had to be built to face a significant increase in traffic. The airport is equipped to handle medium-sized passenger jets. Since 2007 the ban on night flying has been strictly enforced for the benefit of local residents. The terminal building closes between the hours of 23:30 and 06:30.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The airport has two terminals, some restaurants, snack bars, and shopping areas, both airside and in the publicly accessible area. An Ibis Budget hotel, which will provide 76 rooms, is currently being built next to Terminal 2.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and
