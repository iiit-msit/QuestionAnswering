lang="gl">Aeroporto de Santiago-Rosal\xc3\xada de Castro</i>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Aeropuerto de Santiago-Rosal\xc3\xada de Castro</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>SCQ</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LEST</b>), previously named <i>Lavacolla Airport</i> and also known as <b>Santiago de Compostela Airport</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> serving the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> and <a href="/wiki/1833_territorial_division_of_Spain" title="1833 territorial division of Spain">historical region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_busiest_airports_in_Spain" title="List of the busiest airports in Spain">2nd busiest airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Green_Spain" title="Green Spain">northern Spain</a> after <a href="/wiki/Bilbao_Airport" title="Bilbao Airport">Bilbao Airport</a>. It has been named after the <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galician</a> <a href="/wiki/Romanticism" title="Romanticism">romanticist</a> writer and <a href="/wiki/Poetess" class="mw-redirect" title="Poetess">poetess</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro" title="Rosal\xc3\xada de Castro">Rosal\xc3\xada de Castro</a>, since 12 March 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airport is located in the parish of Lavacolla, 12&#160;km from <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Compostela" title="Santiago de Compostela">Santiago de Compostela</a> and handled 2,903,427 passengers in 2019. It is the main <a href="/wiki/Focus_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Focus city">focus city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vueling" title="Vueling">Vueling</a> in the northwest <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>\'s only <a href="/wiki/Focus_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Focus city">focus city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Green_Spain" title="Green Spain">Northern Spain</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Christian_pilgrimage" title="Christian pilgrimage">Christian pilgrimage</a> route of the <a href="/wiki/Camino_de_Santiago" title="Camino de Santiago">Camino de Santiago</a> runs near the airport.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport was set up by a group of aviation enthusiasts in October 1932 and two months directors were chosen to select where the airport was going to be built. In 1935 construction work started at the airport where two years later on 27 September 1937 the first scheduled flight from Santiago de Compostela took place.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> After the Spanish Civil war, political prisoners (who were held in the concentration camp of Lavacolla) were forced to work in the construction of the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1969 a new terminal was built at the airport. It has had several expansions taking place since it opened.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> It closed in 2011 following a brand new terminal being built at the airport. In 1981, a cargo terminal was built, giving the airport capacity to handle cargo flights.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During the 1990s, the airport had non-stop service to <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Viasa" title="Viasa">Viasa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 13 October 2011 a new passenger terminal opened at the airport, replacing the old terminal, opened in 1969 and remodeled in 1993.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport currently has one operating terminal.  The old terminal at Santiago de Compostela airport opened in 1969 and was often expanded. The old terminal closed on the night of 13 October 2011 when operations transferred to the new terminal.\n</p><p>The new terminal at Santiago de Compostela Airport officially opened on 13 October 2011 and passenger operations transferred there the following day. It is adjacent to the old terminal and has a size of 74,000&#160;sq m. It has 22 check-in desks, 3 security checkpoints, 4 baggage carousels, and 13 gates of which 5 have airbridges. The baggage hall is split into two zones, one for <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen</a> flights and one for Non-Schengen. It can handle as many as 4 million passengers per year.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The terminal is due to be expanded in the future. This includes adding another five airbridges to five of the current gates as well as three more baggage carousels and an expanded shopping area.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>During the early 2000s, numbers increased significantly at the airport, from 1.24 million in 2002 to peak at 2.46 million in 2011. Because of the financial crisis in Spain, those numbers decreased to 2.1 million in 2014. Cargo has decreased significantly over the last ten years. The Spanish economic recovery in the mid-2010s and the rise of Santiago de Compostela as an international destination are again increasing passenger numbers, breaking the 2.50 million mark for the class="mw-headline" id="Ground_transportation">Ground transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is linked with <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Compostela" title="Santiago de Compostela">Santiago de Compostela</a> (13&#160;km) by the <a href="/wiki/Autov%C3%ADa_A-54" title="Autov\xc3\xada A-54">Autov\xc3\xada A-54</a>. This motorway is currently being extended to <a href="/wiki/Lugo" title="Lugo">Lugo</a> (94.5&#160;km) where it will connect with the <a href="/wiki/Autov%C3%ADa_A-6" title="Autov\xc3\xada A-6">Autov\xc3\xada A-6</a>, providing toll-free motorway access to the rest of Spain; and to the French border through the <a href="/wiki/Autov%C3%ADa_A-8" title="Autov\xc3\xada A-8">Autov\xc3\xada A-8</a> that intersects with the <a href="/wiki/Autov%C3%ADa_A-6" title="Autov\xc3\xada A-6">Autov\xc3\xada A-6</a> near <a href="/wiki/Lugo" title="Lugo">Lugo</a>. Nearby <a href="/wiki/Autopista_AP-9" title="Autopista AP-9">Autopista AP-9</a> connects the airport directly to <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coru\xc3\xb1a">A Coru\xc3\xb1a</a> (66&#160;km), <a href="/wiki/Ferrol,_Galicia" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrol, Galicia">Ferrol</a> (88&#160;km), <a href="/wiki/Pontevedra" title="Pontevedra">Pontevedra</a> (75&#160;km), <a href="/wiki/Vigo" title="Vigo">Vigo</a> (100&#160;km) and the Portuguese border. <a href="/wiki/Ourense" title="Ourense">Ourense</a> (116&#160;km) is reachable through the <a href="/wiki/Autopista_AP-53" title="Autopista AP-53">Autopista AP-53</a> that connects with the <a href="/wiki/Autopista_AP-9" title="Autopista AP-9">Autopista AP-9</a>.\n</p><p>There are several major car rental companies at the airport. The airport has more than 5,000 short and long-term covered parking spaces in the new terminal building. In addition, there are several low-cost, long-term private parking facilities around the airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_services">Bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A city bus service operated by Empresa Freire every 30 minutes connects the airport with the center of Santiago de Compostela, and the bus and train terminals in the city. From the station in Santiago de Compostela, private coach operators run direct services in a multiple daily basis to most cities and towns in Galicia, including <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coru\xc3\xb1a">A Coru\xc3\xb1a</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrol,_Galicia" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrol, Galicia">Ferrol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lugo" title="Lugo">Lugo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ourense" title="Ourense">Ourense</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontevedra" title="Pontevedra">Pontevedra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vigo" title="Vigo">Vigo</a>, as well as long-distance services to the rest of Spain, and international services. In addition, three regional services link the airport directly to <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coru\xc3\xb1a">A Coru\xc3\xb1a</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Lugo" title="Lugo">Lugo</a>, including several stops in the <a href="/wiki/French_Way" title="French Way">French Way</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Camino_de_Santiago" title="Camino de Santiago">Camino de Santiago</a>, and to the <a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Mari%C3%B1a" class="extiw" title="es:La Mari\xc3\xb1a">A Mari\xc3\xb1a</a> coastal area (home to <a href="/wiki/As_Catedrais_beach" title="As Catedrais beach">As Catedrais beach</a>) in the province of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Lugo" title="Province of Lugo">Lugo</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are no rail facilities at the airport. However the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.adif.es/en_US/infraestructuras/estaciones/31400/informacion_000050.shtml">train station in Santiago de Compostela</a>, located 12&#160;km. away, is connected to the airport by the city bus service every 30 minutes. There are combined available train+bus tickets to and from the airport. The train station in Santiago de Compostela has medium and long-distance high-speed <a href="/wiki/Alvia" title="Alvia">Alvia</a> and <a href="/wiki/AVE" title="AVE">AVE</a> services to most cities in Galicia, including <a href="/wiki/A_Coru%C3%B1a" title="A Coru\xc3\xb1a">A Coru\xc3\xb1a</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrol,_Galicia" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrol, Galicia">Ferrol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ourense" title="Ourense">Ourense</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontevedra" title="Pontevedra">Pontevedra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vilagarc%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="Vilagarc\xc3\xada">Vilagarc\xc3\xada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vigo" title="Vigo">Vigo</a>; and further to <a href="/wiki/Madrid_Chamart%C3%ADn_railway_station" title="Madrid Chamart\xc3\xadn railway station">Madrid Chamart\xc3\xadn</a> and the rest of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foot_and_bike">Foot and bike</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Foot and bike">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Camino_de_Santiago" title="Camino de Santiago">Camino de Santiago</a> runs next to the runway of the airport. This is the busiest and final journey in the <a href="/wiki/Camino_de_Santiago" title="Camino de Santiago">Camino de Santiago</a> that goes through the famous <a href="/wiki/Monte_do_Gozo" title="Monte do Gozo">Monte do Gozo</a>. There are dedicated pathways for both pedestrians and bikers towards the city. The walking distance from the runway to the <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Compostela_Cathedral" title="Santiago de Compostela Cathedral">Cathedral</a> is estimated at 10.90&#160;km.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_Incidents">Accidents and Incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Santiago%E2%80%93Rosal%C3%ADa_de_Castro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Accidents and Incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 3 March 1978, a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-8" title="Douglas DC-8">McDonnell Douglas DC-8-63</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Iberia_LAE" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia LAE">Iberia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Madrid%E2%80%93Barajas_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barajas Airport">Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barajas Airport</a> with 211 passengers and 11 crew members, registration EC-BMX. The aircraft touched down far down the runway after a high approach, aquaplaned off the runway, dropped into a hollow 20m deep and caught fire. The crash was settled with 70 injured people, 10 of them seriously injured, and no fatalities.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 7 June 2001, a <a href="/wiki/Beechcraft_Super_King_Air" title="Beechcraft Super King Air">Beechcraft B300C Super King Air 350</a>, registration F-GOAE, departed from Le Mans-Arnage Airport (LME), France, to Santiago De Compostela Airport (SCQ), Spain, on a cargo flight according to instrument flight rules. Near the destination airport, the meteorological conditions were reported to be good, and the crew requested a visual approach to runway 17, even though the active runway was 35. Once cleared to land, the aircraft encountered a fog patch and from this moment it began a high ate descent (2000 to 3000&#160;ft/min). A minute after entering an unexpected and unforeseen fog patch, the aircraft struck some trees in level flight and with an airspeed of 148 kt. The wings and engines detached from the fuselage, and they dragged along a scrubland area until they came to a stop. The crew suffered minor injuries and the aircraft was completely destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 2 August 2012, an Airnor <a href="/wiki/Cessna_500" class="mw-redirect" title="Cessna 500">Cessna 500 Citation I</a>, registration EC-IBA, flying from <a href="/wiki/Asturias_Airport" title="Asturias Airport">Asturias</a> crashed whilst on approach to the airport with the loss of both crew members.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span
