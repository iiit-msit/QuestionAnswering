<i lang="ga"><b>Aerfort Phort L\xc3\xa1irge</b></i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA" class="mw-redirect" title="IATA">IATA</a>: <b>WAT</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO" class="mw-redirect" title="ICAO">ICAO</a>: <b>EIWF</b>) is located in Killowen, 4&#160;<a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">NM</a> (7.4&#160;km; 4.6&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> southeast of <a href="/wiki/Waterford" title="Waterford">Waterford</a>. It serves southeastern Ireland. The airport is operated by Waterford Regional Airport plc.\n</p><p>Its single scheduled route, operated by <a href="/wiki/VLM_Airlines" title="VLM Airlines">VLM Airlines</a>\xe2\x80\x94which used Waterford as its only Irish airport and kept a base there\xe2\x80\x94was discontinued from 13 June 2016 as VLM went out of business, leaving the airport without any scheduled traffic.\n</p><p>On 11 June 2019, the Irish government approved a grant of \xe2\x82\xac5 million together with \xe2\x82\xac7 million from local investors and councils. Included in this, is the extension and widening of the existing runway to 2280 metres x 45 metres wide (bigger than the runway at <a href="/wiki/Cork_Airport" title="Cork Airport">Cork Airport</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> along with taxiway widening and terminal extension<sup id="cite_ref-ch-aviation.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ch-aviation.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> to cater for larger airlines such as Ryanair &amp; Aer Lingus. This extension hopes to boost tourism in the county of Waterford and will hopefully generate more revenue for the county in the long run. \n</p><p>The airport is targeting spring 2021 for the commencement of commercial flight on the newly extended runway.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport\'s development was initiated by Waterford Corporation, with support from the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Government of Ireland</a> and the private sector, in 1979\xe2\x80\x931980. The investment was <a href="/wiki/Irish_pound" title="Irish pound">\xc2\xa3</a>1.76&#160;million.\n</p><p>Waterford Airport opened in 1981, with a 1,200 by 29&#160;m (3,937 by 95&#160;ft) runway for single and twin-engine <a href="/wiki/Light_aircraft" title="Light aircraft">light aircraft</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Portable_building" title="Portable building">portable cabin</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">terminal building</a>. The current terminal building was opened in 1992, and the runway was lengthened to 1,433 by 30&#160;m (4,701 by 98&#160;ft). <a href="/wiki/Avair" title="Avair">Avair</a> provided the first domestic passenger service in 1982, as a stop between <a href="/wiki/Cork_Airport" title="Cork Airport">Cork</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin Airport</a>. In 1985, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> launched the company\'s first international scheduled service from Waterford to <a href="/wiki/London_Gatwick_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="London Gatwick Airport">London Gatwick</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_2000s">Development since the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development since the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Waterford Airport celebrated 21 years of scheduled operations in 2006. On 13 March 2007, a \xe2\x82\xac27.5&#160;million upgrade of the airport was announced. Over the next two years the money would be spent on extending the runway to 7,300 feet (2,200&#160;m) in length, building a new passenger terminal capable of handling one million passengers a year, and introducing scheduled flights to European cities such as Amsterdam, Barcelona, Paris, Prague and Rome. There was also an expectation of charter flights to Mediterranean holiday resorts. The expansion would mean that air travellers in the southeast of Ireland would have a "credible alternative" to Dublin and Cork airports, according to officials at Waterford Airport. According to the chief executive of Waterford Airport, Graham Doyle, the extended runway was to be in place by summer 2009 and capable of handling large <a href="/wiki/Jet_aircraft" title="Jet aircraft">jet aircraft</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320_family" title="Airbus A320 family">Airbus A320</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The fully upgraded and remodelled connecting <a href="/wiki/Regional_road_(Ireland)" title="Regional road (Ireland)">regional road</a>, <a href="/wiki/R708_road_(Ireland)" title="R708 road (Ireland)">R708</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Waterford" title="Waterford">Waterford</a> city to the airport was completed in early July 2008, reducing the journey time from Waterford city centre to the airport to 10 minutes at off-peak times, and from the outer <a href="/wiki/Beltway" class="mw-redirect" title="Beltway">ring road</a>, <a href="/wiki/R470_road_(Ireland)" title="R470 road (Ireland)">R470</a>, to the airport to 5 minutes.\n</p><p>Passenger numbers through Waterford Airport slumped in 2009, owing to the suspension of routes to Bordeaux, Faro, M\xc3\xa1laga and Amsterdam, and to service reductions on routes to Birmingham, London Luton and Manchester. Overall passenger numbers from the UK fell by 9%.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_to_present">2010 to present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 to present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In February 2010, an Economic Impact Assessment of Waterford Airport was published, which highlighted the importance of the airport to the <a href="/wiki/South-East_Region,_Ireland" title="South-East Region, Ireland">South East Region</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Noel_Dempsey" title="Noel Dempsey">Noel Dempsey</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Transport,_Tourism_and_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister for Transport, Tourism and Sport">Transport Minister</a>, announced on 9 March 2010 that owing to difficulties with public finances there would be no funding for the runway extension at the airport, and that security and safety were priorities "in the current financial climate".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In November 2012, <a href="/wiki/Aer_Arann" class="mw-redirect" title="Aer Arann">Aer Arann</a> announced it would terminate all its routes from Waterford. The airline operating under <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus_Regional" title="Aer Lingus Regional">Aer Lingus Regional</a> suspended its service to <a href="/wiki/London_Southend_Airport" title="London Southend Airport">London Southend</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Luton_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="London Luton Airport">London Luton </a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport" title="Manchester Airport">Manchester</a> on the 6th of January.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg/220px-Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg/330px-Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg/440px-Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aer_Arann_Aircraft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1444483.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Aer_Arann" class="mw-redirect" title="Aer Arann">Aer Arann</a> <a href="/wiki/ATR_72" title="ATR 72">ATR 72</a> at Waterford</div></div></div>\n<p>On 12 August 2013, it was reported that a 150m extension of the runway would be funded by the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Transport,_Tourism_and_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Transport, Tourism and Sport">Department of Transport</a> up to a limit of \xe2\x82\xac400,000. The airport itself would have to raise the remaining \xe2\x82\xac850,000 for it to take place.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/VLM_Airlines" title="VLM Airlines">VLM Airlines</a> announced it would terminate its flights to London Luton at short notice by 13 June 2016, leaving Waterford Airport without any scheduled traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-ch-aviation.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ch-aviation.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2017, after a year without commercial flights a new airline Aer Southeast announced three new year round services to <a href="/wiki/London_Luton_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="London Luton Airport">London Luton </a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport" title="Manchester Airport">Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Airport" title="Birmingham Airport">Birmingham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Although 18 months after the announcement the airline shut down due to a licensing issue.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A \xe2\x82\xac12 million plan was announced in June 2019 to extend the airports runway by 850 metres and revamp the airport. Minister for Transport <a href="/wiki/Shane_Ross" title="Shane Ross">Shane Ross</a> would give the airport \xe2\x82\xac5 million only to be given once the extended runway is confirmed and ready for service.\nLocal corporations such as <a href="/wiki/Glanbia" title="Glanbia">Glanbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coolmore_Stud" title="Coolmore Stud">Coolmore Stud</a> and Dawn Meats pledged a total of \xe2\x82\xac5 million with \xe2\x82\xac2 million from local authorities. The extended runway would allow medium-sized commercial passenger jet aircraft such as Boeing 737s and Airbus 320s to use the airport allowing for commercial use. The airport hopes to attract Ryanair back but for now the company has said it\'s \xe2\x80\x9cno plans for the near future\xe2\x80\x9d regarding Waterford.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Spring 2021 is set for the commencement of commercial flight.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Waterford Airport has four check-in desks, an information desk, two boarding gates and two baggage carousels. The airport has two hundred car-parking spaces. Free WiFi is available within the airport terminal. There are two snack bars in the airport: one before security and one in the departures hall. There are also car rental services, a taxi service rank, First Aid, Baby/Parent Room, and Disabled Access/Facilities.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is on the R708 road, which can be accessed from the <a href="/wiki/R710" class="mw-redirect" title="R710">R710</a> outer ring road in Waterford. Waterford has a by-pass, and thus the airport can be very easily reached from <a href="/wiki/Dungarvan" title="Dungarvan">Dungarvan</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Tipperary" title="County Tipperary">Tipperary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kilkenny" title="Kilkenny">Kilkenny</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlow" title="Carlow">Carlow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wexford" title="Wexford">Wexford</a>. After reaching the outer ring road, four major primary routes can be accessed from Waterford: towards <a href="/wiki/New_Ross" title="New Ross">New Ross</a>, Dublin, Limerick and <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_transportation_connections">Other transportation connections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waterford_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other transportation connections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The nearest bus and coach station is 10&#160;km away in Waterford, from where <a href="/wiki/Bus_%C3%89ireann" title="Bus \xc3\x89ireann">Bus \xc3\x89ireann</a> provides services to Dublin, Cork, <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosslare_Europort" title="Rosslare Europort">Rosslare Europort</a>, Wexford, <a href="/wiki/Clonmel" title="Clonmel">Clonmel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tramore" title="Tramore">Tramore</a>. <a href="/wiki/JJ_Kavanagh_and_Sons" title="JJ Kavanagh and Sons">JJ Kavanagh and Sons</a> offers connections to Dublin Airport, Carlow and Tramore as well. Dublin Coach provides a direct service to Dublin via Kilkenny, and to Cork via Dungarvan. There is also a <a href="/wiki/Waterford_railway_station" title="Waterford railway station">railway station</a> in Waterford; this offers services to <a href="/wiki/Limerick_Colbert_railway_station" title="Limerick Colbert railway station">Limerick</a> via <a href="/wiki/Clonmel_railway_station" title="Clonmel railway station">Clonmel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tipperary_halt" title="Tipperary halt">Tipperary</a>, and to <a href="/wiki/Heuston_railway_station" title="Heuston railway station">Dublin Heuston</a> via <a href="/wiki/Kilkenny_railway_station" title="Kilkenny railway station">Kilkenny</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlow_railway_station" title="Carlow railway station">Carlow</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and The helicopter, Rescue 111, was destroyed by fire and all four crew were killed. The <a href="/wiki/Air_Accident_Investigation_Unit" title="Air Accident Investigation Unit">Air Accident Investigation Unit</a> report blamed the lack of an air traffic controller in Waterford at the time; inadequate weather reports; faulty approach lighting to the airport runway; the fact that the Air Corps crew undertook the rescue mission even though they had never flown in similar conditions (and had already completed 15 hours flying that day); and an unnecessary delay at the rescue scene, caused by the use of an inadequate lifeboat although a fully equipped one was available.<sup
