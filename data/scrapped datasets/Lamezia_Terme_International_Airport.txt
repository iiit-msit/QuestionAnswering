(Italian: <i lang="it">Aeroporto Internazionale di Lamezia Terme "Sant\'Eufemia"</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>SUF</b>, <a The airport opened in June 1976. Its <a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA airport code</a> SUF derives from the name Sant\'Eufemia. <a href="/wiki/Aerolinee_Itavia" class="mw-redirect" title="Aerolinee Itavia">Itavia</a> began scheduled flights to <a href="/wiki/Rome-Fiumicino" class="mw-redirect" title="Rome-Fiumicino">Rome-Fiumicino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan-Linate" class="mw-redirect" title="Milan-Linate">Milan-Linate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catania-Fontanarossa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Catania-Fontanarossa Airport">Catania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Falcone_Borsellino_Airport" title="Falcone Borsellino Airport">Palermo</a> in December of that year. The airport was expanded and modernised in 1982.\n</p><p>Since 1990 it has been managed by SACAL SpA, which is jointly owned by various local government administrations and by private investors.\n</p><p>A contract to extend the runway from the current 2,414&#160;m to 3,000&#160;m was awarded on 27 December 2007. A design competition for a new passenger terminal to replace the present building was held in November 2008, and was won by Engco.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
