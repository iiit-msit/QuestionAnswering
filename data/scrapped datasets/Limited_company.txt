template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>In a <b>limited company</b>, the liability of members or subscribers of the <a href="/wiki/Company" title="Company">company</a> is limited to what they have invested or guaranteed to the company. Limited companies may be limited by <a href="/wiki/Share_(finance)" title="Share (finance)">shares</a> or by <a href="/wiki/Guarantee" title="Guarantee">guarantee</a>. The former may be further divided in public companies (<a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">public limited companies</a>) and private companies (<a href="/wiki/Private_limited_company" title="Private limited company">private limited companies</a>). Who may become a member of a private limited company is restricted by law and by the company\'s rules. In contrast, anyone may buy shares in a public limited company.\n</p><p>Limited companies can be found in most countries, although the detailed rules governing them vary widely. It is also common for a distinction to be made between the publicly tradable companies of the <i>plc</i> type (for example, the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Aktiengesellschaft" title="Aktiengesellschaft">Aktiengesellschaft</a> (AG), <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">PLC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> <a href="/wiki/Akciov%C3%A1_spole%C4%8Dnost" class="mw-redirect" title="Akciov\xc3\xa1 spole\xc4\x8dnost">a.s.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Societ%C3%A0_per_azioni" title="Societ\xc3\xa0 per azioni">S.p.A.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> Nyrt. and the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> <a href="/wiki/S.A._(corporation)" title="S.A. id="Private_company_limited_by_guarantee">Private company limited by guarantee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Private company limited by guarantee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_guarantee" title="Private company limited by guarantee">Private company limited by guarantee</a></div>\n<p>This is a company that does not have share capital, but is guaranteed by its <i>members</i>, who agree to pay a fixed amount in the event of the company\'s liquidation. <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Charitable organisation">Charitable organisations</a> are often incorporated using this form of limited liability. Another example is the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Conduct_Authority" title="Financial Conduct Authority">Financial Conduct Authority</a>. In Australia, only an unlisted public company can be limited by guarantee.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Private_company_limited_by_shares">Private company limited by shares</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Private company limited by shares">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_shares" title="Private company limited by shares">Private company limited by shares</a></div>\n<p>Has shareholders with limited liability and its shares may not be offered to the general public. Shareholders of private companies limited by shares are always bound to offer the shares to their fellow shareholders prior to selling them to a third party.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_limited_company">Public limited company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Public limited company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">Public limited company</a></div>\n<p>A public limited company can be <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">publicly traded</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Stock_exchange" title="Stock exchange">stock exchange</a>; this is similar to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">Corporation</a> (Corp.) and the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Aktiengesellschaft" title="Aktiengesellschaft">Aktiengesellschaft</a> (AG).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_specific_countries">In specific countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In specific countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The private company equivalent in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Proprietary_company" title="Proprietary company">Proprietary Limited company</a> (Pty Ltd). An Australian company with only <i>Limited</i> or <i>Ltd</i> after its name is a <a href="/wiki/Public_company" title="Public company">public company</a>, such as a company listed on the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Securities_Exchange" title="Australian Securities Exchange">ASX</a>. Australia does not have a direct equivalent to the plc.\n</p><p>A shareholder in a limited company, in the event of its becoming <a href="/wiki/Insolvency" title="Insolvency">insolvent</a> (equivalent to <a href="/wiki/Insolvency" title="Insolvency">insolvency</a> in the United Kingdom) would be liable to contribute the amount remaining unpaid on the shares (usually zero, as most shares are issued fully paid). "Paid" here relates to the amount paid to the company for the shares on first issue, and should not be confused with amounts paid by one shareholder to another to transfer ownership of shares between them. A shareholder is thus afforded limited liability.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, a limited company is registered as any other type of company. To register it, you must pay an accountant to research the name of your future business to check if it wasn\'t already registered, then the accountant contacts the offices responsible for giving you the CNPJ (the national code for company identification), which are the commercial joint of the state and the IRS. After that the Ltda. or Lda. (rarely used) suffixes can be placed after the companies name or with Cia. (abbreviation for <i>companhia</i>, company in Portuguese): [company name] &amp; Cia. Ltda.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In Canada, a person wishing to register a limited company must file <i><a href="/wiki/Articles_of_Incorporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Articles of Incorporation">Articles of Incorporation</a></i> with either their provincial government or the federal government.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the time of incorporation, a company must elect to use "Limited" (Ltd.), "Incorporated" (Inc.) or "Corporation" (Corp.) as part of their name.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In India, there are three types of limited company: a public limited company, a private limited company, and a one-person company. A company\'s liability may be limited by shares, in which case the liability of the company\'s members is limited to the amount of the shares held by them, or it may be limited by guarantee, in which case the liability is limited to a predetermined amount the company\'s members have agreed to contribute if the company is dissolved with outstanding liabilities. A private limited company is a limited company incorporated under the <a href="/wiki/Companies_Act_2013" title="Companies Act 2013">Companies Act 2013</a> (or one of its predecessor acts), with a minimum paid-up <a href="/wiki/Share_capital" title="Share capital">share capital</a> (if any) of <span class="nowrap"><span style="white-space: nowrap">\xe2\x82\xb9</span>1 lakh</span> (US$1,400), with an article that restricts the transfer of its shares; it may have between two and two hundred members, and its name ends with "Private Limited" (abbreviated Pvt Ltd). A public limited company must have a paid-up share capital of at least <span class="nowrap"><span style="white-space: nowrap">\xe2\x82\xb9</span>5 lakh</span> (US$7,000), and at least seven members; its name ends  "Limited" (abbreviated Ltd). A one-person company (OPC) is a private company with similar proprietorship and privileges to a private limited company, but with fewer requirements; this type of company may have only one director and member.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><b>Latest Updates:</b> \n</p><p><b>No Minimum Paid up Capital</b> \xe2\x80\x93 Earlier the business organisations which wanted to take up a company as the preferred form of business organisation had to fulfil the requirement of minimum paid-up share capital of not less than \xe2\x82\xb9 5 lakhs in case of public company and \xe2\x82\xb91 lakh in case of private companies by way of Section 2(71) and 2(68) respectively. However, after in the recent <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.indiacode.nic.in/acts-in-pdf/2015/201521.pdf">Companies Amendment Act 2015</a>, this requirement is scrapped, and a company can go ahead with its incorporation without fulfilling this criterion.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, there are two types of limited companies namely: a company limited by guarantee and a company limited by <a href="/wiki/Shares" class="mw-redirect" title="Shares">shares</a>. The company limited by shares is further divided into two namely a <a href="/wiki/Private_limited_company" title="Private limited company">Private limited company</a> (Ltd.) and a <a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">Public limited company</a>  (Plc.) In Nigeria <a href="/wiki/Shareholders" class="mw-redirect" title="Shareholders">shareholders</a> of limited companies are only liable for the amount of money they contributed to the company. All Nigerian companies are governed by the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 1990 and regulated by the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, the term "Proprietary Limited", abbreviated "(Pty) Ltd", is used to refer to a private limited company. All South African companies are regulated by the CIPC (Companies and Intellectual Property Commission).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, businesses can be registered as Private Limited Company "(Pvt) Ltd", Public Limited Company "PLC" or under a sole proprietorship. Registering as Private Limited Company will be more secure, and have added benefits. Therefore, you and your company will act as two independent parties; ensuring that your business assets and liabilities will be separate. All companies are registered under the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Companies_Act,_No._7_of_2007.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Companies Act, No. 7 of 2007. (page does not exist)">Companies Act, No. 7 of 2007.</a>, through the Registrar of Companies, which operates its office in <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The registration of companies in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> is done through <a href="/wiki/Companies_House" title="Companies House">Companies House</a>, which operates offices in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>.\n</p><p>Prior to 1 October 2009, the registration of companies in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> was the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Enterprise,_Trade_and_Investment" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Enterprise, Trade and Investment">Department of Enterprise, Trade and Investment</a> (a department of the devolved government). On the commencement of the <a href="/wiki/Companies_Act_2006" title="Companies Act 2006">Companies Act 2006</a>, Northern Ireland\'s previously distinct company law was repealed and the new companies code instituted by that Act was extended to Northern Ireland.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_law_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Corporate law in the United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporations</a> have limited liability and the expression <i>corporation</i> is preferred to <i>limited company</i>. A "<a href="/wiki/Limited_liability_company" title="Limited liability company">limited liability company</a>" (LLC) is a different entity. However, <a href="/wiki/Types_of_business_entity#State,_Territory_or_Commonwealth_incorporated" class="mw-redirect" title="Types of business entity">some states</a> permit corporations to have the designation <i>Ltd.</i><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> (instead of the usual <i>Inc.</i>) to signify their corporate status. A Limited company must file annual tax returns ("corporation" tax returns) with the Securities and Exchange Commission.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limited_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Zimbabwe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> the term "(Pvt) Ltd" refers to a private company limited by share capital.
