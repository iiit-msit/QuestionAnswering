the <b>IT Act</b>) is an Act of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Parliament">Indian Parliament</a> (No 21 of 2000) notified on 17 October 2000. It is the primary law in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> dealing with <a href="/wiki/Cybercrime" title="Cybercrime">cybercrime</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_commerce" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic commerce">electronic commerce</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" href="/w/index.php?title=Information_Technology_Act,_2000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The bill was passed in the budget session of 2000 and signed by President <a href="/wiki/K._R._Narayanan" title="K. R. Narayanan">K. R. Narayanan</a> on 9 June 2000. The bill was finalised  by a group of officials headed by then <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Communications_and_Information_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Communications and Information Technology">Minister of Information Technology</a> <a href="/wiki/Pramod_Mahajan" title="Pramod Mahajan">Pramod Mahajan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ComeIntoForce_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ComeIntoForce-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Information_Technology_Act,_2000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The original Act contained 94 sections, divided into 13 chapters and 4 <a href="/wiki/Schedule" title="Schedule">schedules</a>. The laws apply to the whole of India. If a crime involves a computer or network located in India, persons of other nationalities can also be indicted under the law, .<sup id="cite_ref-PawarKolekar2015_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PawarKolekar2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Act provides a legal framework for electronic governance by giving recognition to <a href="/wiki/Electronic_records" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic records">electronic records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_signature" title="Digital signature">digital signatures</a>. It also defines cyber crimes and prescribes penalties for them. The Act directed the formation of a Controller of Certifying Authorities to regulate the issuance of digital signatures. It also established a Cyber Appellate Tribunal to resolve disputes rising from this new law.<sup id="cite_ref-PawarKolekar2015_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PawarKolekar2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Act also amended various sections of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Penal_Code" title="Indian Penal Code">Indian Penal Code, 1860</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Evidence_Act" title="Indian Evidence Act">Indian Evidence Act, 1872</a>, the Banker\'s Book Evidence Act, 1891, and the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Bank_of_India_Act,_1934" title="Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934">Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934</a> to make them compliant with new technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-PawarKolekar2015_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PawarKolekar2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amendments">Amendments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Information_Technology_Act,_2000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Amendments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A major amendment was made in 2008. It introduced Section 66A which penalized sending "offensive messages". It also introduced Section 69, which gave authorities the power of "interception or monitoring or decryption of any information through any computer resource". Additionally, it introduced provisions addressing - <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">pornography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Child_porn" class="mw-redirect" title="Child porn">child porn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyber_terrorism" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyber terrorism">cyber terrorism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voyeurism" title="Voyeurism">voyeurism</a>. The amendment was passed on 22 December 2008 without any debate in Lok Sabha. The next day it was passed by the Rajya Sabha. It was signed into law by President <a href="/wiki/Pratibha_Patil" title="Pratibha Patil">Pratibha Patil</a>, on 5 February 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Sec66A_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sec66A-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Snooping_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snooping-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DeafDumb_5-0"
