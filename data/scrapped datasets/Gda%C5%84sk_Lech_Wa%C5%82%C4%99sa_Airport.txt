Gda\xc5\x84sk im. Lecha Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sy</i>, formerly <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <i lang="pl">Port Lotniczy Gda\xc5\x84sk-R\xc4\x99biechowo</i>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Flughafen Danzig Lech Walesa</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>GDN</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EPGD</b>) is an international <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> located 12&#160;km (7.5&#160;mi) northwest<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk" title="Gda\xc5\x84sk">Gda\xc5\x84sk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, not far from the city centres of the <a href="/wiki/Tricity,_Poland" title="Tricity, Poland">Tricity</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolitan area</a>: Gda\xc5\x84sk (12&#160;km (7.5&#160;mi)), <a href="/wiki/Sopot" title="Sopot">Sopot</a> (10&#160;km (6.2&#160;mi)) and <a href="/wiki/Gdynia" title="Gdynia">Gdynia</a> (23&#160;km (14&#160;mi)). Since 2004 the airport has been named after <a href="/wiki/Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa" title="Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa">Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa</a>, the former Polish president. With around 5.4 million passengers served in 2019, it is the 3rd largest airport in Poland in terms of passenger traffic.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="Early_years_.281910s.E2.80.931950s.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_(1910s\xe2\x80\x931950s)">Early years (1910s\xe2\x80\x931950s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years (1910s\xe2\x80\x931950s)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first passenger flights in Gda\xc5\x84sk were operated in the year 1919 from an airfield in the Langfuhr district of the <a href="/wiki/Free_City_of_Danzig" title="Free City of Danzig">Free City of Danzig</a> (nowadays <a href="/wiki/Wrzeszcz" title="Wrzeszcz">Wrzeszcz</a> district of Gda\xc5\x84sk).<sup id="cite_ref-airhis_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airhis-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was possible thanks to a transformation of that military location into a civilian facility. The airport was at that time additionally used for airmail services and by the police.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the next years the airport continued acquisitions of further areas which allowed it to expand and invest in modern infrastructure at that time. The technical development of Gdansk Wrzeszcz Airport (Danzig-Langfuhr Airport) was followed by the launching of regular routes to <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg" title="K\xc3\xb6nigsberg">K\xc3\xb6nigsberg</a> and other important cities of the region. The flights were already operated by numerous international aviation companies.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ju_f13_02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Ju_f13_02.jpg/280px-Ju_f13_02.jpg" decoding="async" width="280" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Ju_f13_02.jpg/420px-Ju_f13_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/50/Ju_f13_02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="208" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ju_f13_02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Junkers_F.13" class="mw-redirect" title="Junkers F.13">Junkers F.13</a> at Gda\xc5\x84sk-Wrzeszcz in 1921.</div></div></div>\n<p>The first Polish route was served between Gda\xc5\x84sk, Warsaw and <a href="/wiki/Lviv" title="Lviv">Lviv</a> by <a href="/wiki/Aerolloyd" class="mw-redirect" title="Aerolloyd">Aerolloyd</a> and it was started in September 1922.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The company initially used <a href="/wiki/Junkers_F.13" class="mw-redirect" title="Junkers F.13">Junkers F.13</a> aircraft on that daily route. This domestic service was the beginning of the company which later gradually became the <a href="/wiki/LOT_Polish_Airlines" title="LOT Polish Airlines">LOT Polish Airlines</a>, which is still Poland\'s <a href="/wiki/National_carrier" class="mw-redirect" title="National carrier">national carrier</a>.\n</p><p>A railway connection to Gdansk Wrzeszcz Airport was built in the 1920s. This was followed by new <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tram</a> tracks in 1930. The airport itself had a concrete runway which had light and navigation facilities allowing aircraft to land and to take off at night. In the 1930s Gdansk Wrzeszcz Airport served less than 2 thousand passengers annually<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> but it was an important international hub that connected four countries and provided transport facilities between Polish cities.\n</p><p>The airport in Wrzeszcz was an important base of the German air forces during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Even before that, German pilots had been trained on the grounds of this airport. Heavy bombings and other military activities led to the destruction of the airport.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> However, it was reconstructed after the war and it was provided with modern technologies which allowed the airport to develop. Old domestic routes as well as many international destinations were relaunched. The airport had regular connections to countries like Hungary, Germany, Bulgaria, Denmark and Sweden.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_location_since_1970s">New location since 1970s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New location since 1970s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>As the airport facilities became old and nonfunctional at the end of the 1960s (including runway lights which forced airport authorities to ban night flights), a new location near the village of <a href="/wiki/R%C4%99biechowo,_Kartuzy_County" title="R\xc4\x99biechowo, Kartuzy County">R\xc4\x99biechowo</a> was chosen. The airport had to be built somewhere else also because of further <a href="/wiki/Urban_development" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban development">urban development</a>. New areas were needed in order to make it possible for the public investors to construct new buildings for the growing population of Gda\xc5\x84sk. Furthermore, a new longer runway was necessary in order to allow larger <a href="/wiki/Jet_airliner" title="Jet airliner">jet airliners</a> to take off and land safely as the era of modern jet aircraft began. The old airport in Wrzeszcz was officially closed on 1 May 1974 and it practically ceased to exist as a large <a href="/wiki/Housing_estate" title="Housing estate">housing estate</a> was built on its grounds. Nowadays only a few remaining elements of the old Wrzeszcz airport infrastructure can be found, including remnants of its main north-south oriented runway in what is now the Zaspa district.\n</p><p>After the closure of the old airport, the new one was built and it opened in 1974 near the village of R\xc4\x99biechowo<sup id="cite_ref-airhis_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airhis-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (on westernmost land incorporated into the Gda\xc5\x84sk borough of Matarnia in 1973). The airport acquired its current name in 2004. There was some controversy as to whether the name should be spelled <i>Lech Walesa</i> (without diacritics, but better recognizable in the world) or <i>Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa</i> (with Polish letters, but difficult to write and pronounce for foreigners, the closest English phonetic approximation being "Vawensa").\n</p><p>Since 1993, Gda\xc5\x84sk Airport has been owned 31.45% by the authorities of <a href="/wiki/Pomeranian_Voivodeship" title="Pomeranian Voivodeship">Pomeranian Voivodeship</a>, 29.45% by the city of Gda\xc5\x84sk, 1.14% by the city of <a href="/wiki/Gdynia" title="Gdynia">Gdynia</a>, 0.35% by the city of <a href="/wiki/Sopot" title="Sopot">Sopot</a> and 37.61% by Polish Airports State Enterprise. In 2006, the airport served for the first time in its history more than 1&#160;million passengers per year. In 2010, the passenger number was higher than 2&#160;million. After the construction of a new modern passenger terminal with extended capacities (the opening took place in April 2012 ahead of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a> football championships), the airport continued its development and it served for the first time over 3&#160;million passengers in a single year in 2014. In summer season 2015, airlines flying to and from the airport served over 50 regular routes as well as charter and cargo flights.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airport_infrastructure">Airport infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Airport infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airside_facilities">Airside facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Airside facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Gda\xc5\x84sk Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa Airport has a single asphalt-concrete runway in the direction of 11/29.<sup id="cite_ref-infr_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-infr-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The runway is 2,800&#160;m (9,200&#160;ft) long and it is equipped with a modern <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">ILS CAT II</a> allowing aircraft to land in foggy weather. The <a href="/wiki/Instrument_approach#Decision_height_or_altitude" title="Instrument approach">decision height</a> established for the approach system in Gda\xc5\x84sk is 30&#160;m (98&#160;ft), whereas the visibility minimum for pilots varies from 125&#160;m (410&#160;ft) to 300&#160;m (980&#160;ft), the higher value being required in the initial one-third of the runway\'s length.<sup id="cite_ref-newsilsii_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsilsii-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newsils2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsils2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the navigation facilities at the airport are supplemented with a modern <a href="/wiki/Wide_area_multilateration" title="Wide area multilateration">wide area multilateration</a> system.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The runway as well as taxiways are equipped with light aids system using <a href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode" title="Light-emitting diode">LED</a> technology. The lighting of the runway can be remotely controlled from the flight control tower. The runway has eight exit taxiways leading to five parking aprons where up to 18 middle-sized aircraft (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a> or <a href="/wiki/Airbus_321" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus 321">Airbus 321</a>) can be parked. The airport also has an extra apron used in winter for de-icing of aircraft. The deicing pad has been constructed in a way which allows keeping up ecological standards.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_terminals">Passenger terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Passenger terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Departures area at Terminal T2</div></div></div>\n<p>Gda\xc5\x84sk Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa Airport has two passengers terminals (T1 and T2), of which only Terminal T2 is fully functional. Terminal T1 was built in 1993 and is currently being used for departures (passport control and boarding) to countries which are not part of <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen Area</a>, mainly to Great Britain, Ireland and holiday destinations e.g. to Egypt. There are no check-in desks in Terminal T1 but passengers waiting for their flights can do the shopping at duty-free stores located close to the departure gates. The building is connected with the newer Terminal T2 with a bridge. The old building is planned to be torn down in the future when the new terminal is extended.\n</p><p>The new terminal, called Terminal T2, is a modern building with characteristic architectural elements, the roof imitating waves at sea being an example of them. It was constructed west of Terminal T1. The first part (departures only) was completed in 2012, and the arrival area started operations in September 2015. Since then, Terminal 1 has not been serving any arriving flights.\n</p><p>The overall area of Terminal 2 amounts to approx. 54,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (580,000&#160;sq&#160;ft). The terminal has a direct link to a railway station. It has 25 check-in desks, a highly modern luggage transport and sorting facility and a spacious departure area with gates. At the airport in Gda\xc5\x84sk, five <a href="/wiki/Jet_bridge" title="Jet bridge">jet bridges</a> are available for airlines to use. The terminal handled 3.3 million passengers in 2014 which was about 66% of its maximum capacity. However, after the completion of the new arrival area, the maximum capacity of the airport is estimated at 7 million passengers per year. The terminal offers services such as tourist information, shops, kiosks, bars, currency exchange desks, different car rentals and an <a href="/wiki/Airport_lounge" title="Airport lounge">executive lounge</a>. There is also a conference room and facilities for handling VIP passengers and <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> flights.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_development">Future development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gda%C5%84sk_Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Future development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>According to the airport authorities,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Gda\xc5\x84sk Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa Airport will be further developed in the next years. Plans include an installation of an even more modern landing system ILS CAT IIIb which will allow pilots to operate at very poor visibility caused by fog and bad weather.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Gda\xc5\x84sk Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa Airport will be the second airport in Poland, after Warsaw Chopin Airport, to have this facility.\n</p><p>The existing terminal is also planned to be extended despite the fact that it is new and can easily handle the current passengers flows. A new concourse will be constructed exclusively for departing flights. When it is finished, the old terminal building serving Non-Schengen flights will be either demolished or rebuilt for other purposes which will not be directly aimed at passenger services. The construction of the new part of the terminal is planned to be finished in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After the modernization and extension work, the new capacity of the Gda\xc5\x84sk Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa Airport will be at the estimated level of 9 million passengers annually.\n</p><p>Also, a completely new district of Gdansk is planned to be built directly next to the airport. It will be called Gdansk Airport City and it will consist mainly of new buildings with offices and services as well as hotels. The construction works are planned to begin in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, some facilities that will become a part of the Airport City in the future already exist. These include the Allcon Park offering office areas, as well as the first of the planned five buildings of BCB Business Park. The existing <a href="/wiki/Hampton_by_Hilton" title="Hampton by Hilton">Hampton by Hilton</a> hotel located opposite the airport terminal will also be part of the new Airport City district in Gdansk.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a title="Pomorska Kolej Metropolitalna">Pomorska Kolej Metropolitalna</a> (PKM, the \'Pomeranian Metropolitan Railway) connects Gda\xc5\x84sk Lech Wa\xc5\x82\xc4\x99sa Airport with <a Tickets are also sold in the trains by the conductors and they should be in this case purchased immediately after getting on the train. Regular tickets to and from Gda\xc5\x84sk (City ticket) cost 3,50 PLN one way. Regular tickets to/from Gdynia (Tricity ticket) cost 6,50 PLN one way. It is possible to change trains and to get to other areas of Tricity.
