id="Confusion_with_Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93Krzesiny_military_airport">Confusion with Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93Krzesiny military airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pozna%C5%84%E2%80%93%C5%81awica_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Confusion with Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93Krzesiny military airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93\xc5\x81awica airport has been confused by <a href="/wiki/Aviator" class="mw-redirect" title="Aviator">pilots</a> with a nearby <a href="/wiki/Airbase" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbase">airbase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84%E2%80%93Krzesiny_Airbase" class="mw-redirect" title="Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93Krzesiny Airbase">Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93Krzesiny Airbase</a> (ICAO code: <b>EPKS</b>), which also has a 2,500&#160;m (8,200&#160;ft) runway. The runways are at approximately the same orientation: \xc5\x81awica\'s is 11/29 (true heading: 108/288) and Krzesiny\'s is 12/30 (true heading: 117.9/297.9). The two runways lie in a nearly straight line, with Krzesiny coming up first on approaches from the east, the ones used most often. On the other hand, the Krzesiny airbase has two runways and lies southeast from the city centre, while Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93\xc5\x81awica lies just west of it.\n</p><p>One notable incident involving confusion between \xc5\x81awica and Krzesiny happened on 15 August 2006, when a <a href="/wiki/Sky_Airlines" title="Sky Airlines">Sky Airlines</a> aircraft - a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Next_Generation" title="Boeing 737 Next Generation">Boeing 737-800</a> running flight number SKY335 - mistook the runway at Krzesiny for the one being used in \xc5\x81awica, landing at the military base by accident.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The aircraft later took off on a repositioning flight to the correct airfield.\n</p><p>According to Krzysztof Krawcewicz, a pilot and the <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> of the Polish monthly <i><a href="https://pl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Przegl%C4%85d_Lotniczy_Aviation_Revue" class="extiw" title="pl:Przegl\xc4\x85d Lotniczy Aviation Revue">Przegl\xc4\x85d Lotniczy/Aviation Revue</a></i>, this was at least the seventh mistaken aircraft that landed at the Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93Krzesiny airfield in 2006 alone. He faults, among others, the "scandalous procedures which are in use by the <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">air traffic control</a> at Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93\xc5\x81awica" and the lack of <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a> use in controlling aircraft landing, which, although a radar system is present at \xc5\x81awica, has been shut off, most likely due to the nearby military base.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pozna%C5%84%E2%80%93%C5%81awica_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 10 June 1952, a <a href="/wiki/Petlyakov_Pe-2" title="Petlyakov Pe-2">Petlyakov Pe-2</a> bomber from the 21st Reconnaissance Regiment took off from \xc5\x81awica air base for a training flight, but crashed shortly thereafter near the <a href="/wiki/Warta" title="Warta">Warta</a> river as a result of engine failure. The crash killed the bomber\'s crew: <a href="/wiki/Chor%C4%85%C5%BCy" class="mw-redirect" title="Chor\xc4\x85\xc5\xbcy">chor\xc4\x85\xc5\xbcy</a> Zdzis\xc5\x82aw Lara (pilot), chor\xc4\x85\xc5\xbcy Stanis\xc5\x82aw Ku\xc4\x87 (navigator) and corporal J\xc3\xb3zef Bednarek (rear gunner/radio operator), as well as six civilians on the ground. Due to the fact that the aircraft was made in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, the crash was covered up by the Communist authorities and the official reports put the blame on the pilot instead of equipment. In 2008, a monument was unveiled at the crash site.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_ground_transportation">Public ground transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pozna%C5%84%E2%80%93%C5%81awica_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Public ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Pozna\xc5\x84 transit (MPK Pozna\xc5\x84) lines number 159 and 242 stop at the arrival area of the airport and provide a connection to <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84_G%C5%82%C3%B3wny_railway_station" title="Pozna\xc5\x84 G\xc5\x82\xc3\xb3wny railway station">Pozna\xc5\x84 G\xc5\x82\xc3\xb3wny railway station</a>. The trip takes approximately 20 minutes.
