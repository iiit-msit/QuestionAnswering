class="external text" href="https://www.executiveoffice-ni.gov.uk/">www<wbr />.executiveoffice-ni<wbr />.gov<wbr />.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>First Minister</b> and <b>deputy First Minister</b> of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> are the joint <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">heads of government</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> and have overall responsibility for the running of the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Office_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Executive Office (Northern Ireland)">Executive Office</a>.\n</p><p>The two positions have the same governmental power, resulting in a <a href="/wiki/Duumvirate" class="mw-redirect" title="Duumvirate">duumvirate</a>; despite the name, the deputy First Minister is not subordinate to the First Minister. Created under the terms of the 1998 <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a>, both were initially nominated and appointed by members of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> on a joint <a href="/wiki/Ticket_(election)" title="Ticket (election)">ticket</a> by a <a href="/wiki/Cross-community_vote" title="Cross-community vote">cross-community vote</a>, using <a href="/wiki/Consociationalism" title="Consociationalism">consociational</a> principles. That process was changed following the 2006 <a href="/wiki/St_Andrews_Agreement" title="St Andrews Agreement">St Andrews Agreement</a>, meaning that the First Minister is nominated by the largest party, and the deputy First Minister is nominated by the largest party in the second largest community designation.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a First Minister and deputy First Minister share equal responsibilities within government, and their decisions are made jointly. The First Minister is, though, the first to greet official visitors to Northern Ireland and shares the same title as their counterparts in <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_of_Scotland" title="First Minister of Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_of_Wales" title="First Minister of Wales">Wales</a>.\n</p><p>Specifically, they are tasked with co-chairing meetings of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a>, "dealing with and co-ordinating" the work of the Executive, and the response of the administration to external relationships.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The First Minister and deputy First Minister agree the agenda of Executive meetings<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and can jointly determine "significant or controversial matters" to be considered by the Executive.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The ministers\' policy responsibilities include:<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Economic_policy" title="Economic policy">economic policy</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Equality_before_the_law" title="Equality before the law">equality before the law</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> issues</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a></li>\n<li>the machinery of government (including the Ministerial Code)</li>\n<li>public appointments policy</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Standards_in_Public_Life" title="Committee on Standards in Public Life">standards in public life</a></li></ul>\n<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Junior_Minister_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Junior Minister (Northern Ireland)">junior ministers</a> assist the First Minister and deputy First Minister in carrying out the work of OFMDFM.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They are jointly accountable to the First Minister and deputy First Minister. The incumbent junior ministers were <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Lyons" title="Gordon Lyons">Gordon Lyons</a> (<a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Declan_Kearney" title="Declan Kearney">Declan Kearney</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a>).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Election">Election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As originally established under the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a>, the First Minister was elected by the Assembly on a joint ticket with the deputy First Minister through a <a href="/wiki/Cross-community_vote" title="Cross-community vote">cross-community vote</a>. It was created to enable the leaders of the main <a href="/wiki/Unionism_in_Ireland" title="Unionism in Ireland">unionist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_nationalism" title="Irish nationalism">nationalist</a> parties to work together, with guaranteed joint representation of both main communities. For the purposes of a cross-community vote, MLAs were designated as unionist, nationalist, or other.\n</p><p>The nominees for First Minister and deputy First Minister required the support of:\n</p>\n<ul><li>a majority of the MLAs voting in the election overall;</li>\n<li>a majority of the designated unionist MLAs voting; and</li>\n<li>a majority of the designated nationalist MLAs voting.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>This procedure was used on 2 December 1999 to elect <a href="/wiki/David_Trimble" title="David Trimble">David Trimble</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a>, UUP) and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Mallon" title="Seamus Mallon">Seamus Mallon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">Social Democratic and Labour Party</a>, SDLP). Following several suspensions of the Northern Ireland Executive, Trimble was not re-elected on 2 November 2001 due to opposition from other unionist parties. He was subsequently re-elected alongside <a href="/wiki/Mark_Durkan" title="Mark Durkan">Mark Durkan</a> (SDLP) on 6 November 2001; on that occasion, three <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party of Northern Ireland</a> MLAs redesignated from \'other\' to \'unionist\' to support Trimble\'s nomination.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/St_Andrews_Agreement" title="St Andrews Agreement">St Andrews Agreement</a> in October 2006, the appointment procedure was changed to allow for:\n</p>\n<ul><li>a First Minister nominated by the largest party of the largest designation;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>a deputy First Minister nominated by the largest party of the second largest designation.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>This procedure, which removed the need for a joint ticket between the unionist <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a> and the nationalist <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a> party, was used to appoint <a href="/wiki/Ian_Paisley" title="Ian Paisley">Ian Paisley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_McGuinness" title="Martin McGuinness">Martin McGuinness</a> on 8 May 2007. It was again used to appoint <a href="/wiki/Peter_Robinson_(Northern_Ireland_politician)" title="Peter Robinson (Northern Ireland politician)">Peter Robinson</a> (DUP) alongside McGuinness on 5 June 2008 and again on 12 May 2011; and to appoint <a href="/wiki/Arlene_Foster" title="Arlene Foster">Arlene Foster</a> (DUP) alongside McGuinness on 11 January 2016.\n</p><p>The new rules from 2006 also state that, if the largest party of the largest designation happens not to also be the largest party in the assembly overall, then the appointment procedure would be as follows:\n</p>\n<ul><li>a First Minister nominated by the largest party overall;</li>\n<li>a deputy First Minister nominated by the largest party of the largest designation.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>As of 2020, this latter method has never yet been used.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Justice_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Department of Justice (Northern Ireland)">Minister of Justice</a> is now the only <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> minister elected by cross-community vote. All other ministers are party appointees, with parties taking turns using the <a href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_method" title="D&#39;Hondt method">D\'Hondt method</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vacancy">Vacancy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Vacancy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The First Minister or deputy First Minister may also appoint another <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> Minister to exercise the functions of the office during a vacancy; currently for a continuous period up to six weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Vacancies have occurred on four occasions to date:\n</p>\n<dl><dt>First Minister</dt></dl>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sir_Reg_Empey" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Reg Empey">Sir Reg Empey</a> for <a href="/wiki/David_Trimble" title="David Robinson</a> (10 September 2015 \xe2\x80\x93 20 October 2015)</li></ul>\n<dl><dt>Deputy First Minister</dt></dl>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/John_O%27Dowd" title="John O&#39;Dowd">John O\'Dowd</a> for <a href="/wiki/Martin_McGuinness" title="Martin McGuinness">Martin McGuinness</a> (20 September 2011 \xe2\x80\x93 31 October 2011)</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles_in_Irish_and_Ulster_Scots">Titles in Irish and Ulster Scots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Titles in Irish and Ulster Scots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the Irish language, the literal translation of these positions is "C\xc3\xa9ad-Aire agus an leas Ch\xc3\xa9ad-Aire". The titles appear in both English and Irish in published literature by the North-South Ministerial Council, one of the "mutually inter-dependent" institutions laid out in the Good Friday Agreement, along with the Northern Ireland Assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-northsouthministerialcouncil.org_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-northsouthministerialcouncil.org-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Various ways of translating the titles "First Minister and deputy First Minister" into the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Scots_dialects" class="mw-redirect" title="Ulster Scots dialects">Ulster Scots dialects</a> have been attested in official communications, including <i lang="sco" title="Scots-language text">Heid M\xc3\xa4nnyst\xc3\xa8r an tha Heid M\xc3\xa4nnyst\xc3\xa8r depute</i>, <i lang="sco" title="Scots-language text">First Meinister an First Meinister depute</i>,<sup href="#cite_note-nsmc2010-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <i lang="sco" title="Scots-language text">First Meenister an First Meenister depute</i><sup id="cite_ref-nsmc2009_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nsmc2009-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and <i lang="sco" title="Scots-language text">First Minister an First Minister depute</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-nsmc2008_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nsmc2008-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Capitalisation_of_.22deputy.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Capitalisation_of_&quot;deputy&quot;">Capitalisation of "deputy"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Capitalisation of &quot;deputy&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The second position has been spelt "Deputy" or "deputy" First Minister, due to differing preferences by civil servants (and potentially ministers), although the spelling of the title has no constitutional consequences in practice.\n</p><p>The first two holders of the office, <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Mallon" title="Seamus Mallon">Seamus Mallon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Durkan" title="Mark Durkan">Mark Durkan</a>, were both referred to during their periods of office as "Deputy First Minister", with a capital D. In the Good Friday Agreement, also known as the Belfast Agreement, which established the executive in Northern Ireland, the two positions are spelt "First Minister and Deputy First Minister" (with a capital D).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> This was also adopted in 1999 for the logo of the OFMDFM. Several weeks after Martin McGuinness took up office as Deputy First Minister in 2007, civil servants in his department began asking the Assembly\'s <a href="/wiki/Hansard" title="Hansard">Hansard</a> team to replace the capital D with a lower-case d, pointing out that the title was spelled this way in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a>, the legislation which established the office. Some believe that the case was changed to highlight the fact that the position holds the same power as the position of First Minister, but a spokesman for McGuinness said that neither McGuinness nor his advisers had asked for the change. <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Speaker of the Northern Ireland Assembly">Speaker</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Hay,_Baron_Hay_of_Ballyore" class="mw-redirect" title="William Hay, Baron Hay of Ballyore">William Hay</a> ordered the change and the capital D was dropped from Hansard references.\n</p><p>Officials edited the department\'s archive of press releases to make that change (despite its use by Mallon and Durkan when in office) but the capital D still appears in some places, and a spokesman confirmed on 20 March 2008 that the office had "no plans" to change the OFMDFM logo. However, the Assembly committee that scrutinises their work is now listed as the "Committee for the Office of the First Minister and deputy First Minister". Ultimately it was decided that McGuinness should be the deputy First Minister, unless all the other letters in the title are in capitals. Confusion isn\'t completely resolved however; if McGuinness wrote to the Assembly committee that scrutinised his work, his note would have a letterhead that comes from the Office of the First Minister and Deputy First Minister, but he would get a reply back from the Committee for the Office of the First Minister and deputy First Minister.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In official language, the positions are sometimes abbreviated to <b>FM/dFM</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_titles_for_the_deputy_First_Minister">Alternative titles for the deputy First Minister</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Alternative titles for the deputy First Minister">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Sinn F\xc3\xa9in started using the phrases "Joint First Minister" and "Co-First Minister" in 2009 to describe the deputy First Minister to highlight the fact that the First Minister and deputy First Minister operated in tandem. Martin McGuinness used the term Joint First Minister himself when he arrived for a meeting of the <a href="/wiki/North/South_Ministerial_Council" title="North/South Ministerial Council">North/South Ministerial Council</a> in February 2009; the DUP denounced the term as "republican speak" and it is not used in legislation.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc7895470_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc7895470-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jim_Allister" title="Jim Allister">Jim Allister</a>, the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Unionist_Voice" title="Traditional Unionist Voice">Traditional Unionist Voice</a>, has long been calling Robinson and McGuinness "the joint first ministers", to highlight the joint nature of the office and to demonstrate his opposition to the power-sharing arrangements.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc7895470_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc7895470-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>With the restoration of power sharing in 2020 Sinn F\xc3\xa9in started describing the position as "joint head of government".<sup McGuinness</a> in 2008.</div></div></div>\n<p>Following <a href="/wiki/1998_Belfast_Agreement_referendum" class="mw-redirect" title="1998 Belfast Agreement referendum">a referendum</a> on the Belfast Agreement on 23 May 1998 and subsequent the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> was established in 1998 with a view to assuming devolved powers from the <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament">Westminster Parliament</a>. On 1 July 1998, <a href="/wiki/David_Trimble" title="David Trimble">David Trimble</a> (UUP) and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Mallon" title="Seamus Mallon">Seamus Mallon</a> (SDLP) were nominated and elected First Minister and Deputy First Minister designates respectively. Eventually, on 2 December 1999, power was devolved and Trimble and Mallon formally took office as joint heads of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a>.\n</p><p>On 6 November 2001, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Durkan" title="Mark Durkan">Mark Durkan</a> (SDLP) became Deputy First Minister after Seamus Mallon\'s retirement. The Executive and the two positions were suspended between 15 October 2002 and 8 May 2007 following a breakdown in trust between the parties.\n</p><p>On 8 May 2007, <a href="/wiki/Ian_Paisley" title="Ian Paisley">Ian Paisley</a> (DUP) and Martin McGuinness (Sinn F\xc3\xa9in) were nominated and appointed First Minister and Deputy First Minister respectively in line with the announcement by their two parties on 26 March 2007.\n</p><p>Paisley announced his intention to resign on 4 March 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> His deputy as DUP leader, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Robinson_(Northern_Ireland_politician)" title="Peter Robinson (Northern Ireland politician)">Peter Robinson</a> was ratified as Democratic Unionist Party leader designate on 17 April 2008<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and took office in that role on 31 May 2008. As leader-designate of the largest designated <a href="/wiki/Irish_unionism" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish unionism">unionist</a> party in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> he was also in effect the First Minister designate and became First Minister on 5 June 2008. Arlene Foster succeeded Peter Robinson as DUP leader on 18 December 2015, and as First Minister on 11 January 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Separately, between 12 February 2000<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and 30 May 2000,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and 15 October 2002<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and 8 May 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> however, devolution was suspended, and along with it the offices of First Minister and deputy First Minister. The Office of the First Minister and deputy First Minister became the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a>. There were also two 24-hour periods of suspension on 11 August 2001<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> to allow timetables for negotiation to restart.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Devolution was
