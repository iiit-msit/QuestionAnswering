(Declan, Shane and Cathal)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b> Thomas Anthony "Tony" Ryan</b> (2 February 1936 \xe2\x80\x93 3 October 2007) was an Irish billionaire, philanthropist and businessman.\n</p><p>Through his establishment of <a href="/wiki/Guinness_Peat_Aviation" title="Guinness Peat Aviation">Guinness Peat Aviation</a> in 1975 he began a course of events which ultimately led to the development of the international <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_leasing" class="mw-redirect" title="Aircraft leasing">aircraft leasing</a> industry, although he is best known in the public mind as the founder of the eponymous <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> with Christopher Ryan and Liam Lonergan.<sup id="cite_ref-sunday_times_obit_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunday_times_obit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Ryanair was believed to be the main source of his wealth in later life: the company is now one of the biggest airlines in Europe and is valued at over 15 billion Euros as of December 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ryan held <a href="/wiki/Honorary_doctorate" class="mw-redirect" title="Honorary doctorate">honorary doctorates</a> from several universities, including <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Ireland,_Galway" class="mw-redirect" title="National University of Ireland, Galway">National University of Ireland, Galway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Limerick" title="University of Limerick">University of Limerick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_career">Business career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Ryan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Thurles" title="Thurles">Thurles</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Tipperary" title="County Tipperary">County Tipperary</a>, Ryan worked for <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a>, before going on to found their aircraft leasing arm, <a href="/wiki/Wet_lease" class="mw-redirect" title="Wet lease">wet-leasing</a> out their aircraft in the quieter winter months.\n</p><p>In 1975, with Aer Lingus and the Guinness Peat Group, he founded Guinness Peat Aviation (later GPA Group), an aircraft leasing company, with a $50,000 investment.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> GPA grew to be the world\'s biggest aircraft lessor, worth $4 billion at its peak. But its value dramatically collapsed in 1992 after the cancellation of its planned <a href="/wiki/Initial_Public_Offering" class="mw-redirect" title="Initial Public Offering">IPO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Ryan made \xe2\x82\xac55m from the sale of <a href="/wiki/AerFi" class="mw-redirect" title="AerFi">AerFi</a> (the successor to GPA) in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ryan was a <a href="/wiki/Tax_exile" title="Tax exile">tax exile</a> who lived in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> but also owned a <a href="/wiki/Stud_farm" title="Stud farm">stud farm</a> near his home in Dolla <a href="/wiki/County_Tipperary" title="County Tipperary">County Tipperary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was the 7th wealthiest individual from Ireland in the <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List_2007" title="Sunday Times Rich List 2007">Sunday Times Rich List 2007</a> with over \xe2\x82\xac1.5bn(\xc2\xa31bn).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ryan over the years helped nurture two successful business prot\xc3\xa9g\xc3\xa9s \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Denis_O%27Brien" title="Denis O&#39;Brien">Denis O\'Brien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_O%27Leary_(Ryanair)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael O&#39;Leary (Ryanair)">Michael O\'Leary</a> \xe2\x80\x93 both of whom became billionaires.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Ryan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Ryan was an active and innovative funder of university education in Ireland. He donated a marine science institute to <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Ireland,_Galway" class="mw-redirect" title="National University of Ireland, Galway">NUI Galway</a> in 1993 which was named the Martin Ryan Marine Science Institute in honour of his father.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He showed interest in marine science and aquaculture development in the west of Ireland. He also funded The Ryan Academy for Entrepreneurship at the <a href="/wiki/Citywest" title="Citywest">Citywest</a> park, that is run by <a href="/wiki/Dublin_City_University" title="Dublin City University">Dublin City University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2001, Ryan acquired <a href="/wiki/Castleton_Farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Castleton Farm">Castleton Farm</a> near <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Kentucky" title="Lexington, Kentucky">Lexington, Kentucky</a> from the Van Lennep Family Trust. Ryan renamed it Castleton Lyons and undertook renovations to the property while returning to its original roots as a thoroughbred operation.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the time of his death he owned 16% of <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiger Airways">Tiger Airways</a>, a discount carrier based in Singapore which was founded in December 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_Honors">Awards and Honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Ryan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards and Honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>1994, Order of the Aztec Eagle<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>2002, Golden Plate Award of the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Achievement" title="Academy of Achievement">American Academy of Achievement</a> - presented at the International Achievement Summit in Dublin, Ireland<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>2012, National Aviation Award - the inaugural award was presented to his family by Minister for Transport Leo Varadkar<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Ryan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Ryan, who lived at <a href="/wiki/Lyons_Demesne" title="Lyons Demesne">Lyons Demesne</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ardclough" title="Ardclough">Ardclough</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Kildare" title="County Kildare">County Kildare</a>, died on 3 October 2007, aged 71, following an 18-month battle with <a href="/wiki/Pancreatic_cancer" title="Pancreatic cancer">pancreatic cancer</a>. He had other homes in London, <a href="/wiki/Castleton_Lyons" title="Castleton Lyons">Castleton Lyons</a> stud in <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_Lascombes" title="Ch\xc3\xa2teau Lascombes">Ch\xc3\xa2teau Lascombes</a> near <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a> and on <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> His eldest son, Cathal, died just three months later, aged 48, after being diagnosed with cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
