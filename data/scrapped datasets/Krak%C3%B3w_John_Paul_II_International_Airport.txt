class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Krak%C3%B3w_John_Paul_II_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Krak%C3%B3w_John_Paul_II_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport opened for civil aviation in 1964. The Balice airport was a military site until 28 February 1968. Four years later, the first passenger terminal was built there.\n</p><p>In 1988, the authorities decided to build a new terminal that was opened for public use in 1993. In 1995, the entire apron was modernized.\n</p><p>In 1995, the airport\'s name was changed from Krak\xc3\xb3w\xe2\x80\x93Balice Airport to John Paul II International Airport Krak\xc3\xb3w\xe2\x80\x93Balice, to honor <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a>, who spent many years of his life in Krak\xc3\xb3w and had served as <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Krak%C3%B3w" title="Archbishop of Krak\xc3\xb3w">Archbishop of Krak\xc3\xb3w</a> from 1963 until his elevation to the Papacy in 1978. For marketing reasons, the official name was further "streamlined" on 4 September 2007 as <i>Krak\xc3\xb3w Airport im. Jana Paw\xc5\x82a II</i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_2000s">Development since the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Krak%C3%B3w_John_Paul_II_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development since the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport was modernized once more in 2002, and since then new international connections have been established.\n</p><p>In 2003, when <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Irish</a> low-cost carrier <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> became interested in starting a service from the John Paul II International Airport, the airport authorities refused to reduce the <a href="/wiki/Landing_fee" title="Landing fee">landing fees</a>. In response, the regional authorities of Krak\xc3\xb3w and <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Poland_Voivodeship" title="Lesser Poland Voivodeship">Lesser Poland Voivodeship</a> decided to build a new airport near the existing one, using the infrastructure of the military airbase adjacent to the shared runway. Finally an agreement was reached, and the existing airport was opened to Ryanair and other low-cost carriers such as <a href="/wiki/Germanwings" title="Germanwings">Germanwings</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Centralwings" title="Centralwings">Centralwings</a>.\n</p><p>On 1 March 2007, a separate domestic terminal (T2) was opened. At that time, plans were underway to begin construction of a new terminal.\n</p><p>A seven-story parking garage opposite T1 became fully operational in May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 12 December 2012, Irish low-cost carrier Ryanair announced it would be opening its second Polish base in Krak\xc3\xb3w basing two <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Next_Generation" title="Boeing 737 Next Generation">Boeing 737-800</a> aircraft at the airport from 31 March 2013, which allows the carrier to increase the number of the routes from Krak\xc3\xb3w to 31.\n</p><p>Krak\xc3\xb3w Airport is the second busiest airport in the country after <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Chopin_Airport" title="Warsaw Chopin Airport">Warsaw Chopin Airport</a>. The airport has good growth prospects, as almost 8 million people live within 100&#160;km (62&#160;mi) of it. The airport also has a favorable location on the network of existing and planned <a href="/wiki/Motorway" class="mw-redirect" title="Motorway">motorways</a> in this region of Poland.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Krak%C3%B3w_John_Paul_II_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Krak%C3%B3w_John_Paul_II_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>11 April 2013 saw the beginning of construction works of a new airport terminal, which is adjacent to the existing old terminal building. The works on the new terminal were completed in December 2016. The terminal serves all-year-round, 24 hours a day, both domestic as well as international flights. The expected maximum capacity of the terminal is up to 8 million passengers handled in a year (over twice as much as the airport served in 2012). It is also possible to handle transfer passengers irrespective of the routes (<a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area#Air_travel" title="Schengen Area">Schengen</a>/Non-Schengen destinations). The terminal has a new luggage handling system and a roofed footbridge connecting the terminal to a hotel, a multi-level parking lot and the railway station, with direct railway link to <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w_G%C5%82%C3%B3wny_railway_station" title="Krak\xc3\xb3w G\xc5\x82\xc3\xb3wny railway station">Krak\xc3\xb3w G\xc5\x82\xc3\xb3wny</a> by <a href="/wiki/Koleje_Ma%C5%82opolskie" title="Koleje Ma\xc5\x82opolskie">Koleje Ma\xc5\x82opolskie</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Krak%C3%B3w_John_Paul_II_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has one concrete <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a>, number 07/25, 2,550&#160;m \xc3\x97&#160;60&#160;m (8,366&#160;ft
