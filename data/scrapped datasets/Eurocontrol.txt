class="plainlinksneverexpand"><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.eurocontrol.int">http://www.eurocontrol.int</a></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>European Organisation for the Safety of Air Navigation</b>, commonly known as <b>Eurocontrol</b> (stylized <i>EUROCONTROL</i>), is an <a href="/wiki/International_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="International organisation">international organisation</a> working to achieve safe and seamless <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_management" title="Air traffic management">air traffic management</a> across <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. Founded in 1960, Eurocontrol currently has 41 member states and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It has several local sites as well, including R&amp;D activities in <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%A9tigny-sur-Orge" title="Br\xc3\xa9tigny-sur-Orge">Br\xc3\xa9tigny-sur-Orge</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, the Institute of Air Navigation Training (IANS) in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, and the Maastricht Upper Area Control Centre (MUAC) in <a href="/wiki/Maastricht" title="Maastricht">Maastricht</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">the Netherlands</a>. The organisation employs approximately two thousand people, and operates with an annual budget in excess of half a billion <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Report_2014_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Report_2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Although Eurocontrol is not an <a href="/wiki/Agency_of_the_European_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Agency of the European Union">agency of the European Union</a>, the EU has delegated parts of its <a href="/wiki/Single_European_Sky" title="Single European Sky">Single European Sky</a> regulations to Eurocontrol, making it the central organisation for coordination and planning of air traffic control for all of Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The EU itself is a signatory of Eurocontrol and all EU member states are presently also members of Eurocontrol.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The organisation works with national authorities, <a href="/wiki/Air_navigation_service_provider" title="Air navigation service provider">air navigation service providers</a>, civil and <a href="/wiki/Military_airspace" title="Military airspace">military airspace</a> users, <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airports</a>, and other organisations. Its activities involve all gate-to-gate air navigation service operations: strategic and tactical flow management, controller training, regional control of airspace, safety-proofed technologies and procedures, and collection of air navigation charges.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurocontrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg/280px-Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="280" height="187" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg/420px-Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg/560px-Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Eurocontrol_hoofdkwartier_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Headquarters of Eurocontrol in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The Eurocontrol Convention was signed in 1960 and ratified in 1963. Before the Convention entered into force in 1963, there were already indications that the matter of national sovereignty would complicate the full implementation of the organisation\'s founding mission. The first European plan for a harmonised <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">air traffic control</a> (ATC) system, proposed in 1962, was beset by the refusal of both <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a> to comply, largely due to reasons closely linked with their national military airspace control. The other four original members (the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Republic of Germany">Federal Republic of Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>) agreed in 1964 to set up a single international air traffic control centre to manage their upper airspace, settling in the Dutch city of <a href="/wiki/Maastricht" title="Maastricht">Maastricht</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-History_Book_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History_Book-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> at the time expressed concern about the lack of clear intergovernmental agreements to ensure common air traffic control services across the continent. In 1979, Eurocontrol signed a working cooperation agreement with the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>, attempting to create a synergy of Eurocontrol\'s technical expertise and EU\'s regulatory authorities. Several initiatives originating in this period become a lasting element of the organisation, such as the Eurocontrol forecasting service, which became STATFOR, as well as the Aeronautical Information Service. By 1986, the pressures on the European ATC network was so big that a new, wider mandate was already being considered for Eurocontrol, with much of the initiative coming from <a href="/wiki/European_Civil_Aviation_Conference" title="European Civil Aviation Conference">ECAC</a>\xe2\x80\x99s Ministers of Transport.  Subsequently, ECAC urged all of its member states to join Eurocontrol.<sup id="cite_ref-History_Book_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History_Book-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL,_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL%2C_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg/280px-Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL%2C_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg" decoding="async" width="280" height="186" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL%2C_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg/420px-Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL%2C_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL%2C_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg/560px-Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL%2C_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4912" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Meeting_of_EUROCONTROL,_the_European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A meeting of Eurocontrol members</div></div></div>\n<p>A revised Eurocontrol Convention was signed in 1997, renewing the organisation\'s optimism for greater political support, surpassing the original vision of the 1960 Convention. In June 1998, Eurocontrol, the <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">European Space Agency</a> (ESA) and the European Commission (EC) also signed an agreement formalising cooperation in the realm of satellite navigation systems and services. In 1999 the European Commission presented its plan for a <a href="/wiki/Single_European_Sky" title="Single European Sky">Single European Sky</a> (SES) to the European Parliament, followed by two high-Level groups (HLG). The HLG reports on SES led to the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/European_Aviation_Safety_Agency" title="European Aviation Safety Agency">European Aviation Safety Agency</a> (EASA) and reinforced the European Commission\'s role as the sole European aviation safety regulator, while acknowledging Eurocontrol\'s technical expertise in the implementation of said regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-History_Book_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History_Book-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The early 2000s were marred by several fatal accidents in Europe, such as the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Linate_Airport_disaster" title="Linate Airport disaster">Linate Airport disaster</a> and the 2002 <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Cberlingen_mid-air_collision" class="mw-redirect" title="\xc3\x9cberlingen mid-air collision">\xc3\x9cberlingen mid-air collision</a>, both of which were related to air traffic navigation shortcomings. The pressure was further compounded by the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, increasing the need for a rapid Europe-wide regulatory and coordinating body. By May 2003, Eurocontrol and <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> had signed a memorandum of cooperation, followed by a similar memorandum with the European Commission in December 2003. In February 2004, Eurocontrol started work on first mandates from the European Commission and in April 2004, it adopted the Single European Sky Regulations (Package 1). In March 2006, the European Commission\'s Single European Sky ATM Research (SESAR) Program was launched by the Stakeholder Consultation Group (SCG) under Eurocontrol\'s aegis.<sup id="cite_ref-History_Book_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History_Book-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Functions_and_centres">Functions and centres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurocontrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Functions and centres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Eurocontrol provides a set of different services:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Maastricht Upper Area Control Centre (MUAC)</li>\n<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Network_Manager_Operations_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Network Manager Operations Centre (page does not exist)">Network Manager Operations Centre</a> (NMOC) \xe2\x80\x93 coordinates flight plans and actual traffic.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/European_AIS_Database" title="European AIS Database">EAD</a> \xe2\x80\x93 centralised access to AIS information.</li>\n<li>Central Route Charges Office (CRCO) \xe2\x80\x93 collects en-route (and aerodrome approach) charges on behalf of Air Navigation Service providers (<a href="/wiki/Air_Navigation_Service_Provider" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Navigation Service Provider">ANSPs</a>).</li>\n<li>Eurocontrol Experimental Centre (EEC) \xe2\x80\x93 ATM research, simulations, etc.</li>\n<li>Institute of Air Navigation Services (IANS) \xe2\x80\x93 training and e-learning.</li>\n<li>Eurocontrol Safety Regulatory Requirement (ESARRs) \xe2\x80\x93 basis requirements for certification and designation according to <i>EC regulation 2096/2005</i>.</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maastricht_Upper_Area_Control_Centre">Maastricht Upper Area Control Centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurocontrol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Maastricht Upper Area Control Centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Eurocontrol\'s Maastricht Upper Area Control Centre (MUAC), ICAO designator EDYY,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> located at <a href="/wiki/Maastricht_Aachen_Airport" title="Maastricht Aachen Airport">Maastricht Aachen Airport</a>, provides <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">air traffic control</a> for traffic above 24,500&#160;ft over <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and north-west <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. In 2017 it became the first multinational, cross-border, civil-military, air navigation service provider since it integrated the military air traffic control of the German and Dutch upper airspace.\n</p><p>It is the third busiest upper area <a href="/wiki/Area_control_center" title="Area control center">area control centre</a> (ACC) in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> after the <a href="/wiki/London_Area_Control_Centre" title="London Area Control Centre">London Area Control Centre</a> and Karlsruhe ACC in terms of traffic numbers, but the first in terms of flight hours and distance.\n</p><p>MUAC has put in operation innovative technology and productivity enhancements: a new generation Flight Data Processing System,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Integrated Flow Management Position,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Short_Term_Conflict_Alert" class="mw-redirect" title="Short Term Conflict Alert">Short Term Conflict Alert</a> (STCA), <a href="/wiki/Controller_Pilot_Data_Link_Communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Controller Pilot Data Link Communications">Controller Pilot Data Link Communications</a> (CPDLC) and stripless controller working positions.\n</p><p>Typically, air traffic control sectors at MUAC can handle 55 or more flights per hour. The average flight duration is approximately 21 minutes and typically 80% of the traffic is climbing from or descending to the major European airports of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>.\nMaastricht UAC has undoubtedly one of the most complex airspace structures in the world and the traffic flow (up to 5,670 aircraft a day) can be disrupted by the many surrounding military airspaces.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership_criteria">Membership criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
