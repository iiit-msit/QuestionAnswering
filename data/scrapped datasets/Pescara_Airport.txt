href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LIBP</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a>, Italy. It is located approximately 4&#160;km (2.5 miles) from the centre of Pescara, about 180&#160;km (112 miles) from Rome, a 2-hour drive by car on a motorway across the <a href="/wiki/Appennine_mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Appennine mountains">Appennine mountains</a>. The airport is located on the state road 5 <a href="/wiki/Via_Tiburtina_Valeria" class="mw-redirect" title="Via Tiburtina Valeria">Via Tiburtina Valeria</a> and is well connected to important road (<a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A25_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A25 (Italy)">Autostrada A25</a>, <a href="/wiki/A14_motorway_(Italy)" class="mw-redirect" title="A14 motorway (Italy)">Autostrada A14</a>, SS714 Tangenziale di Pescara) and railway connections (<a href="/wiki/Rome%E2%80%93Sulmona%E2%80%93Pescara_railway" title="Rome\xe2\x80\x93Sulmona\xe2\x80\x93Pescara railway">Rome\xe2\x80\x93Sulmona\xe2\x80\x93Pescara railway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_railway" title="Adriatic railway">Adriatic railway</a>).\n</p><p>Being the only international airport in the <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">Abruzzo</a> region, it plays a fundamental role for the transportation and aerial connection of the area and for that of neighbouring regions, catching people also from <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marche" title="Marche">Marche</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gargano" title="Gargano">Gargano</a> area. The airport has seen a steady increase in the number of transit passengers over the years, mainly due to a growth in low-cost airlines and flights. The terminal built in 1996 has been extended in 2011 and recently restructured in 2018.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" class="toclevel-1 tocsection-19"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">8</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The history of the airport starts in 1917 when, after the bombing of <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a> by bombers of the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Aviation_Troops" title="Austro-Hungarian Aviation Troops">k.u.k. Luftfahrtruppen</a>, the aerial component of the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Army" title="Austro-Hungarian Army">Austro-Hungarian Army</a>, along the <a href="/wiki/Via_Tiburtina_Valeria" class="mw-redirect" title="Via Tiburtina Valeria">Via Tiburtina Valeria</a> road a military airfield is built by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Italian_Army" title="Royal Italian Army">Regio Esercito</a>, which arrived on 26 October 1917 with two <a href="/w/index.php?title=Farman_14&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Farman 14 (page does not exist)">Farman 14</a>. On 31 July 1918 the 302\xc2\xb0 Squadron is formed, equipped with biplanes <a href="/wiki/Ansaldo_SVA" title="Ansaldo SVA">Ansaldo SVA</a>, among the best fighter-bombers of the time.\n</p><p>From 1921, the airport has been named after Pasquale Liberi, an aviator friend of the famous poet <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_d%27Annunzio" class="mw-redirect" title="Gabriele d&#39;Annunzio">Gabriele d\'Annunzio</a>. In 1927 the airport was extended to 50 hectares and modernized.\n</p><p>In the 1930s, with the start of commercial aviation, the airline <a href="/wiki/Societ%C3%A0_Aerea_Mediterranea" title="Societ\xc3\xa0 Aerea Mediterranea">Societ\xc3\xa0 Aerea Mediterranea</a> (SAM) started in 1933 a triweekly service from Pescara to <a href="/wiki/Rome_Urbe_Airport" title="Rome Urbe Airport">Rome Urbe Airport</a>, with an intermediate stop at the airfield of Piana di Bagno near <a href="/wiki/L%27Aquila" title="L&#39;Aquila">L\'Aquila</a>.\n</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/WWII" class="mw-redirect" title="WWII">WWII</a>, the first airline to restart commercial service was Societ\xc3\xa0 anonima di navigazione aerea transadriatica (Transadriatica), which inaugurated the service Pescara-<a href="/wiki/Rome_Urbe_Airport" title="Rome Urbe Airport">Roma-Urbe</a> on 5 May 1947 using a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-3" title="Douglas DC-3">Douglas DC-3</a>. At the same time, Transadriatica started another interesting route: <a href="/wiki/Venice_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Venice Airport">Venezia</a>-<a href="/wiki/Marche_Airport" title="Marche Airport">Ancona</a>-<a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a>-<a href="/wiki/Brindisi_Airport" title="Brindisi Airport">Brindisi</a>-<a href="/wiki/Catania_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Catania Airport">Catania</a>. Furthermore, the airline <a href="/wiki/Avio_Linee_Italiane" title="Avio Linee Italiane">Avio Linee Italiane</a> decided to start a route connecting North to South Italy for the first time: <a href="/wiki/Milan_Linate_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Milan Linate Airport">Milan</a>-<a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a>-<a href="/wiki/Foggia_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Foggia Airport">Foggia</a>-<a href="/wiki/Bari_Karol_Wojtyla_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Bari Karol Wojtyla Airport">Bari</a>-<a href="/wiki/Brindisi_Airport" title="Brindisi Airport">Brindisi</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1960s">1960s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1960s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Itavia" title="Itavia">Itavia</a>, constituted in 1958 to operate between secondary airports not served by <a href="/wiki/Alitalia-Linee_Aeree_Italiane" class="mw-redirect" title="Alitalia-Linee Aeree Italiane">Alitalia</a>, opened its first route Pescara-<a href="/wiki/Rome_Urbe_Airport" title="Rome Urbe Airport">Roma-Urbe</a> on 15 July 1959 with a 8-seats <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Dove" title="De Havilland Dove">de Havilland Dove</a>. With the development of Itavia also the number of routes increased between the end of 1960s and beginning of 1970s, including <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Roma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancona" title="Ancona">Ancona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crotone" title="Crotone">Crotone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forl%C3%AC" title="Forl\xc3\xac">Forl\xc3\xac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lecce" title="Lecce">Lecce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bergamo" title="Bergamo">Bergamo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treviso" title="Treviso">Treviso</a>-<a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venezia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s">1970s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1970s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1973, the English historian <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Barrymore_Halpenny" title="Bruce Barrymore Halpenny">Bruce Barrymore Halpenny</a>, then living in Abruzzo, organised a <a href="/wiki/British_Caledonian" title="British Caledonian">British Caledonian</a> <a href="/wiki/BAC_111" class="mw-redirect" title="BAC 111">BAC 111</a> to take off from <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa Airport</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a> to test the feasibility of the approach and landing at Pescara of a large commercial aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-IlTempoN.294_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IlTempoN.294-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> With the British Caledonian chief pilot being an ex-<a href="/wiki/RAF" class="mw-redirect" title="RAF">RAF</a> pilot and Halpenny (also Ex-<a href="/wiki/RAF" class="mw-redirect" title="RAF">RAF</a>) on board this maneuver was successfully accomplished,<sup id="cite_ref-IlTempoN.294_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IlTempoN.294-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> opening up the airport and the region for tourism.<sup id="cite_ref-IlTempoN.294_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IlTempoN.294-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Halpenny then arranged for a British Caledonian BAC 111 to take off from <a href="/wiki/Gatwick_Airport" title="Gatwick Airport">Gatwick Airport</a>, land in Pescara and return to Gatwick, demonstrating the feasibility of <sup id="cite_ref-IlTempoN.294_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IlTempoN.294-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">commercial</a> flights to the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-IlTempoN.294_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IlTempoN.294-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><br />\nAs of 1 February 1979, <a href="/wiki/Itavia" title="Itavia">Itavia</a> was forced to suspend its flights to <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a> due to the revision of the minimum landing requirements on some Italian airports implemented by ANPAC, the National Association of Civil Aviation Pilots. The raise of the requirements in question (distance to the runway and altitude at which the pilot must decide whether to continue the landing) were dependent on proper radio support (such as <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">ILS</a>, Instrument Landing System) and a clear view to allow the landing of aircraft in conditions of poor visibility. Such instruments were not installed at the time at the airport. Itavia itself was never going to be back at the airport, as it ceased operations in June 1980, after the <a href="/wiki/Itavia_Flight_870" title="Itavia Flight 870">Ustica disaster</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After the dark period during which the airport did not have any connection, the situation improved with the reopening of a route to <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milano</a> through <a href="/wiki/Marche_Airport" title="Marche Airport">Ancona</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/Aermediterranea" title="Aermediterranea">Aermediterranea</a>, a company belonging to <a href="/wiki/Istituto_per_la_Ricostruzione_Industriale" title="Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale">Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale</a> (IRI) and controlled by Alitalia, which was operated between 1981 and 1983.\n</p><p>From 1984, the flight to <a href="/wiki/Milan_Linate_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Milan Linate Airport">Milan Linate Airport</a> was managed by another company of the Alitalia group: <a href="/wiki/Aero_Trasporti_Italiani" title="Aero Trasporti Italiani">Aero Trasporti Italiani</a> (ATI), based in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>. In 1988, it was the turn of Alitalia itself to operate the much-sought direct connection to Milan, using the modern <a href="/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_MD-80" title="McDonnell Douglas MD-80">McDonnell Douglas MD-80</a> jetliners until 1994.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Air_One" title="Air One">Air One</a> was founded as Aliadriatica in 1983 as a flight school and air taxi company for services in Abruzzo. From 1994, it was this company who took operation of the route to <a href="/wiki/Milan_Linate_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Milan Linate Airport">Milan</a>.\n</p><p>In 1996, the current terminal opened and the old building was converted for technical and logistical support. The project enabled the airport to offer more comfort and efficiency of service.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Developments_since_the_2000s">Developments since the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Developments since the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2010, Canadian airline <a href="/wiki/Air_Transat" title="Air Transat">Air Transat</a> began flying a seasonal nonstop flight to <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> operated with an <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A310" title="Airbus A310">Airbus A310</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This service was discontinued in 2012<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The development of the airport, which has seen an increment of passengers from 114,000 in 2000 to over 600,000 in 2015, is mainly due to the low-cost phenomenon that has affected all of Europe and most small Italian airports, benefiting Pescara.\n</p><p>Today the airport has a catchment area of over 600,000 passengers annually and connects the city of <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a> and the entire region with many Italian and European destinations, in  particular with the services offered by the airlines <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volotea" title="Volotea">Volotea</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Redevelopment_and_expansion_plans">Redevelopment and expansion plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span id="Ground_transportation">Ground transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is connected to the center of Pescara with the TUA (regional public transportation company) bus lines 8 and 38, with which it is possible to reach Piazza della Repubblica, the Bus Terminal serving domestic and international destinations. The bus lines 8 and 38 pass also near the main railway station in the city, <a href="/wiki/Pescara_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Pescara railway station">Pescara railway station</a>.\n</p><p>The airport is connected to the center of Pescara and Chieti with the TUA Chieti-Pescara line, with which it is possible to reach the university campus "G. D\'Annunzio" in Chieti Scalo. The TUA stop is opposite to the pedestrian exit of the airport park on Via Tiburtina Valeria.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cars">Cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Cars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Coming from Rome: from <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A24_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A24 (Italy)">Autostrada A24</a> take the Chieti-Pescara exit which merges with the SS656 in the direction of Pescara. Then take the exit Sambuceto-Airport and follow signs to the airport.\n</p><p>Coming from Bologna-Ancona: <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A14_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A14 (Italy)">Autostrada A14</a>, exit at Chieti-Pescara Ovest, turn immediately right (twice) to get onto the road SS5 <a href="/wiki/Via_Tiburtina_Valeria" class="mw-redirect" title="Via Tiburtina Valeria">Via Tiburtina Valeria</a> in the direction of Pescara, then drive about 6&#160;km (3.7 miles) until you reach the airport.\n</p><p>Coming from Bari-Foggia: take the <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A14_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A14 (Italy)">Autostrada A14</a> Chieti-Pescara Ovest exit, turn immediately right (twice) to get onto Strada statale 5 Via Tiburtina Valeria in the direction of Pescara, then drive about 6&#160;km (3.7 miles) until you reach the airport.\n</p><p>Coming from Naples: <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A1_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A1 (Italy)">Autostrada A1</a> towards Rome, exit at <a href="/wiki/Caianello" title="Caianello">Caianello</a>, follow the signs to <a href="/wiki/Isernia" title="Isernia">Isernia</a>, arrived at Isernia go to <a href="/wiki/Vasto" title="Vasto">Vasto</a> until you see the <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A14_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A14 (Italy)">Autostrada A14</a> signs. From the Autostrada A14, exit at Chieti-Pescara Ovest, turn immediately right (twice) to join the Strada statale 5 Via Tiburtina Valeria in the direction of Pescara, then drive about 6&#160;km (3.7 miles) until you reach the airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trolley-line">Trolley-line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abruzzo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Trolley-line">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In addition, the airport will soon be served by the new trolley-line currently under construction, which will connect Pescara and its points of reference such as <a href="/wiki/Pescara_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Pescara railway station">Pescara railway station</a> and other neighboring towns such as <a href="/wiki/Montesilvano" title="Montesilvano">Montesilvano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Francavilla_al_Mare" title="Francavilla al Mare">Francavilla al Mare</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
