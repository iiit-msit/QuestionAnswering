href="/w/index.php?title=Brindisi_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>This airport was originally established as a military airbase in the 1920s. The first commercial flights serving <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> began in the 1930s with the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Ala_Littoria" title="Ala Littoria">Ala Littoria</a> in 1934. After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a> took over the route and added a flight to <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a>. As of 2008, it has officially changed its legal status into civilian airport, still maintaining operational the military facilities attached to it. These are identified with its original name "Military Airport <a href="/wiki/Orazio_Pierozzi" title="Orazio Pierozzi">Orazio Pierozzi</a>", named in memory of an Italian airman of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a>.\n</p><p>The airport is officially named after Antonio Papola, in memory of the Italian aviator died on 13 February 1948 in an air accident who had a special bond with the city. It is also officially known as "Casale" with reference to the contiguous neighborhood in Brindisi with the same name and also as "<a href="/wiki/Salento" title="Salento">Salento</a> Airport" with reference to the geographic region where it is located.\n</p><p>The strategic position of the airport in the Mediterranean region, along with its multi-modal connections with the highway and the port a few kilometers away, have made it a base of crucial importance for both national defense and <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" It has since then been managed by the <a href="/wiki/World_Food_Programme" title="World Food Programme">World Food Programme</a> and officially known as the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Humanitarian_Response_Depot" title="United Nations Humanitarian Response Depot">United Nations Humanitarian Response Depot</a> (UNHRD).
