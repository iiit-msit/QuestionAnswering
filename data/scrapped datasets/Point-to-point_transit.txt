train travels directly to a destination, rather than going through a central <a href="/wiki/Transport_hub" title="Transport hub">hub</a>. This differs from the <a href="/wiki/Spoke-hub_distribution_paradigm" class="mw-redirect" title="Spoke-hub distribution paradigm">spoke-hub distribution paradigm</a> in which the transportation goes to a central location where passengers change to another train, bus, or plane to reach their destination.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Point-to-point_transit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Use in airlines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The point-to-point model is used widely by <a href="/wiki/Low_cost_carrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Low cost carrier">low cost carriers</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Allegiant_Air" title="Allegiant Air">Allegiant Air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Airlines" title="Southwest Airlines">Southwest Airlines</a> in the U.S., and European carriers such as <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">easyJet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wizzair" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizzair">Wizzair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Many such airlines sell each flight leg independently and have no concept of round-trip ticketing or connecting flights so baggage must be collected and rechecked even to transfer between flights booked at the same time on the same airline. Although there are many point-to-point airlines, most have at least a "homebase" airport where most flights originate or depart. The United States airport system was point-to-point, controlled by <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aeronautics_Board" title="Civil Aeronautics Board">CAB</a>, until <a href="/wiki/Airline_Deregulation_Act" title="Airline Deregulation Act">deregulation</a>  late 1960s/early 1970s, and eventually the <a href="/wiki/1978_Airline_Deregulation_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="1978 Airline Deregulation Act">1978 Airline Deregulation Act</a> when they switched to the hub concept, until the advent of low-cost carriers, when point-to-point air transport increased. Some full-service network carriers operate the point-to-point model alongside the hub-and-spoke system for certain high-density routes between <a href="/wiki/Focus_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Focus city">focus cities</a>. In Europe, for example, most traditional full-service airlines operate seasonal point-to-point service outside their hubs to serve <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alpine</a> holiday resorts.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advantages">Advantages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Point-to-point_transit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Advantages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>It eliminates the need for connections.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>It considerably reduces travel time.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>It considerably reduces risk of baggage loss or baggage arriving much later than the passenger, as baggage is not transferred as fast as passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Total fuel and pollution per passenger is lower: a passenger flying directly from Brussels to San Francisco will burn less fuel than flying via London or New York.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Without the need to satisfy connections for passengers, trips in a point-to-point system are less interdependent, but the operational constraints of needing to have sufficient equipment and personnel in each location at the right time to satisfy the timetable remain. That minimizes the risk of the domino effect in which the delayed arrival of one trip into a place leads to delayed departures of the (often) multiple trips with which its passengers had to make connections, cascading delays through the network. Therefore, a point-to-point system is less prone to delays.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>It has proven advantageous in the <a href="/wiki/Air_cargo" title="Air cargo">air cargo</a> industry in which freight is carried in the unused baggage hold space on passenger flights ("belly cargo"). Traditionally, large cargo aircraft are scheduled to fly between large hubs so freight often has to be forwarded on by additional flights or by rail or road. Belly cargo carried point to point can be delivered closer to its final destination.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disadvantages">Disadvantages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Point-to-point_transit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Disadvantages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>If a desired origin\xe2\x80\x93destination pair is not served, passengers will have to make a connection as in the <a href="/wiki/Spoke-hub_distribution_paradigm" class="mw-redirect" title="Spoke-hub distribution paradigm">hub model</a> or travel by another mode of transportation.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Low-cost carriers typically do not facilitate or honor connections, even if the consecutive flights are operated by that carrier and arrive and depart from the same airport.</li>\n<li>The frequency of trips may be reduced because the number of origin\xe2\x80\x93destination <a href="/wiki/Permutation" title="Permutation">pairs</a> is orders of magnitude larger.<sup id="cite_ref-compareUse_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compareUse-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span
