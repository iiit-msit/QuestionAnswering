title="Piet\xc3\xa0, Malta">Piet\xc3\xa0, Malta</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Malta Air</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_airline" class="mw-redirect" title="Low-cost airline">low-cost airline</a> that operates out of Malta. <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Malta" title="Government of Malta">Government of Malta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Malta Air does not yet use its own corporate design and operates all flights branded as Ryanair.\n</p><p>The new airline initially operated six former Ryanair aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ryanair planned to assign 62 routes it operates to and from Malta to this new airline and there were plans to grow the network beyond that.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, these plans were revised in May 2020, when redundancy for one third of the full complement of 179 pilots and cabin crew was announced in response the COVID-19 crisis.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Malta_Air_logo_doorway.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Operated by Malta Air" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Malta_Air_logo_doorway.jpg/220px-Malta_Air_logo_doorway.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="335" class="thumbimage" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Malta_Air_logo_doorway.jpg/440px-Malta_Air_logo_doorway.jpg 2x" data-file-width="872" data-file-height="1326" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Malta_Air_logo_doorway.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ryanair flight "Operated by Malta Air" in November 2019</div></div></div>\n<p>On 9 June 2019, Ryanair announced together with the Government of Malta, that they are going to set up a subsidiary airline called Malta Air which will consist of an initial fleet of 10 aircraft and assume the 61 flights currently operated by Ryanair from the island. The fleet will be registered in Malta while a new repair and maintenance hangar will also be set up.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ryanair will transfer all its existing Maltese operations to the new airline with its fleet expected to increase from 6 to 10 Boeing 737-800 aircraft and in Malta Air colours by mid-2020.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>News aggregator Corporate Dispatch reported the first sighting of a Ryanair aircraft displaying an "operated by Malta Air" sticker just outside its front passenger door on 20 June 2019 at <a href="/wiki/London_Stansted_Airport" title="London Stansted Airport">Stansted Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> By the end of September 2019, further reports of Malta Air branding on Ryanair flights were being reported, including safety cards on the back of all passenger seats, as well as flight attendant and cockpit announcements,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> even though no further official notice of this was circulated to the general public.\n</p><p>When faced with the Covid-19 crisis in May 2020, Malta Air announced substantial redundancies for its pilots and cabin crew, after first proposing a 10% salary cut.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Around 20 pilots and 40 cabin crew from the full complement of 179 pilots and cabin crew had their employment terminated from 30 June 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The company has planned to operate 66 routes from its <a href="/wiki/Malta_International_Airport" title="Malta International Airport">Malta International Airport</a> base starting in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In addition to all the former Ryanair routes to and from Malta, Malta Air has added these destinations: Paphos in Cyprus; Brindisi, Trapani, and Trieste in Italy; Ni\xc5\xa1 in Serbia; and Santiago de Compostela in Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Malta Air will also operate a service between <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna</a> from 1 April 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a
