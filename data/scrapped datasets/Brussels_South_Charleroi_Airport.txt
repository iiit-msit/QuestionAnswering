<b>Brussels-Charleroi Airport</b>, <b>Charleroi Airport</b> or rarely <i>Gosselies Airport</i>, (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>CRL</b>, <a Province">Province of Hainaut</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. The airport is 4 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (7.4&#160;km; 4.6&#160;mi) north<sup id="cite_ref-aip_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aip-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of Charleroi and 46&#160;km (29&#160;mi) south of central <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>. In terms of passengers and aircraft movements, it is the second busiest airport in Belgium having served 7,303,720 passengers in 2016 (75,038 movements). It is also a busy <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> airfield, being home to 3 flying schools.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9ropole_Science_Park" title="A\xc3\xa9ropole Science Park">A\xc3\xa9ropole</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Science_Parks_of_Wallonia" title="Science Parks of Wallonia">Science Parks of Wallonia</a>, is located near the airport.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" The British aircraft manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Fairey_Aviation" class="mw-redirect" title="Fairey Aviation">Fairey Aviation</a> settled a subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Avions_Fairey" title="Avions Fairey">Avions Fairey</a> on the site (then known as <i>Mont des Bergers</i>) in 1931.\n</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the site was arranged as an <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Landing_Ground" title="Advanced Landing Ground">Advanced Landing Ground</a> (<a href="/wiki/Advanced_Landing_Ground#A-81_to_A-98" title="Advanced Landing Ground">A-87</a>) for the allied air forces, from 14 September 1944 until 10 August 1945.\n</p><p>Gosselies airfield became a public aerodrome after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, but the main activities of the site remained aeronautical constructions (installation of <a href="/wiki/SABCA" title="SABCA">SABCA</a> in 1954, then <a href="/wiki/SONACA" title="SONACA">SONACA</a> in 1978, taking the place of Fairey).\n</p><p>In the 1970s, the Belgian national airline <a href="/wiki/Sabena" title="Sabena">Sabena</a> launched a <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Li\xc3\xa8ge">Li\xc3\xa8ge</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Charleroi" title="Charleroi">Charleroi</a>\xe2\x80\x93London service, but this was soon dropped because of poor results. Gosselies was left with almost no passenger traffic, the airport being mainly used for private or pleasure flights, training flights and occasional charters to leisure destinations around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> or to <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_1990s">Development since the 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels_South_Charleroi_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development since the 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Operations at Brussels South Charleroi grew in the 1990s, with a new commercial management structure (BSCA \xe2\x80\x93 Brussels South Charleroi Airport) and the arrival of Irish low-cost airline <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> in 1997, which opened its first continental base at Charleroi a few years later.<sup id="cite_ref-anna.aero_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anna.aero-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Although criticised for the subsidies paid by the <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Walloon</a> government to help its installation, Ryanair opened new routes from Brussels South Charleroi (they also closed two destinations: <a href="/wiki/London%E2%80%93Stansted" class="mw-redirect" title="London\xe2\x80\x93Stansted">London\xe2\x80\x93Stansted</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, although Stansted was re-introduced in June 2007 before being suspended again).<sup id="cite_ref-anna.aero_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anna.aero-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Other low-cost carriers later joined Ryanair in Brussels South Charleroi, such as <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a>. The Polish airline <a href="/wiki/Air_Polonia" title="Air Polonia">Air Polonia</a> operated services from here to <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katowice" title="Katowice">Katowice</a> before going bankrupt in August 2004.\n</p><p>In September 2006 it was announced that Moroccan low-cost airline <a href="/wiki/Jet4you" title="Jet4you">Jet4you</a> would launch three weekly flights to <a href="/wiki/Casablanca" title="Casablanca">Casablanca</a> (on Wednesday, Thursday and Sunday) starting 1 November 2006, in code-share cooperation with Belgian airline <a href="/wiki/Jetairfly" class="mw-redirect" title="Jetairfly">Jetairfly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A new terminal opened in January 2008. It has a capacity of up to 5 million passengers a year, which means that it has reached its maximum capacity in 2010 (5,195,372 passengers). <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> objected to assistance the airport offered to <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, since the airport is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a> regional government and thus the discounts and other benefits could be considered state aid.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, the Court of First Instance (a European Union court) decided on 17 December 2008 that the Commission\'s decision finding that illegal aid had been granted to Ryanair should be annulled and quashed as being erroneous in law. However, in March 2012, the Commission reopened the case in order to take this judgment into account.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2017, a second terminal (Terminal 2) was opened in order to relieve the T1 during rush hours and to be able to accommodate 10 million passengers a year in the future.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span several shuttles to different cities in the neighbouring countries (Luxembourg, Metz, Thionville, Lille) plus a regular coach service that runs from the airport to <a href="/wiki/Brussels-South_railway_station" title="Brussels-South railway station">Brussels-South railway station</a>. A combined bus and train ticket to any other Belgian railway station can be bought in the terminal.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels_South_Charleroi_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is accessible by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorways_in_Belgium" title="List of motorways in Belgium">A54</a>/<a href="/wiki/European_route_E420" title="European route E420">E420</a> highway\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels_South_Charleroi_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 8 April 2011, a Dutch F-16 had to make an emergency landing because of a technical failure of one of its sets of landing gear. The plane landed on its belly. The pilot did not suffer any injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 9 February 2013, a small <a href="/wiki/Cessna" title="Cessna">Cessna</a> plane crashed near the runway after suffering technical problems during take-off, killing all 5 people on board.
