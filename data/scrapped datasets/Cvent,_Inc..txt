1.35em;"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cvent.com/">www.cvent.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cvent, Inc.</b> is a privately held <a href="/wiki/Software-as-a-service" class="mw-redirect" title="Software-as-a-service">software-as-a-service</a> (SaaS) company that specializes in meetings, events, and hospitality management technology. The company offers web-based software for meeting site selection, online event registration, <a href="/wiki/Event_management" title="Event management">event management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Email_marketing" title="Email marketing">email marketing</a>, and web surveys.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2 id="mw-toc-heading">Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Software_and_services"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Software and services</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cvent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Cvent was founded in September 1999 by Reggie Aggarwal. That same year it received $17 million in <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> and grew its staff to 125 employees. Following the <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_bubble" title="Dot-com bubble">dot-com bubble</a> burst and the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, Cvent faced near-bankruptcy and was forced to cut 80% of its staff.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The company became profitable again by 2003. In 2011, Cvent was growing by 50% a year and received $136 million of funding from <a href="/wiki/New_Enterprise_Associates" title="New Enterprise Associates">New Enterprise Associates</a> in July 2011, which, at the time, was the largest investment in a U.S. software company since 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On June 13, 2012, Cvent announced the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin</a>-based startup CrowdTorch, previously known as Seed Labs, for $4.2 million.<sup id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Seven days later, it announced its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>-based application developer CrowdCompass for $10 million.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Cvent filed an S-1 with the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> on July 8, 2013, proposing an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> of 5.6 million shares.<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It went public on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> on August 9, 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> at an initial price of $21.<sup id="cite_ref-Cvent_stock_drifts_back_to_earth_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cvent_stock_drifts_back_to_earth-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The company raised $117.6 million and received a <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market capitalization</a> of more than a billion dollars. The IPO was referenced in regards to its use of the <a href="/wiki/Jumpstart_Our_Business_Startups_Act" title="Jumpstart Our Business Startups Act">JOBS Act</a>, which enabled the company to quickly offer an IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-Cvent_stock_drifts_back_to_earth_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cvent_stock_drifts_back_to_earth-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016, the company was acquired by venture capital company Vista Equity Partners for $USD 1.65 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ashok_Trivedi" title="Ashok Trivedi">Ashok Trivedi</a>, the co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Mastech_Digital" title="Mastech Digital">Mastech Digital</a> and <a href="/wiki/IGATE" title="IGATE">iGate</a> was an early investor of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 23, 2018, Cvent announced that it had acquired Quickmobile, a Vancouver-based mobile event app developer.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>On June 5, 2018, Cvent announced that it had acquired Kapow, an online booking platform for venues and experiences.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 16, 2018, Cvent announced that it had acquired <a href="/wiki/Social_Tables" title="Social Tables">Social Tables</a>, an event diagramming, seating and collaboration platform based in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 22, 2019, Cvent announced that it had acquired Wedding Spot, a wedding venue sourcing platform that allows users to find venues based on budget, location, style and guest count.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> At the time of the acquisition, Wedding Spot, which was founded in 2013 in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco, California</a>, had partnerships with over 12,000 venues across the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On June 10, 2019, Cvent announced that it had acquired mobile event technology provider DoubleDutch.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software_and_services">Software and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cvent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Software and web-based tool for event planners to manage invitations and collect registration fees.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, it introduced a product for conducting online surveys,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> which was followed by the introduction of the Cvent Supplier Network two years later. The Supplier Network is a free, online marketing place that connects meeting planners with venues and services.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, the company began offering <a href="/wiki/Professional_services" title="Professional services">professional services</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>An app development tool, CrowdTorch, was launched in 2009.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. different destinations.<sup id="cite_ref-conference-news.co.uk_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conference-news.co.uk-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> A "Strategic Meetings Management" helps
