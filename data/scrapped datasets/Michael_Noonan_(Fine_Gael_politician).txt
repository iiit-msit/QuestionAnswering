href="/wiki/St_Patrick%27s_College,_Dublin" title="St Patrick&#39;s College, Dublin">St Patrick\'s College, Dublin</a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Michael Noonan</b> (born 21 May 1943) is an Irish former <a href="/wiki/Fine_Gael" title="Fine Gael">Fine Gael</a> politician who served as <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Finance_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Finance (Ireland)">Minister for Finance</a> from 2011 to 2017, <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_(Ireland)" title="Leader of the Opposition (Ireland)">Leader of the Opposition</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_Fine_Gael" title="Leader of Fine Gael">Leader of Fine Gael</a> from 2001 to 2002, <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Health_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Health (Ireland)">Minister for Health</a> from 1994 to 1997, <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Enterprise,_Trade_and_Employment" title="Minister for Enterprise, Trade and Employment">Minister for Industry and Commerce</a> from 1986 to 1987, <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Tourism,_Culture,_Arts,_Gaeltacht,_Sport_and_Media" title="Minister for Tourism, Culture, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sport and Media">Minister for Energy</a> from January 1987 to March 1987 and <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Justice_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Justice (Ireland)">Minister for Justice</a> from 1982 to 1986. He served as a <a href="/wiki/Teachta_D%C3%A1la" title="Teachta D\xc3\xa1la">Teachta D\xc3\xa1la</a> (TD) from 1981 to 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-oireachtas_db_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oireachtas_db-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Noonan had been a member of every Fine Gael cabinet since 1982, serving in the cabinets of <a href="/wiki/Garret_FitzGerald" title="Garret FitzGerald">Garret FitzGerald</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Bruton" title="John Bruton">John Bruton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enda_Kenny" title="Enda Kenny">Enda Kenny</a>. During these terms of office he held the positions of Justice, Energy, Industry and Commerce, Health and Finance. When Fine Gael lost power after the <a href="/wiki/1997_Irish_general_election" title="1997 Irish general election">1997 general election</a>, Noonan remained an important figure in the party when he became Opposition Spokesperson for Finance.\n</p><p>He succeeded John Bruton as <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_Fine_Gael" title="Leader of Fine Gael">Leader of Fine Gael</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_(Ireland)" title="Leader of the Opposition (Ireland)">Leader of the Opposition</a> in 2001, however, he resigned following Fine Gael\'s disastrous showing at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Irish_general_election" title="2002 Irish general election">2002 general election</a>. After eight years as a backbencher, during which time he served as <a href="/wiki/Public_Accounts_Committee_(Ireland)" title="Public Accounts Committee (Ireland)">Chairman of the Public Accounts Committee</a>, Fine Gael leader Enda Kenny appointed Noonan to his <a href="/wiki/Fine_Gael_Front_Bench" title="Fine Gael Front Bench">front bench</a> in 2010, to his former portfolio of Spokesperson for Finance.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The son of a local school teacher, Noonan was born in <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a> in 1943, but raised in Loughill, <a href="/wiki/County_Limerick" title="County Limerick">County Limerick</a>. He was educated at the local <a href="/wiki/National_school_(Ireland)" title="National school (Ireland)">National School</a> and St. Patrick\'s Secondary School in <a href="/wiki/Glin,_County_Limerick" title="Glin, County Limerick">Glin</a>, before studying to be a primary school teacher at <a href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_College_of_Education_(Drumcondra)" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Patrick&#39;s College of Education (Drumcondra)">St. Patrick\'s College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drumcondra,_Dublin" title="Drumcondra, Dublin">Drumcondra, Dublin</a>. He subsequently completed a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">BA</a> and <a href="/wiki/H.Dip." class="mw-redirect" title="H.Dip.">H.Dip.</a> in English and Economics at <a href="/wiki/University_College_Dublin" title="University College Dublin">University College Dublin</a>. He began to work as a secondary school teacher in Dublin. Noonan developed an interest in politics from his mother, whose family had been heavily involved in <a href="/wiki/Fine_Gael" title="Fine Gael">Fine Gael</a> at local level in Limerick, and joined the Dublin branch of the party after graduating from university. He returned to Limerick in the late 1960s, where he took up a teaching post at <a href="/wiki/Crescent_College" title="Crescent College">Crescent College</a>. Here he continued his involvement in politics, canvassing for the Fine Gael candidate, James O\'Higgins, in the <a href="/wiki/1968_Limerick_East_by-election" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 Limerick East by-election">Limerick East by-election</a> in 1968, caused by the death of <a href="/wiki/Donogh_O%27Malley" title="Donogh O&#39;Malley">Donogh O\'Malley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="Early_years:_1974.E2.80.931982"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years:_1974\xe2\x80\x931982">Early years: 1974\xe2\x80\x931982</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years: 1974\xe2\x80\x931982">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Having been involved in the local Fine Gael organisation in Limerick since the late 1960s, Noonan first held political office in 1974, when he was elected as a member of <a href="/wiki/Limerick_County_Council" title="Limerick County Council">Limerick County Council</a>. Having built up a local profile he contested the <a href="/wiki/1981_Irish_general_election" title="1981 Irish general election">1981 general election</a> for the party and secured a seat in <a href="/wiki/Limerick_East" class="mw-redirect" title="Limerick East">Limerick East</a>. Upon taking his D\xc3\xa1il seat, Noonan became a full-time politician, giving up his teaching post and resigning his seat on Limerick County Council.<sup id="cite_ref-elecs_irl_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elecs_irl-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Though Fine Gael formed a coalition government with the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Ireland)" title="Labour Party (Ireland)">Labour Party</a>, Noonan, as a first time TD, remained on the backbenches.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Cabinet_minister:_1982.E2.80.931987"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cabinet_minister:_1982\xe2\x80\x931987">Cabinet minister: 1982\xe2\x80\x931987</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cabinet minister: 1982\xe2\x80\x931987">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Fine Gael lost power following the <a href="/wiki/February_1982_Irish_general_election" title="February 1982 Irish general election">first general election in early 1982</a>, however, Noonan subsequently joined the party\'s new front bench as Spokesperson for Education. A <a href="/wiki/November_1982_Irish_general_election" title="November 1982 Irish general election">second general election in late 1982</a> following the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna F\xc3\xa1il">Fianna F\xc3\xa1il</a> government, saw another Fine Gael-Labour Party coalition come to power. Just eighteen months after entering the D\xc3\xa1il, Noonan was appointed to the highly sensitive position of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Justice_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Justice (Ireland)">Minister for Justice</a>. After a few weeks in office he revealed the <a href="/wiki/Irish_phone_tapping_scandal" title="Irish phone tapping scandal">illegal phone-tapping of journalists\' phones</a>, carried out by the previous Fianna F\xc3\xa1il administration that preceded it in power. That government had authorised illegal <a href="/wiki/Telephone_tapping" title="Telephone tapping">phone tapping</a> of the journalists <a href="/wiki/Geraldine_Kennedy" title="Geraldine Kennedy">Geraldine Kennedy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Arnold_(author)" title="Bruce Arnold (author)">Bruce Arnold</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Browne" title="Vincent Browne">Vincent Browne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Se%C3%A1n_Doherty_(Roscommon_politician)" title="Se\xc3\xa1n Doherty (Roscommon politician)">Se\xc3\xa1n Doherty</a> signed warrants for the taps while serving as Minister for Justice. Noonan quickly earned a reputation as a tough and uncompromising Minister, regarded by many as one of the best incumbents of the position. His successes included the introduction of a new Criminal Justice Bill while also bringing in reforms in the <a href="/wiki/Garda_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1na" title="Garda S\xc3\xadoch\xc3\xa1na">Garda S\xc3\xadoch\xc3\xa1na</a>, the courts and the prison service and the facing down of a difficult prison officers\' dispute. He also dealt with the wording of <a href="/wiki/Eighth_Amendment_of_the_Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Eighth Amendment of the Constitution of Ireland">the controversial abortion referendum in 1983</a>. Noonan, however, also presided over the justice ministry when inmates in an overcrowded and understaffed <a href="/wiki/Spike_Island,_County_Cork" title="Spike Island, County Cork">Spike Island</a> prison set fire to the building.<sup id="cite_ref-Southernstar_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Southernstar-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A cabinet reshuffle in 1986, saw Noonan demoted to the position of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Enterprise,_Trade_and_Employment" title="Minister for Enterprise, Trade and Employment">Minister for Industry and Commerce</a>. Following the withdrawal of the Labour Party from government in 1987, Noonan also briefly took office as <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Tourism,_Culture,_Arts,_Gaeltacht,_Sport_and_Media" title="Minister for Tourism, Culture, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sport and Media">Minister for Energy</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Opposition:_1987.E2.80.931994"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Opposition:_1987\xe2\x80\x931994">Opposition: 1987\xe2\x80\x931994</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Opposition: 1987\xe2\x80\x931994">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Fine Gael lost power following the <a href="/wiki/1987_Irish_general_election" title="1987 Irish general election">1987 general election</a> and were confined to the opposition benches. The new Fine Gael leader, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Dukes" title="Alan Dukes">Alan Dukes</a>, appointed Noonan to the senior position of front bench Spokesperson for Finance and the Public Service. The party did poorly under Dukes and he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/John_Bruton" title="John Bruton">John Bruton</a> in 1990. Noonan was retained on the new front bench, however, he was demoted to the position of Spokesperson for Transport, Energy and Communications. In 1991, he returned to local politics as a member of Limerick County Council, serving again until 1994. Noonan\'s period in opposition often saw him at odds with his party leader. Not long after his demotion as Spokesperson for Finance, he announced that he would be taking "positions of leadership" on a wide range of important issues. This was seen as a veiled threat to John Bruton\'s leadership, with Noonan positioning himself as an alternative party leader. In 1994, a number of Fine Gael TDs attempted to oust Bruton as party leader following poor showings in opinion polls. Noonan aligned himself with the rebels and stated that he would stand for the leadership should Bruton be defeated. The latter survived as leader and Noonan resigned from the front bench.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Minister_for_Health:_1994.E2.80.931997"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_for_Health:_1994\xe2\x80\x931997">Minister for Health: 1994\xe2\x80\x931997</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Minister for Health: 1994\xe2\x80\x931997">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1994, the \'Rainbow Coalition\' was formed and Noonan became <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Health_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Health (Ireland)">Minister for Health</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Health_(Ireland)" title="Department of Health (Ireland)">Department of Health</a> was embroiled in a scandal at the time regarding <a href="/wiki/Blood_products" class="mw-redirect" title="Blood products">blood products</a> contaminated with <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C" title="Hepatitis C">Hepatitis C</a> virus, caused by the negligence of the Blood Transfusion Service Board. Noonan consistently held an authoritarian line on the case of <a href="/wiki/Brigid_McCole" title="Brigid McCole">Brigid McCole</a> and would not budge on his views, suffering as a result of the scandal.<sup id="cite_ref-BridgetMcColeVictimEnemy_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BridgetMcColeVictimEnemy-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Groundhogday_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Groundhogday-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He threatened to take Bridget\'s mother Ellen to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Ireland" title="Supreme Court of Ireland">Supreme Court</a>, when she wondered why her daughter had contracted the disease. Noonan was forced to establish the Hepatitis C Tribunal of Inquiry and to issue several apologies for his handling of the affair.<sup id="cite_ref-Southernstar_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Southernstar-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Noonan remained as Minister for Health until the <a href="/wiki/1997_Irish_general_election" title="1997 Irish general election">1997 general election</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Irish_Times" title="The Irish Times">The Irish Times</a></i> said "the woman involved had been infected by a negligent State agency, in the biggest health scandal since its foundation."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> When RT\xc3\x89 broadcast a drama, <i>No Tears</i>, on Noonan\'s treatment of Bridget McCole, <a href="/wiki/Justine_McCarthy" title="Justine McCarthy">Justine McCarthy</a> wrote in the <i>Irish Independent</i> that Noonan "compounded the perception of a heartless pedant by whingeing about the way he was depicted in the drama\'s final episode, broadcast on the same night that he declined to appear on <i><a href="/wiki/Questions_and_Answers_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Questions and Answers (TV series)">Questions &amp; Answers</a></i> and when it was reported that yet another woman who was infected by the State had died from the illness."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>His <a href="/wiki/Youth_Defence#Picketing_of_Politicians&#39;_houses" title="Youth Defence">home was picketed</a> by anti-abortion campaign group <a href="/wiki/Youth_Defence" title="Youth Defence">Youth Defence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Return_to_Opposition:_1997.E2.80.932011"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Opposition:_1997\xe2\x80\x932011">Return to Opposition: 1997\xe2\x80\x932011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Opposition: 1997\xe2\x80\x932011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span id="Leader_of_Fine_Gael:_2001.E2.80.932002"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Leader_of_Fine_Gael:_2001\xe2\x80\x932002">Leader of Fine Gael: 2001\xe2\x80\x932002</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Leader of Fine Gael: 2001\xe2\x80\x932002">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Despite increasing their seats to 54, Fine Gael returned to opposition and Noonan became Opposition Spokesman for Finance following the <a href="/wiki/1997_Irish_general_election" title="1997 Irish general election">1997 general election</a>. In 2001, following a series of disastrous opinion polls, Noonan and his colleague, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Mitchell_(politician)" title="Jim Mitchell (politician)">Jim Mitchell</a>, tabled a <a href="/wiki/Motion_of_no_confidence" title="Motion of no confidence">motion of no confidence</a> in the leader, <a href="/wiki/John_Bruton" title="John Bruton">John Bruton</a>. The motion was successful in ousting Bruton as leader, with Noonan becoming leader of Fine Gael and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Irish_Leaders_of_the_Opposition" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Irish Leaders of the Opposition">Leader of the Opposition</a> with Mitchell becoming deputy leader. Noonan avoided requests to be interviewed on TV and radio programmes, including some on RT\xc3\x89 and <a href="/wiki/Today_FM" title="Today FM">Today FM</a>, ahead of the leadership election.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2002_Irish_general_election" title="2002 Irish general election">2002 general election</a>, Fine Gael had a disastrous result, dropping from 54 seats to 31 and a number of high-profile front bench member losses, including <a href="/wiki/Alan_Dukes" title="Alan Dukes">Alan Dukes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deirdre_Clune" title="Deirdre Clune">Deirdre Clune</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shatter" title="Alan Shatter">Alan Shatter</a> and deputy leader Jim Mitchell.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Noonan resigned as Fine Gael leader on the night of the election.\n</p>\n<h4><span id="TD:_2002.E2.80.932011"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="TD:_2002\xe2\x80\x932011">TD: 2002\xe2\x80\x932011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: TD: 2002\xe2\x80\x932011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Enda_Kenny" title="Enda Kenny">Enda Kenny</a>, the runner-up to Noonan in the <a href="/wiki/2001_Fine_Gael_leadership_election" title="2001 Fine Gael leadership election">2001 leadership election</a>. However, Noonan remained as a TD, and was re-elected at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Irish_general_election" title="2007 Irish general election">2007 general election</a>, and went on to serve on Kenny\'s Front bench. He was Vice-Chairperson of the Joint <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas" title="Oireachtas">Oireachtas</a> Committee on Constitutional Amendment and Children.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Minister_for_Finance:_2011.E2.80.932017"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_for_Finance:_2011\xe2\x80\x932017">Minister for Finance: 2011\xe2\x80\x932017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Minister for Finance: 2011\xe2\x80\x932017">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In July 2010, Noonan was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Opposition_Front_Bench_(Ireland)" title="Opposition Front Bench (Ireland)">Fine Gael Front Bench</a> as Spokesperson for Finance.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In an August 2010 interview with the Sunday Independent, Noonan said he hoped to become Minister for Finance.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> \nAt the <a href="/wiki/2011_Irish_general_election" title="2011 Irish general election">2011 general election</a>, he was re-elected as a TD for Limerick City, receiving 13,291 (30.8% 1st preference) votes.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On 9 March 2011, he was appointed <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Finance_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Finance (Ireland)">Minister for Finance</a> by Taoiseach Enda Kenny.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2011, the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> (IMF) and the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> (EU) backed the proposed programme for government and gave the coalition the green light to make changes to the terms of the multibillion-euro bailout. After meeting officials from the IMF \xe2\x80\x93 including Ireland mission head Ajai Chopra \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/European_Central_Bank" title="European Central Bank">European Central Bank</a> (ECB) and <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> (EC), Noonan said it was agreed the terms of the rescue deal would be altered, as long as the financial targets remain the same. "What was being discussed in general terms was our proposal that the conditions and the memorandum of understanding would be changed for alternative conditions which are in the programme for government," Noonan said.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2011, Noonan speaking after an EU summit on 21 July said that the new deal agreed with euro zone leaders means a second bailout for Ireland is "off the table". The deal will see a <a href="/wiki/Post-2008_Irish_economic_downturn" title="Post-2008 Irish economic downturn">reduction in the interest rate</a> on bailout loans to <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. This followed a 10-hour emergency summit at which eurozone leaders agreed to provide a second international bailout worth \xe2\x82\xac109 billion to Greece. The plan will be funded by eurozone countries, the proceeds of privatisations and the anticipated \xe2\x82\xac12.6 billion benefit of a debt buyback programme. Noonan said a provision in the agreement meant Ireland would not have to go back to markets when the programme ended if the country had not reached its deficit target. "There\'s a commitment that if countries continue to fulfil the conditions of their programme, the European authorities will continue to supply them with money even when the programme concludes," he said. There would be "little or no easing" of budgetary conditions for this year, but there could be more positive implications in later years. "I\'m afraid we still have to face the music in December," he said.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In November 2011, he said the payment of more than \xe2\x82\xac700 million to <a href="/wiki/Anglo_Irish_Bank" title="Anglo Irish Bank">Anglo Irish Bank</a> bondholders was "the lesser of two evils".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On 16 May 2012, Noonan caused controversy with his Greek "holidays" and "<a href="/wiki/Feta" title="Feta">feta</a> cheese" comment at a breakfast briefing with <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_L.P." title="Bloomberg L.P.">Bloomberg</a> news agency. Noonan said these were the only links between Ireland and Greece.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He attended the <a href="/wiki/Bilderberg_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilderberg Group">2012 Bilderberg Conference</a> in his capacity of Minister for Finance at <a href="/wiki/Chantilly,_Virginia" title="Chantilly, Virginia">Chantilly, Virginia</a>, from 31 May-3 June 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 5 December 2012, he delivered his <a href="/wiki/Irish_budget,_2013" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish budget, 2013">second budget</a> as Minister for Finance, which included a new <a href="/wiki/Irish_Property_Tax" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Property Tax">property tax</a> to be introduced in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2013, a deal was reached with the European Central Bank (ECB) in relation to the <a href="/wiki/Promissory_note" title="Promissory note">promissory note</a> used to bail out the former Anglo Irish Bank. Noonan said that the government had achieved its objectives in the negotiations with the ECB, and that the arrangement meant that there would be \xe2\x82\xac1bn less taken from them in terms of taxes and spending cuts up to 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-PN-RT\xc3\x8901_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PN-RT\xc3\x8901-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Noonan said that the government did not ask for a write down on the Anglo Irish debt during negotiations with the ECB as "the ECB does not do write downs".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\nOn 15 October 2013, he delivered the budget for <a href="/wiki/Irish_budget,_2014" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish budget, 2014">2014</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following improvements in Ireland\'s unemployment rate and outlook for growth,<sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> the securing of the February 2013 Anglo Irish Bank promissory note deal with the European Central Bank,<sup id="cite_ref-PN-RT\xc3\x8901_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PN-RT\xc3\x8901-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and Ireland\'s <a href="/wiki/Post-2008_Irish_economic_downturn#Exit_from_bailout" title="Post-2008 Irish economic downturn">exit</a> from the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a>/<a href="/wiki/IMF" class="mw-redirect" title="IMF">IMF</a>/<a href="/wiki/European_Central_Bank" title="European Central Bank">ECB</a> <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9314_Irish_banking_crisis#EU-IMF_.22bailout.22_of_November_2010" class="mw-redirect" title="2008\xe2\x80\x9314 Irish banking crisis">bailout programme</a><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-Indo01_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-Indo01-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-Exam01_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-Exam01-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and successful return to the bond markets,<sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-Indo01_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-Indo01-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-Exam01_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-Exam01-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Noonan was named Europe\'s best Finance Minister for the previous year in January 2014 by the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a>-owned magazine <a href="/wiki/The_Banker" title="The Banker">The Banker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-RT\xc3\x8901-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-Indo01_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-Indo01-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BestEFM-Exam01_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BestEFM-Exam01-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>When the European Central Bank raised the limit on the amount of emergency liquidity assistance (ELA) available to Greek banks by about \xe2\x82\xac2 billion at the height of the country\'s <a href="/wiki/Greek_government-debt_crisis" title="Greek government-debt crisis">government-debt crisis</a> in June 2015, Noonan joined his German counterpart <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Sch%C3%A4uble" title="Wolfgang Sch\xc3\xa4uble">Wolfgang Sch\xc3\xa4uble</a> in arguing forcefully for limits on the amount of ELA approved by the central bank unless <a href="/wiki/Capital_control" title="Capital control">capital controls</a> were introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Statistics_Office_(Ireland)" title="Central Statistics Office (Ireland)">Central Statistics Office</a> announced 2015 Irish GDP growth of 26.3% and Irish GNP growth of 18.7%. The growth became known internationally by the pejorative term, <a href="/wiki/Leprechaun_economics" title="Leprechaun economics">leprechaun economics</a>. Noonan attributed the figures to multinational restructuring following the closure of the <a href="/wiki/Double_Irish" class="mw-redirect" title="Double Irish">double Irish</a> tax scheme,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> however they were subsequently attributed <a href="/wiki/Leprechaun_economics#Proof_of_Apple_(2018)" title="Leprechaun economics">to Apple</a> in 2018<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> (widely suspected in 2016<sup id="cite_ref-kw1_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kw1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>). Noonan came to the defense of <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> when the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> announced in August 2016 that it had found against Apple in its <a href="/wiki/EU_illegal_State_aid_case_against_Apple_in_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="EU illegal State aid case against Apple in Ireland">investigation of illegal State aid</a>. Noonan led the rejection of any claim by Ireland to the EU Commission\'s \xe2\x82\xac13 billion fine on Apple, calling it an "attack" by the Commission,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> and was supported by the main opposition party.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In October 2016, Noonan introduced changes in the 2016 Finance Act to curb tax abuses of <a href="/wiki/Irish_Section_110_Special_Purpose_Vehicle_(SPV)" title="Irish Section 110 Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)">section 110 special purpose vehicles</a>, (securitisation vehicles for <a href="/wiki/International_Financial_Services_Centre" title="International Financial Services Centre">IFSC</a> firms), by US distressed debt funds in Ireland (pejoratively called <a href="/wiki/Vulture_funds" class="mw-redirect" title="Vulture funds">vulture funds</a> in the Irish media).<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rte1_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rte1-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Investigations into these abuses by the financial media,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> showed the scale and rapid gains these funds were making from <a href="/wiki/National_Asset_Management_Agency" title="National Asset Management Agency">NAMA\'s</a> disposal program, and that these gains were free of Irish taxes.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> It led to some revision as to whether Noonan had been too quick in selling State assets to distressed debt funds,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> and had given overly generous tax benefits and incentives.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In May 2017, he announced he would be stepping down as Minister for Finance in the coming weeks when <a href="/wiki/2017_Fine_Gael_leadership_election" title="2017 Fine Gael leadership election">a new Taoiseach was appointed</a>, and as a member of the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il" class="mw-redirect" title="D\xc3\xa1il">D\xc3\xa1il</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2020_Irish_general_election" title="2020 Irish general election">next general election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Noonan_(Fine_Gael_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Noonan married Florence Knightley, a native of <a href="/wiki/Castlemaine,_County_Kerry" title="Castlemaine, County Kerry">Castlemaine</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Kerry" title="County Kerry">County Kerry</a> and a primary school teacher, in 1969. They had three sons: Tim, John and Michael, and two daughters: Orla and Deirdre. In May 2010, Noonan appeared on <a href="/wiki/Raidi%C3%B3_Teilif%C3%ADs_%C3%89ireann" title="Raidi\xc3\xb3 Teilif\xc3\xads \xc3\x89ireann">RT\xc3\x89</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease" title="Alzheimer&#39;s disease">Alzheimer\'s disease</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Florence Noonan died on 23 February 2012 of <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56"
