code</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Code" title="Code">code</a> that assigns letters or numbers as identifiers or classifiers for <a href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">languages</a>.  These codes may be used to organize <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">library</a> collections or presentations of <a href="/wiki/Data" title="Data">data</a>, to choose the correct localizations href="/w/index.php?title=Language_code&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Difficulties of classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Language code schemes attempt to classify the complex world of human languages, <a href="/wiki/Dialects" class="mw-redirect" title="Dialects">dialects</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Variety_(linguistics)" title="Variety (linguistics)">variants</a>. Most schemes make some compromises between being general and being complete enough to support specific dialects.\n</p><p>For example, most people in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> speak <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>.  Spanish spoken in <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Spanish" title="Mexican Spanish">Mexico</a> will be slightly different from Spanish spoken in <a href="/wiki/Peruvian_Spanish" title="Peruvian Spanish">Peru</a>.  Different regions of Mexico will have slightly different dialects and accents of Spanish.  A language code scheme might group these all as "Spanish" for choosing a keyboard layout, most as "Spanish" for general usage, or separate each dialect to allow region-specific idioms.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_schemes">Common schemes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Language_code&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Common schemes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Some common language code schemes include:\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr style="vertical-align:top">\n<th>Scheme\n</th>\n<th>Notes\n</th>\n<th>Example for English\n</th>\n<th>Example for Spanish\n</th></tr>\n<tr style="vertical-align:top">\n<td><i><a href="/wiki/Glottolog" title="Glottolog">Glottolog</a></i> codes\n</td>\n<td>Created for minority languages as a scientific alternative to the industrial ISO 639\xe2\x80\x913 standard.<br />Intentionally do not resemble abbreviations.\n</td>\n<td>\n<ul><li><b>stan1293</b> \xe2\x80\x93 standard English</li>\n<li><b>macr1271</b> \xe2\x80\x93 macro-English (Modern English, incl. creoles)</li>\n<li><b>midd1317</b> \xe2\x80\x93 Middle English</li>\n<li><b>merc1242</b> \xe2\x80\x93 Mercian (Middle \xe2\x80\x93 Modern English)</li>\n<li><b>olde1238</b> \xe2\x80\x93 Old English</li>\n<li><b>angl1265</b> \xe2\x80\x93 Anglian (Old \xe2\x80\x93 Modern English, incl.
