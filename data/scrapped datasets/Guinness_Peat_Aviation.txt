href="/wiki/Commercial_Aircraft_Sales_and_Leasing" class="mw-redirect" title="Commercial Aircraft Sales and Leasing">Commercial Aircraft Sales and Leasing</a> <a href="/wiki/Company_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Company (law)">company</a> set up in 1975 by <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_Peat_Group" title="Guinness Peat links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinness_Peat_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>GPA was based in <a href="/wiki/Shannon,_County_Clare" title="Shannon, County Clare">Shannon</a>, Ireland. During the 1980s it became the world\'s largest commercial aircraft lessor and expanded its shareholding to include <a href="/wiki/Air_Canada" title="Air Canada">Air Canada</a>, General Electric, Short Term Credit Bank of Japan and companies in the <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi" title="Mitsubishi">Mitsubishi</a> group. GPA also had financing joint ventures with key aircraft manufactures, including Airbus, Fokker and McDonnell Douglas.  At its peak, the company was valued at $4 bn. Net income reached $265 mn in the year to 31 March 1992.\n</p><p>Former <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> <a href="/wiki/Garret_FitzGerald" title="Garret FitzGerald">Garret FitzGerald</a>, former British <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_exchequer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chancellor of the exchequer">chancellor of the exchequer</a> <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Lawson" title="Nigel Lawson">Nigel Lawson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sutherland" title="Peter Sutherland">Peter Sutherland</a> and the former Chairman of ICI, Sir John Harvey-Jones, were among GPA\'s non-executive directors.<sup id="cite_ref-Irish_Independent_1999_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Irish_Independent_1999-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Lawson joined the board of GPA in February 1990 but he also became chairman of GPA Financial, a subsidiary company.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1990, GPA stunned the aviation world by placing a $17 billion order for 700 new aircraft over the following decade. A new company, GPA Helicopters Ltd., was set up in June 1990 as a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/CHC_Helicopter" title="CHC Helicopter">CHC Helicopter</a> to acquire, own and lease helicopters worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Downfall">Downfall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinness_Peat_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Downfall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The decision to float the company on the <a href="/wiki/Stock_market" title="Stock market">stock market</a> in 1992, during an aviation industry downturn following the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">1991 Gulf War</a>, proved disastrous, as <a href="/wiki/International_financial_institutions" title="International financial institutions">international financial institutions</a> refused to buy shares.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Unable to raise the capital it needed to continue its ambitious operations, the company plunged into crisis, with some $10 billion in debts.\n</p><p>The story of GPA\'s downfall is told by Christopher Brown, aviation lawyer and GPA Senior Vice-President, in his 2009 book <i>Crash Landing \xe2\x80\x93 An Inside Account of the Fall of GPA</i>. The book is a personal memoir of the history, background and run-in to the failed initial public offering (share flotation), GPA\'s subsequent financial collapse and later restructuring and involvement of GE Capital (GECAS). It is based on a contemporaneous diary of events kept by the author from 1990 to 1996 while employed by GPA and subsequently GECAS.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Restructuring">Restructuring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinness_Peat_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Restructuring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In a subsequent <a href="/wiki/Restructuring" title="Restructuring">restructuring</a> completed in November 1993, GPA avoided default on its debts by selling some of its aircraft to a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>, GE Capital Aviation Services (GECAS), which also took over the operational management of GPA\'s fleet and most of GPA\'s technical and marketing staff. <a href="/wiki/GE_Capital" title="GE Capital">GE Capital</a> also acquired an option to purchase 90% of GPA\'s ordinary shares at a low price. GPA used the cash from this transaction to repay all its unsecured debt. Chairman and chief executive Tony Ryan transferred to GECAS. He was replaced by Patrick Blaney as chief executive and by <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Stevenson,_Baron_Stevenson_of_Coddenham" title="Dennis Stevenson, Baron Stevenson of Coddenham">Dennis Stevenson</a> (later Lord Stevenson of Coddenham) as chairman.\n</p><p>GPA continued to own a substantial fleet and in March 1996 sold 229 aircraft for $4 bn in what was at the time the second largest securitisation transaction ever. GPA used the cash from this sale to repay all of its secured debt and the company returned to profit in the year to 31 March 1996 with net income of $65 mn.\n</p><p>GPA further consolidated its position in the years to 31 March 1997, 1998 and 1999, reporting net income of $108 mn, $64 mn and $47 mn. In November 1998, <a href="/wiki/Texas_Pacific_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Texas Pacific Group">Texas Pacific Group</a> acquired 62% of the company\'s shares and GE Capital\'s 1993 option was replaced by one to acquire an interest of 23%. As part of this transaction, the name of the company was changed to <b>AerFi Group plc</b>.\n</p><p>In December 1999, AerFi acquired Indigo Aviation, a Swedish aircraft lessor, and by 31 March 2000 was managing a fleet of 104 aircraft and reporting a profit of $68 mn.\n</p><p>In November 2000, AerFi was acquired by debis AirFinance, an affiliate of <a href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="DaimlerChrysler AG">DaimlerChrysler AG</a>, for $750 mn. AerFi\'s fleet and staff were then merged into those of debis AirFinance.\n</p><p>In March 2005, debis AirFinance was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Cerberus_Capital" class="mw-redirect" title="Cerberus Capital">Cerberus Capital</a> and subsequently renamed <a href="/wiki/AerCap" title="AerCap">AerCap</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinness_Peat_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Many of the directors and staff of GPA subsequently went on to found or work for other aircraft lessors, such as <a href="/wiki/GE_Capital_Aviation_Services" title="GE Capital Aviation Services">GECAS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Genesis_Lease" title="Genesis Lease">Genesis Lease</a>, <a href="/wiki/CIT_Group" title="CIT Group">CIT</a>, <a href="/wiki/AerCap" title="AerCap">AerCap</a> (a successor to Guinness Peat and previously debisAirFinance), <a href="/wiki/International_Lease_Finance_Corporation" title="International Lease Finance Corporation">ILFC</a> (now merged into AerCap), Pembroke Capital, International Aircraft Management Group (subsequently RBS Aviation Capital and later SMBC Aviation Capital), <a href="/wiki/Babcock_%26_Brown" title="Babcock &amp; Brown">Babcock &amp; Brown</a> (now Fly Leasing) and <a href="/wiki/Aircastle" title="Aircastle">Aircastle</a>. The availability of this <a href="/wiki/En_cadre" class="mw-redirect" title="En cadre">cadre</a> of highly trained specialists in Ireland is one of the principal reasons (along with a favourable <a href="/wiki/Corporate_tax" title="Corporate tax">corporate tax</a> environment associated with the <a href="/wiki/International_Financial_Services_Centre" title="International Financial Services Centre">International Financial Services Centre</a> (IFSC) in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>) why the country has become one of the worldwide centres of the commercial aircraft financing and leasing industry, with over 40 companies, most located in the IFSC. GPA\'s founder, Tony Ryan, set up his own airline, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> that was Europe\'s biggest in 2014, carrying over 83.8 mn passengers annually.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorship">Sponsorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinness_Peat_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sponsorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>During the <a href="/wiki/1983%E2%80%931985_famine_in_Ethiopia" title="1983\xe2\x80\x931985 famine in Ethiopia">Ethiopian famine</a>, GPA sponsored two <a href="/wiki/Airlift" title="Airlift">airlifts</a> of emergency supplies in October and November 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> A number of other, mainly artistic endeavours benefited from sponsorship by GPA that included the following: In 1988 the first GPA Dublin International Piano Competition took place and was won by French pianist <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Cassard" title="Philippe Cassard">Philippe Cassard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 1984 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Armstrong_(Irish_artist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Robert Armstrong (Irish artist) (page does not exist)">Robert Armstrong</a> won the Guinness Peat Aviation Awards for Emerging Artists<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and in 1986 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eithne_Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eithne Jordan (page does not exist)">Eithne Jordan</a> won during the show held in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>. One of the exhibitors was <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincent_Killowry&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vincent Killowry (page does not exist)">Vincent Killowry</a> and in 1987 GPA bought most of the works at his first one-man show in <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 1989 <a href="/wiki/John_Banville" title="John Banville">John Banville</a> received the Guinness Peat Aviation Book Award for his novel <i>The Book of Evidence</i>, also <a href="/wiki/List_of_winners_and_shortlisted_authors_of_the_Booker_Prize_for_Fiction" class="mw-redirect" title="List of winners and shortlisted authors of the Booker Prize for Fiction">shortlisted for the Booker Prize for Fiction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Also in 1989 the <a href="/wiki/Foynes" title="Foynes">Foynes</a> <a href="/wiki/Flying_boat" title="Flying boat">Flying boat</a> Museum <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.flyingboatmuseum.com/">[1]</a>, at its inception, was sponsored as the <i>GPA Foynes Flying Boat Museum</i>.\n</p><p>A statue of <a href="/wiki/Daedalus" title="Daedalus">Daedalus</a>, sculpted by <a href="/wiki/John_Behan_(sculptor)" title="John Behan (sculptor)">John Behan</a>, was presented to the people of <a href="/wiki/Ennis" title="Ennis">Ennis</a> to mark the town\'s 750th anniversary in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sources">Sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
