Airport is located 4km from Faro, the capital city of Algarve in Portugal. Situated in the southern coast of Portugal, the airport was constructed during the 1960s and inaugurated in 1965. The <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese Government">Portuguese Government</a> was the owner of Faro airport until the 2010s when <a href="/wiki/Vinci_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Vinci Group">Vinci Group</a> became its owner. The national airport authority <a href="/wiki/ANA_Aeroportos_de_Portugal" title="ANA Aeroportos de Portugal">ANA Aeroportos de Portugal</a> has been its operator. Along with the airports in Lisbon, Porto, Ponta Delgada, Santa Maria, Horta, Flores, Madeira, and Porto Santo, the airport\'s concessions to provide support to civil aviation were conceded to ANA Aeroportos de Portugal on 18 December 1998, under provisions of decree 404/98. With this concession, ANA became responsible for the planning, development and construction of future infrastructure.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Since its opening in 1966 to the 2000s, Faro airport has had two major developments: the new passenger terminal building in 1989, and its enlargement in 2001. Faced with growing traffic demand and passenger safety and satisfaction needs, the development plan for 2009\xe2\x80\x932013 saw Faro airport undergo extensive improvements to runway and infrastructure, as well as a widespread renovation of the airport terminal and commercial areas.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The airport authority announced an expansion programme for Faro airport in February 2010. Phase I of the expansion started in 2010 and was completed by 2011. Phase II began in 2011 and was completed by 2013. Faro International Airport handled 5,447,200 passengers and recorded 39,789 aircraft movements in 2008. When the Phase II expansion was completed, the annual capacity of the airport increased from six million to eight million passengers. Passengers handled per hour increased to 3,000, the number of aircraft handled per hour increased to 30, and aircraft parking bays increased from 22 to 33. Additional shops and waiting areas were constructed as part of the expansion. In Phase I, new aircraft stands and taxiways were planned to be constructed. A new <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">instrument landing system</a> (ILS) was installed at the runway along with the installation of a glide reflection mirror. The security area at the runway was also expanded. Phase II involved the renovation of the passenger terminal and the improvement of the landside access.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of 2019, Faro Airport is capable of handling nine million passengers a year. There are 22 stands of which 16 are remote, with 60 check-in desks and 36 boarding gates.<sup id="cite_ref-Info_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Info-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ground transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is close to the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highways_in_Portugal" title="List of highways in Portugal">A22</a> highway, with connections throughout the <a href="/wiki/Algarve" title="Algarve">Algarve</a> and direct to <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Faro airport has 3 different car parking areas. The closest parking area is called "Parking P0 / P1 \xe2\x80\x93 Classic", used for short-term visitors, while parking areas P2 and P3 are used for longer term car storage.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Kiss and Fly is the name for a form of fast parking at the airport. One can drive to the airport to pick up or drop someone off free of charge for up to ten minutes. To drop off passengers, one can stop at the departures curbside and to pick up passengers, you enter the P2 Car Park.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Airport bus routes 14 and 16 run each day between Faro Airport and Faro city centre bus station. From the bus station there are connections to most other Portuguese cities as well as to many Spanish destinations. The airport bus route is currently run by a company called "Proximo".\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway">Railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The nearest railway station is <a href="/wiki/Faro_station" title="Faro station">Faro station</a> which is about 5.7 kilometres (3.5&#160;mi) away and is located close to Faro city centre bus station.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> A study into a <a href="/wiki/Airport_rail_link" title="Airport rail link">rail link</a> to the airport was undertaken in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxis">Taxis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Taxis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Public taxis are available from the airport and operate 24/7. There are several taxi ranks which you can use to hire a taxi from, or you can use a free phone inside the airport to call a taxi. The majority of curbsidetaxis at the airport will only carry up to four passengers; if you require a bigger taxi, it is best to use the phone inside.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ride_hailing">Ride hailing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ride hailing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Ride_hailing" class="mw-redirect" title="Ride hailing">Ride hailing</a> companies\' services are widely available in the area.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car_rentals">Car rentals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Car rentals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are all major car rental companies, in the arrivals area, that can provide car hire service from the Airport. Companies without an airport desk are located at "Parking P4 - Car Hire".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Airports_Council_International" title="Airports Council International">Airports Council International</a> gave Faro Airport the title of <i>Best Improvement</i> in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Wikipedia_and_sources_that_mirror_or_use_it" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="This claim cites another Wikipedia article. Articles need references to reliable third-party sources. (November 2017)">circular reference</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Faro_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 21 December 1992, <a href="/wiki/Martinair_Flight_495" title="Martinair Flight 495">Martinair Flight 495</a> sustained a hard landing in bad weather at Faro Airport, killing 54 passengers and two crew out of a total of 340 people on board.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 24 October 2011, an overnight storm collapsed portions of terminal roofs and blew out most windows in the control tower.
