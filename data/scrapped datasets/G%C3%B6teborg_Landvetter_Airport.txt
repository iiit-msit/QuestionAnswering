code">IATA</a>: <b>GOT</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>ESGG</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> serving the <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> (Swedish: <i>G\xc3\xb6teborg</i>) region in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. With just over 6.8 million passengers in 2018 it is Sweden\'s second-largest airport after <a href="/wiki/Stockholm%E2%80%93Arlanda" class="mw-redirect" title="Stockholm\xe2\x80\x93Arlanda">Stockholm\xe2\x80\x93Arlanda</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-statistics_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statistics-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Landvetter is also an important freight airport. During 2007, 60.1 thousand tonnes of air cargo passed through Landvetter,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> about 60% of the capacity of Arlanda.\n</p><p>The airport is named after <a href="/wiki/Landvetter" title="Landvetter">Landvetter</a> locality, which is in <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4rryda_Municipality" title="H\xc3\xa4rryda Municipality">H\xc3\xa4rryda municipality</a>. It is 11&#160;<a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">NM</a> (20&#160;km; 13&#160;mi) east-southeast<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of Gothenburg and 40&#160;km (25&#160;mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%A5s" title="Bor\xc3\xa5s">Bor\xc3\xa5s</a>. It is operated by <a href="/wiki/Swedavia" title="Swedavia">Swedavia</a>, the national airport company. Since the closure of <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6teborg_City_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="G\xc3\xb6teborg City Airport">G\xc3\xb6teborg City Airport</a> for commercial operations, it\'s the city\'s only commercial passenger airport.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" href="/w/index.php?title=G%C3%B6teborg_Landvetter_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport was opened by king <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf_of_Sweden" class="mw-redirect" title="Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden">Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden</a> on 3 October 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Passenger services, previously at <a href="/wiki/Torslanda_Airport" title="Torslanda Airport">Torslanda Airport</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> north of Gothenburg, were moved to Landvetter in 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2001, some budget airlines began serving the former military base in S\xc3\xa4ve, which was renamed from S\xc3\xa4ve Flygplats to <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg_City_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Gothenburg City Airport">Gothenburg City Airport</a>. That airport was temporary closed down in winter 2014\xe2\x80\x932015 because of large reconstruction needs, meaning an increase of traffic on Landvetter of almost a million annual passengers. There has been a tendency that international air travel has increased, especially on tourists, while domestic has declined somewhat (mostly business travel).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In 2013 the international terminal was extended significantly with new shops, and in 2014 the domestic and international terminals were joined into a single terminal.\n</p><p>On 14 April 2015 <a href="/wiki/Swedavia" title="Swedavia">Swedavia</a> announced a 10-year long contract with <a href="/wiki/DHL_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="DHL Express">DHL Express</a> to build a new 7500 m<sup>2</sup> large cargo terminal, replacing the old 1700 m<sup>2</sup>. The construction will begin in spring 2015 and is underway for one year. This was a step included in plans for Airport City.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2018-2020 the terminal building will be enlarged, with three new air bridges.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\nThere are also plans to build a shortcut on the railway Gothenburg\xe2\x80\x93Bor\xc3\xa5s with a tunnel and a railway station under the airport. Construction start is planned to be 2020 and operation estimated by 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There has been criticism on the choice of location of the airport, which is fairly foggy, located 150 meters above sea level and often affected by low clouds. The runway direction also often means fairly strong crosswinds which can cause landings to scare passengers. In 2015 an <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">instrument landing system</a> CATIIIb was installed which allows landing in fairly dense fog if corresponding system is fitted onboard aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Many but not all aircraft have that (as of 2018).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, most flights were cancelled. During April 2020, only the <a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a> route to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Airport_Schiphol" title="Amsterdam Airport Schiphol">Amsterdam</a> was consistently operated daily. The passenger figures were 99.5% lower in April 2020 than in April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminals">Terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=G%C3%B6teborg_Landvetter_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Landvetter Airport has traditionally had two terminals, domestic and international, but they have merged into one common terminal. In 2009 all baggage drop was moved to in the international terminal, since all baggage had to be screened with new regulations. In 2014 the two terminals joined into one with all baggage collected at the arrivals hall in the previous international terminal. The transfer area, which has several shops, caf\xc3\xa9s and a restaurant, is accessible for all passengers since that year.\n</p><p>There are eight air bridges, at gates 12\xe2\x80\x9317, 19, and 20.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Gates 10\xe2\x80\x9311, 18A\xe2\x80\x9318H and 21C\xe2\x80\x9321D transport passengers to the aircraft via an airside bus transfer. Gate 21A/B is an aircraft stand without an air bridge, with a short walk to the aircraft instead. The eight air bridges are not enough, so airside bus transfer is regularly used. Traditionally gates 10\xe2\x80\x9315 used to be limited to domestic flights but nowadays cater to all flights within the Schengen Area, which are treated as domestic flights.\nGates 21A\xe2\x80\x9321D are located in the international transit area, used for flights outside the <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen Area</a>, and access is only possible after clearing immigration. Gate 19 and 20 are positionable so that, depending on upcoming flights, reaching them may (signed 19B-20B) or may not (signed 19A-20A) require clearing immigration. The freight terminal uses gate numbers below 10.\n</p><p>The airport has a VIP area, where travellers for a fee can go through a dedicated security check, wait in the VIP lounge and be transported by car to the aircraft, avoiding mix with non VIP paying passengers. The VIP area can also hold <a href="/wiki/Wedding" title="Wedding">wedding</a> ceremonies.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
