<a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial-intelligence</a> backed <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a> for <a href="/wiki/Academic_publications" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic publications">academic publications</a> that was developed at the <a href="/wiki/Allen_Institute_for_Artificial_Intelligence" class="mw-redirect" title="Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence">Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence</a> and publicly released in November 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-MyUser_The_Washington_Post_November_3_2015c_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MyUser_The_Washington_Post_November_3_2015c-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It uses recent advances in natural language processing to provide summaries for scholarly papers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Semantic_Scholar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Semantic Scholar provides one-sentence summary of scientific literature. One of its aims was to address the challenge of reading numerous titles and lengthy abstracts on mobile devices.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It also seeks to ensure that the three million scientific papers published yearly reach readers since it is estimated that only half of this literature are ever read.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Artificial intelligence is used to capture the essence of a paper, generating it through an "abstractive" technique.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The project uses a combination of <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">natural language processing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Machine_vision" title="Machine vision">machine vision</a> to add a layer of <a href="/wiki/Semantic_analysis_(linguistics)" title="Semantic analysis (linguistics)">semantic analysis</a> to the traditional methods of <a href="/wiki/Citation_analysis" title="Citation analysis">citation analysis</a>, and to extract relevant figures, entities, and venues from papers.<sup id="cite_ref-Bohannon_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bohannon-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In comparison to <a href="/wiki/Google_Scholar" title="Google Scholar">Google Scholar</a> and <a href="/wiki/PubMed" title="PubMed">PubMed</a>, Semantic Scholar is designed to highlight the most important and influential papers, and to identify the connections between them. Each paper hosted by Semantic Scholar is assigned a unique <a href="/wiki/Identifier" title="Identifier">identifier</a> called the Semantic Scholar Corpus ID (or S2CID for short), for
