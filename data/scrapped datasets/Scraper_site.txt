The content is then mirrored with the goal of creating revenue, usually through advertising and sometimes by selling user data. Scraper sites come in various forms. Some provide little, if any material or information, and are intended to obtain user information such as e-mail addresses, to be targeted for spam e-mail. Price aggregation and shopping sites access multiple listings of a product and allow a user to rapidly compare the prices.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" id="Examples_of_scraper_websites">Examples of scraper websites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scraper_site&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Examples of scraper websites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">Search engines</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> could be considered a type of scraper site. Search engines gather content from other websites, save it in their own databases, index it and present the scraped content to their search engine\'s own users. The majority of content scraped by search engines is copyrighted.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The scraping technique has been used on various dating websites as well.  These sites often combine their scraping activities with <a href="/wiki/Facial_recognition_system" title="Facial recognition system">facial recognition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Scraping is also used on general image recognition websites, and websites specifically made to identify images of crops with pests and diseases<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Made_for_advertising">Made for advertising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scraper_site&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Made for advertising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Some scraper sites are created to make money by using advertising programs. In such case, they are called <i>Made for <a href="/wiki/AdSense" class="mw-redirect" title="AdSense">AdSense</a></i> sites or MFA. This derogatory term refers to websites that have no redeeming value except to lure visitors to the website for the sole purpose of clicking on advertisements.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Made for AdSense</i> sites are considered <a href="/wiki/Spamdexing" title="Spamdexing">search engine spam</a> that dilute the search results with less-than-satisfactory search results. The scraped content is redundant to that which would be shown by the search engine under normal circumstances, had no MFA website been found in the listings.\n</p><p>Some scraper sites link to other sites to improve their <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_optimization" title="Search engine optimization">search engine ranking</a> through a <a href="/wiki/Blog_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Blog network">private blog network</a>. Prior to Google\'s update to its search algorithm known as <a href="/wiki/Google_Panda" title="Google Panda">Panda</a>, a type of scraper site known as an <a href="/wiki/Spam_blog" title="Spam blog">auto blog</a> was quite common among black hat marketers who used a method known as <a href="/wiki/Spamdexing" title="Spamdexing">spamdexing</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legality">Legality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scraper_site&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Scraper sites may violate <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Copyright law">copyright law</a>. Even taking content from an <a href="/wiki/Open_content" class="mw-redirect" title="Open content">open content</a> site can be a <a href="/wiki/Copyright_violation" class="mw-redirect" title="Copyright violation">copyright violation</a>, if done in a way which does not respect the license. For example, sites with large amounts of content such as airlines, consumer electronics, department stores, etc. might be routinely targeted by their competition just to stay abreast of pricing information.\n</p><p>Another type of scraper will pull snippets and text from websites that rank high for keywords they have targeted. This way they hope to rank highly in the <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_results_page" title="Search engine results page">search engine results pages</a> (SERPs), piggybacking on the original page\'s <a href="/wiki/Page_rank" class="mw-redirect" title="Page rank">page rank</a>. <a href="/wiki/RSS_(file_format)" class="mw-redirect" title="RSS (file format)">RSS</a> feeds are vulnerable to scrapers.\n</p><p>Other scraper sites consist of advertisements and paragraphs of words randomly selected from a dictionary. Often a visitor will click on a <a href="/wiki/Pay_per_click" class="mw-redirect" title="Pay per click">pay-per-click</a> advertisement on such  site because it is the only comprehensible text on the page. Operators of these scraper sites gain financially from these clicks. Advertising networks claim to be constantly working to remove these sites from their programs, although these networks benefit directly from the clicks generated at this kind of site. From the advertisers\' point of view, the networks don\'t seem to be making enough effort to stop this problem.\n</p><p>Scrapers tend to be associated with <a href="/wiki/Link_farm" title="Link farm">link farms</a> and are sometimes perceived as the same thing, when multiple scrapers link to the same target site. Whole businesses focus on understanding all<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Doing so will allow SEOs to utilize the already-established <a href="/wiki/Backlink" title="Backlink">backlinks</a> to the domain name. Some spammers may try to match the topic of the expired site or copy the existing content from the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Archive" title="Internet Archive">Internet Archive</a> to maintain the authenticity of the site so that the backlinks don\'t drop.  For example, an expired website about a photographer may be re-registered to create a site about photography tips or use the domain name in their <a href="/wiki/Blog_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Blog network">private blog network</a> to power their own photography site.\n</p><p>Services at some expired domain name registration agents provide both the facility to find these expired domains and to gather the HTML that the domain name used to have on its web site.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
