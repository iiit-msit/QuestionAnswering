over 70 villages in <a href="/wiki/The_Bush_(Alaska)" title="The Bush (Alaska)">Bush Alaska</a> out of hubs in <a href="/wiki/Aniak,_Alaska" title="Aniak, Alaska">Aniak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bethel,_Alaska" title="Bethel, Alaska">Bethel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emmonak,_Alaska" title="Emmonak, Alaska">Emmonak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kotzebue,_Alaska" title="Kotzebue, Alaska">Kotzebue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nome,_Alaska" title="Nome, Alaska">Nome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Mary%27s,_Alaska" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Mary&#39;s, Alaska">St. Mary\'s</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Unalakleet,_Alaska" title="Unalakleet, Alaska">Unalakleet</a>. Offering primarily cargo services, Ryan Air also operates scheduled passenger service out of <a href="/wiki/Aniak,_Alaska" title="Aniak, Alaska">Aniak</a>, and passenger or cargo charters throughout Alaska.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2 id="mw-toc-heading">Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Destinations"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Destinations</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Fleet"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Fleet</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Air_Services&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Ryan Air was established in 1953 as <b>Unalakleet Air Taxi</b> by Wilfred Ryan Sr. as a charter airline. In the 1960s, the company began handling <a href="/wiki/USPS" class="mw-redirect" title="USPS">USPS</a> mail delivery and transportation of schoolteachers for the Bureau of Indian Affairs between communities along the lower <a href="/wiki/Yukon_River" title="Yukon River">Yukon River</a>. In 1977, Wilfred P. Ryan Jr. took over the company after his father died of cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-rahistory_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rahistory-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newra_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newra-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1979,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the company expanded service beyond the <a href="/wiki/Norton_Sound" title="Norton Sound">Norton Sound</a> and changed their name to <b>Ryan Air</b>. With a new fleet of <a href="/wiki/Beech_1900" class="mw-redirect" title="Beech 1900">Beech 1900s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beech_99" class="mw-redirect" title="Beech 99">Beech 99s</a>, Ryan Air grew to the largest commuter carrier in Alaska by 1987, serving 85 cities and villages with a fleet of 28 planes.<sup id="cite_ref-rahistory_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rahistory-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newra_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newra-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, a series of 12 accidents, culminating in a fatal crash in November 1987 that killed 18 of the 21 people on board, led the FAA to shut down the airline in January 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-faainquiry_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faainquiry-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-faashutsdown_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faashutsdown-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 1990s, Ryan Air converted to a cargo-only airline and changed their name to <b>Arctic Transportation Services</b> (ATS), adding <a href="/wiki/CASA_212" class="mw-redirect" title="CASA 212">CASA 212</a> cargo planes to their fleet. They changed their name back to Ryan Air in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-rahistory_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rahistory-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newra_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newra-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nometrash_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nometrash-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011, Ryan Air began passenger charters out of Anchorage, using a newly acquired <a href="/wiki/Pilatus_PC-12" title="Pilatus PC-12">Pilatus PC-12</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2014, Ryan Air resumed regularly scheduled passenger services out of Aniak, AK using their Cessna 207 aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-BeginScheduledPassengerService_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BeginScheduledPassengerService-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015 Ryan Air acquired a fifth <a href="/wiki/CASA_212" class="mw-redirect" title="CASA 212">CASA 212</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
