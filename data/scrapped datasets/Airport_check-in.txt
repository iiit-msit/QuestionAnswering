Airport</a>, Turkmenbashy</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Airport check-in</b> is the process whereby passengers are accepted by an airline at the airport prior to travel.  The airlines typically use service counters found at <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airports</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Check-in" title="Check-in">check-in</a> is normally handled by an <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a> itself or a <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_ground_handling" title="Aircraft ground handling">handling agent</a> working on behalf of an airline. <a href="/wiki/Passenger" title="Passenger">Passengers</a> usually hand over any <a href="/wiki/Checked_baggage" title="Checked baggage">baggage</a> that they do not wish or are not allowed to carry in to the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>\'s cabin and receive a <a href="/wiki/Boarding_pass" title="Boarding pass">boarding pass</a> before they can proceed to board their aircraft.\n</p><p>Check-in is usually the first procedure for a passenger when arriving at an airport, as airline regulations<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> require passengers to check in by certain times prior to the departure of a flight. This duration spans from 15 minutes to 2 hours depending on the destination and airline (with self check in, this can be expanded to 24 hours, if checking in by online processes<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>).  During this process, the passenger has the ability to ask for special accommodations such as seating preferences, inquire about flight or destination information, accumulate <a href="/wiki/Frequent_flyer_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Frequent flyer program">frequent flyer program</a> miles, or pay for upgrades. The required time is sometimes written in the reservation, sometimes written somewhere in websites, and sometimes only referred as "passengers should allow sufficient time for check-in". \n</p><p>The airline check-in\'s main function, however, is to accept luggage that is to go in the aircraft\'s <a href="/wiki/Cargo" title="Cargo">cargo</a> hold and issue boarding passes.\n</p><p>In-town check-in service is a service offered by some cities such as <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_International_Airport" title="Abu Dhabi International Airport">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Incheon_International_Airport" title="Incheon International Airport">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_International_Airport" title="Hong Kong International Airport">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi_International_Airport" title="Indira Gandhi International Airport">Delhi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_International_Airport" title="Kuala Lumpur International Airport">Kuala Lumpur\xe2\x80\x93International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Arlanda_Airport" title="Stockholm Arlanda Airport">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Pearson_International_Airport" title="Toronto Pearson International Airport">Toronto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Taoyuan_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport">Taipei</a>, where passengers may check in luggage in designated places within the city but outside the airport. This reduces check-in time and queuing at the airport.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" id="Types_of_Check-in">Types of Check-in</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types of Check-in">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Destination_or_Point_Check-in">Destination or Point Check-in</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destination or Point Check-in">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>If passenger is checked in for only one sector of the flight, then it is called destination or point check-in.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Through_Check-in">Through Check-in</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Through Check-in">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>If a passenger is checked on all the onward flights and has boarding passes for all the connecting flights and bags are also through checked i.e. passenger does not need to recheck him/herself and the baggages again on the transit, then the check-in is known as through check-in.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_identity_registration">Passenger identity registration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Passenger identity registration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Border_control#Informal_prescreening" title="Border control">Border control \xc2\xa7&#160;Informal prescreening</a></div>\n<p>At the time of check-in, one of the agent\'s primary duties is to check for valid documents. This includes tickets, passports, <a href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" class="mw-redirect" title="Visa (document)">visas</a>, letters of consent, and in some cases, passengers\' address and contact details to comply with immigration requirements.\n</p><p>Some airlines may ask passengers to present the original credit card used for payment.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Baggage_registration">Baggage registration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Baggage registration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>At the time of check-in, the passenger hands over baggage which is checked by the airport security and may be sealed (subject to the security regulations in that country). Anything that is above the weight limit or which is not allowed to be carried by the passenger themselves in the aircraft cabin is usually handed over to the agent at the time of check-in. The baggage allowance, if any, is prescribed by the airline and anything in excess will warrant additional surcharges. \n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Self-service_bag_drop">Self-service bag Passengers then attach the baggage tag and drop the bag at the baggage drop belt.  Passengers without checked luggage can go straight to the lounge (if entitled to lounge access) and check in at the kiosk there using their ePass (a small <a href="/wiki/RFID" class="mw-redirect" title="RFID">RFID</a> device only for its premium customers)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> or proceed straight to the departure gate.  Many airlines use electronic check-in such as ePass, mPass, or similar mobile apps, and these applications serve as the boarding pass.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Seating_assignment">Seating assignment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Seating assignment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Usually at the time of check-in, an option of selecting a specific seat is offered, with passengers asked if they prefer a window or an aisle seat.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_check-in">Online check-in</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Online check-in">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Online check-in is the process in which passengers confirm their presence on a flight via the Internet and typically print their own boarding passes. Depending on the carrier and the specific flight, passengers may also enter details such as meal options and baggage quantities and select their preferred seating.\n</p><p>This service is generally promoted by the airlines to passengers as being easier and faster because it reduces the time a passenger would normally spend at an airport check-in counter. Some airlines, however, would still require passengers to proceed to a check-in counter at the airport, regardless of preferred check-in method, for document verification (e.g., to travel to countries where a visa is required, or to ensure the credit card used to purchase is genuine and/or matches the identity of the person who made the purchase). If passengers need to continue the check-in process at the airport after performing an online check-in, a special lane is typically offered to them to reduce wait times unless all desks are designated as baggage drop-off points. Furthermore, online check-in for a flight is often available earlier than its in-person counterpart.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The process then transfers to passengers\' control over their check-in. Airlines may use the system because self-service is frequently more efficient to operate, with a greater ability to cope with surges in passenger numbers. It also lessens activity at the airport, saving airlines money and reducing passenger waiting times.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> charges passengers a fee which can amount to 60 Euros for <i>not</i> using online check-in, except in certain limited circumstances. Furthermore, by the start of 2010, all passengers were required to check in online, therefore abolishing the use of check-in desks.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, passengers are still charged to print their boarding cards out.\n</p><p>Online check-in is increasingly becoming required in other legacy carriers, particularly in Europe as the airport check-in desks are being relegated as baggage drop points only.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alaska_Airlines" title="Alaska Airlines">Alaska Airlines</a> was the first to offer online check-in. The system was first offered on a limited basis starting in the second quarter of 1999, and was available to the general public on selected flights the following quarter.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Since then, a growing number of airlines have introduced the system.\n</p><p>British Airways were the first airline to deploy online check-in globally having sealed approval from both the CAA (Civil Aviation Authority and FAA (Federal Aviation Authority) for use of the 3-D Barcode technology. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Typically, web-based check-in for airline travel is offered on the airline\'s website not earlier than 24 hours before a flight\'s scheduled departure or seven days for Internet Check-In Assistant.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> However, some airlines allow a longer time, such as Ryanair, which opens online check-in 60 and 4 days beforehand (depending on whether the passenger paid for a seat reservation), AirAsia, which opens it 14 days prior to departure, and <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">easyJet</a>, which opens it 30 days beforehand. Depending on the airline, there can be benefits of better seating or upgrades to first class or business class offered to the first people to check in for a flight. In order to meet this demand, some sites have offered travelers the ability to request an airline check-in prior to the 24-hour window and receive airline boarding passes by email when available from the airline. Some airlines charge for the privilege of early check-in before the 24-hour window opens, thus capitalising on the demand for desirable seats such as those immediately behind a bulkhead or emergency exit row.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_check-in">Mobile check-in</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mobile check-in">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the mid-late 2000s, checking in was made possible using a passenger\'s mobile phone or <a href="/wiki/Personal_digital_assistant" title="Personal digital assistant">PDA</a>. A GPRS or 3G-capable <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> or an internet-capable <a href="/wiki/Personal_digital_assistant" title="Personal digital assistant">PDA</a> is required in most instances (Finnair allows check-in by text message<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>), and the check-in feature may be accessed by keying in a website on the mobile phone\'s browser or by downloading a dedicated application. The process is then similar to that which one would expect when checking in using a personal computer.\n</p><p>At the end of the mobile check-in process, some airlines send a mobile boarding pass to a passenger\'s mobile device, which can be scanned at the airport during security checks and boarding. However, others send an electronic confirmation with a barcode that can be presented to the staff at check-in or scanned at the kiosks to continue the check-in process (i.e., to have boarding passes issued).\n</p><p>One disadvantage of early check-in is that it puts a restriction on a person\'s agent changing their flight. As the ticket coupon has to be "opened" again prior to any changes being made, this may take some time to arrange.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premium_check-in_and_lounge_access">Premium check-in and lounge access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airport_check-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Premium check-in and lounge access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>If the passenger carries a first or business class ticket or presents a certain <a href="/wiki/Frequent_flyer_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Frequent flyer program">frequent flyer program</a> membership card (usually the higher-level tiers), or any other arrangements with the carrier, access to the premium check-in area and/or the <a href="/wiki/Airport_lounge" title="Airport lounge">lounge</a> may be offered.\n</p><p>Premium check-in areas vary among airlines and airports. The main airport in which an airline hub is located normally offers a more thorough and exclusive premium check-in experience, normally inside a separate check-in lounge. For example, <a href="/wiki/Air_New_Zealand" title="Air New Zealand">Air New Zealand</a>\'s Auckland International premium check-in lounge provides a dedicated customs clearance counter and direct shortcut access to the security checkpoints. Airlines operating in minor airports generally offer an exclusive and separate premium check-in queue lane, often combined for its first, business, and/or premium economy passengers.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Singapore_Airlines" title="Singapore Airlines">Singapore Airlines</a> also offers this service to First Class and Suites passengers, whose flights depart Singapore <a href="/wiki/Changi_Airport" title="Changi Airport">Changi Airport</a>\'s Terminal 3. These passengers have a dedicated curb side entrance and can wait at couches while staff assist them in checking-in. They are then led to a dedicated passport control counter.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Emirates_(airline)" title="Emirates (airline)">Emirates</a> provides its first-class/business-class customers with individual and separate check-in lane at its hub <a href="/wiki/Dubai_International_Airport" title="Dubai International Airport">DXB</a>, to divide most economy-class customers from main check-in lobby apart, and then ensuring those first-class/business-class customers\' privacy.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/SkyTeam" title="SkyTeam">SkyTeam</a> provides the priority check-in service "SkyPriority", whose members could access the check-in counter to authorize the prioritized check-in service, along with the larger luggage capacity.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In-town_check-in">In-town check-in</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
