(<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <i lang="hr">Zra\xc4\x8dna luka Franjo Tu\xc4\x91man Zagreb</i>) or <b>Zagreb Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <i lang="hr">Zra\xc4\x8dna luka Zagreb</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>ZAG</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LDZA</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>. It is the largest and busiest airport in Croatia. In 2019, it handled 3.45 million passengers and some 13,000 tons of cargo.\n</p><p>Named after <a href="/wiki/Franjo_Tu%C4%91man" title="Franjo Tu\xc4\x91man">Franjo Tu\xc4\x91man</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_Croatia" title="President of Croatia">President of Croatia</a>, the airport is located some 10&#160;km (6.2&#160;mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Zagreb_Central_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Zagreb Central Station">Zagreb Central Station</a><sup id="cite_ref-AIP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Velika_Gorica" title="Velika Gorica">Velika Gorica</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Airline_hub" title="Airline hub">hub</a> of the Croatian <a href="/wiki/Flag_carrier" title="Flag carrier">flag carrier</a> <a href="/wiki/Croatia_Airlines" title="Croatia Airlines">Croatia Airlines</a> and a focus city for <a href="/wiki/Trade_Air" title="Trade Air">Trade Air</a>. The main base of the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Air_Force" title="Croatian Air Force">Croatian Air Force</a> is also located at the airport\'s premises. Moreover, the Croatian Air Traffic Control has its administratration situated on the grounds of the airport.\n</p><p>The airport was awarded to the ZAIC consortium (Zagreb Airport International Company) in a 30-year <a href="/wiki/Concession_(contract)" title="Concession (contract)">concession</a> under the terms of a contract signed by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Croatia" title="Government of Croatia">Government of Croatia</a> with the aforementioned. The contract includes the financing, designing and construction of a new passenger terminal which was opened in March 2017. For the purpose of managing the airport ZAIC registered a company called <a href="/wiki/Joint-stock_company" title="Joint-stock company">MZLZ d.d.</a> (Me\xc4\x91unarodna zra\xc4\x8dna luka Zagreb d.d.) that is now the operator of the Airport.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zagreb_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The history of Zagreb civil aviation began in 1909 when the first airfield was built close to the western city neighbourhood (<a href="/wiki/Zagreb#city_districts" title="Zagreb">city district</a>) of <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Crnomerec" title="\xc4\x8crnomerec">\xc4\x8crnomerec</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>With the creation of the first Yugoslav flag carrier <a href="/wiki/Aeroput" title="Aeroput">Aeroput</a> in 1927, the airport was relocated to the <a href="/wiki/Borongaj" title="Borongaj">Borongaj</a> airfield in 1928 which began serving the ever-growing number of passengers on 15 February of that year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Although several European airliners connected the city it was mostly Aeroput which connected Zagreb to major destinations across Europe and thus significantly increased traffic at Zagreb in the period preceding the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>.\n</p><p>Following World War II, commercial services were moved to a former military airbase near the village of <a href="/wiki/Lu%C4%8Dko" title="Lu\xc4\x8dko">Lu\xc4\x8dko</a> south-west of the city in 1947. <a href="/wiki/Jat_Airways" title="Jat Airways">JAT Yugoslav Airlines</a> took the role of Aeroput and made Zagreb its second hub. At its peak in 1959, Lu\xc4\x8dko served 167,000 passengers.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg/280px-Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg" decoding="async" width="280" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg/420px-Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg/560px-Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5258" data-file-height="2958" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Novi_putni%C4%8Dki_terminal_.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Zagreb Airport terminal</div></div></div>\n<p>The current location of the airport at Pleso in the south-east of Lu\xc4\x8dko opened in 1962 with a 2,500&#160;m (8,200&#160;ft) long <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> and 1,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (11,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">terminal</a>. By 1966, Zagreb Airport got a new 5,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (54,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) state-of-the-art passenger terminal. The runway capacity was lengthened to its current 3,252&#160;m (10,669&#160;ft) in 1974.\n</p><p>In the 1980s, Zagreb Airport was the second largest in Yugoslavia by passenger and aircraft movements. Yugoslav flag-carrier JAT maintained a hub in Zagreb and connected the city to numerous destinations including New York, Chicago, Toronto which inevitably had a major impact on air traffic at Zagreb during that period.\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a> in the 1990s, the airport became a <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> hub for getting Medical &amp; food supplies to war-ridden <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bosnia" class="mw-redirect" title="Bosnia">Bosnia</a> . The British 24th <a href="/wiki/Field_Ambulance" title="Field Ambulance">Field Ambulance</a> were stationed in a former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_People%27s_Army" title="Yugoslav People&#39;s Army">Yugoslav People\'s Army</a> camp at the airport.\n</p><p>Following an increase in passenger numbers and the necessity to upgrade its infrastructure, the airport installed a CAT-IIIb <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">instrument landing system</a> (ILS) in 2004.\n</p><p>In 2008, a new VIP terminal was added and the terminal extended for extra amenities, restaurants and bars. The terminal was expanded to 15,500&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (167,000&#160;sq&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By 2010, the old terminal has been nearing its maximum annual capacity. That year the passenger terminal received a major facelift in the course of which a viewing platform with a bar was added.\n</p><p>On 12 April 2012, the ZAIC (<a href="/wiki/Groupe_ADP" title="Groupe ADP">Groupe ADP</a>) consortium received a 30-year concession for the airport from the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Croatia" title="Government of Croatia">Government of Croatia</a>. The concession includes financing, designing and constructing a new passenger terminal. The construction of a brand new 70,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (750,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) terminal facility designed by Neidhardt architects of Zagreb and carried out by Bouygues B\xc3\xa2timent International in partnership with Viadukt began on 18 December 2013 with the aim to replace the old terminal. It now has an initial annual capacity of 5.5 million passengers in the first phase and was officially completed in October 2016. The official inauguration of the terminal was on 28 March 2017. ZAIC now operates the entire airport for 30 years including the runways, the current passenger terminal, the cargo terminal, car parks and all future property developments. The concession contract involves a total investment of around \xe2\x82\xac324m (\xc2\xa3259m): \xe2\x82\xac236 million for the design and construction of the new terminal and \xe2\x82\xac88 million for operation of all airport infrastructure for the entire period of the concession.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 27 February 2020, the runway, formerly designated as 05/23, was redesignated to 04/22 due to the change in magnetic declination.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
