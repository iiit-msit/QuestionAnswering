airlines of the United States">major airlines of the United States</a> and the world\'s largest <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carrier</a> airline.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a> and has scheduled service to 111 destinations in the United States and ten additional countries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As of 2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Southwest_Airlines&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, Southwest carried more domestic passengers than any other United States airline.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airline was established on March 15, 1967, by <a href="/wiki/Herb_Kelleher" title="Herb Kelleher">Herb Kelleher</a> as <b>Air Southwest Co.</b> and adopted its current name, Southwest Airlines Co., in 1971, when it began operating as an <a href="/wiki/Intrastate_airline" title="Intrastate airline">intrastate airline</a> wholly within the state of Texas, first flying between Dallas, Houston and San Antonio.<sup id="cite_ref-briefhistory_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-briefhistory-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1966to1971_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1966to1971-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The airline has nearly 60,000 employees and operates about 4,000 departures a day during peak travel season.<sup id="cite_ref-factsheet_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factsheet-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup Airlines">History of Southwest Airlines</a></div>\n<p>Southwest Airlines was founded in 1966 by <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Kelleher" class="mw-redirect" title="Herbert Kelleher">Herbert Kelleher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rollin_King" title="Rollin King">Rollin King</a>, and in 1967 it was incorporated as Air Southwest Co. Three other airlines took legal action to try to prevent the company from its planned strategy of undercutting their prices by flying only within Texas and thus being exempt from various regulations. The lawsuits were resolved in 1970, and in 1971 the airline began operating regularly scheduled flights between <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Love_Field" title="Dallas Love Field">Dallas Love Field</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Bush_Intercontinental_Airport" title="George Bush Intercontinental Airport">Houston</a> and between Love Field and <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_International_Airport" title="San Antonio International Airport">San Antonio</a>, and adopted the name Southwest Airlines Co. In 1975, Southwest began operating flights to various additional cities within Texas, and in 1979 it began flying to adjacent states. Service to the East and the Southeast started in the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Southwest turned a profit for 47 consecutive fiscal years from 1973 through 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southwest_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Southwest_Airlines_destinations" title="List of Southwest Airlines destinations">List of Southwest Airlines destinations</a></div>\n<p>To date, Southwest schedules flights to 115 destinations in 40 states, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, Mexico, Central America, and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Southwest does not use the <a href="/wiki/Spoke-hub_distribution_paradigm" class="mw-redirect" title="Spoke-hub distribution paradigm">hub and spoke</a> system of other major airlines, preferring the <a href="/wiki/Point-to-point_transit" title="Point-to-point transit">point-to-point</a> system, combined with a <a href="/wiki/Depeaking" class="mw-redirect" title="Depeaking">rolling hub</a> model in its base cities.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a title="Boeing 727">Boeing 727-200s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Southwest is the largest operator of the Boeing 737 worldwide, with only 737 aircraft in its fleet.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Using a single basic aircraft type allows Southwest pilots and flight attendants to href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> However, speculation ended in March 2021 when Southwest announced an order for 100 MAX 7 jets and said that negotiations with Airbus were never initiated.<sup id="cite_ref-MAX7_DMN_2021_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAX7_DMN_2021-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_experience">Passenger After completing a testing phase that began in February 2009, Southwest announced on August 21, 2009, that it would begin rolling out in-flight Wi-Fi Internet connectivity via Global Eagle Entertainment\'s satellite-broadband based product. Unlike many competitors\' programs that were based on miles flown, The Company Club credited for trips flown regardless of distance.<sup id="cite_ref-company_club_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company_club-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Southwest Airlines renamed its frequent flyer program <b>Rapid Rewards</b> on April 25, 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-rr_name_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rr_name-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The original Rapid Rewards program offered one credit per one-way flight from an origin to a destination including any stops or connections on Southwest Airlines. Members earn and redeem points based on a three-tier fare scale multiplier and the cost of the ticket. Changes also included no <a href="/wiki/Blackout_dates" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackout dates">blackout dates</a>, seat restrictions or expiring credits. Since October 18, 2019, Rapid Rewards points do not expire, as long as the member is alive.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> It also adds more In July 2008, Herb Kelleher resigned from his position as chairman. Barrett left her post on the board of directors and as a corporate secretary in May 2008 and as president in July 2008. Kelleher was president and CEO of Southwest from September 1981 to June 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On January 10, 2017, Southwest announced changes to the company\'s executive leadership ranks with Thomas M. id="cite_ref-warn2020_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warn2020-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> As a result of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 class="mw-headline" id="Impact_on_carriers">Impact on carriers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southwest_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Impact on carriers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Southwest has been a major inspiration to other low-cost carriers, and its business model has been repeated many times around the world. The competitive strategy combines a high level of employee and aircraft productivity with low unit costs by reducing aircraft turn around time particularly at the gate.<sup id="cite_ref-upinair_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upinair-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Europe\'s <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> are two of the best-known airlines to follow Southwest\'s business strategy in that continent. Other airlines with a business model based on Southwest\'s system include Canada\'s <a href="/wiki/WestJet" title="WestJet">WestJet</a>, Malaysia\'s <a href="/wiki/AirAsia" title="AirAsia">AirAsia</a> (the first and biggest LCC in Asia), India\'s <a href="/wiki/IndiGo" title="IndiGo">IndiGo</a>, Australia\'s <a href="/wiki/Jetstar_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="Jetstar Airways">Jetstar</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Qantas" title="Qantas">Qantas</a> (although Jetstar now operates three aircraft types), <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Cebu_Pacific" title="Cebu Pacific">Cebu Pacific</a>, Thailand\'s <a href="/wiki/Nok_Air" title="Nok Air">Nok Air</a>, Mexico\'s <a href="/wiki/Volaris" title="Volaris">Volaris</a>, Indonesia\'s <a href="/wiki/Lion_Air" title="Lion Air">Lion Air</a> and Turkey\'s <a href="/wiki/Pegasus_Airlines" title="Pegasus Airlines">Pegasus Airlines</a>. Although Southwest has been a major inspiration to many other airlines, including Ryanair, AirAsia, Lion Air, and Jetstar, the management strategies, for example, of Ryanair, AirAsia, Lion Air, and Jetstar differ significantly from those of Southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-upinair_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upinair-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> All these different management strategies can be seen as means of differentiation from other competitors in order to gain competitive advantages.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising">Advertising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southwest_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Advertising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The company has always employed humor in its advertising. Former slogans include "Love Is Still Our Field," "Just Plane Smart," "The Somebody Else Up There Who Loves You," "You\'re Now Free To Move About The Country," "THE Low Fare Airline," "Grab your bag, It\'s On!" and "Welcome Aboard." The airline\'s current slogan is "Low fares. Nothing to hide. That\'s TransFarency!"<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 1992, shortly after Southwest started using the "Just Plane Smart" motto, Stevens Aviation, which had been using "Plane Smart" for its motto, advised Southwest that it was infringing on its trademark.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Instead of a lawsuit, the CEOs for both companies staged an arm-wrestling match. Held at the now-demolished <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Sportatorium" title="Dallas Sportatorium">Dallas Sportatorium</a> (the famed wrestling facility) and set for two out of three rounds, the loser of each round was to pay $5,000 to the charity of his choice, with the winner gaining the use of the trademarked phrase. A promotional video was created showing the CEOs "training" for the bout (with CEO Herb Kelleher being helped up during a sit-up where a cigarette and glass of <a href="/wiki/Wild_Turkey_(bourbon)" title="Wild Turkey (bourbon)">Wild Turkey</a> 101 whiskey was waiting) and distributed among the employees and also as a video press release along with the video of the match itself. Herb Kelleher lost the match for Southwest, with Stevens Aviation winning the rights to the phrase. Kurt Herwald, CEO of Stevens Aviation, immediately granted the use of "Just Plane Smart" to Southwest Airlines. Southwest Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flying_while_Muslim#Southwest_Airlines" title="Flying while Muslim">Flying while Muslim \xc2\xa7&#160;Southwest Airlines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_MAX_groundings" title="Boeing 737 MAX groundings">Boeing 737 MAX groundings</a></div>\n<p>On June 22, 2011, a March 25 recording of an in-flight transmission of Southwest pilot James Taylor apparently unintentionally broadcasting a conversation with his first officer was released to the press. The conversation was peppered with foul language directed at gay, overweight, and older flight attendants. According to Southwest, the pilot was reprimanded and temporarily <a href="/wiki/Suspended_without_pay" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspended without pay">suspended without pay</a> and received <a href="/wiki/Diversity_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Diversity education">diversity education</a> before being reinstated. Taylor also sent an e-mail apology to all of Southwest\'s employees, especially the crew members who were criticized.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 26, 2017, a woman was removed from a Southwest flight after claiming to have a life-threatening allergy to dogs, two of which were present on the aircraft with one being a certified service animal, and having to be removed by law enforcement after failing to follow the instructions of airline staff. After learning about the woman\'s allergy, Southwest employees requested that she prove her condition with the correct documentation. When she failed to do so, staff asked her to exit the aircraft multiple times. She refused, which prompted law enforcement to step in and remove the passenger. The interactions between the woman and the officers were recorded and posted online to many social media platforms and gained much attention.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On December 29, 2017, a family was removed from a flight from <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Midway_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago Midway Airport">Chicago Midway Airport</a> to Santa Ana, California, because of an unconfirmed lice accusation. The family did not have lice after all and was re-accommodated on a flight two days later. Southwest claims to have refunded the full fare. The family claims that the airline never compensated them for the interruption to their trip.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2019 a Southwest flight attendant filed a lawsuit against the airline, claiming that two pilots had livestreamed footage from a camera hidden in the plane\'s toilet to an iPad and that one of the pilots had admitted that such cameras were a "top-secret security measure" installed in all the airline\'s <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Next_Generation#737-800" title="Boeing 737 Next Generation">737-800</a> planes.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Both Southwest and the pilot union state this never happened, and that it was a "poor attempt at humor" and the video viewed was previously recorded by the pilot which he shot of himself fully clothed.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2020, a report conducted by the Transportation Department inspector general found that Southwest was flying airplanes with safety concerns and that the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> was failing to properly oversee the airline.<sup
