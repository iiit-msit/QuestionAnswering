For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Web_archive_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Web archive (disambiguation)">Web archive (disambiguation)</a>.</div>\n<p class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p><p><b>Web archiving</b> is the process of collecting portions of the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a> to ensure the information is <a href="/wiki/Digital_preservation" title="Digital preservation">preserved</a> in an <a href="/wiki/Archive" title="Archive">archive</a> for future researchers, historians, and the public. Web archivists typically employ <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">web crawlers</a> for automated capture due to the massive size and amount of information on the Web. The largest web archiving organization based on a bulk crawling approach is the <a href="/wiki/Wayback_Machine" title="Wayback Machine">Wayback Machine</a>, which strives to maintain an archive of the entire Web.\n</p><p>The growing portion of human culture created and recorded on the web makes it inevitable that more and more libraries and archives will have to face the challenges of web archiving.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/National_library" title="National library">National libraries</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_archive" class="mw-redirect" title="National archive">national archives</a> and various consortia of organizations are also involved in archiving culturally important Web content.\n</p><p>Commercial web archiving software and services are also available to organizations who need to archive their own web content for corporate heritage, regulatory, or legal purposes.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" tocsection-14"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">8</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_development">History and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While curation and organization of the web has been prevalent since the mid- to late-1990s, one of the first large-scale web archiving project was the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Archive" title="Internet Archive">Internet Archive</a>, a non-profit organization created by <a href="/wiki/Brewster_Kahle" title="Brewster Kahle">Brewster Kahle</a> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-kitsuregawa_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitsuregawa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Internet Archive released its own search engine for viewing archived web content, the <a href="/wiki/Wayback_Machine" title="Wayback Machine">Wayback Machine</a>, in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-kitsuregawa_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitsuregawa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As of 2018, the Internet Archive was home to 40 petabytes of data.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Internet Archive also developed many of its own tools for collecting and storing its data, including Petabox for storing the large amounts of data efficiently and safely, and Hertrix, a web crawler developed in conjunction with the Nordic national libraries.<sup id="cite_ref-kitsuregawa_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitsuregawa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Other projects launched around the same time included Australia\'s <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Archive" class="mw-redirect" title="Pandora Archive">Pandora</a> and Tasmanian web archives and Sweden\'s Kulturarw3.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 2001 <span class="failed-verification-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">to 2010,</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="Neither say that it ended in 2010 (July 2020)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the International Web Archiving Workshop (IWAW) provided a platform to share experiences and exchange ideas.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/International_Internet_Preservation_Consortium" title="International Internet Preservation Consortium">International Internet Preservation Consortium (IIPC)</a>, established in 2003, has facilitated international collaboration in developing standards and open source tools for the creation of web archives.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The now-defunct <a href="/wiki/Internet_Memory_Foundation" title="Internet Memory Foundation">Internet Memory Foundation</a> was founded in 2004 and founded by the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> in order to archive the web in the Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-kitsuregawa_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitsuregawa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This project developed and released many open source tools, such as "rich media capturing, temporal coherence&#160;analysis, spam assessment, and terminology evolution detection."<sup id="cite_ref-kitsuregawa_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitsuregawa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The data from the foundation is now housed by the Internet Archive, but not currently publicly accessible.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Despite the fact that there is no centralized responsibility for its preservation, web content is rapidly becoming the official record. For example, in 2017, the United States Department of Justice affirmed that the government treats the President\xe2\x80\x99s tweets as official statements.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collecting_the_web">Collecting the web</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Collecting the web">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Web archivists generally archive various types of web content including <a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">HTML</a> web pages, <a href="/wiki/Style_sheet_(web_development)" title="Style sheet (web development)">style sheets</a>, <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a>, <a href="/wiki/Digital_image" title="Digital image">images</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Digital_video" title="Digital video">video</a>. They also archive <a href="/wiki/Metadata" title="Metadata">metadata</a> about the collected resources such as access time, <a href="/wiki/MIME_type" class="mw-redirect" title="MIME type">MIME type</a>, and content length. This metadata is useful in establishing <a href="/wiki/Authentication" title="Authentication">authenticity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Provenance" title="Provenance">provenance</a> of the archived collection.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methods_of_collection">Methods of collection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Methods of collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Web_archiving_initiatives" title="List of Web archiving initiatives">List of Web archiving initiatives</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Remote_harvesting">Remote harvesting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Remote harvesting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The most common web archiving technique uses <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">web crawlers</a> to automate the process of collecting <a href="/wiki/Web_page" title="Web page">web pages</a>. Web crawlers typically access web pages in the same manner that users with a browser see the Web, and therefore provide a comparatively simple method of remote harvesting web content. Examples of web crawlers used for web archiving include:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Heritrix" title="Heritrix">Heritrix</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/HTTrack" title="HTTrack">HTTrack</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Wget" title="Wget">Wget</a></li></ul>\n<p>There exist various free services which may be used to archive web resources "on-demand", using web crawling techniques. These services include the <a href="/wiki/Wayback_Machine" title="Wayback Machine">Wayback Machine</a> and <a href="/wiki/WebCite" title="WebCite">WebCite</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Database_archiving">Database archiving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Database archiving">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Database archiving refers to methods for archiving the underlying content of database-driven websites. It typically requires the extraction of the <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> content into a standard <a href="/wiki/Logical_schema" title="Logical schema">schema</a>, often using <a href="/wiki/XML" title="XML">XML</a>. Once stored in that standard format, the archived content of multiple databases can then be made available using a single access system. This approach is exemplified by the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://deeparc.sourceforge.net/">DeepArc</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110227202744/http://www.nla.gov.au/xinq/">Xinq</a> tools developed by the <a href="/wiki/Biblioth%C3%A8que_Nationale_de_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Biblioth\xc3\xa8que Nationale de France">Biblioth\xc3\xa8que Nationale de France</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Australia" title="National Library of Australia">National Library of Australia</a> respectively. DeepArc enables the structure of a <a href="/wiki/Relational_database" title="Relational database">relational database</a> to be mapped to an <a href="/wiki/XML_schema" title="XML schema">XML schema</a>, and the content exported into an XML document. Xinq then allows that content to be delivered online. Although the original layout and behavior of the website cannot be preserved exactly, Xinq does allow the basic querying and retrieval functionality to be replicated.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transactional_archiving">Transactional archiving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transactional archiving">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Transactional archiving is an event-driven approach, which collects the actual transactions which take place between a <a href="/wiki/Web_server" title="Web server">web server</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a>. It is primarily used as a means of preserving evidence of the content which was actually viewed on a particular <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">website</a>, on a given date. This may be particularly important for organizations which need to comply with legal or regulatory requirements for disclosing and retaining information.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A transactional archiving system typically operates by intercepting every <a href="/wiki/HTTP" class="mw-redirect" title="HTTP">HTTP</a> request to, and response from, the web server, filtering each response to eliminate duplicate content, and permanently storing the responses as bitstreams.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Difficulties_and_limitations">Difficulties and limitations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Difficulties and limitations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crawlers">Crawlers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Crawlers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Web archives which rely on web crawling as their primary means of collecting the Web are influenced by the difficulties of web crawling:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Robots_exclusion_protocol" class="mw-redirect" title="Robots exclusion protocol">robots exclusion protocol</a> may request crawlers not access portions of a website. Some web archivists may ignore the request and crawl those portions anyway.</li>\n<li>Large portions of a web site may be hidden in the <a href="/wiki/Deep_Web_(search_indexing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Deep Web (search indexing)">Deep Web</a>. For example, the results page behind a web form can lie in the Deep Web if crawlers cannot follow a link to the results page.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Crawler_trap" class="mw-redirect" title="Crawler trap">Crawler traps</a> (e.g., calendars) may cause a crawler to download an infinite number of pages, so crawlers are usually configured to limit the number of dynamic pages they crawl.</li>\n<li>Most of the archiving tools do not capture the page as it is. It is observed that ad banners and images are often missed while archiving.</li></ul>\n<p>However, it is important to note that a native format web archive, i.e., a fully browsable web archive, with working links, media, etc., is only really possible using crawler technology.\n</p><p>The Web is so large that crawling a significant portion of it takes a large number of technical resources. The Web is changing so fast that portions of a website may change before a crawler has even finished crawling it.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_limitations">General limitations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: General limitations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Some web servers are configured to return different pages to web archiver requests than they would in response to regular browser requests. This is typically done to fool search engines into directing more user traffic to a website, and is often done to avoid accountability, or to provide enhanced content only to those browsers that can display it.\n</p><p>Not only must web archivists deal with the technical challenges of web archiving, they must also contend with intellectual property laws. Peter Lyman<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> states that "although the Web is popularly regarded as a <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a> resource, it is <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyrighted</a>; thus, archivists have no legal right to copy the Web". However <a href="/wiki/National_library" title="National library">national libraries</a> in some countries<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> have a legal right to copy portions of the web under an extension of a <a href="/wiki/Legal_deposit" title="Legal deposit">legal deposit</a>.\n</p><p>Some private non-profit web archives that are made publicly accessible like <a href="/wiki/WebCite" title="WebCite">WebCite</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Archive" title="Internet Archive">Internet Archive</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Memory_Foundation" title="Internet Memory Foundation">Internet Memory Foundation</a> allow content owners to hide or remove archived content that they do not want the public to have access to. Other web archives are only accessible from certain locations or have regulated usage. WebCite cites a recent lawsuit against Google\'s caching, which <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> won.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Laws">Laws</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_archiving&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Laws">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2017 the <a href="/wiki/Financial_Industry_Regulatory_Authority" title="Financial Industry Regulatory Authority">Financial Industry Regulatory Authority, Inc.</a> (FINRA), a United States financial regulatory organization, released a notice stating all the business doing digital communications are required to keep a record. This includes website data, social media posts, and messages.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Some <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Copyright law">copyright laws</a> may inhibit Web archiving. For instance, academic archiving by <a href="/wiki/Sci-Hub" title="Sci-Hub">Sci-Hub</a> falls outside the bounds of contemporary copyright law. The site provides enduring access to academic works including those that do not have an <a href="/wiki/Open_access" title="Open access">open access</a> license and thereby contributes to the archival of scientific research which may otherwise be lost.<sup id="cite_ref-Claburn_2020_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Claburn_2020-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16"
