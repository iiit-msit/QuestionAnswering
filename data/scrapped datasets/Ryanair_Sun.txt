class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.buzzair.com/">www<wbr />.buzzair<wbr />.com</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Buzz</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> airline headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>. Formerly called Ryanair Sun, it is a subsidiary of the Irish airline company, Ryanair Holdings, and a sister airline to <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair DAC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair_UK" title="Ryanair UK">Ryanair UK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta_Air" title="Malta Air">Malta Air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laudamotion" class="mw-redirect" title="Laudamotion">Laudamotion</a>. \n</p><p>Formed in 2017 and initially positioned as a <a href="/wiki/Charter_airline" class="mw-redirect" title="Charter airline">charter airline</a> without any scheduled services, Buzz operates scheduled flights on behalf of Ryanair, and charter flights in its own right, out of Poland.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In March 2019, Ryanair announced that Ryanair Sun would be rebranded as Buzz in autumn 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Buzz commenced operations in January 2020.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On April 3, 2018, the carrier received an <a href="/wiki/Air_operator%27s_certificate" title="Air operator&#39;s certificate">air operator\'s certificate</a> granted by the Polish <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(Poland)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (Poland)">Civil Aviation Authority</a>. It started operations on 23 April 2018 and the first flight took place on 26 April 2018, on the route from <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84%E2%80%93%C5%81awica_Airport" title="Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93\xc5\x81awica Airport">Pozna\xc5\x84\xe2\x80\x93\xc5\x81awica Airport</a> to <a href="/wiki/Zakynthos_International_Airport" title="Zakynthos International Airport">Zakynthos International Airport</a>. In Poznan and Wroclaw the carrier will use the infrastructure of already existing bases, while in Katowice and Warsaw new ones were created. \n</p><p>Representatives of the carrier announced that all five aircraft in the Ryanair Sun fleet would receive Polish registration numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During summer 2018, Ryanair Sun operated one own Boeing 737-800 registered as SP-RSA. The aircraft was employed on charter flights from Warsaw-Chopin.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations_on_behalf_of_Ryanair_mainline">Operations on behalf of Ryanair mainline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buzz_(Ryanair)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations on behalf of Ryanair mainline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In September 2018 Ryanair announced the closure of its own Polish bases by 1 January 2019. Operations were to be transferred to Ryanair Sun, meaning Ryanair Sun would operate on behalf of its parent company. In late October 2018, the then-only Ryanair Sun aircraft SP-RSA ceased charter operations out of Warsaw Chopin. The aircraft subsequently replaced Ryanair mainline capacity out of Warsaw Modlin, now operating scheduled services on behalf of its parent company.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Starting from November 2018, several former Ryanair-operated Boeing 737 were moved from the Irish to the Polish registry and now operated by Ryanair Sun on behalf of its parent companies scheduled flights out of Poland.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_(10a).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_%2810a%29.jpg/220px-SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_%2810a%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_%2810a%29.jpg/330px-SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_%2810a%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_%2810a%29.jpg/440px-SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_%2810a%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5935" data-file-height="3846" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SP-RSL_Boeing_737-800_Ryanair_Sun_PMI_2019-07-02_(10a).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Boeing 737 of Buzz. Buzz was the name of a <a href="/wiki/Buzz_(airline)" title="Buzz (airline)">UK budget airline</a> Ryanair bought from <a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a> in April 2003. Buzz will still operate scheduled and charter flights from its bases in Poland, Czech Republic, and Bulgaria and aims to expand its fleet from 17 Boeing 737-800s to 25 by summer 2019.<sup
