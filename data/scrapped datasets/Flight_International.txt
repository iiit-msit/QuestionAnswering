href="https://www.worldcat.org/search?fq=x0:jrnl&amp;q=n2:0015-3710">0015-3710</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><i><b>Flight International</b></i> is a monthly <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazine</a> focused on <a href="/wiki/Aerospace" title="Aerospace">aerospace</a>. Published in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and founded in 1909 as "A Journal devoted to the Interests, Practice, and Progress of Aerial Locomotion and Transport",<sup id="cite_ref-bruce133_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bruce133-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> it is the world\'s oldest continuously published aviation news magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-Ward_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ward-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Flight International</i> is published by <a href="/wiki/DVV_Media_Group" title="DVV Media Group">DVV Media Group</a>. Competitors include <a href="/wiki/Jane%27s_Information_Group" title="Jane&#39;s Information Group">Jane\'s Information Group</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Aviation_Week" class="mw-redirect" title="Aviation Week">Aviation Week</a></i>.  Former editors of, and contributors include H. F. King, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gunston" title="Bill Gunston">Bill Gunston</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_W._R._Taylor" title="John W. R. Taylor">John W. R. Taylor</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Learmount&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="David Learmount (page does not exist)">David Learmount</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2 id="mw-toc-heading">Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Notes"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flight_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The founder and first editor of <i>Flight</i> was <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Spooner" title="Stanley Spooner">Stanley Spooner</a>. He was also the creator and editor of <i>The Automotor Journal</i>, originally titled <i>The Automotor Journal and Horseless Vehicle</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Spooner1959_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spooner1959-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> From around 1900 the journal had a separate section relating to aviation and aeronautical matters. The 5 April 1908 issue of <i>The Automotor Jornal</i> included a diagram of patent drawings of a plane made by the <a href="/wiki/Wright_Brothers" class="mw-redirect" title="Wright Brothers">Wright Brothers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wright1908_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wright1908-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Stanley kept in contact with them via his friend <a href="/wiki/Griffith_Brewer" title="Griffith Brewer">Griffith Brewer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Spooner1959_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spooner1959-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\nEventually, Spooner decided that a journal focused solely on matters relating to flying should be published\xe2\x80\x94and so, <i>Flight</i> magazine was established as an offshoot of <i>The Automotor Journal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bruce133_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bruce133-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wright1908_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wright1908-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Claiming to be the first aeronautical weekly in the world, <i>Flight</i> first appeared on 2 January 1909 as the official journal of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Aero_Club" title="Royal Aero Club">Aero Club of the United Kingdom</a> (later the Royal Aero Club).<sup id="cite_ref-bruce133_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bruce133-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In April 1934, <i>Flight</i> was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Edward_Iliffe,_1st_Baron_Iliffe" title="Edward Iliffe, 1st Baron Iliffe">Iliffe &amp; Sons</a>, who were proprietors and printers of technical magazines, one of which included <i><a href="/wiki/Autocar_(magazine)" title="Autocar (magazine)">Autocar</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On 4 January 1962 the magazine was renamed <i>Flight International</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-bruce133_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bruce133-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2019, <i>Flight International</i> and its associated divisions (except analytics and consulting divisions which retained by <a href="/wiki/RELX" title="RELX">RELX</a> as Cirium) were sold to <a href="/wiki/DVV_Media_Group" title="DVV Media Group">DVV Media Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In September 2020, <i>Flight International</i> switched from a weekly to monthly publication.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
