B. Pastine"</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Aeroporto Internazionale di Roma&#8211;Ciampino "G. B. Pastine"</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>CIA</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LIRA</b>), is the secondary <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci%E2%80%93Fiumicino_Airport" title="Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport">Rome-Fiumicino Airport "Leonardo da Vinci"</a>. It is a joint civilian, commercial and military airport situated 6.5&#160;<a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">NM</a> (12.0&#160;km; 7.5&#160;mi) south southeast<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of central Rome, just outside the Greater Ring Road (Italian: <i><a href="/wiki/Grande_Raccordo_Anulare" title="Grande Raccordo Anulare">Grande Raccordo Anulare</a></i> or <i>GRA</i>) the circular motorway around the city.\n</p><p>The airport is an important hub for many low-cost carriers and <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> traffic. It also hosts a military airport and the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/31%C2%BA_Stormo" class="mw-redirect" title="31\xc2\xba Stormo">31\xc2\xba Stormo</a> and the 2nd Reparto Genio of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Air_Force" title="Italian Air Force">Italian Air Force</a>. The airport is named after Giovan Battista Pastine, an Italian airship pilot who served in World War I.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Ciampino Airport was opened in 1916<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and is one of the oldest airports still in operation.\n</p><p>From here, on 10 April 1926, <a href="/wiki/Umberto_Nobile" title="Umberto Nobile">Umberto Nobile</a> took off on the airship Norge, the first aircraft to reach the North Pole and the first to fly across the polar ice cap from Europe to America. In October 1930, the first helicopter prototype designed by <a href="/wiki/Corradino_D%27Ascanio" title="Corradino D&#39;Ascanio">Corradino D\'Ascanio</a> was tested at Ciampino Airport, reaching a record altitude of 18 m, 8m45s duration and 1,078 m distance flown.\n</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the airport was captured by Allied forces in June 1944, and afterward became a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> military airfield. Although primarily used as a transport base by <a href="/wiki/C-47_Skytrain" class="mw-redirect" title="C-47 Skytrain">C-47 Skytrain</a> aircraft of the <a href="/wiki/64th_Troop_Carrier_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="64th Troop Carrier Group">64th Troop Carrier Group</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Air_Force" title="Twelfth Air Force">Twelfth Air Force</a> <a href="/wiki/86th_Bombardment_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="86th Bombardment Group">86th Bombardment Group</a> flew <a href="/wiki/A-36_Apache" class="mw-redirect" title="A-36 Apache">A-36 Apache</a> combat aircraft from the airport during the immediate period after its capture from German forces.\n</p><p>When the combat units moved out, <a href="/wiki/Air_Transport_Command_(United_States_Air_Force)" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Transport Command (United States Air Force)">Air Transport Command</a> used the airport as a major transshipment hub for cargo, transiting aircraft and personnel for the remainder of the war.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was Rome\'s main airport until 1960, with traffic amounting to over 2 million passengers per year. After the opening of <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci%E2%80%93Fiumicino_Airport" title="Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport">Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport</a>, Ciampino handled almost exclusively charter and executive flights for more than three decades. However, the terminal facilities were extended at the beginning of 2007 to accommodate the growing number of <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carrier</a> operations.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_terminal">Passenger terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Passenger terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport features a single, one-story passenger terminal building containing the departures and arrivals facilities. The departures area consists of a main hall with some stores and service facilities as well as 31 check-in counters and 16 departure gates using walk or bus boarding as there are no jet-bridges. The arrivals area has a separate entrance and features four baggage belts as well as some more service counters.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_usage">Other usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport hosts a fleet of <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_415" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombardier 415">Bombardier 415</a> <a href="/wiki/Aerial_firefighting" title="Aerial firefighting">aerial firefighting</a> aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It is also used by <a href="/wiki/Courier" title="Courier">express logistics companies</a> such as <a href="/wiki/DHL_Aviation" title="DHL Aviation">DHL</a>, by official flights of the Italian Government and by planes of dignitaries visiting the Italian capital. There is also an additional smaller general aviation terminal, although private flights have now mainly been transferred to <a href="/wiki/Rome_Urbe_Airport" title="Rome Urbe Airport">Rome Urbe Airport</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a Passenger traffic in 2008 was 4,788,931 with a decrease of 11.31% compared to 2007 due to economic crisis and <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> gradually moving routes to <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci%E2%80%93Fiumicino_Airport" title="Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport">Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport</a>. href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Taxi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Taxis licensed by the City of Rome have a fixed tariff for the single journey between Ciampino Airport and the area of the City of Rome within the Aurelian Walls (historical central Rome). The current tariff is 30 euros (plus luggage supplement of 1 euro for the second and subsequent pieces of luggage).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Train">Train</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Train">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>While there is no train service at the airport itself, there are a variety of bus connections between the airport and stations of the Italian mainline railway network or stations of the Rome city metro (subway, tube, underground) system. The different bus or bus + train options are relatively inexpensive, usually under 6 euros for a single journey.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ATAC_city_bus_services">ATAC city bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: ATAC city bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The ATAC Rome city bus line 720 connects the terminal building with the <a href="/wiki/Laurentina_(Rome_Metro)" title="Laurentina (Rome Metro)">Laurentina metro</a> (subway, tube, underground) station (departures every 15 minutes in either direction from 5.30 am to 23.20 pm, travel time is about 15\xe2\x80\x9320 minutes).\nThe ATAC Rome city bus line 520 connects the terminal building with the <a href="/wiki/Cinecitt%C3%A0_(Rome_Metro)" title="Cinecitt\xc3\xa0 (Rome Metro)">Cinecitt\xc3\xa0 metro</a> (subway, tube, underground) station (departures every 15 minutes in either direction,  from 5.30 am to 23.20 pm, travel time is about 15\xe2\x80\x9320 minutes). \nBoth these routes are included in the normal ticket arrangements current for city public transport in Rome, where one Metro train journey costs a single BIT ("biglietto integrato" = "integrated ticket") ticket, while a single journey on either of the named ATAC bus lines (720 or 520) between Ciampino airport and one of the named Rome Metro stations (Laurentina or Anagnina) also costs a single BIT ("integrated ticket") ticket. This means that it is possible to travel between any station in the Rome metro system and Ciampino airport for a total cost (at current rates) of 3 euros (2 BIT tickets per person). Obviously, residents can also use personalized subscription passes. The BIT tickets need to be purchased before boarding the bus and time-stamped by one of the ticket machines inside the bus. The ATAC bus stop for these services is located 2\xe2\x80\x933 minutes walk from the main airport buildings.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ATRAL_bus_services">ATRAL bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: ATRAL bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are ATRAL bus services (www.atral-lazio.com) connecting the airport to the <a href="/wiki/Anagnina_(Rome_Metro)" title="Anagnina (Rome Metro)">Anagnina metro</a> (subway, tube, underground) station or to the local <a href="/wiki/Ciampino_railway_station" title="Ciampino railway station">Ciampino mainline railway station</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Ciampino_railway_station" title="Ciampino railway station">Ciampino railway station</a> is served by trains travelling both to <a href="/wiki/Rome_Termini_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Rome Termini station">Rome Termini station</a> and to other mainline rail destinations, including <a href="/wiki/Frosinone" title="Frosinone">Frosinone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albano_Laziale" title="Albano Laziale">Albano Laziale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Potenza" title="Potenza">Potenza</a>). The bus to the  <a href="/wiki/Ciampino_railway_station" title="Ciampino railway station">Ciampino railway station</a> leaves the airport on weekdays between 5.46 am and 11.20 pm at intervals of between 5 and 55 minutes, with a journey time of 10 minutes. The bus to the <a href="/wiki/Anagnina_(Rome_Metro)" title="Anagnina (Rome Metro)">Anagnina metro</a> station leaves the airport on weekdays between 6.15 am and 10.40 pm at intervals of between 26 and 75  minutes, with a journey time of 20 minutes. Festive day timetables are partly reduced.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Direct_Bus_Services_to_Central_Rome">Direct Bus Services to Central Rome</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Direct Bus Services to Central Rome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span id="Bus_operators_Schiaffini.2C_Terravision_and_BusShuttle"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_operators_Schiaffini,_Terravision_and_BusShuttle">Bus operators  Schiaffini, Terravision and BusShuttle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Bus operators Schiaffini, Terravision and BusShuttle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The Rome Airport Bus service of the Schiaffini company has services to and from Ciampino airport, the town of Ciampino  and the central <a href="/wiki/Roma_Termini_railway_station" title="Roma Termini railway station">Roma Termini mainline railway station</a>. Tickets can be purchased at the company\'s sales point in the Ciampino airport building, on board the bus, or online (www.romeairportbus.com), or at the bus stop in Via Giolitti, Rome (alongside the Termini railway station). (departures every 30 minutes in either direction between 7.50 am and 10 pm with some extra departures from 4 am to 11.45 pm; travel time is about 40 minutes)\nA similar service is operated  by  <a href="/wiki/Terravision" title="Terravision">Terravision</a> (departures from Ciampino airport at intervals of approx. 40\xe2\x80\x9370 minutes between 8.15 am and 16.30 pm) Tickets can be purchased online (www.terravision.eu/rome_ciampino.html), also by   BusShuttle (departures from Ciampino airport at intervals of approx. 30\xe2\x80\x93120 minutes between 7.45 am and midnight) Tickets can be purchased online (www.sitbusshuttle.com).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport_between_Ciampino_and_Fiumicino_Airports">Transport between Ciampino and Fiumicino Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transport between Ciampino and Fiumicino Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are only two ways, other than by private car, of travelling between the two airports. One is to take a taxi, which has a fixed tariff (currently 50 euros). The other is to travel to Rome\'s Termini station and from there to travel out to the required airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foot">Foot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ciampino%E2%80%93G._B._Pastine_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Foot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Appian_Way" title="Appian Way">Appian Way</a> can be reached on foot in 10 minutes (750 m) from the terminal building. This ancient Roman road is a popular walking route. 11&#160;km north-west along this road one reaches the start of the road at the <a href="/wiki/Porta_San_Sebastiano" title="Porta San Sebastiano">Porta San Sebastiano</a>, 3&#160;km south-east along this road one reaches the train station of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Santa_Maria_delle_Mole&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Defects in the design of the <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Comet" title="De Havilland 1954, two weeks after Comets were allowed to resume flying following a temporary grounding resulting from the previous crash, <a href="/wiki/South_African_Airways_Flight_201" title="South African Airways Flight 201">South African Airways Flight 201</a>, another Comet, broke up shortly after takeoff and crashed not far from <a href="/wiki/Ponza" title="Ponza">Ponza</a>.</li></ul></li>\n<li>On Italiane">Alitalia</a> crashed short of the runway on a training flight exercise in landing with two engines inoperative. Both people on board were killed.<sup id="cite_ref-ASN211259_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASN211259-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 10 November 2008, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair_Flight_4102" title="Ryanair Flight 4102">Ryanair Flight 4102</a> from <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport" title="Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn Airport">Hahn</a> suffered damage during landing. The cause of the accident was stated to be <a href="/wiki/Birdstrike" class="mw-redirect" title="Birdstrike">birdstrikes</a> affecting both engines. There were 6 crew and 166 passengers on board.<sup id="cite_ref-ASN_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASN-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The airport was closed for over 24 hours as a result of the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-BT_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BT-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Two crew and eight passengers were taken to hospital with minor injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-Herald_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Herald-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> As well as damage to the engines and undercarriage, the rear fuselage was also damaged by contact with the runway.<sup id="cite_ref-Flight_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Flight-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The final report of the accident, investigated by <a than 10 years after the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a
