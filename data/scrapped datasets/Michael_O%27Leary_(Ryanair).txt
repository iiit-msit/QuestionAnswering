With a net worth of around <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">\xe2\x82\xac</a>848.6 million as of April 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> he is class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_O%27Leary_(businessman)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Michael Kevin O\'Leary<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> was born in Ireland on 20 March 1961, the son of Ger and Teddy O\'Leary. He grew up near <a href="/wiki/Mullingar" title="Mullingar">Mullingar, County Westmeath</a>. His father was the part owner of a textile factory.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Clongowes_Wood_College" title="Clongowes Wood College">Clongowes Wood College</a> near <a href="/wiki/Clane" title="Clane">Clane, County Kildare</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> before studying business and economics at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After graduating in 1983, he worked as a trainee with Stokes Kennedy Crowley (later known as <a href="/wiki/KPMG" title="KPMG">KPMG</a>) and studied the Irish tax system. He left after two years in 1985, setting up profitable newsagents in the <a href="/wiki/Terenure" title="Terenure">Terenure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walkinstown" title="Walkinstown">Walkinstown</a> areas of <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>. While at Stokes Kennedy Crowley, O\'Leary had met <a href="/wiki/Tony_Ryan" title="Tony Ryan">Tony Ryan</a>, head of <a href="/wiki/Guinness_Peat_Aviation" title="Guinness Peat Aviation">Guinness Peat Aviation</a> (GPA), a leasing company. Ryan was one of KPMG\'s clients and O\'Leary advised Ryan on his personal income tax affairs. In 1987, Ryan hired O\'Leary as his personal financial and tax advisor, where Ryan\'s main interest was in GPA.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ryanair_career">Ryanair career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_O%27Leary_(businessman)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ryanair career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Under O\'Leary\'s management, Ryanair further developed the <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost model</a> originated by <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Airlines" title="Southwest Airlines">Southwest Airlines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> O\'Leary described the ancillary revenue model in a 2001 interview, saying "The other airlines are asking how they can put up fares. We are asking how we could get rid of them."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The business model envisioned by O\'Leary uses receipts from on board shopping, internet gaming, car hire and hotel bookings to supplement the ticket revenue from selling airline seats. Savings are also made by negotiating discounts with airports for reduced landing fees. In many cases, regional airports have made no charges so as to secure flights that bring passengers and wealth into their area. The deregulation of Ireland\'s major airports and a transformation of traditional full-service airlines are among his demands.<sup id="cite_ref-rteeamon_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rteeamon-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_O%27Leary_(businessman)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg/220px-Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg/330px-Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg/440px-Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2832" data-file-height="2128" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael-olearys-taxi-oleary-cabs-mg99-mercedes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>O\'Leary\'s personal <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W220" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz W220">Mercedes-Benz S500</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/O%27Leary_Cabs" class="mw-redirect" title="O&#39;Leary Cabs">O\'Leary Cabs</a></div></div></div>\n<p>O\'Leary is known for loose talk in the airline industry and among its regulators. Many press articles have described him as arrogant and prone to making comments which he later contradicts.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He has been extravagantly outspoken in his public statements, sometimes resorting to personal attacks and foul language.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Clark_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clark-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guard_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> His abrasive management style, ruthless pursuit of cost-cutting and his explicitly hostile attitude towards corporate competitors, airport authorities, governments, unions and customers has become a hallmark.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He was reported to have been aggressive and hostile in dealings with a woman who was awarded free flights for life in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2002, O\'Leary said that his company is against any long-haul transatlantic services: "The low-cost model only really works for short-haul flights. [...] If we started flying farther afield, we\'d have to do something stupid like introducing what I call a \'rich class\' to make it pay."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, while at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Paris_Air_Show" title="Paris Air Show">Paris Air Show</a>, he said that he wanted to sell cheap flights from the U.S. to Europe for as low as 10 euros ($13) or $10, if conditions were right. He said that he needed a fleet of at least 30 twin-aisle aircraft and access to ports (e.g. major U.S. and European cities, in the airline industry there are so called slots or sometimes gates, often regulated by law, and without obtaining them it is impossible to have regular service to airports). Despite his claims in 2002, there were so called budget airliners in the past \xe2\x80\x93 for example <a href="/wiki/Laker_Airways" title="Laker Airways">Laker Airways</a> flights from London to New York in the late 1970s or long-hauls at budget-fares on other continents like <a href="/wiki/AirAsia_X" title="AirAsia X">AirAsiaX</a> in Malaysia and the Australian <a href="/wiki/Jetstar" title="Jetstar">Jetstar Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2004, O\'Leary purchased a taxi license plate for his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_S-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz S-Class">Mercedes-Benz S-Class</a> under the name <a href="/wiki/O%27Leary_Cabs" class="mw-redirect" title="O&#39;Leary Cabs">O\'Leary Cabs</a>, enabling it to be classified as a taxi so that he could legally make use of <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>\'s bus lanes to speed up his car journeys around the city.<sup id="cite_ref-Clark_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clark-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> A press report suggested that since he had stopped driving his own taxi, he has employed a driver with full <a href="/wiki/Commercial_driver%27s_license" title="Commercial driver&#39;s license">PSV licence</a>. In 2005, the Irish transport minister expressed concern at this abuse by O\'Leary and others.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007, O\'Leary was forced to retract a claim that Ryanair had cut emissions of carbon dioxide by half over the previous five years because the claim should have been that emissions "per passenger" had been cut by half.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He has been reported to have impersonated a journalist in an attempt to find information passed on to a newspaper following a safety incident on a Ryanair flight.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On occasion, he has apologised for personal attacks under threat of legal action.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He has been criticized by a judge for lying, who said he was lucky not to be found guilty of contempt of court.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He has also been criticised for <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_denial" title="Climate change denial">dismissing concerns</a> about <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> as "complete nonsense".<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Reacting to the decision to close European airspace in April 2010 over worries about the <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_ash" title="Volcanic ash">volcanic ash</a> plume from <a href="/wiki/2010_eruptions_of_Eyjafjallaj%C3%B6kull" title="2010 eruptions of Eyjafjallaj\xc3\xb6kull">an erupting Icelandic volcano</a>, O\'Leary falsely said, "There was no ash cloud. It was mythical. It\'s become evident the airspace closure was completely unnecessary." One study concluded that serious structural damage to aircraft could have occurred if passenger planes had continued to fly.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2014, O\'Leary was highly critical of a 24-hour strike by <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a> cabin crew. Aer Lingus, whose biggest shareholder at the time was Ryanair, had to cancel 200 flights and disrupt travel plans for 200,000 people. O\'Leary accused Aer Lingus of "mismanagement" of its employee relations, called for the sacking of a board member, and said the striking employees should be punished by having their discount travel incentives withdrawn for a year.<sup id="cite_ref-AerLingus_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AerLingus-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Participants in the <a href="/wiki/Bilderberg_meeting" title="Bilderberg meeting">Bilderberg meeting</a> have stated that O\'Leary was to invited attend the 2015 meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He was later seen attending its 2017 meeting.\n</p><p>In February 2020, O\'Leary suggested that airport security should focus on single Muslim men and called obese passengers "monsters".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2020, O\'Leary compared Lufthansa AG to "a crack cocaine junkie looking for state aid." <sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_O%27Leary_(businessman)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In September 2003, O\'Leary married Anita Farrell, with whom he has four children. They live in Gigginstown House near <a href="/wiki/Delvin" title="Delvin">Delvin, County Westmeath</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>O\'Leary breeds <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_Angus_cattle" class="mw-redirect" title="Aberdeen Angus cattle">Aberdeen Angus cattle</a> and horses at his <a href="/wiki/Gigginstown_House_Stud" class="mw-redirect" title="Gigginstown House Stud">Gigginstown House Stud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> His horses <a href="/wiki/War_of_Attrition_(horse)" title="War of Attrition (horse)">War of Attrition</a>, <a href="/wiki/Don_Cossack_(horse)" title="Don Cossack (horse)">Don Cossack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rule_The_World_(horse)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rule The World (horse)">Rule the World</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Roll" title="Tiger Roll">Tiger Roll</a> have won races such as the <a href="/wiki/Cheltenham_Gold_Cup" title="Cheltenham Gold Cup">Cheltenham Gold Cup</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/2016_Grand_National" title="2016 Grand National">2016 Grand National</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2018_Grand_National" title="2018 Grand National">2018 Grand National</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/2019_Grand_National" title="2019 Grand National">2019 Grand National</a>.\n</p><p>O\'Leary has supported English football team <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City F.C.</a> from an early age. He had the opportunity to buy a stake in the club in 2003, but believed the potential benefits did not outweigh the risk, and preferred to instead visit England to watch a few matches each season.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He wore a Manchester City shirt when unveiling Ryanair\'s new destinations to and from <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport" title="Manchester Airport">Manchester Airport</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2015, O\'Leary claimed that he was offered the starring role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Apprentice_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Apprentice (UK TV series)">The Apprentice</a></i> reality TV show, but turned it down for family reasons before it was eventually offered to and accepted by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Sugar" title="Alan Sugar">Alan Sugar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48"
