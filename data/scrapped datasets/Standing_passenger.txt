id="Occurrence">Occurrence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standing_passenger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Occurrence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On crowded <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> urban lines, while most travelers may be seated during off-peak services, only a limited proportion will be seated during the peak services. The longer the journey, the less willing passengers are to stand.  On <a href="/wiki/Intercity_rail" class="mw-redirect" title="Intercity rail">intercity rail</a> or <a href="/wiki/Intercity_bus_service" title="Intercity bus service">coach</a> services, the willingness among passengers to stand is often low, or it may even be prohibited, with <a href="/wiki/Reserved_seating" class="mw-redirect" title="Reserved seating">reserved seating</a> to ensure that all passengers can be seated.\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">aviation</a>, safety measures require all passengers and crew to be seated during take-off and landing, so <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airlines</a> do not allow passengers to travel without a seat. However, in 2010, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_airline" class="mw-redirect" title="Low-cost airline">low-cost airline</a> proposed a "<a href="/wiki/Vertical_seat" title="Vertical seat">vertical seat</a>" design for use by standing passengers on its aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph-2010-07-01_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-2010-07-01-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Seated_to_standing_ratio">Seated to standing ratio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standing_passenger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Seated to standing ratio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The seated-to-standing ratio is the <a href="/wiki/Ratio" title="Ratio">ratio</a> between the number of <a href="/wiki/Passengers" class="mw-redirect" title="Passengers">passengers</a> that can be <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seated</a> and the number of <a href="/wiki/Standing_(position)" class="mw-redirect" title="Standing (position)">standing</a> passengers on a <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a> <a href="/wiki/Vehicle" title="Vehicle">vehicle</a>. A higher standing ratio allows for more passengers in a given area, but detracts the perceived quality of the transport, in particular over long distances.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This metric is normally limited to urban mass transit, due to intercity transport normally only offering seated travel. On longer haul services, <a href="/wiki/Bilevel_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilevel car">bilevel cars</a> are often used to allow for increased seating, though this increases the <a href="/wiki/Terminal_dwell_time" class="mw-redirect" title="Terminal dwell time">dwell time</a> at stations, making increased seating ratio versus service time <a href="/wiki/Tradeoff" class="mw-redirect" title="Tradeoff">tradeoffs</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passengers_per_square_metre">Passengers per square metre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standing_passenger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Passengers per square metre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Passenger" title="Passenger">Passengers</a> per <a href="/wiki/Square_metre" title="Square metre">square metre</a> is a quality of service metric used to determine the standard of comfort provided to standing passengers in a transportation vehicle. Multiplying this number by the total available standing area on a vehicle gives the total standing passenger capacity. Bus services in Europe operate at about four passengers per square metre.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety_and_health">Safety and health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standing_passenger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Safety and health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Standing passengers are susceptible to suffering <a href="/wiki/Fall_(injury)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fall (injury)">falls</a> and other injuries, particularly elderly people.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Shorter people and children may not be able to reach ceiling-mounted handles, straps, or rails. Porous cloth straps are hard to clean, and are being replaced by rubber or plastic straps, and metal fixtures often made of <a href="/wiki/Stainless_steel" title="Stainless steel">stainless steel</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
