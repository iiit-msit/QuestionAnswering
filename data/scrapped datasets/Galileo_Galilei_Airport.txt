lang="it">Aeroporto Internazionale di Pisa</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>PSA</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LIRP</b>), also named <b>Galileo Galilei Airport</b><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is an airport located in <a href="/wiki/Pisa" title="Pisa">Pisa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It is the main airport in <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a> and the 10th in Italy in terms of passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is named after <a href="/wiki/Galileo_Galilei" title="Galileo Galilei">Galileo Galilei</a>, the famous scientist and native of Pisa. The airport was first developed for the military in the 1930s and 1940s.\nThe airport was used by 5,233,118 passengers in 2017. id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pisa_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport had its own <a href="/wiki/Pisa_Aeroporto_railway_station" title="Pisa Aeroporto railway station">railway station</a> with a service to and from Pisa Central railway station but this was closed on December 15, 2013 to allow construction work to begin on a new fully automatic connection known as the Pisa Mover to take passengers to Pisa Central. The Pisa Mover came into operation on March 18, 2017. The airport has 5 passenger and 1 coach parking areas.<sup id="cite_ref-MyUser_Pisa-airport.com_September_17_2015c_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MyUser_Pisa-airport.com_September_17_2015c-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Besides civilian operations, the airport is also used extensively by the <a href="/wiki/Aeronautica_Militare" class="mw-redirect" title="Aeronautica Militare">Aeronautica Militare</a> (Italian Air Force) and is a base for, amongst others, the <a href="/wiki/C-130_Hercules" class="mw-redirect" title="C-130 Hercules">C-130 Hercules</a> and <a href="/wiki/C-27J_Spartan" class="mw-redirect" title="C-27J Spartan">C-27J Spartan</a> transport aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-anna.aero_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anna.aero-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The airport is home to 46\xc2\xaa Brigata Aerea Silvio Angelucci (46th Air Brigade). During the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the airport was used as a base for the <a href="/wiki/15th_Expeditionary_Mobility_Task_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="15th Expeditionary Mobility Task Force">15th Air Force</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pisa_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg/220px-Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="100" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg/330px-Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg/440px-Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="1094" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aerial_Aeroporto_Pisa_San_Giusto.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial view</div></div></div>\n<p>The airport resides at an elevation of 6 feet (2&#160;m) above <a href="/wiki/Mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Mean sea level">mean sea level</a>.
