Dutch <a href="/wiki/Public_broadcasting" title="Public broadcasting">public broadcasting</a> organization founded on 23 April 1925. Broadly <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Catholic</a> in its spiritual outlook, KRO broadcast the bulk of its television output on the <a href="/wiki/NPO_1" title="NPO 1">NPO 1</a> channel. KRO was also responsible for managing broadcasts made by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism in the Netherlands">Catholic Church in the Netherlands</a> in the airtime allocated to <a href="/wiki/Rooms-Katholiek_Kerkgenootschap" title="Rooms-Katholiek Kerkgenootschap">RKK</a>. KRO published the magazines <i>Studio KRO Magazine</i> and <i>Mikro gids</i>.\n</p><p>On 1 January 2014 it merged with <a href="/wiki/Nederlandse_Christelijke_Radio_Vereniging" title="Nederlandse Christelijke Radio Vereniging">NCRV</a> to form <a href="/wiki/KRO-NCRV" title="KRO-NCRV">KRO-NCRV</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katholieke_Radio_Omroep&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Orkest Zonder Naam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>Orkest Zonder Naam</i> (English: "Nameless Orchestra") was the KRO\'s official orchestra during the <a href="/wiki/Pillarization" class="mw-redirect" title="Pillarization">pillarization</a> of <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> society. Their 1952 song <i>Naar de speeltuin</i> is a Dutch evergreen, which sold 25,000 copies.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programmes">Programmes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katholieke_Radio_Omroep&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Programmes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katholieke_Radio_Omroep&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On television, KRO\'s "behind-the-news" show is <a href="/wiki/Netwerk" title="Netwerk">Netwerk</a>, which it produces in cooperation with <a href="/wiki/Evangelische_Omroep" title="Evangelische Omroep">EO</a> and <a href="/wiki/NCRV" class="mw-redirect" title="NCRV">NCRV</a>.\n</p><p>A popular show which KRO began in 2005 is <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boer_zoekt_vrouw&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boer zoekt vrouw (page does not exist)">Boer zoekt vrouw</a></i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Farmer_wants_a_wife" class="mw-redirect" title="Farmer wants a wife">Farmer wants a wife</a></i>), presented by <a href="/wiki/Yvon_Jaspers" title="Yvon Jaspers">Yvon Jaspers</a>. Many Dutch farmers remain single because they find it hard to find a woman who is willing to put up with the long hours, hard work, and lack of holidays which farming life requires. This show, which aims to remedy that situation, was KRO\'s highest-rated television programme in 2008, achieving an average weekly viewership of 4.5 million.\n</p><p>Another KRO speciality is the broadcasting of detective series in the strand <i>KRO Detectives</i>, which mainly focuses on British and Scandinavian productions.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_output">Youth output</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Katholieke_Radio_Omroep&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Youth output">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:120px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Krologo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/8/8e/Krologo.jpg" decoding="async" width="118" height="90" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="118" data-file-height="90" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Krologo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The logo for KRO\'s children\'s programming on NPO 1</div></div></div>\n<p>KRO also has its own children\'s strands, <i>KRO Kindertijd</i> ("KRO children\'s time") and <i>Tien Plus</i> ("Ten plus"), which it transmits during its scheduled slots on Nederland 1. KRO Kindertijd also has some programmes available live across the Netherlands via Omroep NL\'s digital service, <a href="/wiki/Nederland_24#Zappelin_/_Zapp_24" title="Nederland 24">Zappelin / Zapp 24</a>. Some of this output can also be viewed outside the Netherlands via Kindertijd\'s "Video Juke Box" service.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a
