dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Ryanair Pilot Group (RPG)</b> is an unrecognised <a href="/wiki/Labor_union" class="mw-redirect" title="Labor union">labor union</a> formed in 2012 by pilots working for the low cost Irish airline, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>. The three founders were Evert Van Zwol, John Goss and Ted Murphy.<sup id="cite_ref-sue_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As of 2013, more than 50% of pilots in Ryanair were registered and represented by the RPG in all categories.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By 2020, RPG had de-centralised into nation-based pilot associations.<sup id="cite_ref-rpgr_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rpgr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ryanair_Recognition">Ryanair Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryanair_Pilot_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ryanair Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Supreme Court">Irish Supreme Court</a> case of <i>Ryanair Limited v Labour Court &amp; Impact</i> in 2007, Mr Justice Geoghegan ruled that Ryanair was entitled not to deal with trade unions.<sup id="cite_ref-Dohrety2007_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dohrety2007-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of October&#160;2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ryanair_Pilot_Group&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, Ryanair had not officially recognised the Ryanair Pilot Group. In an August 2013 interview, Ryanair CEO Michael O\'Leary dismissed the group as a "joke" and claimed that the pilots group was an attempt by pilot unions from other airlines to unionise Ryanair.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Following a comment from the Ryanair CEO <a href="/wiki/Michael_O%27Leary_(businessman)" title="Michael O&#39;Leary (businessman)">Michael O\'Leary</a> in August 2013 in which he agreed to meet with the RPG if more than 1601 pilots were registered with them, an invitation to talk was sent by the RPG.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomberg1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomberg1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  In response, Ryanair released a short statement, stating that "We don\xe2\x80\x99t comment on the activity or false claims of KLM or Aer Lingus pilots,\xe2\x80\x9d a reference to the five-member interim council of the Ryanair Pilot Group consisting of non-Ryanair pilots.<sup id="cite_ref-Keena_7-0" class="reference"><a
