Search">Yahoo</a>. This is a specific form of <a href="/wiki/Screen_scraping" class="mw-redirect" title="Screen scraping">screen scraping</a> or <a href="/wiki/Web_scraping" title="Web scraping">web scraping</a> dedicated to search engines only.\n</p><p>Most commonly larger <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_optimization" title="Search engine optimization">search engine optimization</a> (SEO) providers depend on regularly scraping keywords from search engines, especially Google, to monitor the competitive position of their customers\' websites for relevant keywords or their <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_indexing" title="Search engine indexing">indexing</a> status.\n</p><p>Search engines like Google do not allow any sort of automated access to their service,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but from a legal point of view, there is no known case or broken law.\n</p><p>The process of entering a website and extracting data in an automated fashion is also often called "<a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">crawling</a>". Search engines like Google, Bing or Yahoo get almost all their data from automated crawling bots.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input class="toclevel-1 tocsection-9"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">9</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Difficulties">Difficulties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_engine_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Difficulties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Google is the by far largest search engine with most users in numbers as well as most revenue in creative advertisements, which makes Google the most important search engine to scrape for SEO related companies.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Google does not take legal action against scraping, likely for self-protective reasons. However, Google uses a range of defensive methods that makes scraping their results a challenging task.\n</p>\n<ul><li>Google is testing the <a href="/wiki/User_agent" title="User agent">User-Agent</a> (Browser type) of <a href="/wiki/Hypertext_Transfer_Protocol" title="Hypertext Transfer Protocol">HTTP</a> requests and serves a different page depending on the User-Agent. Google is automatically rejecting User-Agents that seem to originate from a possible automated bot. [Part of the Google error page: <i>Please see Google\'s Terms of Service posted at http://www.google.com/terms_of_service.html</i> ]  A typical example would be using the command line browser <a href="/wiki/CURL" title="CURL">cURL</a>, Google will directly reject to serve any pages to it while Bing is a bit more forgiving, Bing does not seem to care about User-Agents.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Google is using a complex system of request rate limitation which is different for each Language, Country, User-Agent as well as depending on the keyword and keyword search parameters. The rate limitation can make it unpredictable when accessing a search engine automated as the behaviour patterns are not known to the outside developer or user.</li>\n<li>Network and <a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">IP</a> limitations are as well part of the scraping defense systems. Search engines can not easily be tricked by changing to another IP, while using proxies is a very important part in successful scraping. The diversity and abusive history of an IP is important as well.</li>\n<li>Offending IPs and offending IP networks can easily be stored in a blacklist database to detect offenders much faster. The fact that most ISPs give <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_IP_addresses" class="mw-redirect" title="Dynamic IP addresses">dynamic IP addresses</a> to customers requires that such automated bans be only temporary, to not block innocent users.</li>\n<li>Behaviour based detection is the most difficult defense system. Search engines serve their pages to millions of users every day, this provides a large amount of behaviour information. A scraping script or bot is not behaving like a real user, aside from having non-typical access times, delays and session times the keywords being harvested might be related to each other or include unusual parameters. Google for example has a very sophisticated behaviour analyzation system, possibly using <a href="/wiki/Deep_learning" title="Deep learning">deep learning</a> software to detect unusual patterns of access. It can detect unusual activity much faster than other search engines.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">HTML</a> markup changes, depending on the methods used to harvest the content of a website even a small change in HTML data can render a scraping tool broken until it was updated.</li>\n<li>General changes in detection systems. In the past years search engines have tightened their detection systems nearly month by month making it more and more difficult to reliable scrape as the developers need to experiment and adapt their code regularly.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Detection">Detection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_engine_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Detection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When search engine defense thinks an access might be automated the search engine can react differently.\n</p><p>The first layer of defense is a captcha page<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> where the user is prompted to verify he is a real person and not a bot or tool. Solving the <a href="/wiki/CAPTCHA" title="CAPTCHA">captcha</a> will create a <a href="/wiki/HTTP_cookie" title="HTTP cookie">cookie</a> that permits access to the search engine again for a while. After about one day the captcha page is removed again.\n</p><p>The second layer of defense is a similar error page but without captcha, in such a case the user is completely blocked from using the search engine until the temporary block is lifted or the user changes his IP.\n</p><p>The third layer of defense is a longterm block of the entire network segment. Google has blocked large network blocks for months. This sort of block is likely triggered by an administrator and only happens if a scraping tool is sending a very high number of requests.\n</p><p>All these forms of detection may also happen to a normal user, especially users sharing the same IP address or network class (IPV4 ranges as well as IPv6 ranges).\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Methods_of_scraping_Google.2C_Bing_or_Yahoo"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Methods_of_scraping_Google,_Bing_or_Yahoo">Methods of scraping Google, Bing or Yahoo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_engine_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Methods of scraping Google, Bing or Yahoo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>To scrape a search engine successfully the two major factors are time and amount.\n</p><p>The more keywords a user needs to scrape and the smaller the time for the job the more difficult scraping will be and the more developed a scraping script or tool needs to be.\n</p><p>Scraping scripts need to overcome a few technical challenges:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>IP rotation using Proxies (proxies should be unshared and not listed in blacklists)</li>\n<li>Proper time management, time between keyword changes, pagination as well as correctly placed delays   Effective longterm scraping rates can vary from only 3\xe2\x80\x935 requests (keywords or pages) per hour up to 100 and more per hour for each IP address / Proxy in use. The quality of IPs, methods of scraping, keywords requested and language/country requested can greatly affect the possible maximum rate.</li>\n<li>Correct handling of URL parameters, cookies as well as HTTP headers to emulate a user with a typical browser<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>HTML <a href="/wiki/Document_Object_Model" title="Document Object Model">DOM</a> parsing (extracting URLs, descriptions, ranking position, sitelinks and other relevant data from the HTML code)</li>\n<li>Error handling, automated reaction on captcha or block pages and other unusual responses<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Captcha definition explained as mentioned above  by<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>An example of an open source scraping software which makes use of the above mentioned techniques is GoogleScraper.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This framework controls browsers over the DevTools Protocol and makes it hard for Google to detect that the browser is automated.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming_languages">Programming languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_engine_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Programming languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When developing a scraper for a search engine almost any programming language can be used. Although, depending on performance requirements, some languages will be favorable.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/PHP" title="PHP">PHP</a> is a commonly used language to write scraping scripts for websites or backend services, since it has powerful capabilities built-in (DOM parsers, libcURL); however, its memory usage is typically 10 times the factor of a similar C/<a href="/wiki/C%2B%2B" title="C++">C++</a> code. <a href="/wiki/Ruby_on_Rails" title="Ruby on Rails">Ruby on Rails</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">Python</a> are also frequently used to automated scraping jobs. For highest performance, C++ DOM parsers should be considered.\n</p><p>Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Bash_scripting_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Bash scripting language">bash scripting</a> can be used together with cURL as a command line tool to scrape a search engine.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tools_and_scripts">Tools and scripts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_engine_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tools and scripts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When developing a search engine scraper there are several existing tools and libraries available that can either be used, extended or just analyzed to learn from.\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/IMacros" title="IMacros">iMacros</a> - A free browser automation toolkit that can be used for very small volume scraping from within a users browser <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/CURL" title="CURL">cURL</a> \xe2\x80\x93 a commandline browser for automation and testing as well as a powerful open source HTTP interaction library available for a large range of programming languages.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>google-search - A Go package to scrape Google. <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=GoogleScraper&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="GoogleScraper (page does not exist)">GoogleScraper</a> \xe2\x80\x93 A Python module to scrape different search engines (like Google, Yandex, Bing, Duckduckgo, Baidu and others) by using proxies (socks4/5, http proxy). The tool includes asynchronous networking support and is able to control real browsers to mitigate detection.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>se-scraper - Successor of GoogleScraper. Scrape search engines concurrently with different proxies. <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://serpapi.com">SerpApi</a> \xe2\x80\x93 A real-time API to access extracted search engine results like Google, Bing, Baidu, Yandex, Yahoo. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal">Legal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Search_engine_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When scraping websites and services the legal part is often a big concern for companies, for web scraping it greatly depends on the country a scraping user/company is from as well as which data or website is being scraped. With many different court rulings all over the world.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\nHowever, when it comes to scraping search engines the situation is different, search engines usually do not list intellectual property as they just repeat or summarize information they scraped from other websites.\n</p><p>The largest public known incident of a search engine being scraped happened in 2011 when Microsoft was caught scraping unknown keywords from Google for their own, rather new Bing service. (<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>)  But even this incident did not result in a court case.\n</p><p>One possible reason might be that search engines like Google are getting almost all their data by scraping millions of public reachable websites, also without reading and accepting those terms. A legal case won by Google against Microsoft would possibly put their whole business as risk.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
