id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\n\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t<div search</a>\n\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Web data integration</b> (WDI) is the process of aggregating and managing data from different websites into a single, homogeneous workflow.  This process includes data access, transformation, mapping, quality assurance and fusion of data. Data that is sourced and structured from websites is referred to as "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_data&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Web data (page does not exist)">web data</a>". WDI is an extension and specialization of <a href="/wiki/Data_integration" title="Data integration">data integration</a> that views the web as a collection of heterogeneous databases.\n</p><p>Data integration techniques in the context of the web, forms the foundation for businesses taking advantage of data available on the ever-increasing number of publicly-accessible websites.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Corporate spending on this area amounted to about USD 2.5bn in 2017, and it is expected that by 2020 the market will reach almost USD 7bn.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_data_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Web data integration extends and specializes data integration to see the web as a collection of views of databases accessible over the web protocols, including, but not limited to:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>Open data catalogs</li>\n<li>Government data catalogs</li>\n<li>Web applications and sites\n<ul><li>UI (<a href="/wiki/Web_scraping" title="Web scraping">web scraping</a>)</li>\n<li>API</li></ul></li>\n<li>The semantic web (SPARQL)</li>\n<li>HTML embedded structured data</li>\n<li>HTML data tables</li>\n<li>Spreadsheets</li>\n<li>PDFs</li>\n<li>Online encyclopedias</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_access_and_transformation">Data access and transformation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_data_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Data access and transformation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>WDI has technical challenges different to data integration due to the data access and transformation required for the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_data&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Web data (page does not exist)">web data</a> sources being often <a href="/wiki/Unstructured_data" title="Unstructured data">unstructured</a> or <a href="/wiki/Semi-structured_data" title="Semi-structured data">semi-structured</a> data without a standard query mechanism.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_quality">Data quality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_data_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Data quality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Understanding the quality and veracity of data is even more important in WDI than in data integration, as the data is generally less implicitly trusted and of lower quality than that which is collected from a trusted source. There are attempts to try to automate a trust rating for web data.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Data quality in data integration can generally happen after data access and transformation, but in WDI quality may need to be monitored as data is collected, due to both the time and the cost of re-collecting the data.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications">Applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Web_data_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>WDI has application in many fields, including bioinformatics,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> search engines,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> price comparison,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and forensic search<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> data analysis, business intelligence, ecommerce,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> healthcare, pharmaceutical<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and product development.\n</p><p>Most price comparison engines and recommendation systems use user generated data to create recommendations for their users. Similarly, healthcare systems use results of competitions conducted on websites like Kaggle<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> to see the accuracy of data and to create user-focused products. In fact, IBM estimates that poor quality WDI is costing companies over $3 trillion<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> in revenue each year.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
