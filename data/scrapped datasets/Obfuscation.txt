from an intended audience</div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For the related use of the term in computer science, see <a href="/wiki/Obfuscation_(software)" title="Obfuscation (software)">obfuscation (software)</a>.</div><p><b>Obfuscation</b> is the <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/obscure#Verb" class="extiw" title="wikt:obscure">obscuring</a> of the intended <a href="/wiki/Meaning_(linguistics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Meaning (linguistics)">meaning</a> of <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a> by making the message difficult to understand, usually with <a href="/wiki/Mental_confusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Mental confusion">confusing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ambiguity" title="Ambiguity">ambiguous</a> language. The obfuscation might be either unintentional or <a href="/wiki/Intention" title="Intention">intentional</a> (although intent usually is <a href="/wiki/Connotation" title="Connotation">connoted</a>), and is accomplished with <a href="/wiki/Circumlocution" title="Circumlocution">circumlocution</a> (talking around the subject), the use of <a href="/wiki/Jargon" title="Jargon">jargon</a> (technical language of a profession), and the use of an <a href="/wiki/Argot" class="mw-redirect" title="Argot">argot</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ingroups_and_outgroups" class="mw-redirect" title="Ingroups and outgroups">ingroup</a> language) of limited communicative value to outsiders.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Expository_writing" class="mw-redirect" title="Expository writing">expository writing</a>, unintentional obfuscation usually occurs in <a href="/wiki/Draft_document" title="Draft document">draft documents</a>, at the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Composition_(language)" title="Composition (language)">composition</a>; such obfuscation is illuminated with <a href="/wiki/Critical_thinking" title="Critical thinking">critical thinking</a> and editorial revision, either by the writer or by an editor. Etymologically, the word <i>obfuscation</i> derives from the Latin <i lang="la" title="Latin-language text">obfuscatio</i>, from <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/obfuscate#Etymology" class="extiw" title="wikt:obfuscate"><i>obfusc\xc4\x81re</i> (to darken)</a>; synonyms include the words <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/beclouding" class="extiw" title="wikt:beclouding">beclouding</a> and <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/abstrusity#Noun" class="extiw" title="wikt:abstrusity">abstrusity</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Obfuscation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Doctors are faulted for using <a href="/wiki/Jargon" title="Jargon">jargon</a> to conceal unpleasant facts from a patient; the American author and physician <a href="/wiki/Michael_Crichton" title="Michael Crichton">Michael Crichton</a> said that <a href="/wiki/Medical_writing" title="Medical writing">medical writing</a> is a "highly skilled, calculated attempt to confuse the reader". The psychologist <a href="/wiki/B._F._Skinner" title="B. F. Skinner">B. F. Skinner</a> said that medical notation is a form of multiple audience control, which allows the doctor to communicate to the pharmacist things which the patient might oppose if they could understand medical jargon.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eschew">Eschew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Obfuscation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Eschew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>"Eschew obfuscation", also stated as "eschew obfuscation, espouse elucidation", is a humorous <a href="/wiki/Fumblerules" title="Fumblerules">fumblerule</a> used by English teachers and professors when lecturing about proper writing techniques. Literally, the phrase means "avoid being unclear" or "avoid being unclear, support being clear", but the use of relatively uncommon words causes confusion in much of the audience (those lacking the <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">vocabulary</a>), making the statement an example of <a href="/wiki/Irony" title="Irony">irony</a>, and more precisely a <a href="/wiki/Heterological" class="mw-redirect" title="Heterological">heterological</a> phrase. The phrase has appeared in print at least as early as 1959, when it was used as a section heading in a <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> document.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>An earlier similar phrase appears in <a href="/wiki/Mark_Twain" title="Mark Twain">Mark Twain</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Fenimore_Cooper%27s_Literary_Offenses" title="Fenimore Cooper&#39;s Literary Offenses">Fenimore Cooper\'s Literary Offenses</a></i>, where he lists rule fourteen of good writing as "eschew <a href="/wiki/Surplusage" title="Surplusage">surplusage</a>".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secure_communication">Secure communication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Obfuscation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Secure communication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Secure_communication" title="Secure communication">Secure communication</a></div>\n<p>Obfuscation of oral or written communication achieves a degree of <a href="/wiki/Secure_communication" title="Secure communication">secure communication</a> without a need to rely upon technology. This technique is sometimes referred to as "talking around" and is a form of <a href="/wiki/Security_through_obscurity" title="Security through obscurity">security through obscurity</a>.\n</p><p>A notable example of obfuscation of written communication is a message sent by <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> ringleader <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Atta" title="Mohamed Atta">Mohamed Atta</a> to other conspirators prior to the attacks occurring:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The semester begins in three more weeks. We\'ve obtained 19 confirmations for studies in the faculty of law, the faculty of urban planning, the faculty of fine arts and the faculty of engineering.</p><div class="templatequotecite">\xe2\x80\x94&#8201;<cite><a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Atta" title="Mohamed Atta">Mohamed Atta</a></cite></div></blockquote> \n<p>In this obfuscated message, the following <a href="/wiki/Code_word_(figure_of_speech)" title="Code word (figure of speech)">code words</a> are believed to exist:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>"semester" refers to planned <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a></li>\n<li>"three more weeks" refers to the date when the attacks are scheduled</li>\n<li>"19 confirmations" refers to the <a href="/wiki/Hijackers_in_the_September_11_attacks" title="Hijackers in the September 11 attacks">Hijackers in the September 11 attacks</a></li>\n<li>"faculty of law" refers to a target, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Capitol" title="United States Capitol">United States Capitol</a></li>\n<li>"faculty of urban planning" refers to a target, the <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Center_(1973%E2%80%932001)" title="World Trade Center (1973\xe2\x80\x932001)">World Trade Center</a></li>\n<li>"faculty of fine arts" refers to a target, the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a></li>\n<li>"faculty of engineering" refers to a target, <a href="/wiki/The_Pentagon" title="The Pentagon">The Pentagon</a></li></ul>\n<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">illegal drug trade</a>, obfuscation is commonly used in communication to hide the occurrence of drug trafficking. A notable example is the use of "<a href="/wiki/420_(cannabis_culture)" title="420 (cannabis culture)">420</a>" as a code word to refer to <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_consumption" title="Cannabis consumption">cannabis consumption</a>, an activity which despite <a href="/wiki/Legality_of_cannabis" title="Legality of cannabis">legalisation</a> changes, was once illegal in most jurisdictions. The <a href="/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration" title="Drug Enforcement Administration">Drug Enforcement Administration</a> reported in July 2018 a total of 353 different code words used for <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="White_box_cryptography">White box cryptography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Obfuscation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: White box cryptography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In white-box cryptography, obfuscation refers to the protection of cryptographic <a href="/wiki/Key_(cryptography)" title="Key (cryptography)">keys</a> from extraction when they are under the control of the adversary, e.g., as part of a <a href="/wiki/Digital_rights_management" title="Digital rights management">DRM</a> scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Network_security">Network security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Obfuscation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Network security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Network_security" title="Network security">network security</a>, obfuscation refers to methods used to obscure an attack payload from inspection by network protection systems.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
