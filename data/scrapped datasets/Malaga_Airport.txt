href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LEMG</b>), officially <b>M\xc3\xa1laga\xe2\x80\x93Costa del Sol Airport</b><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Aeropuerto de M\xc3\xa1laga-Costa del Sol</i>) since June 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BOE2011_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOE2011-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is the fourth busiest <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> in Spain<sup id="cite_ref-aenapax_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aenapax-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> after <a href="/wiki/Adolfo_Su%C3%A1rez_Madrid%E2%80%93Barajas_Airport" title="Adolfo Su\xc3\xa1rez Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barajas Airport">Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barajas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona Airport">Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca_Airport" title="Palma de Mallorca Airport">Palma de Mallorca</a>.  It is an important airport for Spanish tourism as it is the main <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> serving the <a href="/wiki/Costa_del_Sol" title="Costa del Sol">Costa del Sol</a>.  It is 8&#160;km (5.0&#160;mi) southwest<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP_2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="M\xc3\xa1laga">M\xc3\xa1laga</a> and 5&#160;km (3.1&#160;mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Torremolinos" title="Torremolinos">Torremolinos</a>.  The airport has flight connections to over 60 countries worldwide, and over 14.4 million passengers passed through it in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-aenapax_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aenapax-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, 18.6 million passengers passed through M\xc3\xa1laga Airport.\n</p><p>The airport operates with three terminals.  The third terminal adjacent to the previous two opened on 15 March 2010, and flight operations started on 16 March 2010.  A second runway opened at the airport on 26 June 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>M\xc3\xa1laga Airport is the busiest international airport of <a href="/wiki/Andaluc%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="Andaluc\xc3\xada">Andaluc\xc3\xada</a>, accounting for 85 per cent of the region\'s non-domestic traffic. It offers a wide variety of international destinations. The airport, connected to the <a href="/wiki/Costa_del_Sol" title="Costa del Sol">Costa del Sol</a>, has a daily link with twenty cities in Spain and over one hundred cities in Europe. Direct flights also operate to Africa, the Middle East and also to North America in the summer season. Airlines with a base at the airport are <a href="/wiki/Air_Europa" title="Air Europa">Air Europa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_(airline)" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian (airline)">Norwegian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Airlines" title="Scandinavian Airlines">Scandinavian Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vueling" title="Vueling">Vueling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Easyjet" class="mw-redirect" title="Easyjet">Easyjet</a> which operates a seasonal base.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input of the airport</div></div></div>\n<p>M\xc3\xa1laga Airport is one of the oldest Spanish airports that has stayed in its original location.\n</p><p>M\xc3\xa1laga Airport opened on 9 March 1919.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  After test flights, the first scheduled air service from M\xc3\xa1laga began on 1 September 1919 when <a href="/wiki/Didier_Daurat" title="Didier Daurat">Didier Daurat</a> began regular flights between <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicante" title="Alicante">Alicante</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tangier" title="Tangier">Tangier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Casablanca" title="Casablanca">Casablanca</a>.\n</p><p>The single runway was extended in the 1960s, and a new terminal was erected in the centre of the site. During this period of development, new navigational equipment was installed, including radar system at the end of the decade, in 1970.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The airport was given its current title in 1965. In 1968 a new passenger terminal was opened. In 1972 a second passenger terminal was opened to cater specifically for non-scheduled traffic. An increase in companies offering <a href="/wiki/Package_holiday" class="mw-redirect" title="Package holiday">package holidays</a> (around 30 by 1965) meant that this type of traffic was providing an increasing proportion of the airport\'s business. The terminal was very similar to the ones that were built in <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca_Airport" title="Palma de Mallorca Airport">Palma de Mallorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicante_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Alicante Airport">Alicante</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ibiza_Airport" title="Ibiza Airport">Ibiza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Girona_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Girona Airport">Girona</a>.\n</p><p>On 30 November 1991, a new passenger terminal opened at the airport which is today\'s Terminal 2. It was designed by <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Bofill_Taller_de_Arquitectura" title="Ricardo Bofill Taller de Arquitectura">Ricardo Bofill Taller de Arquitectura</a>. Almost all services moved their operations to this terminal when it opened with the exception of checking in.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg/220px-Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg/330px-Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg/440px-Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Terminal_3_M%C3%A1laga_Airport_06.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Terminal 3 M\xc3\xa1laga Airport</div></div></div>\n<p>In 1995, the old passenger building was converted into a general aviation terminal, and a new hangar for large aircraft maintenance was built to the north of the airport site. Also constructed was a terminal specifically catering for cargo traffic a year later, along with a hangar for maintenance of big aircraft. In 1997 an enlargement of the parking of gates was built and fuel systems were added at all the gates.\n</p><p>The airport\'s domestic departures section once had the head office of <a href="/wiki/Binter_Mediterraneo" title="Binter Mediterraneo">Binter Mediterraneo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In November 2002 a new control tower was built with a height of 54m,.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2004 the "M\xc3\xa1laga Plan" was started, including ideas for construction of a new terminal, and a new runway.\n</p><p>In 2005 the old passenger terminal from the late 60s was demolished to make room for the planned expansion of the airport.\n</p><p>In November 2005 <a href="/wiki/Monarch_Airlines" title="Monarch Airlines">Monarch Airlines</a> opened a base at M\xc3\xa1laga.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It based an <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320-200" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320-200">Airbus A320-200</a> there and operated scheduled services were added to <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_Airport" title="Aberdeen Airport">Aberdeen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackpool International Airport">Blackpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newquay_Cornwall_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Newquay Cornwall International Airport">Newquay</a>. However, due to their routes being unpopular, the base was closed in 2007. In March 2007, <a href="/wiki/Clickair" title="Clickair">Clickair</a> opened a base at M\xc3\xa1laga after announcing a new route to Barcelona. The base has remained since the airline merged with <a href="/wiki/Vueling" title="Vueling">Vueling</a>.\n</p><p>On 26 February 2009, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81ndalus_L%C3%ADneas_A%C3%A9reas" title="\xc3\x81ndalus L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas">\xc3\x81ndalus L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas</a> started operations from M\xc3\xa1laga, but then ceased operations in August 2010. This was the only airline that had their main base at M\xc3\xa1laga, until <a href="/wiki/Helitt_L%C3%ADneas_A%C3%A9reas" title="Helitt L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas">Helitt L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas</a> opened their base in late 2011. On 16 December 2009, low-cost carrier <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced a base at this airport. This would be their 38th base with an additional 19 routes, bringing Ryanair\'s total routes from M\xc3\xa1laga to 39. The base opened on 23 June 2010. An extra route to <a href="/wiki/Barcelona_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona Airport">Barcelona</a> was announced after the planned opening of their Barcelona base.\n</p><p>On 15 March 2010, the new Terminal 3 was completed. It was opened by <a href="/wiki/King_Juan_Carlos" class="mw-redirect" title="King Juan Carlos">King Juan Carlos</a> of Spain, opening to public use the following day. On 10 September 2010, the suburban railway station at M\xc3\xa1laga Airport was opened, providing access to catch a train to M\xc3\xa1laga from Terminal 3.\n</p><p>In November 2011, <a href="/wiki/Helitt_L%C3%ADneas_A%C3%A9reas" title="Helitt L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas">Helitt L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas</a> opened their base at M\xc3\xa1laga, operating flights to <a href="/wiki/Melilla_Airport" title="Melilla Airport">Melilla Airport</a> as well as opening additional routes. The company ceased operations in November 2014.\n</p><p>On 17 May 2012, the first commercial landings on the second runway took place for the first time. The first aircraft to use it was a PA-28 private 4 seated single engine light aircraft and the first commercial flight was Transavia Flight HV6115 from Amsterdam, operated by a Boeing 737-800. A total of 44 aircraft landed on the new runway. The runway was placed into service after the airport obtained the safety clearance of Civil Aviation on 30 April 2012. The runway officially opened on 26 June 2012 and it was inaugurated by the Spanish Minister of Transport, Ana Pastor.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It is located on the other side of the terminals where the current runway is. It is in the direction of 12/30 and it has three rapid exits.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2017, <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Airlines_Ireland" title="Scandinavian Airlines Ireland">Scandinavian Airlines Ireland</a> announced they were to open a base at M\xc3\xa1laga. The base opened in June 2018 and operated until April 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On 14 March 2018, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Primera_Air" title="Primera Air">Primera Air</a> were also to open a base at the airport with one based aircraft and six new routes. The base was to open on 27 October 2018, however this did not happen as the company ceased operations on 1 October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 8 October 2020, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> would open a seasonal base at M\xc3\xa1laga. The based flights will be operated by subsidiary <a href="/wiki/EasyJet_Europe" title="EasyJet Europe">EasyJet Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminals">Terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>M\xc3\xa1laga Airport has three adjacent terminals, although only two are in use. It also has a General Aviation Terminal and a Cargo Terminal. Passengers can interchange between Terminal 2 and Terminal 3 both landside and airside. The Airport consists of three piers or docks used by airlines: Pier B (with 13 gates, seven with airbridges) and Pier C (with 10 gates, seven with airbridges) in Terminal 2, and Pier D (with 20 gates, 12 with airbridges) in Terminal 3. Pier B is used for non-European traffic (as well as Schengen flights on the lower level), Pier C is used by non-Schengen Traffic and Pier D is used for Schengen Traffic. Flights to the UK and Ireland use both Pier B and Pier C. <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet_Switzerland" title="EasyJet Switzerland">EasyJet Switzerland</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> flights check in at Terminal 2 whilst all others use Terminal 3. The terminals have a total of 164 check-in desks, and have a total of 48 boarding gates of which 26 have airbridges.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal_1">Terminal 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Terminal 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Terminal 1 (styled as T1) opened on 30 June 1972 and was used for flights to non-Schengen destinations, along with flights to Ceuta and Mellila. On 16 March 2010, flights to non-Schengen destinations moved to Pier C in Terminal 3 and flights to Ceuta and Mellila moved to Pier D, leaving Terminal 1 operating no flights, although <a href="/wiki/Jet2.com" title="Jet2.com">Jet2.com</a> continued to checked in flights there for a short time but moved to Terminal 2 shortly afterwards. The terminal has now been closed to the public.\n</p><p>The terminal 1 baggage hall was shown in the episode "The Return of the Seven: Part 1" in series 2 of <i><a href="/wiki/Auf_Wiedersehen,_Pet" title="Auf Wiedersehen, Pet">Auf Wiedersehen, Pet</a></i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal_2">Terminal 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terminal 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg/220px-Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg/330px-Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg/440px-Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1911" data-file-height="1138" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Malaga_aeropuerto.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Terminal 2</div></div></div>\n<p>Terminal 2 (styled as T2) was opened on 30 November 1991, known as the <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Ruiz_Picasso" class="mw-redirect" title="Pablo Ruiz Picasso">Pablo Ruiz Picasso</a> terminal. The building was designed by architect <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Bofill" title="Ricardo Bofill">Ricardo Bofill</a>, and was built to be operated in combination with the pre-existing passenger terminal. It has three floors and a basement, the second floor is for departures and the ground floor is for arrivals. The first floor is used for the lower level for Pier B, and for alleyways leading to arrivals. The basement is for the rental-car pickup desks. To complete the terminal, a building was built for car parking and rental cars, which were built right next to the entrance of the departures and arrivals lounges.\n</p><p>Development work was completed on the terminal in 2008. The original structure leading to Pier C in departures was demolished and replaced with a temporary structure to allow building work for Terminal 3 to be completed in its place. The temporary structure was closed and removed once Terminal 3 opened and Pier C is now accessed from Terminal 3.\n</p><p>Work in the terminal had to be done before the new terminal opened. Because terminal 1 was due to close, all of the gate numbers had to be changed. The only gate that kept its original gate number was B16. The last flight to use the original gate numbers, was an <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a> flight to <a href="/wiki/London_Gatwick_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="London Gatwick Airport">London Gatwick</a>. When Terminal 3 opened, the old arrivals waiting area was converted to a passengers\' transfer between terminals in the arrivals hall where three extra <a href="/wiki/Baggage_carousel" title="Baggage carousel">baggage carousels</a> have since been added.\n</p><p>Terminal 2 has been refurbished since Terminal 3 has opened costing around \xe2\x82\xac2.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-airport-technology.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airport-technology.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The arrivals floor of Terminal 2 was refurbished in early 2011, and all the ceilings were painted between late 2013 and mid 2014. There is also currently work on the old shopping area, currently closed off. In September 2017 it was announced that Terminal 2 was to undergo a further refurbishment costing \xe2\x82\xac8.4 million. This includes the improvements of the toilet facilities, new flooring and the repainting of the exterior of the building.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Pier C closed for refurbishment on 12 November 2018 and reopened on 18 March 2019 while Pier B was due to be refurbished in the winter of 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal_3">Terminal 3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Terminal 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg/220px-M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg/330px-M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg/440px-M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M%C3%A1laga_airport_dock_D.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pier D, opened on 15 March 2010</div></div></div>\n<p>Terminal 3 (styled as T3) is a new terminal at M\xc3\xa1laga Airport. Plans for construction started in 2001 and construction started in 2004. The works were carried out by <a href="/wiki/Ferrovial" title="Ferrovial">Ferrovial</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> It was opened on 15 March 2010 by King <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan Carlos">Juan Carlos</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The new terminal building at M\xc3\xa1laga Airport has been designed by architect Bruce S. Fairbanks. The terminal was built to increase tourism around the Costa Del Sol, and to expand the airport due to increasing number of passengers. It is adjacent to Terminal 2 and has an area of 250,000m\xc2\xb2, which is more than double the size of Terminal 2. It has 86 check-in counters, numbered 301 to 386, 20 new boarding gates, twelve which will have airbridges and 12 baggage reclaim carousels; nine European Union, two non-European Union and one special baggage reclaim carousel.\n</p><p>The terminal has more than doubled capacity to 30 million passengers or 9,000 an hour, is expected to double the number of flights and the 12,813,764 passengers handled during 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and this has increased further since the new runway was completed.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_aviation_terminal">General aviation terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: General aviation terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The general aviation terminal at M\xc3\xa1laga Airport (also known as the private aviation terminal) is located next to the <a href="/wiki/N-340" class="mw-redirect" title="N-340">N-340</a> motorway, and close to runway 31. The terminal was formed from the old passenger terminal building, and has since been renewed and refurbished. It was opened on 29 January 1968 and is used for private jets.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cargo_terminal">Cargo terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cargo terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The cargo terminal was opened in 1996, with 16 docking bays for road transport vehicles.<sup id="cite_ref-M\xc3\xa1laga_Airport_Cargo_Terminal_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M\xc3\xa1laga_Airport_Cargo_Terminal-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It has an area of 5,700&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (61,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) and contains four cold-storage rooms, a vault for valuable merchandise, and an area for hazardous and radioactive materials.<sup id="cite_ref-M\xc3\xa1laga_Airport_Cargo_Terminal_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M\xc3\xa1laga_Airport_Cargo_Terminal-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It is located in the north of the airport, named "Carga Aena" in Spanish.<sup id="cite_ref-M\xc3\xa1laga_Airport_Cargo_Terminal_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M\xc3\xa1laga_Airport_Cargo_Terminal-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_plans">Future plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Future plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<dl><dt>Car park</dt></dl>\n<p>A new car park has been built with seven floors and 2,500 parking spaces, with underground parking for 66 coaches.<sup id="cite_ref-airport-technology.com_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airport-technology.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> A long stay car park opened in mid-2010.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<dl><dt>South power station</dt></dl>\n<p>A new south power station will be built to serve both runways, with a surface area of 5,580m\xc2\xb2.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span (December 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span an underground station for <a href="/wiki/Cercan%C3%ADas_M%C3%A1laga" title="Cercan\xc3\xadas M\xc3\xa1laga">Cercan\xc3\xadas M\xc3\xa1laga</a> <a href="/wiki/Commuter_train" class="mw-redirect" href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> The station opened on 10 September 2010<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> and is located at the arrivals area of Terminal 3. Trains run every 20 minutes between M\xc3\xa1laga City and <a href="/wiki/Fuengirola" title="Fuengirola">Fuengirola</a> via M\xc3\xa1laga Airport.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The line is to be extended to Marbella, but this will not be complete until 2020 at the earliest. Work is currently halted, while awaiting financial assistance from the <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Bank" title="European Investment Bank">European Investment Bank</a>. The line may be extended to Algeciras, but this has yet to be confirmed.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_station">Bus station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Bus station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There is a bus station located underground at the airport. It can be reached from the arrivals level of the transportation hub or from arrivals in terminal 3, which can be accessed from all terminals. There is also a bus stop outside the cargo terminal.\n</p><p>Following a collaborative agreement between the <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_Metropolitan_Transport_Consortium" title="M\xc3\xa1laga Metropolitan Transport Consortium">M\xc3\xa1laga Metropolitan Transport Consortium</a>, the M\xc3\xa1laga Transport Company (EMTSAM) and the Portillo Avanza bus company, a new public information and bus ticket sale point is now in operation at M\xc3\xa1laga Airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car_parks">Car parks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Car parks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Before the new terminal opened the airport had only one large car park, called P2. The airport now has two, with 3,700 spaces (1,200 in P2, 2,500 in the new P1). All outdoor spaces now have covers over them. They can also be reached by the transportation hub.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1laga_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>13 September 1964 \xe2\x80\x93 A <a href="/wiki/Balair" title="Balair">Balair</a> <a href="/wiki/Fokker_F-27" class="mw-redirect" title="Fokker F-27">Fokker F-27</a> (registration HB-AAI) approaching the runway too high. The pilot did a steep descent and the plane landed heavily, causing part of a wing to break off. There were no fatalities. The aircraft was written off.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>20 December 1970 \xe2\x80\x93 A <a href="/wiki/Sobelair" title="Sobelair">Sobelair</a> <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-6B" class="mw-redirect" title="Douglas DC-6B">Douglas DC-6B</a> (registration OO-CTL) returned to M\xc3\xa1laga due to severe weather at the aircraft\'s destination. A hydraulic system failure occurred and the left main undercarriage gear failed. This caused the aircraft to veer left once it landed. There were no fatalities. The aircraft was written off.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>13 September 1982 \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Spantax_Flight_995" title="Spantax Flight 995">Spantax Flight BX995</a> a <a href="/wiki/DC-10" class="mw-redirect" title="DC-10">DC-10</a>-30CF (registration EC-DEG) When the aircraft was rolling for take-off, the pilot felt a strong vibration and aborted the take-off. The flightcrew lost control of the aircraft and were unable to stop in the <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> length available. The aircraft overran the runway, hit an airfield aerial installation, and lost an engine. It crossed the M\xc3\xa1laga\xe2\x80\x93Torremolinos Highway, hitting vehicles before hitting a railway embankment and bursting into flames. An emergency evacuation of the aircraft was carried out but 50 on board died, and a further 110 persons were hospitalized. The cause of the accident was the detachment of fragments from a recapped tread on the right wheel of the nose gear, creating vibration.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>13 October 2000 - The hijacked <a href="/wiki/Sabena" title="Sabena">Sabena</a> Flight 689 operated by an <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A330-200" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A330-200">Airbus A330-200</a> en route from Brussels to Abidjan made an emergency landing at M\xc3\xa1laga where the perpetrator, a Nigerian national, was overpowered by police.</li>\n<li>29 August 2001 \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Binter_Mediterr%C3%A1neo_Flight_8261" title="Binter Mediterr\xc3\xa1neo Flight 8261">Binter Mediterr\xc3\xa1neo Flight BIM8261</a> a <a href="/wiki/CASA_CN-235" class="mw-redirect" title="CASA CN-235">CASA CN-235</a> (registration EC-FBC) was on a flight from Melilla to M\xc3\xa1laga. On final approach the aircraft\'s left engine failed, and the aircraft made an emergency landing. The plane hit the first edge lights and stopped next to the <a href="/wiki/N-340" class="mw-redirect" title="N-340">N-340</a>. Investigation into the accident revealed that shortly after the initial engine failure, the <a href="/wiki/First_Officer_(civil_aviation)" class="mw-redirect" title="First Officer (civil aviation)">First Officer</a> inadvertently shut down both of the aircraft\'s engines, leading to a total loss of power. Four out of the 44 people on board were killed, including the pilot Capt. Fdez. Ruano.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> The aircraft was written off.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a
