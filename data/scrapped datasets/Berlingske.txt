title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[\xcb\x88p\xc9\x9b\xc9\x90\xcc\xafle\xcc\x9d\xc5\x8bsk\xc9\x99 \xcb\x88ts\xca\xb0i\xcb\x90\xc3\xb0n\xcc\xa9\xc9\x99]</a></span>, <i>Berling\'s Times</i>), is a Danish national daily <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspaper</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fact_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fact-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>First published on 3 January 1749,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-con_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-con-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n<i>Berlingske</i> is Denmark\'s, as well as the Nordic region\'s, oldest continually operating newspaper and among the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_oldest_newspapers" title="List of the oldest newspapers">oldest newspapers in the world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_profile">History and profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berlingske&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>Berlingske</i> was founded by Denmark\'s Royal Book Printer <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Henrich_Berling" title="Ernst Henrich Berling">Ernst Henrich Berling</a> and originally titled <i>Kj\xc3\xb8benhavnske Danske Post-Tidender</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> then the <i>Berlingskes Politiske og Avertissements Tidende.</i> The paper was supported by the Conservative Party.<sup id="cite_ref-cashans_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cashans-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Until 1903 it had the official right to publish news about the government.<sup id="cite_ref-cashans_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cashans-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1936, the newspaper\'s title was shortened to <i>Berlingske Tidende</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Madsen_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Madsen-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg/180px-Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg" decoding="async" width="180" height="299" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg/270px-Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg/360px-Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="628" data-file-height="1044" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Berlingske-tidende-1749-side1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The historic first cover page of <i>Berlingske</i> (1749)</div></div></div>\n<p>Mendel Levin Nathanson twice served as the <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> of the paper: between 1838 and 1858 and between 1866 and 1868.<sup id="cite_ref-fact_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fact-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The publisher is <a href="/wiki/Det_Berlingske_Officin" class="mw-redirect" title="Det Berlingske Officin">Det Berlingske Officin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The paper has a conservative stance<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and has no political partisan affiliation.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Due to its traditionalism and its offices on the <a href="/wiki/Pilestr%C3%A6de" title="Pilestr\xc3\xa6de">Pilestr\xc3\xa6de</a>, it is known by the nickname <i>Tanten i Pilestr\xc3\xa6de</i> ("Aunt in Pilestr\xc3\xa6de").<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The paper is also one of the "big three" broadsheet-quality newspapers in Denmark along with <i><a href="/wiki/Jyllands-Posten" title="Jyllands-Posten">Jyllands-Posten</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Politiken" title="Politiken">Politiken</a></i>. Traditionally itself a <a href="/wiki/Broadsheet" title="Broadsheet">broadsheet</a>, <i>Berlingske</i> has been published in the <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a>/<a href="/wiki/Compact_(newspaper)" title="Compact (newspaper)">compact</a> format since 28 August 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Berlingske</i> has won many awards. It is the only newspaper in the world to have won the <a href="/wiki/World_Press_Photo" title="World Press Photo">World Press Photo Award</a> 3 times. It also won the most prestigious journalistic award in Denmark, the Cavling prize, in 2009. In addition, it was awarded the European Newspaper of the Year in the category of national newspaper by the European Newspapers Congress in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following a long period of ownership by the Berling family, the whole Berlingske-group was acquired in 1982 by a group of investors from the Danish corporate establishment including <a href="/wiki/Danske_Bank" title="Danske Bank">Danske Bank</a> and <a href="/wiki/A.P._M%C3%B8ller_M%C3%A6rsk" class="mw-redirect" title="A.P. M\xc3\xb8ller M\xc3\xa6rsk">A.P. M\xc3\xb8ller M\xc3\xa6rsk</a>. This takeover saved the group from an impending bankruptcy caused by a long strike period as well as dwindling circulation and advertising revenues.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In 2000, Det Berlingske Officin was acquired by the Norwegian industrial conglomerate <a href="/wiki/Orkla_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Orkla Group">Orkla Group</a>; the Danish organization was integrated within a multinational Orkla Media group. In 2006 Orkla Media was sold to the British <a href="/wiki/Mecom_Group" title="Mecom Group">Mecom Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-orkla2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orkla2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2011, the newspaper\'s title was abbreviated to <i>Berlingske</i> following a large-scale redesign of the newspaper\'s web and digital presence.<sup id="cite_ref-Madsen_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Madsen-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2015, <i>Berlingske</i> was acquired by the family-owned Belgian media company <a href="/wiki/De_Persgroep" class="mw-redirect" title="De Persgroep">De Persgroep</a> together with the rest of <a href="/wiki/Mecom_Group" title="Mecom Group">Mecom Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Circulation">Circulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berlingske&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Circulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1910 <i>Berlingske Tidende</i> had a circulation of 8,500 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was the second best-selling newspaper in Denmark with a circulation of 149,000 copies in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-wan_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wan-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The circulation of the paper was 142,000 copies in 2003, making it again the second best-selling Danish newspaper.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 2004 the paper had a circulation of 129,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc5_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The circulation of <i>Berlingske</i> was 103,685 copies in 2008 and 103,221 copies in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-ifabc_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ifabc-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was 101,121 copies in 2010 and fell to 96,897 copies in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-ifabc_22-1" class="reference"><a
