class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Published specification for storing digital data</div>\n<p>\nAn <b>open format</b> is a <a href="/wiki/File_format" title="File format">file format</a> for storing <a href="/wiki/Data_(computing)" title="Data (computing)">digital data</a>, defined by a published <a href="/wiki/Specification" class="mw-redirect" title="Specification">specification</a> usually maintained by a <a href="/wiki/Standards_organization" title="Standards organization">standards organization</a>, and which can be used and implemented by anyone. For example, an open format can be implemented by both <a href="/wiki/Proprietary_software" title="Proprietary software">proprietary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free</a> and <a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">open-source software</a>, using the typical <a href="/wiki/Software_license" title="Software license">software licenses</a> used by each. In contrast to open formats, <a href="/wiki/Proprietary_format" title="Proprietary format">closed formats</a> are considered trade secrets. Open formats are also called <b>free file formats</b> if they are not encumbered by any copyrights, patents, trademarks or other restrictions (for example, if they are in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) so that anyone may use them at no monetary cost for any desired purpose.<sup id="cite_ref-Free_File_Format_Definition_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Free_File_Format_Definition-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Depending on the definition, the specification of an open format may require a fee to access or, very rarely, contain other restrictions.<sup id="cite_ref-Free_File_Format_Definition_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Free_File_Format_Definition-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The range of meanings is similar to that of the term <a href="/wiki/Open_standard" title="Open standard">open standard</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-headline" id="Sun_Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_format&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sun Microsystems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a> defines the criteria for open formats as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The format is based on an underlying <a href="/wiki/Open_standard" title="Open standard">open standard</a></li>\n<li>The format is developed through a publicly visible, community driven process</li>\n<li>The format is affirmed and maintained by a vendor-independent <a href="/wiki/Standards_organization" title="Standards organization">standards organization</a></li>\n<li>The format is fully documented and publicly available</li>\n<li>The format does not contain proprietary extensions</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="US_government">US government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_format&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: US government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Within the framework of <a href="/wiki/Open_Government_Initiative" title="Open Government Initiative">Open Government Initiative</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">federal government of the United States</a> adopted the <a href="/wiki/Data.gov#Open_Government_Directive" title="Data.gov">Open Government Directive</a>, according to which: "An open format is one that is platform independent, machine readable, and made available to the public without restrictions that would impede the re-use of that information".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_of_Minnesota">State of Minnesota</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_format&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: State of Minnesota">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">State of Minnesota</a> defines the criteria for open, XML-based file formats as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The format is interoperable among diverse internal and external platforms and applications</li>\n<li>The format is fully published and available royalty-free</li>\n<li>The format is implemented by multiple vendors</li>\n<li>The format is controlled by an open industry organization with a well-defined inclusive process for evolution of the standard</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commonwealth_of_Massachusetts">Commonwealth of Massachusetts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_format&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commonwealth of Massachusetts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Commonwealth of Massachusetts</a> "defines open formats as specifications for data file formats that are based on an underlying open standard, developed by an open community, affirmed and maintained by a standards body and are fully documented and publicly available."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Enterprise Technical Reference Model (ETRM) classifies four formats as "Open Formats":\n</p>\n<ol><li><a Office Open XML Formats (Open XML)</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">Hypertext Document Format v. 4.01</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Plain_text" title="Plain text">Plain Text Format</a></li></ol>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Linux_Information_Project">The Linux Information Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_format&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Linux Information Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>According to <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Linux_Information_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Linux Information Project (page does not exist)">The Linux Information Project</a>, the term <i>open format</i> should refer to "any format that is published for anyone to read and study but which may or may not be encumbered by patents, copyrights or other restrictions on use"<sup id="cite_ref-Free_File_Format_Definition_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Free_File_Format_Definition-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> \xe2\x80\x93 as opposed to a <i>free format</i> which is <i>not</i> encumbered by any copyrights, patents, trademarks or other restrictions.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples_of_open_formats">Examples of open formats</span><span open formats</a></div>\n<p>Open formats (in the royalty-free and free access sense) include:<sup id="cite_ref-Free_File_Format_Definition_2-3" class="reference"><a class="mw-redirect" title="W3C">W3C</a></li></ul>\n<p>The following formats are open (royalty-free with a one-time fee on the standard):\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF">PDF</a>: old
