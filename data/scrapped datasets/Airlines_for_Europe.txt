links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airlines_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A4E was founded in January 2016 by Europe\'s five largest carriers: <a href="/wiki/Air_France%E2%80%93KLM" title="Air France\xe2\x80\x93KLM">Air France\xe2\x80\x93KLM Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">easyJet</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_Airlines_Group" title="International Airlines Group">International Airlines Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa_Group" title="Lufthansa Group">Lufthansa Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair_Holdings" class="mw-redirect" title="Ryanair Holdings">Ryanair Holdings</a> and serves as the united voice of European commercial airlines in Brussels. &#160;\n</p><p>A4E currently counts 16 leading airline groups as its members. &#160;New carriers both big and small, from low-cost to leaser, legacy and cargo have joined A4E. Beyond airlines, global manufacturers such as <a href="/wiki/Airbus" title="Airbus">Airbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Embraer" title="Embraer">Embraer</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">GE</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thales_Group" title="Thales Group">Thales</a> are also members of A4E.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">Objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airlines_for_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The main goal of this association is to address key policy issues affecting passengers and cargo while promoting long-term solutions to benefit Europe\'s aviation sector, including: \n</p>\n<ul><li>&#160;Improving European airspace efficiency and securing a <a href="/wiki/Single_European_Sky" title="Single European Sky">Single European Sky</a>.</li></ul>\n<ul><li>&#160;Revising Europe\'s Air Passenger Rights legislation.</li>\n<li>&#160;Improving safety and security measures for airlines and their passengers. &#160;</li>\n<li>&#160;Tackling airport market power and excessive aviation taxes and charges.</li>\n<li>&#160;Moving towards a zero or low-carbon economy and thereby supporting efforts to achieve the objectives of the Paris Agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
