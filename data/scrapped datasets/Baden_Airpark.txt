<b>FKB</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EDSB</b>) (German: <i>Flughafen Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is the <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>, the second-largest city in the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-W\xc3\xbcrttemberg">Baden-W\xc3\xbcrttemberg</a>, and also serves the <a href="/wiki/Spa_town" title="Spa town">spa town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Baden-Baden" title="Baden-Baden">Baden-Baden</a>. It is the state\'s second-largest airport after <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart_Airport" title="Stuttgart Airport">Stuttgart Airport</a>, and the 18th-largest in Germany with 1,110,500 passengers as of 2016<sup id="cite_ref-ADV_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADV-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and mostly serves low-cost and leisure flights.\n</p><p>The airport itself is part of <i>Baden Airpark</i>, a business park with numerous other tenants.<sup id="cite_ref-airpark_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airpark-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is located in <a href="/wiki/Rheinm%C3%BCnster" title="Rheinm\xc3\xbcnster">Rheinm\xc3\xbcnster</a>, 40&#160;km (25&#160;mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>, 12&#160;km (7.5&#160;mi) west<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Baden-Baden" title="Baden-Baden">Baden-Baden</a>, 25&#160;km (16&#160;mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Haguenau" title="Haguenau">Haguenau</a> and 25&#160;km (16&#160;mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg" title="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_as_a_military_airport">Early years as a military airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years as a military airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The construction of a military airfield began in December 1951 in the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Rhine_Plain" title="Upper Rhine Plain">Upper Rhine Plain</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Black_Forest" title="Black Forest">Black Forest</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> River under the supervision of the <a href="/wiki/French_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="French Air Force">French Air Force</a>. The runway and associated facilities were completed by June 1952. The airfield was granted to <a href="/wiki/Canadian_forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian forces">Canadian forces</a> and became a military base, <i>RCAF Station Baden\xe2\x80\x93Soellingen</i>, later renamed <a href="/wiki/CFB_Baden%E2%80%93Soellingen" title="CFB Baden\xe2\x80\x93Soellingen">CFB Baden\xe2\x80\x93Soellingen</a>, from 1953 until 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-history_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The newly founded Baden Airpark <a href="/wiki/GmbH" class="mw-redirect" title="GmbH">GmbH</a> took over the former military airfield to develop it into a regional airport and business park.<sup id="cite_ref-history_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Technically, the airport itself today is part of the <i>Baden Airpark</i>, which also includes business facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-airpark_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airpark-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_operations">Public operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Public operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first commercial flight from <a href="/wiki/Hapag-Lloyd_Flug" title="Hapag-Lloyd Flug">Hapag-Lloyd Flug</a> (now <a href="/wiki/TUI_fly_Deutschland" title="TUI fly Deutschland">TUI fly Deutschland</a>) took place in April 2001 with service to <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca" title="Palma de Mallorca">Palma de Mallorca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a>. In 2008 the airport counted over one million passengers within a year for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-history_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 25 October 2011, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced it would open its 47th base at Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden in March 2012 with two based aircraft and 20 routes. In addition to Ryanair\'s then existing 12 routes Ryanair opened seven additional routes to Faro, M\xc3\xa1laga, Palma, Riga, Thessaloniki, Vilnius and Zadar.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In July 2018, <a href="/wiki/TUI_fly_Deutschland" title="TUI fly Deutschland">TUI fly Deutschland</a> announced that it would terminate its operations at the airport, cancelling seven year-round and seasonal leisure routes to the Canaries and the Mediterranean.<sup id="cite_ref-TUI_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TUI-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Karlsruhe/Baden-Baden Airport consists of one passenger terminal building equipped with 20 check-in counters and eight departure gates as well as some shops and restaurants.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The apron features eight aircraft stands of which most can be used by mid-sized aircraft such as the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a>. Due to the location of the terminal building walk stands and buses are used for boarding.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span
