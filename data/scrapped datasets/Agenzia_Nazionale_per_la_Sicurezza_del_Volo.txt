<span lang="en">National Agency for the Safety of Flight</span>, is the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> aircraft accident investigation agency. The ANSV is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Italy" title="Prime Minister of Italy">Presidency of the Council of Ministers of Italy</a> oversees the agency.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is the Italian equivalent of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Transportation_Safety_Board" title="National Transportation Safety Board">National Transportation Safety Board</a>.\n</p><p>It was established according to legislative decree No. 66 on 25 February 1999. Prior to the establishment of the ANSV, after each aviation accident or incident, two investigations were carried out, including a perfunctory investigation by the airport director and a formal and technical investigation by an <a href="/wiki/Ad_hoc" title="Ad hoc">ad hoc</a> committee appointed by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Infrastructure_and_Transport_(Italy)" title="Ministry of Infrastructure and Transport (Italy)">Minister of Infrastructure and Transport</a>. Since the establishment of the ANSV, one investigation by the ANSV is carried out after an aviation accident or incident.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a
