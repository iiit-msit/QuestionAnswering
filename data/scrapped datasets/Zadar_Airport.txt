href="/w/index.php?title=Zadar_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As early as 1936, Zadar had regular commercial flights initially provided by <a href="/wiki/Ala_Littoria" title="Ala Littoria">Ala Littoria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\nOver time the airport has grown to become Croatia\'s 4th largest international airport, handling 801,347 annual passengers. It used to be one of a few airports in the world where the <a href="/wiki/Taxiway" title="Taxiway">taxiway</a> crossed a public road. The road was closed on 7 April 2010 due to terms negotiated with the European Union during Croatian accession negotiations.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the beginning of April 2013, Zadar Airport became a <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> base with a stationed <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737-800" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 737-800">Boeing 737-800</a>. It serves 8 European destinations in Belgium, Germany, Italy and Poland. In December 2019, <a href="/wiki/Lauda_(airline)" title="Lauda (airline)">Lauda</a> announced the stationing of three <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320</a> aircraft during the summer 2020 timetable period. A set of 11 new routes were announced by the airline for summer 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Due to the ongoing <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a> the airline postponed the opening of the base to July 2021, where it will station two <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320-200" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320-200">Airbus A320-200</a> and serve 37 destinations.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><br />\n</p><p>The airport also is a <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Air_Force" title="Croatian Air Force">Croatian Air Force</a> main training base.  It used to be a base for <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a>\'s  <i>InterCockpit</i> flight school.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines
