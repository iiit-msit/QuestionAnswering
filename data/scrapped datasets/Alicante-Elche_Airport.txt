lang="es">Aeropuerto de Alicante-Elche</i>, <a href="/wiki/Valencian" class="mw-redirect" title="Valencian">Valencian</a>: Aeroport d\'Alacant-Elx), (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>ALC</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LEAL</b>), originally named <b>El Altet</b>, is \xe2\x80\x94as of 2019\xe2\x80\x94 the fifth-busiest <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a><sup id="cite_ref-EM2017_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EM2017-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> based on passenger numbers, and the main airport serving the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Murcia" title="Region of Murcia">Region of Murcia</a>.  The airport is located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Elche" title="Elche">Elche</a>, about 10 kilometres (6.2 miles) east of this city and about 9&#160;km (5.6&#160;mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Alicante" title="Alicante">Alicante</a>.  Its influence area spans other cities in the <a href="/wiki/Valencia_(province)" class="mw-redirect" title="Valencia (province)">Valencia province</a>\xe2\x80\x94the city of Valencia is located about 160&#160;km (99&#160;mi) north of the airport\xe2\x80\x94and in the <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Murcia" title="Region of Murcia">Region of Murcia</a>. The airport is located about 70&#160;km (43&#160;mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Murcia" title="Murcia">Murcia</a>.\n</p><p>The airport is a base for <a href="/wiki/Air_Nostrum" title="Air Nostrum">Air Nostrum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Air_Shuttle" title="Norwegian Air Shuttle">Norwegian Air Shuttle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vueling" title="Vueling">Vueling</a>. Passenger traffic has increased significantly in the last decade, beating its own yearly record since 2013 to date; in 2019 it set its new consecutive record at 15 million passengers. It is one of <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_busiest_airports_in_Europe_by_passenger_traffic" class="mw-redirect" title="List of the busiest airports in Europe by passenger traffic">the 50 busiest in Europe</a>. Up to 80% of all passenger flights are international.<sup id="cite_ref-EM2017_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EM2017-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The largest numbers of passengers arrive from the United Kingdom, Germany, the Netherlands, Norway, Belgium and Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-AP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Popular domestic destinations are <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca" title="Palma de Mallorca">Palma de Mallorca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" href="/w/index.php?title=Alicante%E2%80%93Elche_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg/220px-Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg/330px-Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg/440px-Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aeropuerto-Alicante-Terminal-Ch%C3%A1rter.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alicante Airport in 1972</div></div></div> \n<p>El Altet airport opened on 4 May 1967, replacing the older aerodrome La Rabassa that had served Alicante since 1936. It took its name after the El Altet area (a part of Elche\'s countryside) where it was built. The first commercial flight that landed in the airport was <a href="/wiki/Convair" title="Convair">Convair Metropolitan</a> by <a href="/wiki/Aviaco" title="Aviaco">Aviaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-airports-worldwide_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airports-worldwide-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Iberia_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia Airlines">Iberia</a> established a regular connections Alicante\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and Alicante\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in November 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-history_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In early 1970s passengers\' traffic reached 1 million, which prompted a construction of a new passenger terminal. In 1980 the runway was extended to three kilometres.<sup id="cite_ref-airports-worldwide_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airports-worldwide-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The next renovation took place during the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-history_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A new office building together with operation and business centres were constructed. For the first time, five air bridges were installed to facilitate boarding.<sup id="cite_ref-history_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A new control tower was inaugurated in 1996 and became fully operational in February 1997. \n</p><p>In 2011, the new terminal was opened at the airport increasing the annual airport capacity to 20 million passengers per year.<sup id="cite_ref-NATAlicante_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NATAlicante-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> All flights arrive and depart from this new terminal. Terminals 1 and 2, which were in service before the opening of the new terminal, are currently closed. Works however will not be completed until 2014, as plans are to construct a connecting hallway between the new terminal and terminals 1 and 2 that will allow to exploit the passenger space of all terminals.<sup id="cite_ref-reopen_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reopen-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Historically, up until 2003, <a href="/wiki/Iberia_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia Airlines">Iberia</a> was the leading airline at the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-annaaero_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annaaero-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> With the decline of conventional airlines, in 2004 low-cost <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> took the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-annaaero_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annaaero-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, the largest European <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_airline" class="mw-redirect" title="Low-cost airline">low-cost airline</a> established a base in Alicante.<sup id="cite_ref-ryanair_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ryanair-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It has become the leading carrier at the airport in 2009, and by 2011 it has increased its presence further with eleven aircraft based, 62 routes, and more than 3 million passengers. However, from the end of October 2011, Ryanair has cut 31 routes due to disagreement with <a href="/wiki/ENAIRE" title="ENAIRE">AENA</a> on the usage of air bridges at the new terminal (before, they would always unload on the tarmac because it is cheaper and the dual door boarding thus enabled quicker turnaround times but the owners of the airport want all planes to use the air bridges in the new building and not unload on the tarmac).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airport is located within <a href="/wiki/Baix_Vinalopo" class="mw-redirect" title="Baix Vinalopo">Elche\'s <i>comarca</i></a> and so there had been a historical petition from Elche to include the city\'s name in the official name of the airport. On 12 July 2013, the name of the airport was officially changed from "Alicante Airport" to "Alicante\xe2\x80\x93Elche Airport"<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> with the <a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA airport code</a> remaining unchanged, ALC.\n</p><p>In 2015, the number of passengers increased by 5,1% to 10,574,484. The passenger traffic has increased in every year since 2000, with the exception of 2009 and 2012. By 2015 the largest number of passengers was carried by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> (2,992,984), followed by <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> (1,285,221) and <a href="/wiki/Vueling" title="Vueling">Vueling</a> (1,093,494). <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Air_Shuttle" title="Norwegian Air Shuttle">Norwegian Air Shuttle</a> (893,319) is the "distant" four.<sup id="cite_ref-aenapax_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aenapax-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intro_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intro-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 23 July 2016 the airport registered its busiest day of operations to date, handling 347 flights\xe2\x80\x94with an average of one flight every 3 minutes\xe2\x80\x94and about 58,000 passengers in a single day.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminals">Terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alicante%E2%80%93Elche_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg/220px-L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg/330px-L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg/440px-L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:L%27Altet._Terminal_antiga_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Interior of Terminal N</div></div></div>\n<p>Terminal N is the only terminal currently in service. Terminals 1 and 2 have been closed since the opening of Terminal N in 2011.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal_N">Terminal N</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alicante%E2%80%93Elche_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Terminal N">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Terminal N was officially opened on 23 March 2011. All flight operations at the airport were moved to this terminal on the following day. The first flight to depart from Terminal N was a <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> flight to <a href="/wiki/Memmingen_Airport" title="Memmingen Airport">Memmingen</a>. The terminal has an area of 333,500 square metres (3,590,000&#160;sq&#160;ft), which is more than six times the size of terminals 1 and 2 together. It includes 96 check-in desks, 40 gates, including 15 with airbridges, and 16 baggage reclaim carousels.<sup id="cite_ref-newterminal_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newterminal-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The terminal is split into two areas, the processor where the C Gates are held, and the dock where the majority of B Gates are located. Flights within the <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen Area</a> use both areas of the terminal while flights to non-Schengen destinations only use the dock. This terminal was constructed to the east of Terminal 1.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_Terminal_1">Former Terminal 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alicante%E2%80%93Elche_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Former Terminal 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Terminal 1 (styled as T1) had 38 check-in desks numbered 1\xe2\x80\x9338, along with one for special baggage which is desk number 39. It included 11 gates of which five have <a href="/wiki/Jet_bridge" title="Jet bridge">airbridges</a>, which were the odd-numbered gates, and nine baggage reclaim carousels. It was closed after the opening of Terminal N on 24 March 2011. There are plans to reopen Terminal 1 in the future, which would require the construction of a connecting walkway with the current terminal, as both terminals are currently unconnected.<sup id="cite_ref-reopen_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reopen-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_Terminal_2">Former Terminal 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alicante%E2%80%93Elche_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Former Terminal 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Terminal 2 (styled as T2) had 14 check-in desks numbered 51\xe2\x80\x9364, six gates (none with airbridges), and two baggage reclaim carousels. This terminal is adjacent to Terminal 1. The terminal was opened in January 2007. Prior to the opening of the new terminal whilst it was still active it was the smaller of the two terminals. Once Terminal N had opened on 24 March 2011, it was closed together with Terminal 1. Terminal 2 now handles General Aviation and will also soon be partially converted into a new overseas Headquarters for <a href="/wiki/Jet2.com" title="Jet2.com">Jet2.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and The bus stop at Alicante airport is located outside the departures area of the terminal on level 2. The airport is connected with the city of Alicante by the C6 bus line.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> There are also hourly bus services to <a href="/wiki/Benidorm" title="Benidorm">Benidorm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torrevieja" title="Torrevieja">Torrevieja</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alicante%E2%80%93Elche_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>In September 2013 a baby died at the airport shortly after arriving with relatives on a flight from <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> after the child\'s mother placed the baby in a carrier onto a <a href="/wiki/Conveyor_belt" title="Conveyor belt">conveyor belt</a> used for bulky luggage items.
