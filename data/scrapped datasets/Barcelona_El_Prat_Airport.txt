href="#cite_note-BOE-JT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>BCN</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LEBL</b>) (<a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>: <i>Aeroport Josep Tarradellas Barcelona\xe2\x80\x93El Prat</i>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i>Aeropuerto Josep Tarradellas Barcelona\xe2\x80\x93El Prat</i>), previously named <i>Barcelona\xe2\x80\x93El Prat</i> and also known as <b>El Prat Airport</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> located 12&#160;km (7.5&#160;mi) southwest<sup id="cite_ref-euro_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-euro-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> of the centre of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, lying in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/El_Prat_de_Llobregat" title="El Prat de Llobregat">El Prat de Llobregat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viladecans" title="Viladecans">Viladecans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sant_Boi_de_Llobregat" title="Sant Boi de Llobregat">Sant Boi</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It has been named after a former <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Catalonia" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Presidents of Catalonia">President</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Generalitat_of_Catalonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Generalitat of Catalonia">Generalitat of Catalonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Josep_Tarradellas" title="Josep Tarradellas">Josep Tarradellas</a>, since 27 February 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-BOE-JT_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOE-JT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It is the second largest and second busiest airport in Spain, and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_busiest_airports_in_Europe" title="List of the busiest airports in Europe">sixth busiest in Europe</a>. In 2019, Barcelona Airport handled a record 52,686,314&#160;million passengers, up 5.0% from 2018. It is a hub for <a href="/wiki/Level_(airline)" class="mw-redirect" title="Level (airline)">Level</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vueling" title="Vueling">Vueling</a>, and a focus city for <a href="/wiki/Air_Europa" title="Air Europa">Air Europa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iberia_(airline)" title="Iberia (airline)">Iberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>.\n</p><p>The Barcelona\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> <a href="/wiki/Air_shuttle" title="Air shuttle">air shuttle</a> service, known as "Pont Aeri" (in <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>) or "Puente A\xc3\xa9reo" (in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>), literally "Air Bridge", was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_busiest_passenger_air_routes" title="List of busiest passenger air routes">world\'s busiest route</a> until 2008, with the highest number of flight operations (971 per week) in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The schedule has been reduced since February 2008, when a <a href="/wiki/Madrid%E2%80%93Barcelona_high-speed_rail_line" title="Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barcelona high-speed rail line">Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barcelona high-speed rail line</a> was opened, covering the distance in 2 hours 30 minutes, and quickly became popular.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josep_Tarradellas_Barcelona%E2%80%93El_Prat_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:LEBL_Layout.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/LEBL_Layout.svg/220px-LEBL_Layout.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/LEBL_Layout.svg/330px-LEBL_Layout.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/LEBL_Layout.svg/440px-LEBL_Layout.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1063" data-file-height="1063" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:LEBL_Layout.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Airport Layout</div></div></div>\n<p>Barcelona\'s first airfield, located at El Remolar, began operations in 1916. However, it did not have good expansion prospects, so a new airport at El Prat opened in 1918. The first plane was a <a href="/wiki/Groupe_Lat%C3%A9co%C3%A8re" title="Groupe Lat\xc3\xa9co\xc3\xa8re">Lat\xc3\xa9co\xc3\xa8re</a> Salmson 300 which arrived from <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a> with final destination <a href="/wiki/Casablanca" title="Casablanca">Casablanca</a>. The airport was used as headquarters of the Aeroclub of Catalonia and the base for the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Navy" title="Spanish Navy">Spanish Navy</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Zeppelin" title="Zeppelin">Zeppelin</a> fleet. Scheduled commercial service began in 1927 with an <a href="/wiki/Iberia_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia Airlines">Iberia</a> service to Madrid <a href="/wiki/Cuatro_Vientos_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuatro Vientos Airport">Cuatro Vientos Airport</a>. This was Iberia\'s first route. During the time of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Second Spanish Republic</a> El Prat was one of the bases of <a href="/wiki/LAPE" title="LAPE">LAPE</a> <i>(L\xc3\xadneas A\xc3\xa9reas Postales Espa\xc3\xb1olas)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1948, a <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> was built (now called runway 07-25); in the same year the first overseas service was operated by <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_World_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="Pan American World Airways">Pan American World Airways</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, using a <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Constellation" title="Lockheed Constellation">Lockheed Constellation</a>. Between 1948 and 1952, a second runway was constructed (runway 16\xe2\x80\x9334), perpendicular to the previous, also taxiways were constructed and a terminal to accommodate passengers. In 1963, the airport reached one million passengers a year. A new <a href="/wiki/Control_tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Control tower">control tower</a> was built in 1965. In 1968, a new terminal was opened, which still exists and is in use as what is now Terminal 2B.<sup id="cite_ref-aenahist_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aenahist-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 3 August 1970, <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_World_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="Pan American World Airways">Pan American World Airways</a> inaugurated regular service between Barcelona, <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> and New York, operated by a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On 4 November of the same year, Iberia began the "Air-shuttle" service between Barcelona and <a href="/wiki/Madrid%E2%80%93Barajas_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barajas Airport">Madrid\xe2\x80\x93Barajas</a>. A few years later, in 1976, a terminal was built specifically for Iberia\'s air-shuttle service and a terminal exclusively for cargo, an annexed mail service and an aircraft ramp for air cargo. In 1977, the airport handled over 5&#160;million passengers annually.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>From the late seventies to the early nineties, the airport was stalled in traffic and investments until the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> held in Barcelona. El Prat underwent a major development consisting of the modernization and expansion of the existing terminal, which became known as Terminal B, and the construction of two further terminals flanking that, known as Terminals A and C respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-aenahist_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aenahist-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The development included <a href="/wiki/Jetway" class="mw-redirect" title="Jetway">jetways</a> for direct access to the aircraft. This reform was designed by <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Bofill_Taller_de_Arquitectura" title="Ricardo Bofill Taller de Arquitectura">Ricardo Bofill Taller de Arquitectura</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In 1992, a new control tower was inaugurated also designed by <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Bofill" title="Ricardo Bofill">Ricardo Bofill</a>, but this was replaced by another much needed control tower in 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The new Terminal 1 was inaugurated on 16 June 2009, covering 545,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (5,866,331&#160;sq&#160;ft). 70% of today\'s flights operate from Terminal 1. The old Terminals A, B and C are now known as Terminals 2A, 2B and 2C.\n</p><p>Due to the strong drop in air traffic after 1999 and the crisis in the aviation sector in 2001 many charter operations from <a href="/wiki/Girona_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Girona Airport">Girona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reus_Airport" title="Reus Airport">Reus</a> were diverted to El Prat, which helped the airport to survive the crisis.\n</p><p>On 1 February 2014, Barcelona\xe2\x80\x93El Prat was the first Spanish airport to receive a daily flight with the Airbus <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">A380-800</a>, on the Emirates route to <a href="/wiki/Dubai_International_Airport" title="Dubai International Airport">Dubai International Airport</a>. Emirates also offers a second daily flight, also operated by the A380-800.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/International_Airlines_Group" title="International Airlines Group">International Airlines Group (IAG)</a> announced in December 2016 flights from Barcelona to the US, Latin America and Asia for the summer of 2017. IAG, formed by British Airways, Iberia, Vueling and Aer Lingus, created <a href="/wiki/Level_(airline)" class="mw-redirect" title="Level (airline)">Level</a>, the second airline, after Norwegian, launching low-cost long haul flights from the Catalan city.<sup id="cite_ref-La_Vanguardia_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-La_Vanguardia-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> They announced flights from June 2017 to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_International_Airport" title="Los Angeles International Airport">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oakland_International_Airport" title="Oakland International Airport">Oakland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punta_Cana_International_Airport" title="Punta Cana International Airport">Punta Cana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ministro_Pistarini_International_Airport" title="Ministro Pistarini International Airport">Buenos Aires</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Writing_better_articles#Stay_on_topic" title="Wikipedia:Writing better articles"><span title="The material near this tag may contain information that is not relevant to the article&#39;s main topic. (June 2017)">relevant?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a> has caused a serious decrease in passenger, operations and cargo numbers during 2020, with numbers way below 2019\'s statistics.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josep_Tarradellas_Barcelona%E2%80%93El_Prat_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Most of the traffic at Barcelona Airport is domestic and European, in which Vueling has an operational base. Intercontinental connections have not generated a significant amount of passenger traffic during the last years. In the early twenty-first century the airport passenger carried numbers and the number of operations increased significantly.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Low-cost_airline" class="mw-redirect" title="Low-cost airline">Low-cost airline</a> traffic grew significantly, especially after the creation of operating bases by Vueling and Clickair at the airport. Vueling and Clickair merged in July 2009, operating under the Vueling name. Other low-cost airlines operate from the airport, including <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Air_International" title="Norwegian Air International">Norwegian Air International</a>, <a href="/wiki/EasyJet_Switzerland" title="EasyJet Switzerland">EasyJet Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transavia.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Transavia.com">Transavia.com</a>. A new base was established at the airport in September 2010.\n</p><p>The airport has 3 runways, two parallel, nominated 07L/25R and 07R/25L (the later opened in 2004), and a cross runway 02/20. There are two terminals: T2, which is the sum of the previous Terminals A, B and C, located on the north side of the airport and T1, on the west side, which opened on 16 June 2009. As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Josep_Tarradellas_Barcelona%E2%80%93El_Prat_Airport&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> the two terminals had a combined total of 268 check-in counters and 64 boarding gates. Operations at the airport are restricted exclusively to <a href="/wiki/Instrument_flight_rules" title="Instrument flight rules">Instrument flight rules</a> (IFR) flights, except for sanitary, emergency and government <a href="/wiki/Visual_Flight_Rules" class="mw-redirect" title="Visual Flight Rules">VFR</a> flights.\n</p><p>A plan for expansion (Plan Barcelona)<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> was completed in 2009, adding a third terminal building (also designed by Ricardo Bofill) and control tower. An additional runway (07R/25L) was also built. The airport became capable of handling 55&#160;million passengers annually (up from 33&#160;million in 2007). The airport expanded in area from 8.45 to 15.33 square kilometres (3.26 to 5.92&#160;sq&#160;mi). Further expansion was planned to be finished by 2012, with a new satellite terminal to raise capacity to 70&#160;million passengers annually, this is better explained in Terminal T1 section.\n</p><p>The airport is the subject of a political discussion over management and control between the <a href="/wiki/Generalitat_of_Catalonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Generalitat of Catalonia">Generalitat of Catalonia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Government">Spanish Government</a>, which has involved <a href="/wiki/AENA" class="mw-redirect" title="AENA">AENA</a> (airport manager) and various airlines, Iberia and Spanair mainly. Part of the controversy is about the benefits that the airport generates, which are used in maintenance and investments in other airports in the network of AENA and government investments in other economic areas.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (May 2012)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminals">Terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a id="Terminal_1">Terminal 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josep_Tarradellas_Barcelona%E2%80%93El_Prat_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terminal 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A new Terminal 1, designed by <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Bofill_Taller_de_Arquitectura" title="Ricardo Bofill Taller de Arquitectura">Ricardo Bofill Taller de Arquitectura</a> was inaugurated on 16 June 2009. The <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">airport terminal</a> has an area of 548,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (5,900,000&#160;sq&#160;ft), an aircraft ramp of 600,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (6,500,000&#160;sq&#160;ft), 13,000 new parking spaces and 45 new gates expandable to 60. This terminal is also capable of handling large aircraft like the Airbus A380-800 or Boeing 747-8I.\n</p><p>The terminal handles both <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Agreement" title="Schengen Agreement">Schengen</a> and non-Schengen flights. It is split into 5 Modules with Module A handling flights to Madrid, Module B handling Schengen flights, Module C handling Air Nostrum flights, Module D handling non-Schengen European flights and Module E handling non-Schengen non-European flights.\n</p><p>Its facilities include:\n</p>\n<ul><li>258 check-in counters</li>\n<li>60 jetways (some are prepared for the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">A380</a>, with double jetway)</li>\n<li>15 baggage carousels (one new carousel is equivalent to 4 carousels in the old terminal) and</li>\n<li>12,000 parking spaces, in addition to the 12,000 already in terminal 2.</li></ul>\n<p>The forecast is that the airport will be able to handle 55&#160;million passengers annually \xe2\x80\x94as opposed to the 30 million people before its construction\xe2\x80\x94 and will reach 90 operations an hour.\n</p><p>The extension of the airport with a total investment of \xe2\x82\xac5.1&#160;billion in the future<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2014)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> will include a new satellite terminal and refurbishment of existing terminals. The civil engineering phase of the South Terminal had a budget of \xe2\x82\xac1&#160;billion.\n</p><p>It is also planned the construction of a satellite terminal \xe2\x80\x94T1S or Terminal 1 Sat\xc3\xa8l\xc2\xb7lit, in Catalan\xe2\x80\x94 considering that the airport is on the verge of overcrowding because terminals cannot handle all passengers because of space shortage. This terminal will be at 1,5 kilometres from the current T1 terminal, behind the 02-20,  transversal, runway. With this action, the airport will be able to increase its passenger capacity to 70 million people annually.\n</p><p>There are four lounges located in Terminal 1.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal_2">Terminal 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Josep_Tarradellas_Barcelona%E2%80%93El_Prat_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Terminal 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Terminal 2 is divided into three linked sections, known as Terminal 2A, 2B and 2C. Terminal 2B is the oldest part of the complex still in use, dating back to 1968. Terminals 2A and 2C were added in order to expand the airport capacity before the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> held in city.<sup id="cite_ref-aenahist_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aenahist-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This expansion was also designed by <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Bofill" title="Ricardo Bofill">Ricardo Bofill</a>.\n</p><p>This terminal is mostly occupied by low-cost airlines, although there are some full-service airlines which also use this terminal.\n</p><p>Following the opening of Terminal 1 in 2009, Terminal 2 became almost empty until the airport authorities lowered landing fees to attract low-cost and regional carriers to fill the terminal. Whilst this has helped, the complex is nowhere near full capacity and Terminal 2A is currently unused for departures. Terminal 2C is used only by <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> and <a href="/wiki/EasyJet_Switzerland" title="EasyJet Switzerland">EasyJet Switzerland</a> flights, with flights to the UK using module M0, whilst flights to the rest of Europe use module M1. Terminal 2B is mostly used by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and others, like Transavia. And T2A is adapted for large airplanes, such as <a href="/wiki/Boeing_777" title="Boeing 777">B777.</a> The terminal is also split into Modules, where flights to schengen destinations use Module U and flights to non Schengen destinations use Modules W and Y.\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span
