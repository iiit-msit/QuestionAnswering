dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Motion in Westminster system parliaments</div>\n<p>An <b>early day motion</b> (<b>EDM</b>), in the <a href="/wiki/Westminster_system" title="Westminster system">Westminster system</a>, is a motion, expressed as a single sentence,<sup id="cite_ref-ukp_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ukp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Table_(parliamentary_procedure)" title="Table (parliamentary procedure)">tabled</a> by <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Members of Parliament</a> that formally calls for debate "on an early day". In practice, they are rarely debated in the House and their main purpose is to draw attention to particular subjects of interest.  <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">Government ministers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whip_(politics)" title="Whip (politics)">Whips</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Private_Secretary" title="Parliamentary Private Secretary">Parliamentary Private Secretaries</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_House_of_Commons_(United_Kingdom)" title="Speaker of the House of Commons (United Kingdom)">Speaker of the House of Commons</a> and Deputy Speakers do not normally sign EDMs.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> EDMs remain open for signature for the duration of the <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_session" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentary session">parliamentary session</a>.\n</p><p>EDMs can be tabled on matters ranging from trivial or humorous topics to those of great importance. The <a href="/wiki/Motion_of_censure" class="mw-redirect" title="Motion of censure">censure motion</a> by which the Labour Government of <a href="/wiki/James_Callaghan" title="James Callaghan">James Callaghan</a> was ejected had its origin in an early day motion (no. 351 of 1978\xe2\x80\x9379), put down on 22 March 1979, by <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Thatcher" title="Margaret Thatcher">Margaret Thatcher</a>.\n</p><p>MPs may ensure the text of an EDM is printed in <a href="/wiki/Hansard" title="Hansard">Hansard</a> by mentioning it by number in questions to the <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_House_of_Commons" title="Leader of the House of Commons">Leader of the House of Commons</a> after the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_Statement&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Business Statement (page does not exist)">Business Statement</a> (normally on a Thursday when the house is in session).\n</p><p>EDMs tabled on serious topics have included one demanding the release of <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a> when he was incarcerated in <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, and one calling for a consultation on the fingerprinting of children in schools without parental permission.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2005 United Kingdom general election">2005 general election</a>, 412 of the 646 MPs signed EDM 178 calling for a <a href="/wiki/Climate_Change_Act_2008" title="Climate Change Act 2008">Climate Change Bill</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> only three other early day motions had ever been signed by more than 400 MPs.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Early_day_motion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Robert_Courts" title="Robert Courts">Robert Courts</a> has been an outspoken critic of EDMs, describing them as "parliamentary graffiti." Courts has said that EDMs are generally tabled by MPs on behalf of "lobbyists or groups keen to show themselves as doing something", that they are "politically impotent" and a waste of taxpayers\' money.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
