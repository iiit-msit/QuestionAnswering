Michaels</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Children</th><td class="infobox-data">5</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>David Bonderman</b> (born November 27, 1942) is an American <a href="/wiki/Billionaire" title="Billionaire">billionaire</a> businessman. He is the founding partner of <a href="/wiki/TPG_Capital" title="TPG Capital">TPG Capital</a> (formerly Texas Pacific Group), and its Asian affiliate, <a href="/wiki/Newbridge_Capital" class="mw-redirect" title="Newbridge Capital">Newbridge Capital</a>. He is also one of the minority owners of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a> as well as the co-founder and co-majority owner (along with <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Bruckheimer" title="Jerry Bruckheimer">Jerry Bruckheimer</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Kraken" title="Seattle Kraken">Seattle Kraken</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Bonderman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bonderman was born to a Jewish family,<sup id="cite_ref-JewishBusinessTimes_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JewishBusinessTimes-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> on November 27, 1942,<sup id="cite_ref-MarketScreener_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarketScreener-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and was educated there at <a href="/wiki/University_High_School_(Los_Angeles,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="University High School (Los Angeles, California)">University High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_19921111_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_19921111-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Bonderman studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a>, where he graduated <a href="/wiki/Phi_Beta_Kappa" title="Phi Beta Kappa">Phi Beta Kappa</a> in 1963, and at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Law_School" title="Harvard Law School">Harvard Law School</a>, where he graduated magna cum laude in 1966. He was also a member of the <i><a href="/wiki/Harvard_Law_Review" title="Harvard Law Review">Harvard Law Review</a></i> and a Sheldon Fellow. During his time at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard</a>, he traveled to <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, Egypt, to study Islamic Legal Jurisprudence and Law, and became proficient in various Islamic legal cliques developing a near-native fluency in <a href="/wiki/Modern_Standard_Arabic" title="Modern Standard Arabic">Modern Standard Arabic</a>. Bonderman began providing the funding for the Bonderman Travel Fellowship at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a> in 1995 which gives eight undergraduate and six graduate students per year with the opportunity to travel the world independently, with very little structure or regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, David Bonderman\'s daughter, Samantha [Holloway] donated the funding to create a similar fellowship at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>. While the fellowships share the same name (the Bonderman Fellowship), they vary in both eligibility and execution.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Bonderman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bonderman was an assistant professor at <a href="/wiki/Tulane_University_Law_School" title="Tulane University Law School">Tulane University Law School</a> during 1967 and 1968; he then was a special assistant to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Attorney_General" title="United States Attorney General">United States Attorney General</a> during 1968 and 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-bbprofile_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbprofile-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1971, he joined the law firm of <a href="/wiki/Arnold_%26_Porter" title="Arnold &amp; Porter">Arnold &amp; Porter</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-journalnews_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journalnews-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> where he became a partner and specialized in corporate, securities, bankruptcy and antitrust litigation.<sup id="cite_ref-bbprofile_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbprofile-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1983, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bass" title="Robert Bass">Robert M. Bass</a> Group, Inc. (RMBG),<sup id="cite_ref-journalnews_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-journalnews-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which now does business as Keystone Inc., and became the chief operating officer.<sup id="cite_ref-bbprofile_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbprofile-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Bonderman has been a principal at TPG Capital in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>, since December 1992, where he is also co-founder and chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-bbprofile_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbprofile-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2008, Bonderman was named as one of the investors of what became the <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_Arena" title="T-Mobile Arena">T-Mobile Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Bonderman was a director of <a href="/wiki/Continental_Airlines" title="Continental Airlines">Continental Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/B%C3%B6we_Bell_%26_Howell" class="mw-redirect" title="B\xc3\xb6we Bell &amp; Howell">B\xc3\xb6we Bell &amp; Howell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ducati_Motor_Holding_S.p.A." title="Ducati Motor Holding S.p.A.">Ducati Motor Holding S.p.A.</a>, Credicom Asia, the National Education Corp., Beringer Wine Estates, Carr Realty, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Cinemas" title="Virgin Cinemas">Virgin Cinemas</a>, <a href="/wiki/CoStar_Group" title="CoStar Group">CoStar Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gemalto" title="Gemalto">Gemalto</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>. He is on the boards of <a href="/wiki/The_Wilderness_Society_(United_States)" title="The Wilderness Society (United States)">The Wilderness Society</a>, the Grand Canyon Trust, the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">World Wide Fund for Nature</a>, The University of Washington Foundation and the American <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayan</a> Foundation. He previously served on the boards of <a href="/wiki/Washington_Mutual" title="Washington Mutual">Washington Mutual</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Savings_Bank" title="American Savings Bank">American Savings Bank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denbury_Resources" title="Denbury Resources">Denbury Resources</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burger_King" title="Burger King">Burger King</a>. He was a board member of <a href="/wiki/Uber" title="Uber">Uber</a> until he resigned from that position in June 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-bbprofile_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbprofile-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2017, Bonderman resigned from the board of <a href="/wiki/Uber" title="Uber">Uber</a> amidst controversy surrounding a sexist response to fellow board member <a href="/wiki/Arianna_Huffington" title="Arianna Huffington">Arianna Huffington</a> during a company all-hands meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> "There\xe2\x80\x99s a lot of data that shows when there\xe2\x80\x99s one woman on the board, it\xe2\x80\x99s much more likely that there will be a second woman on the board," said Huffington. Bonderman replied, "actually, what it shows is that it\'s much more likely to be more talking." The Uber meeting was, among other things, slated to discuss efforts to rein in a toxic and sexist culture at the company.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2018, Bonderman filed an application for a <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> (NHL) <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion team</a> to play at a renovated <a href="/wiki/Key_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="Key Arena">Key Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/NHL_Board_of_Governors" class="mw-redirect" title="NHL Board of Governors">NHL Board of Governors</a> voted to approve the team, named the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Kraken" title="Seattle Kraken">Seattle Kraken</a>, on December 4.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Wildcat Capital Management was originally Bonderman\'s family office.<sup id="cite_ref-Bl01_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bl01-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In early 2019, a <a href="/wiki/Mutual_fund" title="Mutual fund">mutual fund</a> named Infinity Q Diversified Alpha Fund had reportedly said on its website that the "investment team and control functions are largely the same for both Wildcat and Infinity Q." Wildcat also reportedly had $100 million invested in the fund.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Infinity Q had been founded in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In early 2021, the $1.7 billion Infinity Q fund<sup id="cite_ref-wsj2_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> suspended <a href="/wiki/Mutual_fund#Advantages" title="Mutual fund">redemptions</a>, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">SEC</a> was investigating asset valuations and the chief investment officer, James Velissaris, had been placed on administrative leave.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj1_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Leonard Potter, Infinity Q\'s non-executive chairman and owner of Wildcat, was designated to take over Infinity Q\'s management. Wildcat "managed more than $3 billion at the end of 2019, including capital from Bonderman."<sup id="cite_ref-Bl01_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bl01-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In April 2021, the <i>Wall Street Journal</i> analyzed specific valuation problems in the fund portfolios and received some comment on them. It also reported an apparent loss of $500 million in the fund, bringing valuation to $1.2 billion, and some discussion of the loss. Velissaris was identified as having been majority owner and in control of the fund with Bonderman family investment interests as passive investors in it. Infinity Q was expected to present a plan to distribute funds to investors by May 24, the report concluded.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj2_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Bonderman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bonderman is married to Dr. Laurie Michaels; they have five children, and live in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">Fort Worth, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_profile_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_profile-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2002, for his 60th birthday, Bonderman had <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Mellencamp" title="John Mellencamp">John Mellencamp</a> play at his birthday party at <a href="/wiki/The_Joint_(music_venue)" title="The Joint (music venue)">The Joint</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hard_Rock_Hotel_and_Casino_(Las_Vegas)" title="Hard Rock Hotel and Casino (Las Vegas)">Hard Rock Hotel and Casino</a> in Las Vegas. John Mellencamp played for an hour, The Rolling Stones played for an hour and a half, and comedian <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a> entertained guests between acts. The party cost $7 million, making it one of the most expensive private concerts ever held.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2012, for his 70th birthday party, Bonderman held a private concert by former <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> member <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn Las Vegas</a> for 1,020 guests. <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a> also performed a comedy routine.
