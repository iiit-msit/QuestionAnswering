title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>FMM</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EDJA</b>), also known as <i><a href="/wiki/Allg%C3%A4u" title="Allg\xc3\xa4u">Allg\xc3\xa4u</a> Airport Memmingen</i>, is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> in the town of <a href="/wiki/Memmingerberg" title="Memmingerberg">Memmingerberg</a> near <a href="/wiki/Memmingen" title="Memmingen">Memmingen</a>, the third-largest city in the <a href="/wiki/Swabia_(Bavaria)" title="Swabia (Bavaria)">Swabia</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>. It is the smallest of the three commercial airports in the state after <a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Munich Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Airport" title="Nuremberg Airport">Nuremberg Airport</a> and has the highest altitude of any commercial airport in Germany. It is operated by <i>Flughafen Memmingen <a href="/wiki/Gesellschaft_mit_beschr%C3%A4nkter_Haftung" title="Gesellschaft mit beschr\xc3\xa4nkter Haftung">GmbH</a></i>, a limited partnership of mostly local, medium-sized companies and public shares.\n</p><p>Located about 3.8&#160;km (2.4&#160;mi) from the centre of Memmingen and 110&#160;km (68&#160;mi) from the city centre of <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, it serves Memmingen and the <a href="/wiki/Allg%C3%A4u" title="Allg\xc3\xa4u">Allg\xc3\xa4u</a>. It also provides a low-cost alternative to Munich Airport and therefore is sometimes referred to as <i>Memmingen/Munich-West Airport</i>. It serves as a base for <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and features flights to European leisure and some metropolitan destinations and handled over 1,7 million passengers in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2019figures_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019figures-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memmingen_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png/220px-FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png/330px-FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png/440px-FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png 2x" data-file-width="1455" data-file-height="945" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FlughafenMemmingenLuftfoto.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial view of Memmingen Airport</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memmingen_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A military airfield was built at Memmingerberg in 1935. It was used during World War II. After being rebuilt, it was used for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">US Air Force</a> training flights from 1956. From 1959 to 2003, it was the home base of <a href="/wiki/German_Air_Force" title="German Air Force">German Air Force</a> fighterbomber wing 34 ("Allg\xc3\xa4u").\n</p><p>The airport was certified as a regional commercial airport on 20 July 2004 and commenced operation on 5 August 2004, but there were no scheduled or regular chartered flights. Scheduled flights to the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Hannover_Messe" title="Hannover Messe">Hanover Fair</a> were cancelled due to lack of demand. In June 2005, the district of <a href="/wiki/Oberallg%C3%A4u" title="Oberallg\xc3\xa4u">Oberallg\xc3\xa4u</a> granted initial finance of \xe2\x82\xac480,000. A further sum of \xe2\x82\xac200,000 was granted by the city of <a href="/wiki/Memmingen" title="Memmingen">Memmingen</a> after a popular vote on 25 September 2005. In 2006, scheduled flights to <a href="/wiki/Dortmund_Airport" title="Dortmund Airport">Dortmund</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rostock%E2%80%93Laage_Airport" title="Rostock\xe2\x80\x93Laage Airport">Rostock</a> were planned but cancelled because the carrier became insolvent. In autumn 2006, <a href="/wiki/Luftfahrtgesellschaft_Walter" class="mw-redirect" title="Luftfahrtgesellschaft Walter">Luftfahrtgesellschaft Walter</a> offered chartered flights to <a href="/wiki/Dortmund_Airport" title="Dortmund Airport">Dortmund</a> for two months, during which only 100 passengers were carried.\n</p><p>In March 2007, a subsidy of \xe2\x82\xac7,500,000 promised by the <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavarian</a> government was approved by the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>. Until 25 September 2008 it was known as <i>Allg\xc3\xa4u Airport/Memmingen</i>.\n</p><p>On 28 June 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/TUI_fly_Deutschland" title="TUI fly Deutschland">TUIfly</a> started offering domestic flights to <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Tegel_Airport" title="Berlin Tegel Airport">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburg_Airport" title="Hamburg Airport">Hamburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cologne_Bonn_Airport" title="Cologne Bonn Airport">Cologne</a>, and also flights to holiday destinations such as <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca_Airport" title="Palma de Mallorca Airport">Palma de Mallorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heraklion_International_Airport" title="Heraklion International Airport">Heraklion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naples_International_Airport" title="Naples International Airport">Naples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci%E2%80%93Fiumicino_Airport" title="Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venice_Marco_Polo_Airport" title="Venice Marco Polo Airport">Venice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antalya_Airport" title="Antalya Airport">Antalya</a>. In March 2009, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced seven new routes serving Memmingen starting May 2009.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Developments_since_2010">Developments since 2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memmingen_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Developments since 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Ryanair have continued to announce new routes and from May 2010 Ryanair operated 14 routes to and from Memmingen. <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a> also started serving Memmingen and has established eight routes since then. On 24 October 2013, Ryanair announced a new seasonal service to <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Airport" title="Shannon Airport">Shannon</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Palermo%E2%80%93Boccadifalco_Airport" title="Palermo\xe2\x80\x93Boccadifalco Airport">Palermo</a> was added as a destination from the summer season of 2015. For the 2015 summer schedule, Wizz Air announced that it would expand its commitment and serve the cities of Tuzla in Bosnia and Herzegovina, the Bulgarian city of Sofia and the Lithuanian capital Vilnius.<sup id="cite_ref-airliners.de_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airliners.de-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 5 June 2014, the airport welcomed its five-millionth passenger.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 2014, <a href="/wiki/InterSky" title="InterSky">InterSky</a> announced that it would be taking over the domestic flights from Memmingen to <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Tegel_Airport" title="Berlin Tegel Airport">Berlin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hamburg_Airport" title="Hamburg Airport">Hamburg</a> by 1 March 2015, as <a href="/wiki/Germanwings" title="Germanwings">Germanwings</a> had announced that it would cease the same services just a few days earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In earlier years, these routes had already been unsuccessfully served by <a href="/wiki/TUI_fly_Deutschland" title="TUI fly Deutschland">TUIfly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Berlin" title="Air Berlin">Air Berlin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Avanti_Air" title="Avanti Air">Avanti Air</a>. In May 2015, <a href="/wiki/InterSky" title="InterSky">InterSky</a> announced that it would have a presence at Memmingen Airport by October 2015 consisting of one aircraft, adding a new route to <a href="/wiki/Cologne_Bonn_Airport" title="Cologne Bonn Airport">Cologne</a> and increasing frequencies on the already existing services to Berlin and Hamburg.<sup id="cite_ref-interskybase_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interskybase-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Later, that plan was changed as a triangular route of <a href="/wiki/Friedrichshafen_Airport" title="Friedrichshafen Airport">Friedrichshafen</a> - Memmingen - Cologne/Bonn will be established instead of basing an aircraft in Memmingen.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, on 6 November 2015, InterSky ceased all operations due to financial difficulties, leaving Memmingen again without any domestic connections.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ryanair announced in March 2017 plans to establish its second Bavarian base (after <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Airport" title="Nuremberg Airport">Nuremberg Airport</a>) in Memmingen from October 2017 consisting of one aircraft and seven additional routes.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> \nSince 31 March 2019, another Ryanair aircraft has been stationed in Memmingen and the workforce has been increased to 70 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In December 2017, Memmingen Airport received its safety certification by the <a href="/wiki/European_Aviation_Safety_Agency" title="European Aviation Safety Agency">European Aviation Safety Agency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 2017 has been announced the first business year in which the airport achieved a profit at year end since the start of public services.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In August 2018, the airport announced the schedule for its planned expansion which will take place from September 2018 until 2020. While the runway will be widened and its guidance system and lightning upgraded, the luggage facilities will see an expansion.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 6 December 2018, the 10 millionth passenger was welcomed.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In early 2019 the planned expansion works began to widen the runway and expand the handling facilities with the airport being closed for several weeks in September of the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-fmmexpansion_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fmmexpansion-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The widened and refurbished runway has been inaugurated on 1 October 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-flugrevue_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flugrevue-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Flughafenhalle_Memmingen.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Check-in area inside the main hall</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memmingen_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Memmingen Airport has one passenger terminal building equipped with 10 check-in counters and 7 departure gates<sup id="cite_ref-statistics_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statistics-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> used for <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen</a> flights (1-3) on the ground floor and non-Schengen flights (4-7) on the upper floor. The building has no <a href="/wiki/Jet_bridge" title="Jet bridge">jet bridges</a>, therefore walk-boarding and bus-boarding is used. There is also a <a href="/wiki/Duty-free_shop" title="Duty-free shop">duty-free shop</a>, as well as some restaurants and car-hire facilities at the airport. The terminal has a capacity of two million passengers per year.<sup id="cite_ref-statistics_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statistics-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway_and_apron">Runway and apron</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memmingen_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Runway and apron">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Memmingen Airport has an <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">ILS</a> <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system#ILS_categories" title="Instrument landing system">Category 1</a> for runway 24 and is equipped with NDB/DME and GPS RNAV. Originally, the runway was only 30 metres (98&#160;ft) wide with accordingly narrow taxiways due to its former use as a facility for jet fighter aircraft. However, an expansion to the international standard 45 metres (148&#160;ft) had been granted in 2016<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and was completed in September 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-flugrevue_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flugrevue-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Simultaneously, the runway was equipped with new LED lightning while an upgrade of the ILS for direction 06 is under preparation.<sup id="cite_ref-flugrevue_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flugrevue-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Two aprons provide parking spaces for six mid-sized aircraft such as the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320_family#A320" title="Airbus A320 family">Airbus A320</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a> at a time as well as some smaller business jets. There is an additional smaller apron for general aviation aircraft located on the other side of the runway.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and
