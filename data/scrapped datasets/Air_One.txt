Italy">Fiumicino, Italy</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Air One S.p.A.</b> was an Italian low-cost airline which operated as <b>Air One "Smart Carrier"</b>. It operated as Alitalia\'s <a href="/wiki/Low_cost_carrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Low cost carrier">low cost carrier</a> subsidiary with operating bases located in <a href="/wiki/Catania-Fontanarossa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Catania-Fontanarossa Airport">Catania-Fontanarossa Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Falcone%E2%80%93Borsellino_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Falcone\xe2\x80\x93Borsellino Airport">Palermo Falcone\xe2\x80\x93Borsellino Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pisa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Pisa Airport">Pisa Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venice_Marco_Polo_Airport" title="Venice Marco Polo Airport">Venice Marco Polo Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Verona_Villafranca_Airport" title="Verona Villafranca Airport">Verona Villafranca Airport</a>; while <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a> was a focus city.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> "Air One" is a <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> of the English meaning Air One and the Italian word \'airone\', meaning <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a> (the bird depicted in the airline\'s logo), which was also the airline\'s <a href="/wiki/Airline_call_sign" class="mw-redirect" title="Airline call sign">callsign</a>.\n</p><p>Before the merger with Alitalia, Air One was a competitor, the second largest airline in Italy, with a network to 36 destinations in Italy, Europe and North America. Its main bases were <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci-Fiumicino_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leonardo da Vinci-Fiumicino Airport">Leonardo da Vinci-Fiumicino Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linate_Airport" title="Linate Airport">Linate Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turin_Airport" title="Turin Airport">Turin Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FI_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Air One ceased operations on 30 October 2014<sup id="cite_ref-shutdown_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shutdown-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> as part of the new concept of its parent company <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a> based on its new partnership with <a href="/wiki/Etihad_Airways" title="Etihad Airways">Etihad Airways</a>. All routes were either cancelled or taken over by Alitalia itself.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2 id="mw-toc-heading">Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-2"><a href="#First_years"><span class="tocnumber">1.1</span> <span class="toctext">First years</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-3"><a href="#Merger_with_Alitalia"><span class="tocnumber">1.2</span> <span class="toctext">Merger with Alitalia</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-4"><a href="#Air_One_&quot;Smart_Carrier&quot;_-_low_cost_subsidiary"><span class="tocnumber">1.3</span> <span class="toctext">Air One "Smart Carrier" - In 1988 Gruppo Toto, one of the leading building and civil engineering groups in Italy, became the majority shareholder. With the acquisition of its first <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737-200</a> in June 1994, Aliadriatica started scheduled and charter operations. On 27 April 1995 Aliadriatica began scheduled flights between Milan (Linate) and Brindisi and Reggio Calabria and Lamezia Terme.\n</p><p>On 23 November 1995, the new name Air One was adopted and the airline began operating scheduled flights between <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>. In 1996, the first full year of operations, 713,000 passengers were carried and the airline expanded rapidly thereafter.\n</p><p>In the year 2000 Air One announced a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> and nearly all Air One flights became code-shared with <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a>.\n</p><p>As from June 2006, regional destinations were served under the name <a href="/wiki/Air_One_CityLiner" class="mw-redirect" title="Air One CityLiner">Air One CityLiner</a> with the brand new fleet of <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_CRJ-900" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombardier CRJ-900">Bombardier CRJ-900s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FI_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007, Air One carried approximately 5.5 million passengers (scheduled and charter), thus becoming the second largest Italian airline in terms of passenger traffic. 2007 revenues totaled EUR750 million with a net profit of EUR6.8 million.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_with_Alitalia">Merger with Alitalia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Merger with Alitalia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:EI-DNX-2008-02-20-FCO.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:EI-DNX-2008-02-20-FCO.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Air One <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Classic" title="Boeing 737 Classic">Boeing 737-300</a> in 2008</div></div></div>\n<p>In August 2008, it was announced that Air One was to be merged with Alitalia.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Air One was almost bankrupt in 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the merger with Alitalia-CAI was the only viable solution to save it.\n</p><p>On 13 January 2009, Air One officially became part of Alitalia, though the two airlines were to be combined into one over time.  A detailed integration plan was at that time yet to be announced.\n</p><p>On 28 March 2009, due to the merger with Alitalia, the cooperation between Lufthansa\'s <a href="/wiki/Miles_%26_More" title="Miles &amp; More">Miles &amp; More</a> and Air One ended.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On 28 June 2009, also due to the Alitalia merger, the partnerships with United Airlines\' <a href="/wiki/Mileage_Plus" class="mw-redirect" title="Mileage Plus">Mileage Plus</a> program and Air Canada\'s <a href="/wiki/Aeroplan" title="Aeroplan">Aeroplan</a> program ended.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Later in 2009, the Alitalia and Air One booking procedures were unified so that the airlines effectively became the same.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Air_One_.22Smart_Carrier.22_-_low_cost_subsidiary"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_One_&quot;Smart_Carrier&quot;_-_low_cost_subsidiary">Air One "Smart Carrier" - low cost subsidiary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_One&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Air One &quot;Smart Carrier&quot; - low cost subsidiary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg/220px-Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg/330px-Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg/440px-Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1023" data-file-height="681" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Air_One_Airbus_A320-200_KvW.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Air One <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320-200" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320-200">Airbus A320-200</a></div></div></div>\n<p>On 28 March 2010, Air One <i>Smart Carrier</i> as a separate brand from Alitalia began operating <a href="/wiki/Low_Cost_Carrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Low Cost Carrier">low-cost</a> flights out of <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> <a href="/wiki/Malpensa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Malpensa Airport">Malpensa Airport</a>. Nine domestic and five international destinations were initially served, using five <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320s</a> painted in the Air One livery and formatted to 180 seats.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In its first full quarter of operations, Air One Smart Carrier transported 320 000 passengers, with an on-time performance of 89%.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 2 February 2011, Air One Smart Carrier announced that, in the summer 2011 season, it would expand its operations at Milan Malpensa and also open a new base at <a href="/wiki/Pisa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Pisa Airport">Pisa Airport</a>. The base opened on 1 July 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Air One Smart Carrier predicted it would carry 1,6 million passengers in 2011, compared to 0,9 million in 2010, and a new website was also launched.\n</p><p>On 21 December 2011, Air One Smart Carrier announced a new base at <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Marco Polo Airport">Marco Polo Airport</a>, with flights starting in May 2012. On 1 October 2012, operations commenced at Air One Smart Carrier\'s new base in <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a> <a href="/wiki/Fontanarossa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Fontanarossa Airport">Fontanarossa Airport</a>, the fourth base.  In September 2013 the opening of a fifth base at <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a> <a href="/wiki/Falcone-Borsellino_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Falcone-Borsellino Airport">Falcone-Borsellino Airport</a> was announced with flights to commence in March 2014 including a new route to London Gatwick Airport, a destination not served by Air One since the ending of flights from Milan Malpensa Airport.\n</p><p>On 26 October 2013, Air One closed its operating base in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> <a href="/wiki/Malpensa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Malpensa Airport">Malpensa Airport</a> and announced a new base at <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a> <a href="/wiki/Falcone%E2%80%93Borsellino_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Falcone\xe2\x80\x93Borsellino Airport">Falcone\xe2\x80\x93Borsellino Airport</a>. The flights from Palermo started in March 2014.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Closure">Closure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
