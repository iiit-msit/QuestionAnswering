2013</a></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Francisco S\xc3\xa1 Carneiro Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>OPO</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LPPR</b>) or simply <b>Porto Airport</b> (formerly <i>Pedras Rubras Airport</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> near <a href="/wiki/Porto" title="Porto">Porto</a> (Oporto), <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. It is located 11&#160;km (6.8&#160;mi) northwest of the <a href="/wiki/Cl%C3%A9rigos_Church" title="Cl\xc3\xa9rigos Church">Cl\xc3\xa9rigos Tower</a> in the centre of Porto, in the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Maia_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Maia Municipality">Maia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matosinhos" title="Matosinhos">Matosinhos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vila_do_Conde" title="Vila do Conde">Vila do Conde</a> and is run by <a href="/wiki/ANA_%E2%80%93_Aeroportos_de_Portugal" class="mw-redirect" title="ANA \xe2\x80\x93 Aeroportos de Portugal">ANA \xe2\x80\x93 Aeroportos de Portugal</a>. The airport is currently the second-busiest in the country, based on aircraft operations; and the second-busiest in passengers, based on Aeroportos de Portugal traffic statistics, after <a href="/wiki/Lisbon_Airport" title="Lisbon Airport">Lisbon Airport</a> and before <a href="/wiki/Faro_Airport" title="Faro Airport">Faro Airport</a>. The airport is a base for <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">easyJet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/TAP_Air_Portugal" title="TAP Air Portugal">TAP Air Portugal</a> and its subsidiary <a href="/wiki/TAP_Express" title="TAP Express">TAP Express</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport is surrounded by the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Matosinhos_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Matosinhos Municipality">Matosinhos</a> (to the south and west) and <a href="/wiki/Vila_do_Conde_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Vila do Conde Municipality">Vila do Conde</a> (to the north) and <a href="/wiki/Maia_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Maia Municipality">Maia</a> (to the east). It covers the parishes of Santa Cruz do Bispo, <a href="/wiki/Perafita" title="Perafita">Perafita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lavra" title="Lavra">Lavra</a> (in Matosinhos); Aveleda and Vilar do Pinheiro (Vila do Conde); and Vila Nova da Telha and <a href="/wiki/Moreira_(Maia)" title="Moreira (Maia)">Moreira</a> (Maia).<sup id="cite_ref-PDA1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDA1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It includes an area of between 72 metres (236&#160;ft) in the extreme south and 43 metres (141&#160;ft) in the north.<sup id="cite_ref-PDA1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDA1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The southern portion of the airport intersects the hydrographic watershed of the Le\xc3\xa7a River, while the north is crossed by effluents of Onda River.<sup id="cite_ref-PDA1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDA1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport around Porto opened in 1945 and was initially known as <i>Pedras Rubras Airport</i>, after the name for the locality where the airport is located: <a href="/wiki/Pedras_Rubras" title="Pedras Rubras">Pedras Rubras</a> ("red rocks"). It is still known by this name in the region. The land on which the airport was built was originally agricultural, characterised by rich soils that permitted the cultivation of various cereals.<sup id="cite_ref-PDA1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PDA1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was renamed in 1990 after former Portuguese prime minister, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_de_S%C3%A1_Carneiro" title="Francisco de S\xc3\xa1 Carneiro">Francisco de S\xc3\xa1 Carneiro</a>, who died in a plane crash when he was traveling to this airport on 4 December 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-porto-airport-car-rental.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-porto-airport-car-rental.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Along with the airports in Lisbon, Faro, Ponta Delgada, Santa Maria, Horta, Flores, Madeira, and Porto Santo, the airport\'s concessions to provide support to civil aviation were conceded to <a href="/wiki/ANA_Aeroportos_de_Portugal" title="ANA Aeroportos de Portugal">ANA Aeroportos de Portugal</a> on 18 December 1998, under provisions of decree 404/98. With this concession, ANA became responsible for the planning, development and construction of future infrastructure.<sup id="cite_ref-porto-airport-car-rental.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-porto-airport-car-rental.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A new terminal building, designed by Portuguese firm ICQ, was built between 2003 and 2006, and became operational in the last quarter of 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-www.airport-technology.com/projects/oporto/_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-www.airport-technology.com/projects/oporto/-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Porto Airport reached ten million passenger per year for the first time on 6 December 2017.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a The station has three platforms and the trains leave the arrival platform and reverse into one of the departure platforms.\n</p><p>The service links the airport to Porto city center and by transfer in <i>Trindade</i> station to high-speed trains at <a href="/wiki/Esta%C3%A7%C3%A3o_de_Porto-Campanh%C3%A3" class="mw-redirect" title="Esta\xc3\xa7\xc3\xa3o de Porto-Campanh\xc3\xa3">Campanh\xc3\xa3</a>, and other urban centres of Greater Porto: in <i>Verdes</i> station to Vila do Conde and <a href="/wiki/P%C3%B3voa_de_Varzim" title="P\xc3\xb3voa de Varzim">P\xc3\xb3voa de Varzim</a> (using line B), <i>Fonte do Cuco</i> station to <a href="/wiki/Maia_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Maia (city)">Maia</a> (line C), <i>Senhora da Hora</i> station to Matosinhos (line A), and <i>Trindade</i> station to V.N.Gaia (line D) and to Rio Tinto/F\xc3\xa2nzeres (line F).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>S\xc3\xa1 Carneiro airport is accessible via the A41 and <a href="/wiki/A28_motorway_(Portugal)" title="A28 motorway (Portugal)">A28</a> motorways, but also the EN13 highway (using the EN107 accessway). These roadways lead to drop-off and pick-up areas and short and long-stay car parks. It can also be reached by the <a href="/wiki/A4_motorway_(Portugal)" title="A4 motorway (Portugal)">A4</a> motorway through the VRI accessway.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Sociedade_de_Transportes_Colectivos_do_Porto" title="Sociedade de Transportes Colectivos do Porto">STCP</a> buses also link the airport and the city. There is also a bus that operates all night from Porto city centre to the airport. Also there is a bus service to/from <a href="/wiki/Vigo" title="Vigo">Vigo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a>/Spain) twice a day on weekdays, and once a day during the weekend.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shuttle">Shuttle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Shuttle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The GetBUS shuttle provides 50 min direct connections to the towns of Braga and Guimar\xc3\xa3es. Tickets can be bought in advance on the shuttle\'s website.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_proposals">Further proposals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Further proposals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The proposed Porto\xe2\x80\x93Vigo high-speed rail line would be built via the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porto_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Airports_Council_International" title="Airports Council International">Airports Council International</a> Airport Service Quality Awards voted the airport <i>Best Airport in Europe</i> in 2007. Additionally, it has placed in the top three of <i>Best Airport in Europe</i> a further nine times \xe2\x80\x93 winning second place in 2010, and third place in 2006, 2008, 2009, 2011, 2013, 2014, 2015, and 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a
