Directive</b> 2011/83/EU<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Consumer_protection" title="Consumer protection">consumer protection</a> class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consumer_Rights_Directive_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Directive applies to most contracts between traders and consumers<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and applied to all contracts concluded after 13 June 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Exceptions include financial services, gambling, healthcare by regulated professionals, package travel,<sup id="cite_ref-abta_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abta-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> property transactions, social services, timeshare<sup id="cite_ref-abta_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abta-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and most aspects of passenger transport. \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consumer_Rights_Directive_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Consumer Rights Directive contains provisions on:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Information to be given before a consumer buys goods or services on the trader\xe2\x80\x99s premises</li>\n<li>Information to be given before a consumer buys goods or services away from the trader\'s premises (e.g. at home or at a fair), or at a distance (internet, telesales)</li>\n<li>Cancellation rights and responsibilities where the consumer buys goods or services away from the trader\'s premises or at a distance</li>\n<li>Delivery times for goods \xe2\x80\x93 clarifying what deadlines for delivery should be and where responsibilities lie if there is a problem</li>\n<li>Post-contract customer helplines, where existing customers must be charged no more than the basic rate for phone calls<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Additional payments (on top of the main price of a purchase) which would need to have active or express consent of the consumer. An example is that pre-ticked boxes which the consumer must \'untick\' will no longer be allowed</li>\n<li>Fees charged for a particular method of payment (e.g. credit card surcharges).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Implementation">Implementation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consumer_Rights_Directive_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Implementation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Rights_Act_2015" title="Consumer Rights Act 2015">Consumer Rights Act 2015</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_contract_law" title="English contract law">English contract law</a></div>\n<p>The UK government held a consultation in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The new laws overhaul a number of consumer protection measures originally enacted long before the rise of internet shopping<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and fit together with a number of other changes<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> to form a new Consumer Bill of Rights replacing more than a dozen older, often overlapping and inconsistent laws.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\nOutdated Spanish mortgage laws have already been shown to be in breach of the new rules and must conform.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
