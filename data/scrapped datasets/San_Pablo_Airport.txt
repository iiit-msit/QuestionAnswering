code">IATA</a>: <b>SVQ</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LEZL</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (<a sixth busiest inland <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> in Spain. It is the main <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> serving Western <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and neighbouring provinces. The airport has flight connections to 42 destinations around Europe and Northern Africa, and handled 7,544,473 passengers in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It serves as a base for the low-cost carriers <a href="/wiki/Vueling_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Vueling Airlines">Vueling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is 10 kilometres (6.2&#160;mi) east of central <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, and some 110 kilometres (68&#160;mi) north-east of <a href="/wiki/Costa_de_la_Luz" title="Costa de la Luz">Costa de la Luz</a>.\n</p><p>The Seville class="toclevel-1 tocsection-12"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">9</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seville_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Seville Airport is capable of handling six million passengers a year. There are 23 stands (all of them are self-maneuvering) of which 16 are remote, with 42 check-in desks and 16 boarding gates. Since its last enlargement in 1991 for the <a href="/wiki/Seville_Expo_%2792" title="Seville Expo &#39;92">Seville Expo \'92</a>, the airport has undergone minor extension works. 2013 saw the opening of a new car parking building with five floors. It is currently being remodeled to expand its capacity to ten million passengers a year, completion of the works by 2021.\n</p><p>In the airport grounds there is an Airbus factory (San Pablo Sur), an Airbus maintenance center (San Pablo Norte) and a Ryanair maintenance center.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seville_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Seville_Airport&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">April 2013</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>In 1914, the first plane flying between the peninsula and <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> landed at the improvised aerodrome of Tablada, which had been fitted out the previous year for an air festival. Following this, the municipal government of <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> handed over a plot of land measuring 240,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (2,600,000&#160;sq&#160;ft) to the Military Aeronautical Society for the construction of an aerodrome. Work on the aerodrome began in 1915 and that same year it began to be used for training pilots and observers.\n</p><p>In 1919, the first commercial flights were operated between Seville and Madrid. The following year, an <a href="/wiki/Airmail" title="Airmail">air postal service</a> was established between Seville and <a href="/wiki/Larache" title="Larache">Larache</a> and in 1921, the first Spanish commercial service between Seville and Larache was set up. In 1923, various facilities such as <a href="/wiki/Hangar" title="Hangar">hangars</a>, workshops and premises were opened and approval was given for the construction of a municipal airport in Tablada at one end of the military aerodrome airfield, measuring 750 by 500&#160;m (2,460 by 1,640&#160;ft).\n</p><p>In April 1927, Uni\xc3\xb3n A\xc3\xa9rea Espa\xc3\xb1ola established the air service Madrid-Seville-Lisbon. In February 1929, the Seville airport project was approved and in March, the Tablada aerodrome was opened to flights and air traffic. It was decided that this service would cease once the planned airport was constructed.\n</p><p>In 1929, the first flight was operated between Madrid and Seville and in 1930, this was extended to the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>. In February 1931, the service between Berlin and Barcelona was extended to Seville. In December 1933, <a href="/wiki/LAPE" title="LAPE">LAPE</a> began a service between Seville and the Canary Islands.\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_civil_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish civil war">Spanish civil war</a>, Seville became the arrival point for African troops, whilst <a href="/wiki/Iberia_(airline)" title="Iberia (airline)">Iberia</a> served air transport with flights between <a href="/wiki/Tetu%C3%A1n" class="mw-redirect" title="Tetu\xc3\xa1n">Tetu\xc3\xa1n</a>-Seville-<a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria</a>, Seville-<a href="/wiki/Salamanca" title="Salamanca">Salamanca</a> and Seville-Larache-<a href="/wiki/Las_Palmas" title="Las Palmas">Las Palmas</a>.\n</p><p>In September 1945, works began on the new Seville transoceanic airport in the land area that occupied the old blimp mooring station, which received the last flight in 1936.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The works started with construction of runways 05/23, 02/20 and 09/27. One year later, it was classified as a customs point and runways 05/23 and 02/20 were asphalted. In 1948, a goniometer was installed, the runway lighting was completed, and the runways became known as 04/22, 18/36 and 09/27. In 1956, runway 09/27 was extended and runway 18/36 became a taxiway. Tablada was relegated to serve as a military aerodrome, until its closure in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1957, works were carried out on the terminal building and the control tower. Seville Airport was then included in the Spanish American Agreement for the installation of a supplies base. The facilities were developed near the threshold of 04, rendering the runway out of service.\n</p><p>In 1965, an Instrument Landing System was installed.\nBetween 1971 and 1975, the terminal area was renovated, the apron was extended, a new terminal building was constructed and new access roads were developed.\n</p><p>In 1989, with a focus on the <a href="/wiki/Seville_Expo_%2792" title="Seville Expo &#39;92">Seville Expo \'92</a>, the apron was extended, and a new access from the national <a href="/wiki/Carretera_Nacional_N-IV" title="Carretera Nacional N-IV">motorway N-IV</a> was opened; a new terminal building and a new control tower to the south of the runway were also built. The old terminal was repurposed as a cargo terminal. On 31 July, the new installations were inaugurated.\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<p>The new terminal expansion program was begun in 2019 to cope with rapid passenger growth and increase its capacity to 10 million passenger by year.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
