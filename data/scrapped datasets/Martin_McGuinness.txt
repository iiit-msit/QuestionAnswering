Irish presidential election">2011 Irish presidential election</a>.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>James Martin Pacelli McGuinness</b> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">S\xc3\xa9amus M\xc3\xa1irt\xc3\xadn Pacelli Mag Aonghusa</i>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 23 May 1950 \xe2\x80\x93 21 March 2017) was an <a href="/wiki/Irish_republican" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish republican">Irish republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a> politician and a former leader within the <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Irish_Republican_Army" title="Provisional Irish Republican Army">Provisional Irish Republican Army</a> (IRA). McGuinness was the <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="First Minister and deputy First Minister">deputy First Minister of Northern Ireland</a> from May 2007 to January 2017<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\nand was the <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament_(United_Kingdom)" title="Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)">MP</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mid_Ulster_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Mid Ulster (UK Parliament constituency)">Mid Ulster</a> from <a href="/wiki/1997_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1997 United Kingdom general election">1997</a> until his resignation in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Like all Sinn F\xc3\xa9in MPs, McGuinness <a href="/wiki/Abstentionism" title="Abstentionism">abstained</a> from participation in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Westminster Parliament</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/St_Andrews_Agreement" title="St Andrews Agreement">St Andrews Agreement</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2007_Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election" title="2007 Northern Ireland Assembly election">Assembly election in 2007</a>, he became deputy First Minister of Northern Ireland on 8 May 2007, with the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a> (DUP) leader <a href="/wiki/Ian_Paisley" title="Ian Paisley">Ian Paisley</a> becoming First Minister. On 5 June 2008, he was re-appointed as deputy First Minister to serve alongside <a href="/wiki/Peter_Robinson_(Northern_Ireland_politician)" title="Peter Robinson (Northern Ireland politician)">Peter Robinson</a>, who succeeded Paisley as First Minister.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> McGuinness previously served as Minister of Education in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> between 1999 and 2002. He was Sinn F\xc3\xa9in\'s candidate for <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President of Ireland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Irish_presidential_election" title="2011 Irish presidential election">2011 election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Working alongside US Special Envoy <a href="/wiki/George_J._Mitchell" title="George J. Mitchell">George Mitchell</a>, McGuinness was also one of the main architects of the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a> which formally cemented the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_peace_process" title="Northern Ireland peace process">Northern Ireland peace process</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 9 January 2017, McGuinness resigned as deputy First Minister in a protest over the <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Heat_Incentive_scandal" title="Renewable Heat Incentive scandal">Renewable Heat Incentive scandal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Resignation_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Resignation-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He announced on 19 January  that he would not be standing for re-election to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2017_Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election" title="2017 Northern Ireland Assembly election">2 March 2017 election</a> due to ill health. He reportedly suffered from <a href="/wiki/Amyloidosis" title="Amyloidosis">amyloidosis</a>, a condition that attacks the <a href="/wiki/Vital_organs" class="mw-redirect" title="Vital organs">vital organs</a>, and retired shortly before his death on 21 March 2017, aged 66.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_McGuinness&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Provisional IRA activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>McGuinness acknowledged that he was a former IRA member, but stated that he left the IRA in 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He originally joined the <a href="/wiki/Official_IRA" class="mw-redirect" title="Official IRA">Official IRA</a>, unaware of the split at the December 1969 Army Convention, switching to the <a href="/wiki/Provisional_IRA" class="mw-redirect" title="Provisional IRA">Provisional IRA</a> soon after. By the start of 1972, at the age of 21, he was second-in-command of the IRA in <a href="/wiki/Derry" title="Derry">Derry</a>, a position he held at the time of <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Sunday_(1972)" title="Bloody Sunday (1972)">Bloody Sunday</a>, when 14 civil rights protesters were killed in the city by British soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Battalion,_Parachute_Regiment" title="1st Battalion, Parachute Regiment">1st Battalion, Parachute Regiment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Saville_Inquiry" class="mw-redirect" title="Saville Inquiry">Saville Inquiry</a> into the events of that day, Paddy Ward stated he had been the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Fianna_%C3%89ireann" title="Fianna \xc3\x89ireann">Fianna</a>, the youth wing of the IRA at the time of Bloody Sunday. He said that McGuinness and an anonymous IRA member gave him bomb parts that morning. He said that his organisation intended to attack city centre premises in Derry on the same day. In response, McGuinness said the statements were "fantasy", while Gear\xc3\xb3id \xc3\x93 hE\xc3\xa1ra (formerly Gerry O\'Hara), a Derry <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a> councillor, stated that he and not Ward was the Fianna leader at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The inquiry concluded that, although McGuinness was "engaged in paramilitary activity" at the time of Bloody Sunday and had probably been armed with a <a href="/wiki/Thompson_submachine_gun" title="Thompson submachine gun">Thompson submachine gun</a>, there was insufficient evidence to make any finding other than they were "sure that he did not engage in any activity that provided any of the soldiers with any justification for opening fire".<sup id="cite_ref-SavilleMcGuinness3119_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SavilleMcGuinness3119-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>McGuinness negotiated alongside <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Adams" title="Gerry Adams">Gerry Adams</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Whitelaw" class="mw-redirect" title="William Whitelaw">Willie Whitelaw</a>, in 1972. In 1973, he was convicted by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Special_Criminal_Court" title="Special Criminal Court">Special Criminal Court</a>, after being arrested near a car containing 250 pounds (110&#160;kg) of explosives and nearly 5,000 rounds of ammunition. He refused to recognise the court, and was sentenced to six months\' imprisonment. In court, he declared his membership of the Provisional IRA without equivocation: "We have fought against the killing of our people... I am a member of <a href="/wiki/%C3%93glaigh_na_h%C3%89ireann" title="\xc3\x93glaigh na h\xc3\x89ireann">\xc3\x93glaigh na h\xc3\x89ireann</a> and very, very proud of it".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After his release, and another conviction in the Republic of Ireland for IRA membership,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (July 2019)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> he became increasingly prominent in Sinn F\xc3\xa9in, the political wing of the <a href="/wiki/Republican_movement_(Ireland)" title="Republican movement (Ireland)">republican movement</a>. He was in indirect contact with British intelligence during the <a href="/wiki/1981_Irish_hunger_strike" title="1981 Irish hunger strike">1981 hunger strikes</a>, and again in the early 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly_(1982)" title="Northern Ireland Assembly (1982)">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> at <a href="/wiki/Parliament_Building_of_Northern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliament Building of Northern Ireland">Stormont</a> in 1982, representing <a href="/wiki/Londonderry_(Assembly_constituency)" title="Londonderry (Assembly constituency)">Londonderry</a>. He was the second candidate elected after <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">Social Democratic and Labour Party</a> (SDLP) leader <a href="/wiki/John_Hume" title="John Hume">John Hume</a>. As with all elected members of Sinn F\xc3\xa9in and the SDLP, he did not take up his seat.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On 9 December 1982, McGuinness, Gerry Adams and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Morrison_(writer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Danny Morrison (writer)">Danny Morrison</a> were banned from entering Great Britain under the <a href="/wiki/Prevention_of_Terrorism_Act_(Northern_Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Prevention of Terrorism Act (Northern Ireland)">Prevention of Terrorism Act</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a>, William Whitelaw.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 1993, he was the subject of a two-part special by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cook_Report" title="The Cook Report">The Cook Report</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/ITV_Central" title="ITV Central">Central TV</a> investigative documentary series presented by <a href="/wiki/Roger_Cook_(journalist)" title="Roger Cook (journalist)">Roger Cook</a>. It accused him of continuing involvement in IRA activity, of attending an interrogation and of encouraging Frank Hegarty, an informer, to return to Derry from a <a href="/wiki/Safe_house" title="Safe house">safe house</a> in England. Hegarty\'s mother Rose appeared on the programme to tell of telephone calls to McGuinness and of Hegarty\'s subsequent murder. McGuinness denied her account and denounced the programme saying "I have never been in the IRA. I don\'t have any sway over the IRA".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Michael_McDowell_(politician)" title="Michael McDowell (politician)">Michael McDowell</a>, the Irish <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A1naiste" title="T\xc3\xa1naiste">T\xc3\xa1naiste</a>, stated McGuinness, along with Gerry Adams and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Ferris" title="Martin Ferris">Martin Ferris</a>, were members of the seven-man <a href="/wiki/IRA_Army_Council" title="IRA Army Council">IRA Army Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> McGuinness denied this, saying he was no longer an IRA member. Experienced <a href="/wiki/The_Troubles" title="The Troubles">Troubles</a> journalist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Taylor_(journalist)" title="Peter Taylor (journalist)">Peter Taylor</a> presented further apparent evidence of McGuinness\'s role in the IRA in his documentary <i>Age of Terror</i>, shown in April 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In his documentary, Taylor alleges that McGuinness was the head of the <a href="/wiki/IRA%27s_Northern_Command" class="mw-redirect" title="IRA&#39;s Northern Command">IRA\'s Northern Command</a> and had advance knowledge of the IRA\'s 1987 <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Day_bombing" title="Remembrance Day bombing">Remembrance Day bombing</a>, which left 12 people dead.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chief_negotiator_and_Minister_of_Education">Chief negotiator and Minister of Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_McGuinness&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chief negotiator and Minister of Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He became Sinn F\xc3\xa9in\'s chief negotiator in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_peace_process" title="Northern Ireland peace process">Northern Ireland peace process</a> negotiations which led to the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a>.  At the time of his death, former US President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> noted that McGuinness was the one who personally oversaw the agreement\'s arms <a href="/wiki/Decommissioning_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Decommissioning in Northern Ireland">decommissioning phase</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  Former British Prime Minister Tony Blair also acknowledged the leading role which McGuinness had in ensuring the agreement would be enforced.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Forum" title="Northern Ireland Forum">Northern Ireland Forum</a> in 1996 representing <a href="/wiki/Foyle_(Assembly_constituency)" title="Foyle (Assembly constituency)">Foyle</a>. Having contested <a href="/wiki/Foyle_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Foyle (UK Parliament constituency)">Foyle</a> unsuccessfully at the 1983, 1987 and 1992 Westminster elections,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> he became MP for <a href="/wiki/Mid_Ulster_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Mid Ulster (UK Parliament constituency)">Mid Ulster</a> in 1997 and after the agreement was concluded, was returned as a member of the Assembly for the <a href="/wiki/Mid_Ulster_(Assembly_constituency)" title="Mid Ulster (Assembly constituency)">same constituency</a>. He was nominated by his party for a ministerial position in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">power-sharing executive</a>, where he became Minister of Education. One of his acts as Minister of Education was his decision to scrap the <a href="/wiki/Eleven_plus_exam" class="mw-redirect" title="Eleven plus exam">11-plus exam</a>, which he had failed as a child.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He was re-elected to the Westminster Parliament in 2001, 2005 and 2010.\n</p><p>In May 2003, transcripts of telephone calls between McGuinness and British officials including <a href="/wiki/Mo_Mowlam" title="Mo Mowlam">Mo Mowlam</a>, the then Secretary of State for Northern Ireland, and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Powell_(Labour_adviser)" title="Jonathan Powell (Labour adviser)">Jonathan Powell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a>\'s Chief of Staff, were published in a biography of McGuinness entitled <i>From Guns to Government</i> by Kathryn Johnston and Liam Clarke. The tapes had been made by <a href="/wiki/MI5" title="MI5">MI5</a> and the authors of the book were arrested under the <a href="/wiki/Official_Secrets_Act" title="Official Secrets Act">Official Secrets Act</a>. The conversations showed an easy and friendly relationship between McGuinness and Powell. He joked with Powell about <a href="/wiki/Unionism_in_Ireland" title="Unionism in Ireland">unionist</a> MPs while Mowlam referred to him as "babe" and discussed her difficulties with Blair. Bush">George W. Bush</a> in the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> with the Northern Ireland First Minister Ian Paisley, McGuinness said to the press, "Up until the 26 March this year, Ian Paisley and I never had a conversation about anything\xe2\x80\x94not even about the weather\xe2\x80\x94and now we have worked very closely together over the last seven&#160;months and there\'s been no angry words between us.... This shows we are set for a new course."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_Irish_presidential_campaign">2011 Irish presidential campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_McGuinness&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011 Irish presidential campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2011_Irish_presidential_election" title="2011 Irish presidential election">2011 Irish presidential election</a></div>\n<p>On 16 September 2011 McGuinness was announced as the Sinn F\xc3\xa9in candidate in the 2011 Irish presidential election.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In the election held on 27 October, McGuinness placed third in the first preference vote, behind <a href="/wiki/Michael_D._Higgins" title="Michael D. Higgins">Michael D. Higgins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Se%C3%A1n_Gallagher" title="Se\xc3\xa1n Gallagher">Se\xc3\xa1n Gallagher</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>McGuinness was the only candidate ineligible to vote in the election as, although an <a href="/wiki/Irish_citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish citizen">Irish citizen</a>, he was not ordinarily resident in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Following his defeat, McGuinness formally returned to the role of deputy First Minister of Northern Ireland on 31 October.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resignation_from_the_House_of_Commons">Resignation from the House of Commons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_McGuinness&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Resignation from the House of Commons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 30 December 2012 McGuinness announced that he had formally resigned as the MP for Mid-Ulster stating "I have served formal notice of my resignation from the position of MP for Mid-Ulster with immediate effect. This is in line with my party\'s commitment to end <a href="/wiki/Double_jobbing" class="mw-redirect" title="Double jobbing">double jobbing</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> To do this, he was made <a href="/wiki/Steward_of_the_Manor_of_Northstead" class="mw-redirect" title="Steward of the Manor of Northstead">Steward of the Manor of Northstead</a> on 2 January 2013 by Chancellor <a href="/wiki/George_Osborne" title="George Osborne">George Osborne</a>, making him an employee of the Crown and thus ineligible for membership of the House of Commons.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resignation_as_deputy_First_Minister">Resignation as deputy First Minister</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_McGuinness&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Resignation as deputy First Minister">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Heat_Incentive_scandal" title="Renewable Heat Incentive scandal">Renewable Heat Incentive scandal</a></div>\n<p>In November 2016, a scandal came to light surrounding the Renewable Heat Incentive, an energy incentive championed by <a href="/wiki/Arlene_Foster" title="Arlene Foster">Arlene Foster</a> when she was <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Economy" title="Department for the Economy">Minister for Enterprise, Trade, and Incentives</a> in 2012. The incentive would cost the Northern Ireland Executive \xc2\xa3480m over 20 years, and was marred by allegations of fraud, which were not acknowledged or acted upon by Foster or the DUP.<sup id="cite_ref-whistleblower_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whistleblower-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> McGuinness and others in Sinn F\xc3\xa9in called for Foster (now First Minister) to step aside to allow for independent inquiries, but Foster refused.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Foster made a statement before the assembly on 19 December without McGuinness\' approval (as required under the power-sharing agreement), resulting in Sinn F\xc3\xa9in and the opposition parties all walking out of the assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sinn F\xc3\xa9in President <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Adams" title="Gerry Adams">Gerry Adams</a> indicated on 8 January 2017 that McGuinness could resign, thus vacating both his and Foster\'s offices, if Foster did not agree to temporarily step aside to allow an independent inquiry.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> McGuinness resigned the following day; in his statements to the press, he said "Today is the right time to call a halt to the DUP\'s arrogance", and said that Foster had a "clear conflict of interest" in the affair.<sup id="cite_ref-Resignation_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Resignation-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Another reason cited for his resignation was the decision by DUP <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Communities" title="Department for Communities">Minister for Communities</a> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Givan" title="Paul Givan">Paul Givan</a> to remove \xc2\xa350,000 in funding from the <a href="/wiki/L%C3%ADofa" title="L\xc3\xadofa">L\xc3\xadofa</a> Gaeltacht Bursary scheme, a yearly programme that allowed 100 school-age children to travel to the <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht#Donegal_Gaeltacht" title="Gaeltacht">Donegal Gaeltacht</a> to learn the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-liofa_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-liofa-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Sinn F\xc3\xa9in refused to nominate a successor to McGuinness before 16 January, resulting in the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Brokenshire" title="James Brokenshire">James Brokenshire</a>, calling for <a href="/wiki/2017_Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election" title="2017 Northern Ireland Assembly election">new elections</a> scheduled for 2 March.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> McGuinness subsequently announced that he would not run for re-election, due to ill health.<sup id="cite_ref-Dominiczak_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dominiczak-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_McGuinness&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>One of McGuinness\'s middle names, Pacelli, is after <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_XII" title="Pope Pius XII">Pope Pius XII</a> (Eugenio Pacelli).<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>McGuinness attended St. Eugene\'s Primary School and later the Christian Brothers technical college, leaving school at the age of 15.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>McGuinness married Bernadette Canning in 1974; they had four children, two girls and two boys.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He was a fan of the <a href="/wiki/Derry_GAA" title="Derry GAA">Derry</a> <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_football" title="Gaelic football">Gaelic football</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurling" title="Hurling">hurling</a> teams<sup id="cite_ref-indep_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-indep-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> and played both sports when he was younger.<sup id="cite_ref-indep_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-indep-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> He grew up just 50 metres from <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Park,_Derry" class="mw-redirect" title="Celtic Park, Derry">Celtic Park</a>, the home of Derry\'s <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_Athletic_Association" title="Gaelic Athletic Association">Gaelic Athletic Association</a> (GAA).<sup id="cite_ref-indep_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-indep-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> His older brother <a href="/wiki/Tom_McGuinness_(Gaelic_footballer)" title="Tom McGuinness (Gaelic footballer)">Tom</a> played Gaelic football for Derry.<sup id="cite_ref-indep_56-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-indep-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> He supported <a href="/wiki/Derry_City_F.C." title="Derry City F.C.">Derry City F.C.</a> where his younger brother Paul played for the Candystripes.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>McGuinness supported <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> from the age of eight.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> McGuinness also had an interest in <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> \xe2\x80\x93 sometimes extending his support to the <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England cricket team</a>, as well as that of <a href="/wiki/Ireland_cricket_team" title="Ireland cricket team">Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2019, McGuinness was posthumously awarded a certificate of honour by mayor of San Francisco <a href="/wiki/London_Breed" title="London Breed">London Breed</a> for his "courageous service in the military." The nomination had been made by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Irish_Societies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="United Irish Societies (page does not exist)">United Irish Societies</a> who had appointed him honorary marshal in the <a href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_Day_parade" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Patrick&#39;s Day parade">St. Patrick\'s Day parade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Breed apologized two days later following controversy.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_concerns_and_death">Health concerns and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
