headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a></div></div></div>\n<p><b>DR</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[de\xcb\x80\xc3\xa6\xc9\x90\xcc\xaf]</a></span>; formerly <b>Danmarks Radio</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> officially the <b>Danish Broadcasting Corporation</b> in English,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_service" title="Public service">public-service</a> <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> broadcasting company.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Founded in 1925 as a public-service organization, it is Denmark\'s oldest and largest electronic media enterprise. DR is a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Broadcasting_Union" title="European Broadcasting Union">European Broadcasting Union</a>.\n</p><p>DR is funded by a <a href="/wiki/Television_licence" title="Television licence">media licence</a> which is charged to all Danish households and companies with television sets, computers, smartphones and other devices with internet access.\n</p><p>Today, DR operates six television channels, all of which are distributed <a href="/wiki/Free-to-air" title="Free-to-air">free-to-air</a> via a nationwide <a href="/wiki/DVB-T" title="DVB-T">DVB-T</a> network.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> DR also operates eight radio channels. All are available nationally on <a href="/wiki/Digital_Audio_Broadcast" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital Audio Broadcast">DAB+ radio</a> and online, with the four original stations also available on <a href="/wiki/FM_radio" class="mw-redirect" title="FM radio">FM radio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DR_(broadcaster)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>DR was founded on 1 April 1925 under the name of <i>Radioordningen</i>, which was changed to <i>Statsradiofonien</i> in 1926, then to <i>Danmarks Radio</i> in 1959,<sup id="cite_ref-um-massmedia_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-um-massmedia-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and to <i>DR</i> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During the German <a href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupation of Denmark">occupation of Denmark</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, radio broadcasts were censored \xe2\x80\x93 under particularly harsh conditions from August 1943 \xe2\x80\x93 leading many Danes to turn to Danish-language broadcasts from the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> or the illegal press,<sup id="cite_ref-befrielsen1945_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-befrielsen1945-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> as well as Swedish radio in 1944\xe2\x80\x931945.<sup id="cite_ref-um-massmedia_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-um-massmedia-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Statsradiofonien\'s second FM radio station, Program 2 (P2), was added in 1951, followed by P3 in 1963.\n</p><p>Experimental television broadcasts started in 1949, with regular programming beginning on 2 October 1951 with the launch of Denmark\'s first television channel. Daily programming began in 1954.<sup id="cite_ref-um-massmedia_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-um-massmedia-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Colour television test broadcasts were started in March 1967, with the first large-scale colour broadcasting occurring for the <a href="/wiki/1968_Winter_Olympics" title="1968 Winter Olympics">1968 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Grenoble" title="Grenoble">Grenoble</a>, France.<sup id="cite_ref-colour_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colour-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Danmarks Radio officially ended its "test" transmissions of colour television on 1 April 1970, although it wasn\'t until 1978 that the organisation\'s last black-and-white television programme (<i>TV Avisen</i>\xe2\x80\x94The News) went over to colour.<sup id="cite_ref-colour_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colour-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At 14.00 local time on 16 May 1983  DR launched its first <a href="/wiki/Teletext" title="Teletext">teletext</a> information service, which is still available on all DR channels.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Danmarks Radio\'s monopoly on national television lasted until 1988, when <a href="/wiki/TV_2_(Denmark)" title="TV 2 (Denmark)">TV 2</a> started broadcasting.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 8 years later DR launched their second television channel, <a href="/wiki/DR2" title="DR2">DR2</a>, on 30 August 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It was sometimes called <i>den hemmelige kanal</i> ("the secret channel") in its early years because it could not be seen nationwide at its launch.\n</p><p>The first trials of <a href="/wiki/Digital_Audio_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital Audio Broadcasting">DAB</a> were carried out in 1995,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> with eight channels officially launching in October 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-dr2000-_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dr2000--15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 7 June 2007 DR launched an online-only news channel <a href="/wiki/DR_Update" title="DR Update">DR Update</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It was later added as a traditional channel. With the switch to over-the-air digital signals on 1 November 2009, DR added three new channels to its lineup<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/DR_K" title="DR K">DR K</a> - an intercultural, documentary and "odd-film" channel.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/DR_HD" title="DR HD">DR HD</a> \xe2\x80\x93 Denmark\'s first <a href="/wiki/Free-to-air" title="Free-to-air">free-to-air</a> <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high-definition</a> channel intended to air successful shows from the other DR channels in true HD only, with no upscaling.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/DR_Ramasjang" title="DR Ramasjang">DR Ramasjang</a>, a children\'s channel.</li></ul>\n<p>In 2013 a new logo in which the letters "DR" featured in a white <a href="/wiki/Sans-serif" title="Sans-serif">sans-serif</a> font on a black background was introduced, and the line-up of television channels was changed once again. A new channel targeting young people, <a href="/wiki/DR3" title="DR3">DR3</a> replaced DR HD. Another channel for children, <a href="/wiki/DR_Ultra" title="DR Ultra">DR Ultra</a> replaced DR Update. The closure of DR Update was the start of a revamping of <a href="/wiki/DR_2" class="mw-redirect" title="DR 2">DR 2</a> as a channel for news and society.\n</p><p>A nationwide switch from DAB to the newer DAB+ format took place on 1 October 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> All of DR\'s stations plus the privately owned, public service channel - Radio24Syv, moved to the second national DAB+ multiplex (DAB-blok 2).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Logo_history">Logo history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-traditional">\n\t\t<li class="gallerybox" style="width: 155px"><div style="width: 155px">\n\t\t\t<div class="thumb" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007 approximately 180,000 households did not pay media licence.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a years starting in 2019 through 2022 the media licence will be replaced by general taxation, as announced on 16 March 2018 by a majority in the <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">Danish Parliament</a> consisting of <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" class="mw-redirect" title="Conservative In response, DR set up a "watchdog committee" intended to detect and report upon any bias. Members of the watchdog committee are unknown.\n</p><p>The first large-scale scientific content analysis of political news coverage on DR published by the <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Journalism_(University_of_Southern_Denmark)" title="Centre for Journalism (University of Southern Denmark)">Centre for Journalism</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_Denmark" title="University of Southern Denmark">University of Southern Denmark</a>, studying election news coverage in the years 1994\xe2\x80\x932007, documented no persistent political bias towards either the left or the right.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> News coverage of political actors and parties was found to be largely similar to the news coverage on DR\'s competitor <a href="/wiki/TV_2_(Denmark)" title="TV 2 (Denmark)">TV 2</a>. The study concluded that political news coverage on both broadcasters was guided by journalistic professional criteria as to the newsworthiness of political actors and political issues, not by
