href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[bwelin]</a></span><small>, stressing the <i>e</i></small>; <a href="/wiki/Bolsa_de_Madrid" title="Bolsa de Madrid">BMAD</a>:&#160;<a of airlines of Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_airline" class="mw-redirect" title="Low-cost airline">low-cost airline</a> based at <a href="/wiki/El_Prat_de_Llobregat" title="El Prat de title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It is the largest airline in Spain, measured by fleet size and number of destinations. Vueling serves over 100 destinations in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>. href="/w/index.php?title=Vueling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Vueling was established in February 10, 2004 and commenced operations on 1 July 2004 with a flight between <a href="/wiki/Barcelona_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Barcelona Airport">Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ibiza_Airport" title="Ibiza Airport">Ibiza</a>. The initial fleet consisted of two <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320</a> aircraft, based in Barcelona serving <a href="/wiki/Brussels_Airport" title="Brussels Airport">Brussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ibiza_Airport" title="Ibiza Airport">Ibiza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca_Airport" title="Palma de Mallorca Airport">Palma de Mallorca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle_Airport" title="Charles de Gaulle Airport">Paris-Charles de Gaulle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The name Vueling was formed by combining the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> word <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/vuelo#Noun" class="extiw" title="wikt:vuelo">vuelo</a></i> (flight) with the <a href="/wiki/English_gerund" class="mw-redirect" title="English gerund">English gerund</a> suffix <i><a href="/wiki/-ing" title="-ing">-ing</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Initially, major shareholders of Vueling Airlines were <a href="/wiki/Apax_Partners" title="Apax Partners">Apax Partners</a> (40%), Inversiones Hemisferio (<a href="/wiki/Grupo_Planeta" class="mw-redirect" title="Grupo Planeta">Grupo Planeta</a>) (30%), Vueling\'s management team (23%) and V.A. Investor (<a href="/wiki/JetBlue_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="JetBlue Airways">JetBlue Airways</a>) (7%). During its nascent stages, the company\'s general manager was L\xc3\xa1zaro Ros, while Carlos Mu\xc3\xb1oz was CEO. In November 2007, Vueling appointed managing director of <a href="/wiki/Spanair" title="Spanair">Spanair</a> Lars Nygaard as CEO to replace Carlos Mu\xc3\xb1oz, who remained a member of the Board of Directors.\n</p><p>Madrid was added as the airline\'s second base in 2005, followed by its first base outside Spain at Paris Charles de Gaulle in 2007. <a href="/wiki/San_Pablo_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="San Pablo Airport">Seville</a> followed in December 2009.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_concerns_and_management_re-shuffle">Financial concerns and management re-shuffle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vueling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Financial concerns and management re-shuffle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>2007 was a difficult year for Vueling; Apax Partners sold its then-21% stake in the carrier in June of that year, followed by two profit warnings issued in August and October. Two company directors and the chairman resigned shortly before the second profit warning, citing differences over commercial strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Shares in the company were also temporarily suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This led to <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Cassani" title="Barbara Cassani">Barbara Cassani</a>, former Chief Executive of UK low-cost airline <a href="/wiki/Go_Fly" class="mw-redirect" title="Go Fly">Go</a>, joining Vueling as chairman of the board in September 2007. The airline then embarked on a restructuring exercise and posted its first profit in mid-2009.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vueling_and_Clickair_merger">Vueling and Clickair merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vueling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vueling and Clickair merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In June 2008, Vueling and rival Spanish low-cost airline <a href="/wiki/Clickair" title="Clickair">Clickair</a> announced their intention to merge. The merger was designed to create a carrier better able to compete in the competitive Spanish airline market and mitigate high fuel costs with Iberia as the main industrial partner. While the new company would trade under the Vueling name, Clickair\'s Alex Cruz was named as chief executive.<sup id="cite_ref-merger_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merger-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-travelmole_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travelmole-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The deal was subject to scrutiny and approval by European competition regulators, who were concerned that the merged airline would have a significant competitive advantage on around 19 routes. The regulators demanded the release of slots at Barcelona and other European airports as a condition of the merger.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 15 July 2009 the merger of Vueling and <a href="/wiki/Clickair" title="Clickair">Clickair</a> was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-travelmole_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travelmole-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The new merged airline operates under the Vueling brand, with Clickair flights and aircraft re-branded under the Vueling name. It became the second largest Spanish carrier flying 8.2 million passengers in 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-pax_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pax-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> to almost 50 destinations.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Co-operation_with_MTV">Co-operation with MTV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Two of Vueling\'s A320 aircraft (EC-KDG<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and EC-KDH<sup id="cite_ref-eckdh_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eckdh-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>) were re-painted into MTV liveries with some MTV styling on-board too. The designs of both liveries were created by <a href="/wiki/Custo_Dalmau" title="Custo Dalmau">Custo Dalmau</a> and both liveries were removed at the end of 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In the summer season of 2010, EC-KDG<sup id="cite_ref-eckdh_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eckdh-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> had again been re-painted into an MTV livery, and in 2011 it was re-painted into a livery based on the <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a>; the livery has since been removed and co-operation with MTV has since ended.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vueling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Recent developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In November 2010, Vueling announced a new base at <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Toulouse Airport">Toulouse Airport</a> in France from April 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> followed in December 2010 by the announcement of a new base in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Airport_Schiphol" title="Amsterdam Airport Schiphol">Amsterdam</a>, also to open during April 2011. The Toulouse base opened on 23 April 2011, but has since closed.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2011, further expansion was announced with Vueling adding a further nine aircraft to its fleet, including <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A319" title="Airbus A319">Airbus A319</a> aircraft. Six Airbus A320s were delivered between April and June 2011, whilst the remaining two A320s were delivered by the end of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 21 March 2012, it was announced by CEO Alex Cruz that <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci%E2%80%93Fiumicino_Airport" title="Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport">Rome</a> would be added as a new base. The base launched on 25 March 2012 with one aircraft based there, the airline has since expanded at Rome with numerous new destinations.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On 5 December 2012, Vueling announced the opening of a new base of operations in <a href="/wiki/Florence_Airport,_Peretola" class="mw-redirect" title="Florence Airport, Peretola">Florence</a>, the carrier is to base one aircraft there and serve four new European destinations.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  Ten months later, on 25 October 2013, Vueling launched <a href="/wiki/Florence_Airport,_Peretola" class="mw-redirect" title="Florence Airport, Peretola">Florence</a>-Catania, its first domestic route in Italy.\n</p><p>Since November 2013, the airline has continued to expand from its <a href="/wiki/Airline_hub" title="Airline hub">hub</a> at Barcelona.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 6 November 2013, Vueling announced a new base with one aircraft in <a href="/wiki/Brussels_Airport" title="Brussels Airport">Brussels</a>, with seven new destinations from May 2014, in addition to the four previous routes from Brussels.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Previously sourced to an Internet Message Board (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Also in November 2013, Vueling announced an expansion of its base at <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_da_Vinci%E2%80%93Fiumicino_Airport" title="Leonardo da Vinci\xe2\x80\x93Fiumicino Airport">Rome-Fiumicino</a>. From mid-2014, 8 aircraft would be based there, operating more than 30 routes. This expansion meant Rome-Fiumicino would become Vueling\'s second hub, after Barcelona.\n</p><p>During the first weekend of July 2016, Vueling had many delays and cancellations, which resulted in an investigation by the Spanish authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> During the same month, Vueling cancelled all the flights to <a href="/wiki/Sheremetyevo_International_Airport" title="Sheremetyevo International Airport">Sheremetyevo International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vilnius_Airport" title="Vilnius Airport">Vilnius Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rabat%E2%80%93Sal%C3%A9_Airport" title="Rabat\xe2\x80\x93Sal\xc3\xa9 Airport">Rabat\xe2\x80\x93Sal\xc3\xa9 Airport</a>. Clients are able to get a refund or fly to the nearest airport where Vueling flies.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In October 2016, Vueling shut down their bases in Brussels, Catania and Palermo as part of restructuring measures.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2017, Vueling has cancelled its route from Barcelona to <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Airport" title="Frankfurt Airport">Frankfurt Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 29 December 2017, it was announced that IAG would acquire Austrian airline <a href="/wiki/Niki_(airline)" title="Niki (airline)">Niki</a> as a subsidiary for Vueling.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, Niki was later acquired by <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, the owner of <a href="/wiki/Laudamotion" class="mw-redirect" title="Laudamotion">Laudamotion</a>, with investment from <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> A few months after losing the bid for Niki, IAG instead established <a href="/wiki/Level_Europe" title="Level Europe">Anisec Luftfahrt</a> as a subsidiary of Vueling, operating as <a href="/wiki/Level_(airline)" class="mw-redirect" title="Level (airline)">LEVEL</a>, using four former Niki aircraft that had not been purchased by rival <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> (and leased to <a href="/wiki/Lauda_(airline)" title="Lauda (airline)">Lauda</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Vueling_Austria_Level_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vueling_Austria_Level-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2020, Vueling has announced new routes added linking <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris, France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dubrovnik" title="Dubrovnik">Dubrovnik, Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville, Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marrakesh" title="Marrakesh">Marrakech, Morocco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vueling_headquarters_el_Prat.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Vueling_headquarters_el_Prat.JPG/220px-Vueling_headquarters_el_Prat.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Vueling_headquarters_el_Prat.JPG/330px-Vueling_headquarters_el_Prat.JPG 1.5x, class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vueling_headquarters_el_Prat.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Vueling\'s head office in El Prat de Llobregat, Spain</div></div></div>\n<p>In November 2012, <a href="/wiki/International_Airlines_Group" title="International Airlines Group">International Airlines Group</a>, whose subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Iberia_(airline)" title="Iberia (airline)">Iberia</a> held a 45.85% stake in Vueling, offered to buy the remaining 54.15% of the company with both Iberia and IAG owning both shares and not resulting in the company being wholly owned by IAG through 100% of shares. IAG, also the owner of <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a>, plans to use Vueling to help stem losses at Iberia. However, market trends (increased profits and improved figures from Vueling resulting in a higher share-price) had made IAG\'s offer a significant undervaluation of the airline. Vueling had urged its shareholders to reject IAG\'s offer and its shareholders had until the 8th of April 2013 to decide upon the recommendation.<sup id="cite_ref-IAG_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IAG-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 27 March 2013, IAG improved its offer for Vueling, raising its offer per share from \xe2\x82\xac7 to \xe2\x82\xac9.25. Vueling shares quickly surged following the announcement, rising by 8.8% to \xe2\x82\xac9.23 following a temporary suspension as <a href="/wiki/Bolsa_de_Madrid" title="Bolsa de Madrid">BMAD</a> waited on an official comment from Vueling regarding the updated offer. The acceptance period was also increased by 48 calendar days.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 9 April 2013, the board of Vueling unanimously recommended shareholders accept an improved offer of \xe2\x82\xac9.25 per share from IAG. IAG CEO <a href="/wiki/Willie_Walsh_(Irish_businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Willie Walsh (Irish businessman)">Willie Walsh</a> confirmed that the board had recommended the new offer; however, Walsh also stated that Vueling would not be merged with Iberia, saying, "Vueling will operate as a stand-alone entity in IAG group."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 23 April 2013, IAG acquired control of Vueling, which saw the recently purchased 44.66% stake by IAG merged with Iberia\'s existing 45.85% stake to form a 90.51% shareholding. Vueling remains a standalone company now within the IAG, and its management structure is unchanged; however, Vueling\'s CEO reports directly to IAG CEO Willie Walsh.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Frequent_flyer_programme">Frequent flyer programme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vueling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Frequent flyer programme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Vueling\'s <a href="/wiki/Frequent_flyer_program" class="mw-redirect" title="Frequent flyer program">frequent flyer programme</a> is Vueling Club, which allows members to earn and redeem <a href="/wiki/Avios" class="mw-redirect" title="Avios">Avios</a> for award flights or fare discounts on Vueling and <a href="/wiki/International_Airlines_Group" title="International Airlines Group">IAG</a> airlines <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flybe" title="Flybe">Flybe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iberia_(airline)" title="Iberia (airline)">Iberia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Level_(airline)" class="mw-redirect" title="Level (airline)">Level</a>, and for award travel on <a href="/wiki/Oneworld" title="Oneworld">Oneworld</a> airline alliance partners. Vueling Club replaced Vueling\'s original programme, Punto (Spanish for <i>point</i>), on 27 October 2017, after being announced prior in August 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Punto allowed account holders to earn and redeem points for Vueling flights.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span
