title="Billion">billion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Long_and_short_scales" title="Long and short scales">short scale</a>; one thousand million or milliard, yard,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> long scale) is the <a href="/wiki/Natural_number" title="Natural number">natural number</a> following 999,999,999 and preceding 1,000,000,001. One billion can also be written as <b>b</b> or <b>bn</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EUStyleGuide_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EUStyleGuide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In standard form, it is written as <b>1 \xc3\x97 10<sup>9</sup></b>. The <a href="/wiki/Metric_prefix" title="Metric prefix">metric prefix</a> <a href="/wiki/Giga" class="mw-redirect" title="Giga">giga</a> indicates 1,000,000,000 times the base unit. Its symbol is <b><a href="/wiki/Giga-" title="Giga-">G</a></b>.\n</p><p>One billion years may be called an <i><a href="/wiki/Aeon" title="Aeon">eon</a></i> in astronomy or geology.\n</p><p>Previously in <a href="/wiki/British_English" title="British English">British English</a> (but not in <a href="/wiki/Comparison_of_American_and_British_English#Numbers" title="Comparison of American and British English">American English</a>), the word "billion" referred exclusively to a million millions (1,000,000,000,000). However, this is no longer common, and the word has been used to mean one thousand million (1,000,000,000) for several decades.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The term <i><b>milliard</b></i> can also be used to refer to 1,000,000,000; whereas "milliard" is rarely used in English,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> variations on this name often appear in <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/milliard#Translations" class="extiw" title="wikt:milliard">other languages</a>.\n</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/South_Asian_numbering_system" class="mw-redirect" title="South Asian numbering system">South Asian numbering system</a>, it is known as 100 <a href="/wiki/Crore" title="Crore">crore</a> or 1 <a href="/wiki/South_Asian_numbering_system" class="mw-redirect" title="South Asian numbering system">arab</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The facts below give a sense of how large 1,000,000,000 (10<sup>9</sup>) is in the context of time according to current scientific evidence:\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Time">Time</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,000,000,000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li>10<sup>9</sup> seconds (1 gigasecond) equal 11,574 days, 1 hour, 46 minutes and 40 seconds (approximately 31.7 years, or 31 years, 8 months, 8 days).</li>\n<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> minutes ago, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> was flourishing and Christianity was emerging. (10<sup>9</sup> minutes is roughly 1,901 years.)</li>\n<li>About 10<sup>9</sup> hours ago, modern human beings and their ancestors were living in the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a> (more precisely, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Paleolithic" title="Middle Paleolithic">Middle Paleolithic</a>).
