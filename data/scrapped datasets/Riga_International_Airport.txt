lang="lv">Starptautisk\xc4\x81 lidosta "R\xc4\xabga"</i>; <a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>RIX</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EVRA</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, and the largest airport in the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltic state">Baltic states</a> with direct flights to 76 destinations as of November 2019. It serves as a hub for <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a>, <a href="/wiki/SmartLynx_Airlines" title="SmartLynx Airlines">SmartLynx Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/RAF-Avia" title="RAF-Avia">RAF-Avia</a>, and as one of the base airports for <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a>. The Latvian national carrier airBaltic is the largest carrier that serves the airport, followed by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>. The airport is located in the <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81rupe_Municipality" title="M\xc4\x81rupe Municipality">M\xc4\x81rupe Municipality</a> west of Riga, approx. 10&#160;km from its city centre.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport was built in 1973 as an alternative to <a href="/wiki/Spilve_Airport" title="Spilve Airport">Spilve Airport</a>, which had become outdated. It is a state-owned <a href="/wiki/Joint_stock_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Joint stock company">joint-stock company</a>, with the owner of all shares being the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Latvia" title="Government of Latvia">government of Latvia</a>. The holder of the state capital share is Latvia\'s <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_(Latvia)" title="Ministry of Transport (Latvia)">Ministry of Transport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Renovation and modernization of the airport was completed in 2001, coinciding with the 800th anniversary of the founding of the city. In 2006 and 2016, the new north terminal extensions were opened. A maintenance, repair and overhaul facility was opened in the autumn of 2006, to be run as a joint venture between two local companies: Concors and SR-Technik. In 2010, the first dedicated <a href="/wiki/Business_aviation" class="mw-redirect" title="Business aviation">business aviation</a> terminal of the Baltics opened at the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport features a single, two-storey passenger terminal building which has been expanded and upgraded to modern standards several times in recent years. The landside consists of a main hall containing a single row of 36 check-in counters as well as some shops and the security area on the upper floor while the arrivals area, baggage reclaim and some service counters are located on the ground floor below.<sup id="cite_ref-officialguide_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-officialguide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The airside features departure areas <b>B</b> and <b>C</b> split up into two piers with the former original, smaller boarding area <i>A</i> now only used for some arrivals. Both piers feature overall eight stands with <a href="/wiki/Jetbridge" class="mw-redirect" title="Jetbridge">jetbridges</a> plus four walk-boarding stands from the upper level as well as several more gates for bus boarding on their ground levels.<sup id="cite_ref-officialguide_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-officialguide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The terminal features outlets by <a href="/wiki/Narvesen" title="Narvesen">Narvesen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Coffee" title="Costa Coffee">Costa Coffee</a>, and <a href="/wiki/TGI_Fridays" title="TGI Fridays">TGI Fridays</a> amongst others, as well as a single <a href="/wiki/Airport_lounge" title="Airport lounge">airport lounge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-officialguide_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-officialguide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has a single runway in directions <i>18/36</i>, which is 3,200 m in length and equipped with <a href="/wiki/Category_III_approach" class="mw-redirect" title="Category III approach">ILS CAT II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_facilities">Other facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Both <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Civil_Aviation_Agency" title="Latvian Civil Aviation Agency">Latvian Civil Aviation Agency</a> maintain their head offices at Riga International Airport.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Riga Airport is accessible by bus line 22 and mini bus line 322, operated by <a href="/wiki/R%C4%ABgas_Satiksme" title="R\xc4\xabgas Satiksme">R\xc4\xabgas Satiksme</a>, which runs between Riga city centre and the airport. Moreover, there are international bus connections from the airport to cities in <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Riga Airport can be reached by car via the highway P133 which connects the airport with <a href="/wiki/European_route_E22" title="European route E22">European route E22</a>. The airport has 3 car parking areas, with ~1500 parking spaces, offering both short- and long-term parking.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>An airport train station is included as part of the <a href="/wiki/Rail_Baltica" title="Rail Baltica">Rail Baltica</a> project. A contract for construction design was signed on 20 March 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riga_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 17 September 2016, an <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a> <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_Aerospace" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombardier Aerospace">Bombardier</a> <a href="/wiki/Q400" class="mw-redirect" title="Q400">Dash 8 Q400 NextGen</a> aircraft made an emergency landing on the runway of Riga International Airport without its nose landing gear deployed. The plane was carrying 63 passengers and 4 crew members and was forced to return to Riga International Airport following issues with its front chassis. The runway was closed between 10:26 and 15:55 as a safety precaution following an emergency landing. Seven inbound flights and four outbound flights were cancelled, 17 flights were diverted to <a href="/wiki/Tallinn_Airport" title="Tallinn Airport">Tallinn Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaunas_Airport" title="Kaunas Airport">Kaunas Airport</a> and others were delayed. The aircraft involved was YL-BAI and the flight BT 641 was scheduled to fly from Riga to <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Z\xc3\xbcrich Airport">Z\xc3\xbcrich Airport</a>. No injuries were reported.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 17 February 2017, a <a href="/wiki/VIM_Airlines" title="VIM Airlines">VIM Airlines</a> charter flight to <a href="/wiki/Ufa" title="Ufa">Ufa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> slid off the runway during take-off. The aircraft was carrying the <a href="/wiki/HC_Lada_Togliatti" title="HC Lada Togliatti">Togliatti Lada ice hockey club team</a>, including 40 passengers and 7 crew members.  No injuries were reported. The aircraft\'s engine was damaged as it hit airport equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The runway was inspected and closed for three hours after the incident. Flights were diverted to <a href="/wiki/Tallinn_Airport" title="Tallinn Airport">Tallinn Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaunas_Airport" title="Kaunas Airport">Kaunas Airport</a> and others were delayed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
