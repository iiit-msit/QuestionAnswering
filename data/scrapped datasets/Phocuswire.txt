/></a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Northstar Travel Group</b> is a publications and event management company focused on the travel industry. The company\'s brands include Business Travel News, Travel Procurement, The Beat, <a href="/wiki/Travel_Weekly" title="Travel Weekly">Travel Weekly</a>, TravelAge West, Travel Weekly China, Successful Meetings, Meetings &amp; Conventions, Incentive, M&amp;C China, Travel42, Axus Travel App, and Web in Travel. It also owns Phocuswright, a research, business intelligence, and event producer serving the travel technology industry, and its publication, Phocuswire. The company produces more than 80 events in 13 countries related to the travel industry, including the Phocuswright Conference, ALIS (the largest hotel investment conference in the world; produced with the <a href="/wiki/American_Hotel_and_Lodging_Association" title="American Hotel and Lodging Association">American Hotel and Lodging Association</a> annually in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>), The Business Travel Show, The Meetings Show, Web in Travel, CruiseWorld, Global Travel Marketplace, and TEAMS. It owns a majority of Inntopia.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wass_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wass-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Print media accounts for 18% of total revenue, events accounts for 39.2% of revenue, and digital revenue accounts for 42.7% of revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northstar_Travel_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1989, Reed International (now <a href="/wiki/RELX" title="RELX">RELX</a>) acquired <a href="/wiki/Travel_Weekly" title="Travel Weekly">Travel Weekly</a>, Meetings &amp; Conventions and the hotel databases from Murdoch Magazines and formed Reed Travel Group.\n</p><p>In 1998, Reed Travel Group was renamed Cahners Travel Group.\n</p><p>In 2001, Boston Ventures acquired Cahners Travel Group and renamed it Northstar Travel Group.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2012, Boston Ventures Investment Partners sold the company to Wicks Group.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016, the company was acquired by Wasserstein &amp; Co.<sup id="cite_ref-wass_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wass-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2019, the company acquired travAlliancemedia, owner of  TravelPulse.com and TravelPulse Canada, Buying Business Travel, covering the corporate travel market, and CAT Media, which serves the meetings and incentives industries.<sup id="cite_ref-forbes_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
