<b>REU</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LERS</b>) is located by the beaches of <a href="/wiki/Costa_Daurada" title="Costa Daurada">Costa Daurada</a>, equidistant in relation to the town of <a href="/wiki/Constant%C3%AD" title="Constant\xc3\xad">Constant\xc3\xad</a> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Reus" title="Reus">Reus</a> and approximately 8&#160;km (5.0&#160;mi) from the city centre of <a href="/wiki/Tarragona" title="Tarragona">Tarragona</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. The airport receives a large amount of tourist traffic from passengers destined for the beach resorts of <a href="/wiki/Salou,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Salou, Spain">Salou</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cambrils" title="Cambrils">Cambrils</a> as well as for the centre of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, which is approximately 88&#160;km (55&#160;mi) to the northeast. It is also close to one of Europe\'s largest theme resorts, <a href="/wiki/PortAventura_World" title="PortAventura World">PortAventura World</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Mountains_of_Prades" href="/w/index.php?title=Reus_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reus_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport was founded in 1935 by the Aeroclub de Reus. It served as a Republican base during the Spanish Civil War and after the fascist victory served as a Spanish Air Force base.  The base was demilitarised in the late 1990s and became a fully civilian airport administered by AENA, the Spanish airports authority.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_2000s">Development since the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reus_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development since the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has been a <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> base since October 2008 although for the winter 2009\xe2\x80\x932010 season Ryanair reduced the number of flights and destinations from Reus by a substantial amount. This reduction was only temporary for the winter months, and a full flight programme recommenced late March 2010.\n</p><p>On 29 June 2011 Ryanair announced that their base would close on 30 October with the loss of 28 routes after failing to reach an agreement with the local government. Ryanair resumed some flights in March 2012, but they are operated by aircraft not based at Reus.<sup id="cite_ref-P\xc3\xa9rez2011_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P\xc3\xa9rez2011-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reus_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg/220px-Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg/330px-Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg/440px-Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg 2x" data-file-width="922" data-file-height="549" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Airport_Reus_seen_from_air.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial view</div></div></div>\n<p>In order to adapt Reus Airport to future air traffic demand, Aena Aeropuertos has carried out a series of improvements and extended its facilities.  These include a new check-in building between the arrivals and departures buildings, integrating the three buildings into one.  The departures building has also been remodelled for use as a boarding area. The new departures terminal has 23 check-in desks and 12 boarding gates spread over two rooms: gates 1 to 6 are intended for non-<a href="/wiki/Schengen_Area" title="Schengen Area">Schengen</a> flights, and 7 to 12 are dedicated to Schengen destinations. The public area and the passenger only zone have cafeteria and restaurant services and duty-free shops.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and
