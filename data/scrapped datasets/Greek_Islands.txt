The Ionian Sea and most of its islands are not pictured.</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> has many <a href="/wiki/Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Islands">islands</a>, with estimates ranging from somewhere around 1,200<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to 6,000,<sup id="cite_ref-Hellenic_Organization_of_Tourism_(EOT)_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hellenic_Organization_of_Tourism_(EOT)-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> depending on the minimum size to take into account. The number of inhabited islands is variously cited as between 166<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and 227.<sup id="cite_ref-Hellenic_Organization_of_Tourism_(EOT)_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hellenic_Organization_of_Tourism_(EOT)-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The largest Greek island by area is <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>, located at the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a>. The second largest island is <a href="/wiki/Euboea" title="Euboea">Euboea</a>, which is separated from the mainland by the 60m-wide <a href="/wiki/Euripus_Strait" title="Euripus Strait">Euripus Strait</a>, and is administered as part of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Greece_(region)" title="Central Greece (region)">Central Greece</a> region. After the third and fourth largest Greek Islands, <a href="/wiki/Lesbos" title="Lesbos">Lesbos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">Rhodes</a>, the rest of the islands are two-thirds of the area of Rhodes, or smaller.\n</p><p>The Greek islands are traditionally grouped into the following clusters:  the <a href="/wiki/Argo-Saronic_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Argo-Saronic Islands">Argo-Saronic Islands</a> in the Saronic gulf near <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Cyclades" title="Cyclades">Cyclades</a>, a large but dense collection occupying the central part of the Aegean Sea; the <a href="/wiki/North_Aegean_islands" title="North Aegean islands">North Aegean islands</a>, a loose grouping off the west coast of Turkey; the <a href="/wiki/Dodecanese" title="Dodecanese">Dodecanese</a>, another loose collection in the southeast between Crete and Turkey; the <a href="/wiki/Sporades" title="Sporades">Sporades</a>, a small tight group off the coast of Euboea; and the <a href="/wiki/Ionian_Islands" title="Ionian Islands">Ionian Islands</a>, chiefly located to the west of the mainland in the <a href="/wiki/Ionian_Sea" title="Ionian Sea">Ionian Sea</a>. Crete with its surrounding islets and Euboea are traditionally excluded from this grouping.\n</p><p>This article excludes the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnese" title="Peloponnese">Peloponnese</a>, which has technically been an island since the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Corinth_Canal" title="Corinth Canal">Corinth Canal</a> in 1893, but is rarely considered to be an island.\n</p>\n<div id="toc"
