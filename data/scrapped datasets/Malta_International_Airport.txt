href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <i lang="mt">Ajruport Internazzjonali ta\' Malta</i>, <a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>MLA</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LMML</b>) is the only <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> and it serves the whole of the Maltese Islands.\n</p><p>It is located on <a href="/wiki/Malta_(island)" title="Malta (island)">island of Malta</a>, southwest of the Maltese capital <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">Valletta</a> in the town of <a href="/wiki/Gudja" title="Gudja">Gudja</a>, and occupies the location of the former <a href="/wiki/RAF_Luqa" title="RAF Luqa">RAF Luqa</a>. The airport serves as the main <a href="/wiki/Airline_hub" title="Airline hub">hub</a> for <a href="/wiki/Air_Malta" title="Air Malta">Air Malta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medavia" title="Medavia">Medavia</a> besides being a base for <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and its subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Malta_Air" title="Malta Air">Malta Air</a>. It is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Area_Control_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Area Control Center">Area Control Center</a> and hosted the annual Malta Airshow until 2017. href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg/220px-Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="222" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg/330px-Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg/440px-Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg 2x" data-file-width="677" data-file-height="682" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Luqa_airfield_Malta_aerial_photo_1941.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Luqa airfield in 1941</div></div></div>\n<p>The first civil airfield was constructed at <a href="/wiki/Ta%27_Qali" title="Ta&#39; Qali">Ta\' Qali</a>, followed by others at <a href="/wiki/%C4%A6al_Far" title="\xc4\xa6al Far">\xc4\xa6al Far</a> (<a href="/wiki/RAF_Hal_Far" title="RAF Hal Far">RAF Hal Far</a>) and Luqa.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (September 2016)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, the airfields at Ta\' Qali and Hal Far were severely battered and civil operations subsequently centred on Luqa Airport.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The increase in passenger handling and aircraft movements necessitated the construction of a civil air terminal. Preparations started in 1956 and the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">British Government</a> mainly financed what was then a <a href="/wiki/Maltese_lira" title="Maltese lira">Lm</a> 300,000 project.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Malta\'s new passenger air terminal at Luqa was inaugurated on 31 March 1958 by the then <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_Malta" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Governors of Malta">Governor of Malta</a> Sir <a href="/wiki/Robert_Laycock" title="Robert Laycock">Robert Laycock</a>. The air terminal consisted of two floors including some basic facilities such as a restaurant, a post office, a cable and wireless office and a viewing balcony for the public.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In October 1977, a new and longer runway was launched and works commenced on the extension and refurbishment of the air terminal.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> An arrivals lounge and another lounge dedicated to VIPs were added and the original part of the terminal building was used for departures.\n</p><p>This refurbishment was not enough as it still lacked certain essential facilities. Immediately after a change in Government in 1987, the new administration decided that the 35-year-old terminal was past its time (Luqa Terminal) and therefore gave the green light for the construction of a new air terminal along Park 9 (Now located in Gudja).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Until the construction of the new air terminal was completed, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Malta" title="Government of Malta">Government</a> embarked on a further upgrade of the old air terminal.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2016)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The facilities introduced included air conditioning, new <a href="/wiki/Baggage_carousel" title="Baggage carousel">baggage carousels</a>, flight information monitors, computerised <a href="/wiki/Airport_check-in" title="Airport check-in">check-in desks</a>, a new floor surface and new retail outlets including a larger <a href="/wiki/Duty-free_shop" title="Duty-free shop">duty-free area</a>.\n</p><p>The foundation stone of the present air terminal in Gudja was laid in September 1989 and it was inaugurated in record time 29 months later, in February 1992. Malta International Airport became fully operational on 25 March 1992 and the old Luqa passenger terminal was effectively closed down after 35 years.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Over the last several years, passenger numbers have been increasing, climbing from 3.5 million in 2011 to 6.0 million in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-maltairport.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maltairport.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The increase in passenger numbers is mainly due to the increased number of routes served by low-cost carriers. <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> based one aircraft in Malta from May 2010, increasing to two in May 2012, three in March 2016, four in March 2017, five in March 2018 and further to six in April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The largest aircraft visiting Malta International Airport regularly is the daily <a href="/wiki/Emirates_(airline)" title="Emirates (airline)">Emirates</a> <a href="/wiki/Boeing_777-300" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 777-300">Boeing 777-300</a>. The airport has received occasional visits by the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">Airbus A380</a>, usually for repainting at one of the local maintenance facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has a single passenger terminal which became fully operational on 25 March 1992. This replaced the old <a href="/wiki/Luqa" title="Luqa">Luqa</a> terminal which is by 2020 mostly used for cargo. Malta International Airport <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">air terminal</a> operations include general passenger services, and the operation of an extensive range of retail services at the airport, airside and landside shops, restaurants and other outlets, which are all operated on concession agreements.  The airport offers one VIP lounge, the La Valette Club.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The airport also hosts several maintenance facilities including those operated by <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa_Technik" title="Lufthansa Technik">Lufthansa Technik</a> and SR Technics.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_facilities">Other facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The head office of <a href="/wiki/Medavia" title="Medavia">Medavia</a> is on the airport property.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Located within the grounds of Malta International Airport, the Business Centre is the first building in Malta to have applied for <a href="/wiki/BREEAM" title="BREEAM">BREEAM</a> (BRE Environmental Assessment Method) to become the island\'s first Grade A office park. The head office of <a href="/wiki/Air_Malta" title="Air Malta">Air Malta</a> is at Level 2 of the Skyparks Business Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Malta Airport MetOffice<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> is part of the Malta International Airport and provides the function of a national meteorological service for Malta. Although they primarily serve aviation they also service the public sector.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> All equipment, other than the <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">Doppler Weather Radar</a>, is enhanced by <a href="/wiki/Automatic_weather_station" title="Automatic weather station">automatic weather stations</a>, of which eight are situated in Malta and <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Gozo</a>. At the same time an <a href="/wiki/Automated_airport_weather_station" title="Automated airport weather station">aerodrome weather observation system</a> is located at the airport. The MetOffice is able to get information from the <a href="/wiki/Agencia_Estatal_de_Meteorolog%C3%ADa" class="mw-redirect" title="Agencia Estatal de Meteorolog\xc3\xada">Agencia Estatal de Meteorolog\xc3\xada</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK\'s</a> <a href="/wiki/Met_Office" title="Met Office">Met Office</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Numerical_weather_prediction" title="Numerical weather prediction">numerical weather models</a> such as those provided by the <a href="/wiki/European_Centre_for_Medium-Range_Weather_Forecasts" title="European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts">European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_usage">Military usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Military usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Air_Wing_of_the_Armed_Forces_of_Malta" title="Air Wing of the Armed Forces of Malta">Air Wing of the Armed Forces of Malta</a> is based at Malta International Airport. The Air Wing terminal consists of six hangars. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Malta International Airport is served also by several buses operated by private transportation groups and public transport operated by Malta Public Transport.\n<a href="/wiki/Malta_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="Malta bus">Malta Public Transport</a> buses serve the airport. A mixture of Express and local services are available.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is located 5&#160;km (3.1&#160;mi) southwest of the capital, Valletta.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malta_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 5 January 1960, <a href="/wiki/Vickers_Viscount" title="Vickers Viscount">Vickers Viscount</a> G-AMNY of <a href="/wiki/British_European_Airways" title="British European Airways">British European Airways</a> was damaged beyond economic repair at Luqa when it departed the runway after landing following a loss of hydraulic pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-ASN050160_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASN050160-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 25 November 1973, Luqa Airport witnessed the landing of <a href="/wiki/KLM_Flight_861" title="KLM Flight 861">KLM Flight 861</a>. The aircraft, named "Mississippi", was a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> hijacked by three young Arabs over <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraqi</a> airspace on a scheduled <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>-<a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> flight with 247 passengers on board, after the hijackers threatened to blow up the plane when no country would grant landing permission. Most of the passengers and the eight stewardesses were released after negotiations with the Maltese <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malta" title="Prime Minister of Malta">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Dom_Mintoff" title="Dom Mintoff">Dom Mintoff</a>, who argued with the hijackers that the plane could not possibly take off with both the passengers and the 27,000 gallons of fuel they had demanded, given the (then) short runway. With 11 passengers on board the jumbo jet left Malta to <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, where the incident ended without fatalities.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The hijack was claimed by the Arab Nationalist Youth Organization.</li>\n<li>On 23 November 1985, Luqa Airport was the scene of one of the deadliest aircraft <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_hijacking" title="Aircraft hijacking">hijackings</a> in aviation history before the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> <b><a href="/wiki/EgyptAir_Flight_648" title="EgyptAir Flight 648">EgyptAir Flight 648</a></b> was forced to land in Malta en\xe2\x80\x93route to <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>. <a href="/wiki/Unit_777" title="Unit 777">Unit 777</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Counter-terrorism" title="Counter-terrorism">counter-terrorism</a> forces was dispatched to deal with the incident. Storming of the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a>, reluctantly authorised by Maltese officials after five hostages were shot, resulted in the death of over 60 passengers plus several security personnel and aircrew as well as the hijackers, members of the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Nidal" title="Abu Nidal">Abu Nidal</a> Organization.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Abdelbaset_al-Megrahi" title="Abdelbaset al-Megrahi">Abdelbaset al-Megrahi</a> was convicted for the <a href="/wiki/Lockerbie" title="Lockerbie">Lockerbie</a> bombing on the theory that he loaded a bomb onto Air Malta Flight KM180 Malta-<a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> at Luqa Airport which it is alleged found its way via the interline baggage system onto <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_World_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="Pan American World Airways">Pan Am</a> Feeder Flight 103A Frankfurt-<a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and eventually onto <b><a href="/wiki/Pan_Am_Flight_103" title="Pan Am Flight 103">Pan Am Flight 103</a></b> London -<a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>.</li>\n<li>MLA was the origin airport of the <b><a href="/wiki/Air_Malta_Flight_830" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Malta Flight 830">Air Malta Flight 830</a></b> Malta-<a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> hijack which ended in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>.</li>\n<li>On 21 February 2011, two Libyan fighter pilots, both claiming to be colonels, defected and landed their <a href="/wiki/Dassault_Mirage_F1" title="Dassault Mirage F1">Mirage F1</a> jets at the airport after refusing to carry out orders to fire upon a group of civilian <a href="/wiki/2011_Libyan_civil_war" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Libyan civil war">Libyan protesters</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>. On the same day two <a href="/wiki/Eurocopter" class="mw-redirect" title="Eurocopter">Eurocopter</a> <a href="/wiki/Eurocopter_AS332_Super_Puma" title="Eurocopter AS332 Super Puma">Super Puma</a> helicopters registered in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> also landed carrying seven French nationals who were under Italian contracts to work in Libya.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 24 October 2016, a <a href="/wiki/CAE_Aviation" title="CAE Aviation">CAE Aviation</a> <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_Merlin" class="mw-redirect" title="Fairchild Merlin">Fairchild Merlin</a> twin turboprop <a href="/wiki/2016_Malta_Fairchild_Merlin_crash" title="2016 Malta Fairchild Merlin crash">crashed on take-off</a> a short distance from the runway. All five people on board were killed. The aircraft was taking part in a French-led surveillance operation to counter people smuggling.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>On 23 December 2016 <b><a href="/wiki/Afriqiyah_Airways_Flight_209" title="Afriqiyah Airways Flight 209">Afriqiyah Airways Flight 209</a></b>, operated by an <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320</a>, was hijacked while en route from <a href="/wiki/Sabha_Airport" title="Sabha Airport">Sabha Airport</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tripoli_International_Airport" title="Tripoli International Airport">Tripoli International Airport</a> in Libya, and diverted to Malta International.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
