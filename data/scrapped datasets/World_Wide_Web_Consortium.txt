Founded in 1994 and currently led by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" title="Tim Berners-Lee">Tim Berners-Lee</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Consortium" title="Consortium">consortium</a> is made up of member organizations that maintain full-time staff working together in the development of standards for the World Wide Web. outreach, develops software and serves as an open forum for discussion about the Web.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) was founded in 1994 by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" title="Tim Berners-Lee">Tim Berners-Lee</a> after he left the European Organization for Nuclear Research (<a href="/wiki/CERN" title="CERN">CERN</a>) in October, 1994. It was founded at the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> <a href="/wiki/Laboratory_for_Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Laboratory for Computer Science">Laboratory for Computer Science</a> (MIT/LCS) with support from the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Defense_Advanced_Research_Projects_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency">Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency</a> (DARPA), which had pioneered the <a href="/wiki/ARPANET" title="ARPANET">ARPANET</a>, one of the predecessors to the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-consortium_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-consortium-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was located in <a href="/wiki/Technology_Square_(Cambridge,_Massachusetts)" title="Technology Square (Cambridge, Massachusetts)">Technology Square</a> until 2004, when it moved, with CSAIL, to the Stata Center.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The organization tries to foster compatibility and agreement among industry members in the adoption of new standards defined by the W3C. Incompatible versions of HTML are offered by different vendors, causing inconsistency in how web pages are displayed. The consortium tries to get all those vendors to implement a set of core principles and components that are chosen by the consortium.\n</p><p>It was originally intended that CERN host the European branch of W3C; however, CERN wished to focus on particle physics, not information technology. In April 1995, the <a href="/wiki/French_Institute_for_Research_in_Computer_Science_and_Automation" title="French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation">French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation</a> (INRIA) became the European host of W3C, with <a href="/wiki/Keio_University" title="Keio University">Keio University</a> Research Institute at <a href="/wiki/Keio_University_Shonan_Fujisawa_Campus" title="Keio University Shonan Fujisawa Campus">SFC</a> (KRIS) becoming the Asian host in September 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Starting in 1997, W3C created regional offices around the world. As of September 2009, it had eighteen World Offices covering Australia, the Benelux countries (Netherlands, Luxembourg, and Belgium), Brazil, China, Finland, Germany, Austria, Greece, Hong Kong, Hungary, India, Israel, Italy, South Korea, Morocco, South Africa, Spain, Sweden, and, as of 2016, the United Kingdom and Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-Offices_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Offices-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2012, W3C convened a community of major web players and publishers to establish a <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> wiki that seeks to document open web standards called the <a href="/wiki/WebPlatform" class="mw-redirect" title="WebPlatform">WebPlatform</a> and WebPlatform Docs.\n</p><p>In January 2013, <a href="/wiki/Beihang_University" title="Beihang University">Beihang University</a> became the Chinese host.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specification_maturation">Specification maturation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specification maturation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Sometimes, when a specification becomes too large, it is split into independent modules that can mature at their own pace. Subsequent editions of a module or specification are known as levels and are denoted by the first integer in the title (e.g. CSS3 = Level 3). Subsequent revisions on each level are denoted by an integer following a decimal point (for example, CSS2.1 = Revision 1).\n</p><p>The W3C standard formation process is defined within the W3C process document, outlining four maturity levels through which each new standard or recommendation must progress.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Working_draft_.28WD.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Working_draft_(WD)">Working draft (WD)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Working draft (WD)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After enough content has been gathered from \'editor drafts\' and discussion, it may be published as a working draft (WD) for review by the community. A WD document is the first form of a standard that is publicly available. Commentary by virtually anyone is accepted, though no promises are made with regard to action on any particular element commented upon.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At this stage, the standard document may have significant differences from its final form. As such, anyone who implements WD standards should be ready to significantly modify their implementations as the standard matures.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Candidate_recommendation_.28CR.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Candidate_recommendation_(CR)">Candidate recommendation (CR)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Candidate recommendation (CR)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A candidate recommendation is a version of a standard that is more mature than the WD. At this point, the group responsible for the standard is satisfied that the standard meets its goal. The purpose of the CR is to elicit aid from the development community as to how implementable the standard is.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The standard document may change further, but at this point, significant features are mostly decided. The design of those features can still change due to feedback from implementors.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Proposed_recommendation_.28PR.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_recommendation_(PR)">Proposed recommendation (PR)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Proposed recommendation (PR)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A proposed recommendation is the version of a standard that has passed the prior two levels. The users of the standard provide input. At this stage, the document is submitted to the W3C Advisory Council for final approval.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>While this step is important, it rarely causes any significant changes to a standard as it passes to the next phase.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="W3C_recommendation_.28REC.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="W3C_recommendation_(REC)">W3C recommendation (REC)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: W3C recommendation (REC)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>This is the most mature stage of development. At this point, the standard has undergone extensive review and testing, under both theoretical and practical conditions. The standard is now endorsed by the W3C, indicating its readiness for deployment to the public, and encouraging more widespread support among implementors and authors.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Recommendations can sometimes be implemented incorrectly, partially, or not at all, but many standards define two or more levels of conformance that developers must follow if they wish to label their product as W3C-compliant.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_revisions">Later revisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Later revisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A recommendation may be updated or extended by separately-published, non-technical errata or editor drafts until sufficient substantial edits accumulate for producing a new edition or level of the recommendation. Additionally, the W3C publishes various kinds of informative notes which are to be used as references.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Certification">Certification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Certification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Unlike the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Society" title="Internet Society">ISOC</a> and other international standards bodies, the W3C does not have a certification program. The W3C has decided, for now, that it is not suitable to start such a program, owing to the risk of creating more drawbacks for the community than benefits.<sup id="cite_ref-W3CDevProc_7-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3CDevProc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Consortium is jointly administered by the <a href="/wiki/MIT_Computer_Science_and_Artificial_Intelligence_Laboratory" title="MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory">MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory</a> (CSAIL, located in <a href="/wiki/Stata_Center" title="Stata Center">Stata Center</a>) in the United States, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Research_Consortium_for_Informatics_and_Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="European Research Consortium for Informatics and Mathematics (page does not exist)">European Research Consortium for Informatics and Mathematics</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Research_Consortium_for_Informatics_and_Mathematics" class="extiw" title="fr:European Research Consortium for Informatics and Mathematics">fr</a>&#93;</span> (ERCIM) (in <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Antipolis" title="Sophia Antipolis">Sophia Antipolis</a>, France), <a href="/wiki/Keio_University" title="Keio University">Keio University</a> (in Japan) and <a href="/wiki/Beihang_University" title="Beihang University">Beihang University</a> (in China).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The W3C also has World Offices in eighteen regions around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The W3C Offices work with their regional web communities to promote W3C technologies in local languages, broaden the W3C\'s geographical base and encourage international participation in W3C Activities.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The W3C has a staff team of 70\xe2\x80\x9380 worldwide as of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> W3C is run by a management team which allocates resources and designs strategy, led by CEO <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeffrey_Jaffe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jeffrey Jaffe (page does not exist)">Jeffrey Jaffe</a> (as of March 2010), former CTO of <a href="/wiki/Novell" title="Novell">Novell</a>. It also includes an advisory board which supports in strategy and legal matters and helps resolve conflicts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The majority of standardization work is done by external experts in the W3C\'s various working groups.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">Membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Wide_Web_Consortium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Consortium is governed by its membership. The list of members is available to the public.<sup id="cite_ref-MembersList_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MembersList-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Members include businesses, nonprofit organizations, universities, governmental entities, and individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-membershipreq_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-membershipreq-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Membership requirements are transparent except for one requirement: An application for membership must be reviewed and approved by the W3C. Many guidelines and requirements are stated in detail, but there is no final guideline about the process or standards by which membership might be finally approved or denied.<sup
