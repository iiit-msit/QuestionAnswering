dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Contact scraping</b> is the practice of obtaining access to a customer\'s <a href="/wiki/E-mail" class="mw-redirect" title="E-mail">e-mail</a> account in order to retrieve contact information that is then used for marketing purposes.\n</p><p><i>The New York Times</i> refers to the practices of <a href="/wiki/Tagged_(website)" title="Tagged (website)">Tagged</a>, <a href="/wiki/MyLife" title="MyLife">MyLife</a> and desktopdating.net as "contact scraping".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Several commercial packages are available that implement contact scraping for their customers including ViralInviter, TrafficXplode, and TheTsunamiEffect.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Contact scraping is one of the applications of <a href="/wiki/Web_scraping" title="Web scraping">web scraping</a>, and the example of email scraping tools include Uipath, Import.io, and Screen Scraper.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The alternative web scraping tools include UzunExt, <a href="/wiki/R_(programming_language)" title="R (programming language)">R</a> functions, and <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">Python</a> Beautiful Soup. The legal issues of contact scraping is under the legality of web scraping.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Web_scraping_tools">Web scraping tools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contact_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Web scraping tools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following web scraping tools can be used as alternatives for contact scraping:\n</p>\n<ol><li>UzunExt is an approach of data scraping in which <a href="/wiki/String_(computer_science)" title="String (computer science)">string methods</a> and <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">crawling process</a> are applied to extract information without using a <a href="/wiki/Document_Object_Model" title="Document Object Model">DOM Tree</a> .<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>R functions <i>data. rm()</i> and <i>data. rm.a()</i> can be used as a web scraping strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Python <a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Soup_(HTML_parser)" title="Beautiful Soup (HTML parser)">Beautiful Soup</a> libraries can be used to scrape data and converted data into <a href="/wiki/CSV_file" class="mw-redirect" title="CSV file">csv files.</a> <sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ol>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contact_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legal issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contact_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the United States, there exists three most commonly legal claims related to web scraping: compilation copyright infringement, violation of the <a href="/wiki/Computer_Fraud_and_Abuse_Act" title="Computer Fraud and Abuse Act">Computer Fraud and Abuse Act</a> (CFAA), and <a href="/wiki/Trespass_to_chattels" title="Trespass to chattels">electronic trespass to chattel</a> claims. For example, the users of "scraping tools" may violate the electronic trespass to chattel claims.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> One of the well-known cases is <i><a href="/wiki/Intel_Corp._v._Hamidi" title="Intel Corp. v. Hamidi">Intel Corp. v. Hamidi</a>,</i> in which the US court decided that the computer context was not included in the common law trespass claims.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, the three legal claims have been changed doctrinally, and it is uncertain whether the claims will still exist in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> For instance, the applicability of the CFAA has been narrowed due to the technical similarities between web scraping and web browsing.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the case of <i>EF Cultural Travel BV v. Zefer Corp.</i>, the court declined to apply CFAA since EF failed to meet the standard for "damage".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Union">European Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contact_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: European Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>By the Article 14 of the EU\xe2\x80\x99s <a href="/wiki/General_Data_Protection_Regulation" title="General Data Protection Regulation">General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)</a>, data controllers are obligated to inform individuals before processing personal data.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the case of <i>Bisnode vs. Polish Supervisory Authority</i>, Bisnode obtained personal data from the government public register of business activity, and the data were used for business purpose. However, Bisnode only obtained email addresses for some of the people, so the mail notifications were only sent to those individuals. Instead of directly informing other people, Bisnode simply posted a notice on its website, and thus it failed to comply with the GDPR\xe2\x80\x99s Article 14 obligations.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contact_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In Australia, address\xe2\x80\x91harvesting software and harvested\xe2\x80\x91address lists must not be supplied, acquired, or used under the <a href="/wiki/Spam_Act_2003" title="Spam Act 2003">Spam Act 2003</a>. The Spam Act also requires all marketing emails to be sent with the consent of the recipients, and all emails must include an opt-out facility.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The company behind the GraysOnline shopping websites was fined after sending emails that breached the Spam Act. GraysOnline sent messages without an option for recipients to opt-out of receiving further emails, and it sent emails to people who had previously withdrawn their consent from receiving Grays\' emails.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Contact_scraping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Under the Cybersecurity Law of the People\'s Republic of China, web crawling of publicly available information is regarded as legal, but it would be illegal to obtain nonpublic, sensitive personal information without consent.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On November 24, 2017, three people were convicted of the crime of illegally scraping information system data stored on the server of Beijing ByteDance Networking Technology Co., Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
