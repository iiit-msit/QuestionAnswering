class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_companies_of_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: About companies in Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Irish <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">companies</a> fall into three categories:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_shares" title="Private company limited by shares">Private limited companies</a>, which carry the suffix "Limited" (Ltd) or "Teoranta" (Teo), and whose shares are privately held.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">Public limited companies</a>, which carry the suffix "plc" or "<abbr title="cuideachta phoibl\xc3\xad theoranta">cpt</abbr>" and whose shares may be listed on a stock exchange. Where this is the case, it is noted in this article.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_guarantee" title="Private company limited by guarantee">Company limited by guarantee</a>, this type of company has members, not shareholders, as such generally limited to trade associations and not-for-profit bodies.</li></ul>\n<p>Companies in the Republic of Ireland must be registered with the <a href="/wiki/Companies_Registration_Office_(Ireland)" title="Companies Registration Office (Ireland)">Companies Registration Office</a>, and comply with the provisions of the <i>Companies Acts</i>. Companies in Northern Ireland must be registered with the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Economy" title="Department for the Economy">Department for the Economy</a>\'s Companies Registry. Certain Northern Ireland organisations are dealt with on a UK-wide basis and are dealt with by <a href="/wiki/Companies_House" title="Companies House">Companies House</a>.\n</p><p>Some entries on this list may be <i>statutory corporations</i> - a business form governed by a board appointed by an <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Irish Government</a> minister under a particular piece of legislation - not formally companies as such, but are effectively treated as such and are included on this list as they are usually large business. For more details on this form of governance, see <a href="/wiki/State-sponsored_bodies_of_the_Republic_of_Ireland" title="State-sponsored bodies of the Republic of Ireland">state-sponsored bodies of Ireland</a>.\n</p><p>Some <a href="/wiki/Mutual_organization" title="Mutual organization">mutual</a> bodies, such as <a href="/wiki/Building_society" title="Building society">building societies</a>, are also included on this list.\n</p><p>Some entries such as <a href="/wiki/Three_Ireland" title="Three Ireland">Three Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Intel_Ireland" title="Intel Ireland">Intel Ireland</a> are subsidiaries of foreign companies. These subsidiaries are listed because the Irish <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economy</a> is significantly dependent on foreign companies in comparison to other nations and these <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational companies</a> are major Irish employers and <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">tax</a> contributors - <a href="/wiki/Intel_Ireland" title="Intel Ireland">Intel Ireland</a> employs over 5000 people.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Largest_firms">Largest firms</span><span
