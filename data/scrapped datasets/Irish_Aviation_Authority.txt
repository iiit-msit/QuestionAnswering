title="Enlarge"></a></div>Irish Aviation Authority radar tracking installation</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Irish Aviation Authority</b> (<b>IAA</b>) (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">\xc3\x9adaras Eitl\xc3\xadochta na h\xc3\x89ireann</i>) is a commercial semi-state company in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>  responsible for the regulation of safety aspects of air travel. It is also responsible for providing <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">air traffic control</a> services to Ireland\'s three main airports, namely <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Airport" title="Shannon Airport">Shannon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cork_Airport" title="Cork Airport">Cork</a>. Its head office is in The Times Building in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Aviation_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Times_Building,_Dublin_(DSC06420).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/The_Times_Building%2C_Dublin_%28DSC06420%29.jpg/220px-The_Times_Building%2C_Dublin_%28DSC06420%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" data-file-height="2352" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Times_Building,_Dublin_(DSC06420).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Times Building in Dublin, headquarters of the IAA</div></div></div>\n<p>The authority regulates the safety standards of Irish civil aviation and provides air traffic management and aeronautical communications services in Irish controlled airspace. The authority was established under the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20051207003252/http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/ZZA29Y1993.html">Irish Aviation Authority Act, 1993</a>. \n</p><p>The authority controls air traffic for all the major international airports in Ireland.\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>Shannon</b> \xe2\x80\x93 The majority of air traffic controllers in Ireland work in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shannon_ATC_centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shannon ATC centre (page does not exist)">Shannon ATC centre</a>. It is from here that 80% of all flights transiting between <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> are controlled. These are flights that don\'t touch down on Irish soil. Shannon controls these flights as far as <a href="/wiki/15th_meridian_west" title="15th meridian west">15 degrees west</a>, approximately 385 kilometres off the Irish coast. From here they are handed over to <a href="/wiki/Shanwick_Oceanic_Control" title="Shanwick Oceanic Control">Shanwick</a> for their journey across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.</li>\n<li><b>Dublin</b> \xe2\x80\x93 There are approximately 79 air traffic controllers in Dublin. The vertical limit of Dublin\'s airspace is Flight Level 245 (24,500&#160;ft). Dublin Airport is by far the busiest airport in Ireland. Estimates for 2017 show 29.6 million passengers going through the airport.</li>\n<li><b>Cork</b> \xe2\x80\x93 Cork ATC provides an approach and an aerodrome service to traffic arriving and departing Cork. The Cork Control Zone has a radius of 15 nautical miles (28&#160;km) and a vertical limit of 5,000&#160;ft (1,500&#160;m).</li></ul>\n<p>The smaller airports such as <a href="/wiki/Ireland_West_Airport_Knock" class="mw-redirect" title="Ireland West Airport Knock">Ireland West Airport Knock</a> provide their own approach and aerodrome service.\n</p><p>In recent years, the authority has invested more than \xe2\x82\xac115 million in air traffic control systems. This includes a new air traffic control centre (ATCC) at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ballycasey_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ballycasey Cross (page does not exist)">Ballycasey Cross</a>, Shannon. Built on a Greenfield site, this ATCC became fully operational in February 2004 and replaces the ATC Centre at <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Airport" title="Shannon Airport">Shannon Airport</a> which had been in operation since 1966. The new ATC centre houses the Shannon element of the new Air Traffic Management (ATM) system introduced in Shannon and Dublin in 2004.\n</p><p>The authority is also responsible for providing communications in <a href="/wiki/Shanwick_Oceanic_Control" title="Shanwick Oceanic Control">Shanwick</a> airspace, with <a href="/wiki/NATS_Holdings" title="NATS Holdings">NATS</a> providing the ATC from <a href="/wiki/Prestwick" title="Prestwick">Prestwick</a>. Shannon Aeradio, as it is known, is based in Ballygirreen, Newmarket-on-Fergus, County Clare.\n</p><p>In October 2009, it was announced that the regulatory aspects of the IAA\'s remit would be transferred to the Dublin Transport Authority, later renamed the <a href="/wiki/National_Transport_Authority_(Ireland)" title="National Transport Authority (Ireland)">National Transport Authority</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Irish government</a> revealed plans to purchase a long-range <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a> system at a cost of \xe2\x82\xac10 million for the Irish Aviation Authority and <a href="/wiki/Defence_Forces_(Ireland)" title="Defence Forces (Ireland)">Irish Defence Forces</a> (military) to keep track of covert aircraft flying in Irish-controlled airspace, including military aircraft that do not file a flight plan and do not have their transponders switched on. <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Defence_(Ireland)" title="Minister for Defence (Ireland)">Minister for Defence</a> <a href="/wiki/Simon_Coveney" title="Simon Coveney">Simon Coveney</a> said the increased surveillance capability would give better coverage of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> airspace over which the IAA has responsibility for civil and military aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
