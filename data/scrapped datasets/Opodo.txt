rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.opodo.com">www<wbr />.opodo<wbr />.com</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Opodo</b> is an online <a href="/wiki/Travel_agency" title="Travel agency">travel agency</a> which offers deals in regular and charter flights, low-cost airlines, hotels, cruises, car rental, dynamic packages, holiday packages and travel insurance. It is a pan-European enterprise, founded by a consortium of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airlines</a>, including <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_France" title="Air France">Air France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iberia_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia Airlines">Iberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Airlines" title="Austrian Airlines">Austrian Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finnair" title="Finnair">Finnair</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Travel_technology" title="Travel technology">travel technology</a> provider <a href="/wiki/Amadeus_IT_Group" title="Amadeus IT Group">Amadeus</a> owned 99.4% of the company until 2011.\n</p><p>Opodo operates out of fourteen European countries, with headquarters in London, UK. It operates Opodo-branded sites in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, as well as Travellink-branded sites in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>. It also operates several other French travel websites.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2 id="mw-toc-heading">Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#IPO"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">IPO</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Criticism"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Criticism</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Opodo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Opodo was conceptualised, designed and developed by a core team at Sapient London in 2000. It launched its first site in Germany in November 2001, its UK site in January 2002, its French site in April 2002 and its Italian site in January 2006. <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Bisignani" title="Giovanni Bisignani">Giovanni Bisignani</a>, former CEO of <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a> and present CEO of <a href="/wiki/IATA" class="mw-redirect" title="IATA">IATA</a> (International Air Transport Association) was Opodo\'s first CEO. <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_De_Santis" title="Nicolas De Santis">Nicolas De Santis</a> former CMO of web currency <a href="/wiki/Beenz.com" title="Beenz.com">beenz.com</a> and president of strategy consultancy and incubator CorporateVision.io was Opodo\'s first director of marketing, sales and strategy. In 2002, <a href="/wiki/David_P._Scowsill" class="mw-redirect" title="David P. Scowsill">David P. Scowsill</a> was appointed as Opodo\'s CEO, replacing interim CEO Simon Tucker.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Prior to founding of Opodo, <a href="/wiki/Orbitz" title="Orbitz">Orbitz</a> was negotiating with European air carriers, trying to get a deal with them, but they were hesitant to participate in a company where the majority was owned by US carriers.<sup id="cite_ref-skift_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skift-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2004, the company was bought by <a href="/wiki/Amadeus_IT_Group" title="Amadeus IT Group">Amadeus</a> for 62 million euros.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since 2011, Opodo is part of the largest online travel company in Europe, <a href="/wiki/EDreams_ODIGEO" title="EDreams ODIGEO">eDreams ODIGEO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="IPO">IPO</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Opodo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: IPO">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Parent company eDreams went public in 2014.   \n</p><p>Independent Board Member James Hare (James Otis Hare II) oversaw the public launch on April 4, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-edreamsodigeo1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edreamsodigeo1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>eDreams offered its stock at 10.25 Euros per share.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>That stock price had fallen to 1.02 Euros by October 24 of 2014, wiping out over one billion Euros of market capitalization.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Some commentators called the launch \xe2\x80\x9cEurope\xe2\x80\x99s worst performing IPO of 2014\xe2\x80\x9d.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>eDreams moved quickly, asking their shareholders for authorization to \xe2\x80\x9cDischarge to Mr. James Otis Hare for the exercise of his mandate as director of the Company until his resignation as of 25 March, 2015.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>eDreams issued the following announcement: \xe2\x80\x9cEffective March 25, 2015, eDreams ODIGEO (\xe2\x80\x9cthe Company\xe2\x80\x9d) accepts the resignation of Mr. James Hare as an Independent member from the Board of Directors\xe2\x80\x9d.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2015, CEO Dana Dunne introduced a new strategy focusing on mobile, revenue diversification and customer experience improvements, which led to a strong turnaround in business performance.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Opodo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Opodo.com and opodo.co.uk have a \xe2\x80\x9cpoor\xe2\x80\x9d rating of 2 stars out 5 on the online review platform Trustpilot,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and a rating of 1.2 out of 5 on the review platform Reviewcentre.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> \nThe majority of customer complaints focus on a serious lack of responsiveness from the Opodo customer care team, for instance not receiving a reply to an email in over a month, for them being "unreachable by phone," as well as many instances in which tickets booked and confirmed through Opodo are never actually reserved with the airline.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2020 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">The Observer</a></i> gave Opodo an award for the year\'s "worst customer service" after it began charging customers \xc2\xa316.49 to receive confirmation emails, "speedy" refunds and "free customer service".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a
