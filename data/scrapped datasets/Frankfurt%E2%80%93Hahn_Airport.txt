lang="de">Flughafen Frankfurt-Hahn</i>, <a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>HHN</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EDFH</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Hahn,_Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Hahn, Rhineland-Palatinate">Hahn, Rhineland-Palatinate</a>, Germany.\n</p><p>The airport is 10&#160;km (6.2&#160;mi) from the town of <a href="/wiki/Kirchberg_(Hunsr%C3%BCck)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kirchberg (Hunsr\xc3\xbcck)">Kirchberg</a> and 20&#160;km (12&#160;mi) from both <a href="/wiki/Simmern" title="Simmern">Simmern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traben-Trarbach" title="Traben-Trarbach">Traben-Trarbach</a>. The airport is equidistant between <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Luxembourg (city)">Luxembourg</a> \xe2\x80\x93 about 120&#160;km (75&#160;mi) to each city by road. The closest major cities are <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz</a> at about 70&#160;km (43&#160;mi) and <a href="/wiki/Mainz" title="Mainz">Mainz</a> at about 90&#160;km (56&#160;mi). The airport served 1.4&#160;million passengers in 2019, down from 2.60&#160;million in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-2019figures_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019figures-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-traffic_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-traffic-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Frankfurt-Hahn Airport charges its airline operators less than <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Airport" title="Frankfurt Airport">Frankfurt Airport</a>. The only airlines that operate commercial passenger service to/from the airport are <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wizzair" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizzair">Wizzair</a>, both of which are <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carriers</a>. It is also a prominent cargo airport as a result of its location and 24-hour operating licence.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It had a turnover of 156,000 tons of cargo in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2019figures_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2019figures-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airport is 82.5% owned by <a href="/wiki/HNA_Group" title="HNA Group">HNA Group</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_Global_500" title="Fortune Global 500">Fortune Global 500</a> company based in China and 17.5% owned by the state of <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>. The airport is not profitable and the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> has agreed to allow Rhineland-Palatinate to cover up to \xe2\x82\xac25.3&#160;million of losses between 2017 and 2021 while HNA makes improvements to the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-ecpublic_4-0" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_base">Military base</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military base">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hahn_Air_Base" title="Hahn Air Base">Hahn Air Base</a></div>\n<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, at which time an invasion of <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> was a possibility, <a href="/wiki/Hahn_Air_Base" title="Hahn Air Base">Hahn Air Base</a> was a frontline air base, and home of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> 50th Tactical Fighter Wing (now the <a href="/wiki/50th_Space_Wing" title="50th Space Wing">50th Space Wing</a>), in various designations, as part of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Forces_in_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Air Forces in Europe">United States Air Forces in Europe</a> (USAFE). It was one of several USAFE bases in Germany within 100 kilometres (62&#160;mi) of each other including <a href="/wiki/Zweibr%C3%BCcken" title="Zweibr\xc3\xbccken">Zweibr\xc3\xbccken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ramstein_Air_Base" title="Ramstein Air Base">Ramstein Air Base</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sembach" title="Sembach">Sembach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bitburg_Air_Base" class="mw-redirect" title="Bitburg Air Base">Bitburg Air Base</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spangdahlem_Air_Base" title="Spangdahlem Air Base">Spangdahlem Air Base</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rhein-Main_Air_Base" title="Rhein-Main Air Base">Rhein-Main Air Base</a>. These air bases were well situated to reach all locations within Europe and the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin" title="Mediterranean Basin">Mediterranean Basin</a>. Hahn Air Base had more than 13,000 people and three squadrons of F-16 tactical fighters. At the end of the Cold War, the United States was left with a huge excess capacity of expensive airfields in Europe.\n</p><p>As a result, the squadrons at the base were inactivated: the <a href="/wiki/496th_Tactical_Fighter_Squadron" title="496th Tactical Fighter Squadron">496th Tactical Fighter Squadron</a> was inactivated on 15 May 1991, the <a href="/wiki/313th_Tactical_Fighter_Squadron" title="313th Tactical Fighter Squadron">313th Tactical Fighter Squadron</a> was inactivated on 1 July 1991, and the <a href="/wiki/10th_Flight_Test_Squadron" title="10th Flight Test Squadron">10th Tactical Fighter Squadron</a> was inactivated on 30 September 1991. The 50th Tactical Fighter Wing was inactivated on 30 September 1991 and then activated as the <a href="/wiki/50th_Space_Wing" title="50th Space Wing">50th Space Wing</a> at Falcon AFB (now <a href="/wiki/Schriever_Air_Force_Base" title="Schriever Air Force Base">Schriever Air Force Base</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a> on 30 January 1992. The inactivations had a significant effect on the local economy.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Most of Hahn Air Base was returned to civil German authorities on 30 September 1993, though USAFE retained a small portion as a radio communications site until its final return to German authorities in 2012. It is still frequently used for military charters operated by, amongst others, Atlas Air, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a>.\n</p><p>The German government decided to turn Hahn Air Base into a civil airport with the goal of reducing traffic to <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurt International Airport">Frankfurt International Airport</a>. One of the main investors in the development of the airport was <a href="/wiki/Fraport_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="Fraport AG">Fraport AG</a>, the operator of Frankfurt International Airport, which received a 65% <a href="/wiki/Ownership" title="Ownership">ownership</a> stake in the airport.\n</p><p>In 1996, the faculty and police training school of the <a href="/wiki/Rheinland-Pfalz_State_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Rheinland-Pfalz State Police">Rheinland-Pfalz State Police</a> were combined at a new joint facility located at the air base\'s former housing area.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_controversy">Name controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Name controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2001, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> began flying to the airport, using it as a second base for its European operations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At the request of Ryanair, the name of the airport was officially changed from <i>Hahn Airport</i> to <i>Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn Airport</i>. <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> began legal proceedings against Ryanair in 2002, claiming the usage of "Frankfurt" in the name to be <a href="/wiki/False_advertising" title="False advertising">false advertising</a>. Ryanair was allowed to keep the name but was forced to clarify in its advertising that the airport is actually 120 kilometers (75 miles) by road from Frankfurt.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Losses_and_ownership_transfers">Losses and ownership transfers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Losses and ownership transfers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2003, the airport reported a loss of \xe2\x82\xac17&#160;million, compared to \xe2\x82\xac20&#160;million in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Etihad_Cargo" class="mw-redirect" title="Etihad Cargo">Etihad Cargo</a> switched its German freighter services from Frankfurt International airport to Frankfurt-Hahn airport.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Effective 1 January 2009, <a href="/wiki/Fraport" title="Fraport">Fraport</a> sold its 65% interest in the airport to the government of <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> for the symbolic price of \xe2\x82\xac1. The airport had been losing money and Fraport did not want to continue to fund losses. The transaction increased the stake owned by the government to 82.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2009, a cargo flight departing from Hahn using the <a href="/wiki/Antonov_225" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonov 225">Antonov 225</a> made the world record for the heaviest single piece of air cargo, a 189.98 metric tonne generator for a <a href="/wiki/Fossil-fuel_power_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Fossil-fuel power station">Fossil-fuel power station</a> in Armenia.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2013, <a href="/wiki/Etihad_Cargo" class="mw-redirect" title="Etihad Cargo">Etihad Cargo</a>, a major customer of the airport, announced the relocation of its cargo operations from Hahn to Frankfurt Airport.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2014, the airport announced it had accumulated debts of \xe2\x82\xac125&#160;million while passenger and cargo traffic were decreasing.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The same year, the government pledged \xe2\x82\xac80&#160;million to the airport so that it would avoid bankruptcy.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In February 2014, security staff at the airport initiated a strike action.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\nIn the summer of 2014, Ryanair reduced capacity on several routes for and removed 3 of 9 aircraft based at the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2015, <a href="/wiki/Yangtze_River_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Yangtze River Express">Yangtze River Express</a>, the largest freight customer of the airport with 4 cargo destinations and accounting for 50,000 of the airport\'s 130,000 tons of annual volume, announced it would cease its cargo operations at Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn Airport in favor of <a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Munich Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Months earlier, <a href="/wiki/Qatar_Airways" title="Qatar Airways">Qatar Airways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aeroflot" title="Aeroflot">Aeroflot</a> had also ceased their cargo operations at the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In June 2016, the cargo subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Air_France-KLM" class="mw-redirect" title="Air France-KLM">Air France-KLM</a> announced it would shut down its cargo reloading point at the airport, which was used to collect freight and transfer it to Paris by truck.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In August 2016, <a href="/wiki/RAF-Avia" title="RAF-Avia">RAF-Avia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> announced basing two aircraft at the airport to operate ad-hoc charter flights.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Also in June 2016, the government of <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> announced the sale of its 82.5% interest in the airport to Shanghai Yiqian Trading Company.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> However, the deal fell apart a month later after the buyer failed to get approval to make the payment.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017, Suparna, formerly known as Yangtze River Express, began operating a 747-400F at the airport and <a href="/wiki/AirBridgeCargo" title="AirBridgeCargo">AirBridgeCargo</a> and Etihad also expanded cargo operations.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In August 2017, <a href="/wiki/HNA_Group" title="HNA Group">HNA Group</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Fortune_Global_500" title="Fortune Global 500">Fortune Global 500</a> company based in China acquired the 82.5% stake in the airport owned by the government of <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a> for \xe2\x82\xac15.1&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In conjunction with the acquisition, the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> agreed to cover up to \xe2\x82\xac25.3&#160;million of losses between 2017 and 2021 while HNA makes improvements to the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-ecpublic_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ecpublic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2018, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced the shift of part of its operations from Hahn to <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Airport" title="Frankfurt Airport">Frankfurt Airport</a>, where it opened a base in 2017. One of five aircraft were moved to Frankfurt Airport and four routes were cut at Frankfurt-Hahn.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> A year later, Ryanair announced further major cuts with a reduction to just 16 routes - from over 40 in earlier years - for the 2019/2020 winter season.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2020, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced plans to close their Hahn base by November 2020 after a labour union dispute. Hahn has been Ryanair\'s second base in continental <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, inaugurated in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> However, as of September 2020, no final decision has been made.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg/220px-Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg/330px-Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg/440px-Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Inside_terminal1_hahn_airport.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Check-in area</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminals">Terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport consists of two passenger terminals and one cargo terminal. The passenger terminals, designated A and B, include shops and restaurants including a <a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald&#39;s">McDonald\'s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The apron has 11 stands for mid-sized aircraft, such as the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a>, which are reached on foot. The cargo apron has 3 stands for large aircraft such as the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747-8F" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 747-8F">Boeing 747-8F</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn has a long runway of 3,800 metres (12,467&#160;ft) in the direction of 03/21. This, combined with a large apron, allows it to handle some of the world\'s biggest aircraft such as the <a href="/wiki/Antonov_An-124" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonov An-124">Antonov An-124</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_Dreamlifter" title="Boeing Dreamlifter">Boeing Dreamlifter</a>. While the Antonov is a frequent visitor, the Dreamlifter landed only twice at the airport, both times in 2010. It has an <a href="/wiki/Instrument_Landing_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrument Landing System">Instrument Landing System</a> available to both sides, with runway 21 being <a href="/wiki/Instrument_Landing_System#ILS_categories" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrument Landing System">category 3 approved</a>; low visibility conditions are a frequent problem at the airport, especially during autumn and winter.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coach">Coach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Coach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Hahn is served by several, mostly private, coach operators that run regular services to <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_am_Main" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurt am Main">Frankfurt am Main</a> (105 minutes, via <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Airport" title="Frankfurt Airport">Frankfurt Airport</a>, Terminal 2), <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> (135 minutes), <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Luxembourg (city)">Luxembourg</a> (105 minutes) and several other cities in western Germany and the region.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has no railway station. The nearest train station is in <a href="/wiki/Traben-Trarbach" title="Traben-Trarbach">Traben-Trarbach</a> (20 kilometers by road, 10 kilometers <a href="/wiki/As_the_crow_flies" title="As the crow flies">as the crow flies</a>), the terminus of the <a href="/wiki/P%C3%BCnderich%E2%80%93Traben-Trarbach_railway" title="P\xc3\xbcnderich\xe2\x80\x93Traben-Trarbach railway">P\xc3\xbcnderich\xe2\x80\x93Traben-Trarbach railway</a>. The nearest long-distance railway stations are <a href="/wiki/Bullay" title="Bullay">Bullay</a> (15 kilometers northwest, on the Koblenz\xe2\x80\x93Trier\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Saarbr%C3%BCcken" title="Saarbr\xc3\xbccken">Saarbr\xc3\xbccken</a> line), and <a href="/wiki/Idar-Oberstein" title="Idar-Oberstein">Idar-Oberstein</a> (26 kilometers south) on the Mainz\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Bad_Kreuznach" title="Bad Kreuznach">Bad Kreuznach</a>\xe2\x80\x93Saarbr\xc3\xbccken line. Frequent buses also run to the main railway station of nearby cities, the closest being <a href="/wiki/Mainz" title="Mainz">Mainz</a> (70 minutes, 60&#160;km or 37&#160;mi to the east) and <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz</a> (65 minutes, 50 kilometers northeast). Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn Airport is almost equidistant from Frankfurt and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Luxembourg (city)">Luxembourg</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The nearest <a href="/wiki/Autobahn" title="Autobahn">Autobahn</a> connections are approximately 40 kilometres (25&#160;mi) to the west (<a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_1" title="Bundesautobahn 1">A1</a>) or east (A 61). Parking and car rental are available at the airport.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
