The village of <a href="/wiki/Knock,_County_Mayo" title="Knock, County Mayo">Knock</a> is 20&#160;km (12.43&#160;mi) away. 750,000 passengers used the airport in 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> making it the fourth-busiest in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> (after <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cork_Airport" title="Cork links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ireland_West_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airport opened on 25 October 1985 with three <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a> charter flights to Rome: the official opening was on 30 May 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-History_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The site, on a hill in boggy terrain, was thought by many to be unrealistic, but the airport was built following a long and controversial campaign by <a href="/wiki/James_Horan_(monsignor)" title="James Horan (monsignor)">Monsignor James Horan</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-History_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the story of which has even inspired a musical.<sup id="cite_ref-Musical_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Musical-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The primary motivation was for building it was to attract pilgrims to <a href="/wiki/Knock_Shrine" title="Knock Shrine">Knock Shrine</a>. Despite criticisms that the site was too boggy and too foggy, Horan delivered an airport within five years, primarily financed by a Government grant of <a href="/wiki/Irish_pound" title="Irish pound">\xc2\xa3</a>9.8 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He died shortly after the opening of the airport, and his funeral was held at the then-named Horan International Airport. In recent times, Horan has been celebrated with a bronze statue erected at the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By 1988, over 100,000 passengers had passed through. Aer Lingus commenced flights from the airport to <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Airport" title="Birmingham Airport">Birmingham</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-History_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 1 June 2003, hundreds of people gathered to view an Air Atlanta Icelandic Boeing 747 land with 500 returning pilgrims from <a href="/wiki/Lourdes" title="Lourdes">Lourdes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_years">Recent years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ireland_West_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cnock_airport_2013.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Cnock_airport_2013.png/220px-Cnock_airport_2013.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="110" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Cnock_airport_2013.png/330px-Cnock_airport_2013.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Cnock_airport_2013.png/440px-Cnock_airport_2013.png 2x" data-file-width="4252" data-file-height="2118" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cnock_airport_2013.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Apron view</div></div></div>\n<p>Since 2003, flag-carrier, low-cost and regional airlines including <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a>, <a href="/wiki/MyTravel_Airways" title="MyTravel Airways">MyTravelLite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bmibaby" title="Bmibaby">Bmibaby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aer_Arann" class="mw-redirect" title="Aer Arann">Aer Arann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flybe" title="Flybe">flybe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> and <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a> have added routes to the UK and mainland Europe. Not all have proven successful, but by 2005 the airport was handling 500,000 passengers per annum.<sup id="cite_ref-History_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was voted the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>\'s best <a href="/wiki/Regional_airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional airport">regional airport</a> in 2006 and 2009 by the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_commerce" title="Chamber of commerce">Chambers of Commerce</a> of Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-History_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>2007 was a notable year, with scheduled <a href="/wiki/Transatlantic_flight" title="Transatlantic flight">transatlantic</a> services to <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_International_Airport" title="John F. Kennedy International Airport">New York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Logan_International_Airport" title="Logan International Airport">Boston</a> commencing in May, operated by the now-defunct <a href="/wiki/Flyglobespan" title="Flyglobespan">Flyglobespan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A record 629,000 passengers used the airport in 2008, a 13% rise compared to the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-History_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The installation of the Category II Instrument Landing System in April 2009 has resulted in a significant reduction in the number of flight diversions to other airports due to poor visibility \xe2\x80\x93 the airport is 200 metres above sea level.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> August 2009 was the busiest month for three years, with 81,000 passengers: 28 August was the busiest day in the airport\'s history, with over 4,500 passengers.\n</p><p>In 2011, the month of August was the busiest in the airport\'s history with 84,052 passengers. 2011 was the most successful year to date with 654,553 passengers. The year saw the commencement of routes to <a href="/wiki/Lanzarote" title="Lanzarote">Lanzarote</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gran_Canaria" title="Gran Canaria">Gran Canaria</a> operated by Ryanair and to <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> operated by flybe.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During September 2011 Ryanair celebrated its four-millionth passenger through the airport, while <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> announced it would be commencing weekly flights to D\xc3\xbcsseldorf in May 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rte_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rte-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In November 2011, Ryanair announced flights to <a href="/wiki/Beauvais" title="Beauvais">Beauvais</a>-<a href="/wiki/Till%C3%A9" title="Till\xc3\xa9">Till\xc3\xa9</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport" title="Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn Airport">Frankfurt-Hahn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bergamo" title="Bergamo">Bergamo</a>-<a href="/wiki/Orio_al_Serio" title="Orio al Serio">Orio al Serio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Girona" title="Girona">Girona</a>-<a href="/wiki/Costa_Brava" title="Costa Brava">Costa Brava</a> from March 2012. In January 2012 the 20th scheduled route was announced\xe2\x80\x94flybe to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, its third from the airport, from March 2012. Budget carrier BmiBaby announced in May 2012 that it was to axe its only route to Birmingham from 10 June, owing to the airline\'s takeover by IAG.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Flights to Beauvais-Till\xc3\xa9 and Frankfurt-Hahn have since ended.\n</p><p>In 2013, Ryanair launched a weekly summer route to M\xc3\xa1laga on Thursdays. Aer Lingus Regional, which took over the Birmingham route operating a daily service using ATR 72s ended service on 26 October. Flybe began four-times-weekly flights on the route on 27 October. On 31 October 2013, in response to the scrapping of the Irish travel tax, Ryanair unveiled three new routes from Knock to Glasgow-Prestwick, Kaunas and Eindhoven. However, these routes had all been withdrawn by the fourth quarter of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 16 August 2015, Aer Lingus operated its first transatlantic flight into the airport when carrying members of the <a href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Archdiocese of New York">Archdiocese of New York</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Cardinal_Dolan" class="mw-redirect" title="Timothy Cardinal Dolan">Timothy Cardinal Dolan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishop of New York">Archbishop of New York</a>. The Archbishop of New York subsequently opened the National Novena the following week after a tour around the entire island (all thirty-two counties). The aircraft used for the flight was a Boeing 757-200.\n</p><p>It was announced in November 2017 that \xe2\x82\xac15 million would be invested in improving and upgrading the airport in 2018 and 2019, to coincide with strong passenger growth.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> These plans include upgrading of car parks, passenger facilities, the terminal and resurfacing of the runway.\n</p><p>On Thursday 20 February 2020, the first <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">Airbus A380</a> (F-HPJB) to be retired by Air France arrived from <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, for scrapping. The A380 was only ten years old.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_assistance">Government assistance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ireland_West_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government assistance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The building of the airport was primarily financed by Government grants totaling \xc2\xa39.858 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The completion of the airport was funded by a \xc2\xa31.3 million grant from the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, payable on condition that the airport developers provided an equal sum from their own resources.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 21 February 2007, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Government of Ireland</a> announced that it was making a \xe2\x82\xac27 million capital grant. The airport stated that it would continue the implementation of its \xe2\x82\xac46 million infrastructural investment programme with over \xe2\x82\xac20 million of spend anticipated for 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Work commenced on a number of significant civil and building projects in this year. A\xe2\x82\xac5.5 million extension to the <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">terminal</a> building was completed in April 2009. The implementation of <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">Category II Instrument Landing System</a> (CAT II ILS) on runway 27, to enhance reliability in low visibility, has been completed and approved. An extension to the Runway End Safety Areas (RESAs) and runway turnpad was completed in March 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Departing passengers aged 12 years and over pay a "Development Fee" of \xe2\x82\xac10. The fee is a critical contributor to the ongoing sustainability of the airport and provides a vital funding source to support the ongoing development works of the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2005, the airport changed its name to Ireland West Airport Knock.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As of August 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Aeronautical_Information_Publication" title="Aeronautical Information Publication">Aeronautical Information Publication</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Aeronautical_chart" title="Aeronautical chart">aeronautical charts</a> available at <a href="/wiki/European_Organisation_for_the_Safety_of_Air_Navigation" class="mw-redirect" title="European Organisation for the Safety of Air Navigation">European Organisation for the Safety of Air Navigation</a>, showed it as Ireland West.<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ireland_West_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is near the <a href="/wiki/N17_road_(Ireland)" title="N17 road (Ireland)">N17 road</a>, about halfway between <a href="/wiki/Galway" title="Galway">Galway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sligo" title="Sligo">Sligo</a>. It is also close to the <a href="/wiki/N5_road_(Ireland)" title="N5 road (Ireland)">N5</a> <a href="/wiki/Westport,_County_Mayo" title="Westport, County Mayo">Westport</a> to <a href="/wiki/Longford" title="Longford">Longford</a> road. Over 1,500 short-term and long-term parking spaces are available at the airport.\n</p><p>The nearest large towns, <a href="/wiki/Castlebar" title="Castlebar">Castlebar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ballina,_County_Mayo" title="Ballina, County Mayo">Ballina</a>, are both 38 kilometres (24&#160;mi) distant, while <a href="/wiki/Sligo" title="Sligo">Sligo</a> is 54&#160;km (34&#160;mi) from the airport. <a href="/wiki/Galway" title="Galway">Galway</a> is 89&#160;km (55&#160;mi) away and <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> is 220&#160;km (140&#160;mi) from the airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a Train">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The nearest railway stations, accessible by taxi and bus are:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ballyhaunis_railway_station" title="Ballyhaunis railway station">Ballyhaunis</a> - 22&#160;km (13.67&#160;mi)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Foxford_railway_station" title="Foxford railway station">Foxford</a> - 27&#160;km (16.78&#160;mi)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Claremorris_railway_station" title="Claremorris railway station">Claremorris</a> - 31&#160;km (19.26&#160;mi)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Ballymote_railway_station" title="Ballymote railway station">Ballymote</a> - 33&#160;km (20.51&#160;mi)</li></ul>\n<p>The proposed reopening of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Rail_Corridor" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Rail Corridor">Western Rail Corridor</a> from <a href="/wiki/Claremorris" title="Claremorris">Claremorris</a> onto <a href="/wiki/Sligo" title="Sligo">Sligo</a> would ultimately have closer railway access to the airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxi">Taxi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a serviced by specially licensed Hackneys and must be pre booked by the laws set down by the national transport authority in Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-Transport_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Transport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car_hire">Car hire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a international car rental companies offer rental facilities at Ireland West Airport including Budget, Avis, Europcar and Hertz.<sup id="cite_ref-Transport_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Transport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ireland_West_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 23 March 2006, a Ryanair <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737-800</a> "only marginally avoided controlled flight into terrain", during an approach on a flight from London Gatwick, according to the <a href="/wiki/Air_Accident_Investigation_Unit" title="Air Accident Investigation Unit">Irish Air Accident Investigation Unit</a>. An unbriefed descent while the flight crew concentrated on reprogramming for a new approach meant they arrived over the airport at 410&#160;ft with landing gear and flaps up. The aircraft landed successfully at a second approach attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ireland_West_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>The construction of the airport is the subject of "Knock Song" by Irish folk singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Christy_Moore" title="Christy Moore">Christy Moore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>The musical "On a Wing and a Prayer" deals with the life and times of Monsignor Horan, focusing on his struggle to get the airport built. It premi\xc3\xa8red in The Royal Theatre, <a href="/wiki/Castlebar" title="Castlebar">Castlebar</a>, on 25 November 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>The airport was recently used in the up and coming film <a href="/wiki/Wild_Mountain_Thyme_(film)" title="Wild Mountain Thyme (film)">Wild
