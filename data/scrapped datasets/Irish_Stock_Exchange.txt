lang="ga">Stocmhalart\xc3\xa1n na h\xc3\x89ireann</i>) is <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland\'s</a> main <a href="/wiki/Stock_exchange" title="Stock exchange">stock exchange</a>, and has been in existence since 1793.\n</p><p>The Euronext Dublin lists debt and fund securities and is used as a European gateway exchange for companies seeking to access investors in Europe and beyond. With over 35,000 securities listed on its markets, the exchange is used by over 4,000 issuers from more than 85 countries to raise funds and access international investors.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A study by Indecon (international economic consultants) published in 2014 on the Irish Stock Exchange found that having a local stock market and securities industry directly supports 2,100 jobs in Ireland and is worth \xe2\x82\xac207 million each year to the Irish economy. It also found that having a domestic securities industry centred on the Irish Stock Exchange generates \xe2\x82\xac207 million in estimated direct economic impact (measured in Gross Value Added or GDP) and \xe2\x82\xac230 million in direct tax for the Irish exchequer (including stamp duty on trading in Irish shares).\n</p><p>The exchange is regulated by the <a href="/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Ireland" title="Central Bank of Ireland">Central Bank of Ireland</a> under the Markets in Financial Instruments Regulations (<a href="/wiki/Mifid" class="mw-redirect" title="Mifid">MiFID</a>) and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/World_Federation_of_Exchanges" title="World Federation of Exchanges">World Federation of Exchanges</a> and the Federation of European Stock Exchanges.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MaltonCH.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/MaltonCH.jpg/220px-MaltonCH.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="221" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fc/MaltonCH.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="239" data-file-height="240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MaltonCH.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An engraving of the exchange in the early 1800s by <a href="/wiki/James_Malton" title="James Malton">James Malton</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The Irish Stock Exchange was first recognised by legislation in 1799 when the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Ireland" title="Parliament of Ireland">Irish Parliament</a> passed the Stock Exchange (Dublin) Act. The exchange originally operated from the <a href="/wiki/City_Hall,_Dublin" title="City Hall, Dublin">Royal Exchange</a> and was built so that businessmen could sell goods and commodities and trade <a href="/wiki/Bills_of_exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Bills of exchange">bills of exchange</a>. In different periods of its history, the ISE included a number of regional exchanges, including the <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> exchanges. In 1973, the Irish exchange merged with British and other Irish stock exchanges becoming part of the International Stock Exchange of Great Britain and Ireland (now called the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>).\n</p><p>In 1995, it became independent again and in April 2014 it demutualised changing its corporate structure and becoming a plc which is owned by a number of stockbroking firms.\n</p><p>At the time of its demutualisation, the country\'s main stockbrokers received shares in the \xe2\x82\xac56m-valued exchange and dividing up \xe2\x82\xac26m in excess cash. <a href="/wiki/Davy_Stockbrokers" class="mw-redirect" title="Davy Stockbrokers">Davy Stockbrokers</a> took the largest stake, at 37.5 per cent, followed by <a href="/wiki/Goodbody_Stockbrokers" title="Goodbody Stockbrokers">Goodbody Stockbrokers</a> with 26.2 per cent; <a href="/wiki/Investec" title="Investec">Investec</a> with 18 per cent; the then <a href="/wiki/NatWest_Group" title="NatWest Group">Royal Bank of Scotland Group</a> 6.3 per cent; <a href="/wiki/Cantor_Fitzgerald" title="Cantor Fitzgerald">Cantor Fitzgerald</a> 6 per cent; and Campbell O\xe2\x80\x99Connor with 6 per cent.\n</p><p>In March 2018, <a href="/wiki/Euronext" title="Euronext">Euronext</a> completed the purchase of the ISE, and renamed the ISE as <b>Euronext Dublin</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" Atlantic Securities Market (ASM), a market dedicated to companies who wish to dual list in Ireland and the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a 1837</div></div></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equity_markets_for_listed_companies">Equity markets for listed companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Equity markets for listed companies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are currently 50 companies with shares listed on the markets of the Irish Stock Exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There are three markets on which for companies to list equities on the ISE:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The Main Securities Market (MSM), suited to larger companies with substantial funding needs</li>\n<li>The Enterprise Securities Market (ESM), for high-growth companies raising equity in earlier stages of development</li>\n<li>The Atlantic Securities Market (ASM), for multinational companies that want to broaden their investor reach to include dollar and euro pools of capital in a streamlined manner</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Money_raised">Money raised</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Money raised">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2016, 11 companies trading on the ISE raised \xe2\x82\xac513m<sup id="cite_ref-auto_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> in equity funds from international investors. New equity listings in 2016 came from Venn Life Sciences, Draper Esprit, and Dalata Hotel Group, which transferred its listing from the ESM to a primary listing on the MSM in June 2016.\n</p><p>In 2015 the largest biotech ever to IPO in Europe took place on the ISE when Malin Corporation, the Irish-based global life sciences company, raised \xe2\x82\xac330m from international investors in an exclusive listing on the ISE. Three other IPOs took place in 2015 raising a further \xe2\x82\xac650m: Applegreen, Permanent TSB and Hostelworld.\n</p><p>In 2014 three companies joined the ISE\'s markets: Dalata Hotel Group, the largest hotel operator in Ireland, Irish Residential Properties REIT plc, the first residentially focused REIT to list in Ireland and Mainstay Medical, an Irish medical device company.\n</p><p>These three companies raised a combined total of \xe2\x82\xac700 at IPO. Total fund raising in 2014 of listed companies was \xe2\x82\xac1.7 billion.\n</p>\n<h2><span id=".23IPOready"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="#IPOready">#IPOready</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: #IPOready">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2015 the ISE launched #IPOready, a structured leadership programme for high potential companies which enhances the knowledge and skills of Chief Executive Officers (CEOs) and Chief Financial Officers (CFOs) in raising capital, investor relations and business management.\n</p><p>The inaugural programme, supported by <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Ireland" title="Enterprise Ireland">Enterprise Ireland</a>, consisted of three phases over a period of 15 months which saw companies from a range of sectors including technology, security, food and printing take part.\n</p><p>The second #IPOready programme will commence in 2017.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trading_and_membership">Trading and membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Trading and membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ISE offers domestic and international membership for trading in shares, <a href="/wiki/ETF_Securities" title="ETF Securities">ETFs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Irish Government</a> bonds and other securities using world-class, easily accessible and cost-effective trading and post-trade infrastructure.\n</p><p>The ISE\'s <a href="/wiki/Electronic_trading_platform" title="Electronic trading platform">electronic trading platform</a> is called ISE <a href="/wiki/Xetra_(trading_system)" title="Xetra (trading system)">Xetra</a> and is provided in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_B%C3%B6rse" title="Deutsche B\xc3\xb6rse">Deutsche B\xc3\xb6rse</a> since the ISE closed its trading floor in Anglesea Street (a listed building) Dublin 2 on 6 June 2000. Shares trading on the ISE are settled by <a href="/wiki/Euroclear" title="Euroclear">Euroclear</a> UK &amp; Ireland via the CREST system and cleared by Eurex Clearing AG.\n</p><p>The ISE is the main centre of liquidity in Irish shares. The company with the highest turnover on the ISE in 2016 was CRH. This was followed Ryanair, <a href="/wiki/Flutter_Entertainment" title="Flutter Entertainment">Paddy Power Betfair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Ireland" title="Bank of Ireland">Bank of Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kerry_Group" title="Kerry Group">Kerry Group</a>.\n</p><p>Trading volumes on the exchange in 2011 were about a quarter of the 2007 peak. In June 2012, following the collapse of a stockbroker, the <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Independent_(Ireland)" title="Sunday Independent (Ireland)">Sunday Independent</a> asked "Will there even be a stand-alone Irish equity market in five years\' time? The omens are not good."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However this has proven not to be the case as in 2016 the number of equity trades had increased by 17.4% to 6.6m, up from 5.6m in 2015, the first time trading has reached over 6m trades and the sixth consecutive year of growth in trade numbers.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Debt_markets">Debt markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Debt markets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ISE is among the leading centres globally for the listing of debt securities with statistics showing debt listings growing by more than 7% in 2016 to reach over 29,000 securities. The ISE was ranked at #2 among global exchanges according to rankings released by the <a href="/wiki/World_Federation_of_Exchanges" title="World Federation of Exchanges">World Federation of Exchanges</a> (WFE) at the end of December 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The ISE offers two markets for the listing of debt securities: the Global Exchange Market (GEM), an exchange-regulated market and <a href="/wiki/Multilateral_trading_facility" title="Multilateral trading facility">multilateral trading facility</a> (MTF), for banks, companies and sovereigns listing debt, and the Main Securities Market (MSM). Major global companies listing debt on ISE\'s markets include <a href="/wiki/Vodafone" title="Vodafone">Vodafone plc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whirlpool_Corporation" title="Whirlpool Corporation">Whirlpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/CPP_Investment_Board" title="CPP Investment Board">Canada Pension Plan Investment Board</a>, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, <a href="/wiki/Barclays" title="Barclays">Barclays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fund_markets">Fund markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fund markets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ISE is the #1 centre for listing investment funds and Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) globally according to statistics released by the World Federation of Exchanges (WFE), with 1,041 new fund classes admitted in 2016. Global investment managers can choose from one of two markets when listings funds: the MSM or GEM.\n</p><p>Notable investment managers listing funds on ISE markets include Tideway, BNY Mellon, PIMCO and Fidelity Investments.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="ISEFundHub">ISEFundHub</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: ISEFundHub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2014 the ISE launched ISEFundHub, an information portal for funds listed on the ISE and funds domiciled in Ireland. The portal displays important information such as fund net asset values (NAVs) and key fund documents as well as extensive performance-based analytics. The service is offered by the ISE in partnership with FundConnect, a Danish-based funds infrastructure provider in the European market.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_services">Other services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><b>Legal Entity Identifier (LEI) services</b>\n</p><p>The ISE is endorsed by the Regulatory Oversight Committee (ROC) and sponsored by the Central Bank of Ireland as a Local Operating Unit (LOU) for the processing of Legal Entity Identifier (LEI) services for Ireland. LEIs are codes designed to create a global reference data system that uniquely identifies every legal entity or structure, in any jurisdiction, that is party to a financial transaction. Businesses may apply for an LEI code through <a href="/w/index.php?title=ISEdirect&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ISEdirect (page does not exist)">ISEdirect</a>. LEIs were brought in by global regulators as part of the response to the global financial crisis.\n</p><p><b>International Securities Identity Number (ISIN) services</b>\n</p><p>An International Securities Identity Number (<a href="/wiki/International_Securities_Identification_Number" title="International Securities Identification Number">ISIN</a>) is a code that uniquely identifies a specific securities issue. The ISE is the National Numbering Agency in Ireland for ISIN codes and is a member of the global industry body the Association of National Numbering Agencies (ANNA).\n</p><p><b>Information services</b>\n</p><p>The ISE is the official source of market data on ISE markets and publishes a range of market indices. The published index of shares is known as the Irish Stock Exchange Quotient or ISEQ Overall Index. Other equity indexes of the exchange include the ISEQ ESM Index, the <a href="/wiki/ISEQ_20" title="ISEQ 20">ISEQ 20</a>, the ISEQ General, ISEQ SmallCap, and ISEQ Financial. The ISE also has two other ISEQ 20 based indices, the ISEQ 20 Capped Index and the ISEQ 20 Leveraged Strategy Index.\n</p><p>The ISE also publishes a range of bond indices.\n</p><p><b>Announcement services</b>\n</p><p>The ISE files and publish regulatory documents and announcements for issuers enabling them to comply with their regulatory obligations.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Euronext_Dublin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Two reports of an investigation into the "wholly inappropriate sale of perpetual bonds" by Davy Stockbrokers to credit unions failed to involve any of the credit unions affected, leaving them "in the dark and powerless to add any value to the findings of this investigation". The ISE, who have Davy as one of its largest shareholders, then declined to give them access to the reports. The Chairman of one the Credit Union\'s who suffered large losses told his members \xe2\x80\x9cThe failure to publish the reports is to place the complaints process in a shroud of secrecy. Such a failure of openness, transparency and fairness can only serve to undermine confidence in the complaints process, forcing those with grievances into the courts. Such a course of action is not in the interest of any of the stakeholders.\xe2\x80\x9d\n</p><p>In April 2010, Financial Regulator at the Central Bank of Ireland told the same committee that "senior management of the exchange should step up to the plate" after failing to help charities, credit unions and rich individuals who received letters informing them that many investments made by stockbrokers over the past decade are now worthless.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Central Bank began an unprecedented investigation into the entire stockbroking community in December 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The 12-month-long investigation found "major problems" and that there would have to be mergers of firms to ensure the industry survives. This merger process will have to be done in a controlled way \xe2\x80\x93 because any instability in the sector could pose a danger to many people\'s savings, and prevent new businesses from raising money at a time when banks are not lending.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2012 it was forced to issue a correction after it reported figures to the market that suggested a surge in trading in government bonds, in one case as much as 85pc of the year\'s volume of trades were reported to have gone through the system in a single day.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Information">Other Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
