For example, a user could combine the addresses and photographs of their library branches with a Google map to create a map mashup.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The term implies easy, fast integration, frequently using open application programming interfaces (<a href="/wiki/Open_API" title="Open API">open API</a>) and data sources to produce enriched results that were not necessarily the original reason for producing the raw source data.\nThe term mashup originally comes from creating something by combining elements from two or more sources.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In recent English parlance it can refer to music, where people seamlessly combine audio from one song with the vocal track from another - thereby mashing them together to create something new.\n</p><p>The main characteristics of a mashup are combination, visualization, and aggregation. It is important to make existing data more useful, for personal and professional use. To be able to permanently access the data of other services, mashups are generally <a href="/wiki/Client_(computing)" title="Client (computing)">client applications</a> or hosted online.\n</p><p>In the past years<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, more and more Web applications have published APIs that enable software developers to easily integrate data and functions the <a href="/wiki/Service-oriented_architecture" title="Service-oriented architecture">SOA</a> way, instead of building them by themselves. Mashups can be considered to have an active role in the evolution of <a href="/wiki/Social_software" title="Social software">social software</a> and <a href="/wiki/Web_2.0" title="Web 2.0">Web 2.0</a>. Mashup composition tools are usually simple enough to be used by end-users. They generally do not require programming skills and rather support visual wiring of <a href="/wiki/GUI_widget" class="mw-redirect" title="GUI widget">GUI widgets</a>, services and components together. Therefore, these tools contribute to a new vision of the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">Web</a>, where users are able to contribute.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="User contributions were part of the original vision of the WWW, right? (January 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The term "mashup" is not formally defined by any standard-setting body.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The broader context of the history of the Web provides a background for the development of mashups. Under the <a href="/wiki/Web_1.0" class="mw-redirect" title="Web 1.0">Web 1.0</a> model, organizations stored consumer data on <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">portals</a> and updated them regularly. They controlled all the consumer data, and the consumer had to use their products and services to get the information.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The advent of <a href="/wiki/Web_2.0" title="Web 2.0">Web 2.0</a> introduced Web standards that were commonly and widely adopted across traditional competitors and which unlocked the consumer data. At the same time, mashups emerged, allowing mixing and matching competitors\' APIs to develop new services.\n</p><p>The first mashups used mapping services or photo services to combine these services with data of any kind and therefore to produce visualizations of data.<sup id="cite_ref-holmes_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holmes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (January 2018)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\nIn the beginning, most mashups were consumer-based, but recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (January 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the mashup is to be seen<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (January 2018)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> as an interesting concept useful also to enterprises. Business mashups can combine existing internal data with external services to generate new views on the data.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_mashup">Types of mashup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Types of mashup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are many types of mashup, such as business mashups, consumer mashups, and data mashups.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The most common type of mashup is the consumer mashup, aimed at the general public.\n</p>\n<ul><li><i>Business</i> (or <i>enterprise</i>) <i>mashups</i> define applications that combine their own resources, application and data, with other external <a href="/wiki/Web_service" title="Web service">Web services</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-holmes_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holmes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They focus data into a single presentation and allow for collaborative action among businesses and developers. This works well for an <a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development" title="Agile software development">agile development</a> project, which requires collaboration between the developers and customer (or customer proxy, typically a product manager) for defining and implementing the business requirements. Enterprise mashups are secure, visually rich Web applications that expose actionable information from diverse internal and external information sources.</li>\n<li><i>Consumer mashups</i> combine data from multiple public sources in the browser and organize it through a simple browser user interface.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i>(e.g.: <a href="/wiki/Wikipediavision" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipediavision">Wikipediavision</a> combines Google Map and a Wikipedia API)</i></li>\n<li><i>Data mashups</i>, opposite to the consumer mashups, combine similar types of media and information from multiple sources into a single representation. The combination of all these resources create a new and distinct <a href="/wiki/Web_API" title="Web API">Web service</a> that was not originally provided by either source.</li></ul>\n<h3><span id="Mashup_enabler">Mashup enabler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mashup enabler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In technology, a <b>mashup enabler</b> is a tool for transforming incompatible IT resources into a form that allows them to be easily combined, in order to create a mashup. Mashup enablers allow powerful techniques and tools (such as mashup platforms) for combining data and services to be applied to new kinds of resources. An example of a mashup enabler is a tool for creating an <a href="/wiki/RSS" title="RSS">RSS</a> feed from a spreadsheet (which cannot easily be used to create a mashup). Many mashup editors include mashup enablers, for example, Presto Mashup Connectors, Convertigo Web Integrator or <a href="/wiki/Caspio#Caspio_Bridge" title="Caspio">Caspio Bridge</a>.\n</p><p>Mashup enablers have also been described as "the service and tool providers, [sic] that make mashups possible".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Early mashups were developed manually by enthusiastic programmers. However, as mashups became more popular, companies began creating platforms for building mashups, which allow designers to visually construct mashups by connecting together mashup components.\n</p><p>Mashup editors have greatly simplified the creation of mashups, significantly increasing the productivity of mashup developers and even opening mashup development to end-users and non-IT experts. Standard components and connectors enable designers to combine mashup resources in all sorts of complex ways with ease. Mashup platforms, however, have done little to broaden the scope of resources accessible by mashups and have not freed mashups from their reliance on well-structured data and open libraries (<a href="/wiki/RSS" title="RSS">RSS</a> feeds and public <a href="/wiki/API" title="API">APIs</a>).\n</p><p>Mashup enablers evolved to address this problem, providing the ability to convert other kinds of data and services into mashable resources.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Web_resources">Web resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Web resources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Of course, not all valuable data is located within organizations. In fact, the most valuable information for business intelligence and decision support is often external to the organization. With the emergence of <a href="/wiki/Rich_web_applications" class="mw-redirect" title="Rich web applications">rich web applications</a> and online Web portals, a wide range of business-critical processes (such as ordering) are becoming available online. Unfortunately, very few of these data sources syndicate content in RSS format and very few of these services provide publicly accessible APIs. Mashup editors therefore solve this problem by providing enablers or connectors.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_integration_challenges">Data integration challenges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Data integration challenges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are a number of challenges to address when integrating data from different sources. The challenges can be classified into four groups: text/data mismatch, object identifiers and schema mismatch, abstraction level mismatch, data accuracy.<sup id="cite_ref-sw.deri.org_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sw.deri.org-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Text.E2.80.93data_mismatch"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Text\xe2\x80\x93data_mismatch">Text\xe2\x80\x93data mismatch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Text\xe2\x80\x93data mismatch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A large portion of data is described in text. Human language is often ambiguous - the same company might be referred to in several variations (e.g. IBM, International Business Machines, and Big Blue). The ambiguity makes cross-linking with structured data difficult. In addition, data expressed in human language is difficult to process via software programs. One of the functions of a <a href="/wiki/Data_integration" title="Data integration">data integration</a> system is to overcome the mismatch between documents and data.<sup id="cite_ref-sw.deri.org_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sw.deri.org-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Object_identity_and_separate_schemata">Object identity and separate schemata</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Object identity and separate schemata">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Structured data are available in a plethora of formats. Lifting the data to a common data format is thus the first step. But even if all data is available in a common format, in practice sources differ in how they state what is essentially the same fact. The differences exist both on the level of individual objects and the schema level. As an example for a mismatch on the object level, consider the following: the SEC uses a so-called Central Index Key (CIK) to identify people (CEOs, CFOs), companies, and financial instruments while other sources, such as DBpedia (a structured data version of Wikipedia), use URIs to identify entities. In addition, each source typically uses its own schema and idiosyncrasies for stating what is essentially the same fact. Thus, Methods have to be in place for reconciling different representations of objects and schemata.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstraction_levels">Abstraction levels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Abstraction levels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Data sources provide data at incompatible levels of abstraction or classify their data according to taxonomies pertinent to a certain sector.  Since data is being published at different levels of abstraction (e.g. person, company, country, or sector), data aggregated for the individual viewpoint may not match data e.g. from statistical offices. Also, there are differences in geographic aggregation (e.g. region data from one source and country-level data from another). A related issue is the use of local currencies (USD vs. EUR) which have to be reconciled in order to make data from disparate sources comparable and amenable for analysis.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_quality">Data quality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Data quality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Data quality is a general challenge when automatically integrating data from autonomous sources. In an open environment the data aggregator has little to no influence on the data publisher. Data is often erroneous, and combining data often aggravates the problem. Especially when performing reasoning (automatically inferring new data from existing data), erroneous data has potentially devastating impact on the overall quality of the resulting dataset. Hence, a challenge is how data publishers can coordinate in order to fix problems in the data or blacklist sites which do not provide reliable data. Methods and techniques are needed to: check integrity and accuracy; highlight, identify and corroborate evidence; assess the probability that a given statement is true; equate weight differences between market sectors or companies; establish clearing houses for raising and settling disputes between competing (and possibly conflicting) data providers; and interact with messy erroneous Web data of potentially dubious provenance and quality. In summary, errors in signage, amounts, labeling, and classification can seriously impede the utility of systems operating over such data.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mashups_versus_portals">Mashups versus portals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Mashups versus portals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Mashups and <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">portals</a> are both <a href="/wiki/Aggregate_data" title="Aggregate data">content aggregation</a> technologies. Portals are an older technology designed as an extension to traditional <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_Web_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Dynamic Web page">dynamic Web applications</a>, in which the process of converting data content into marked-up Web pages is split into two phases: generation of markup "fragments" and aggregation of the fragments into pages. Each markup fragment is generated by a "<a href="/wiki/Portlet" class="mw-redirect" title="Portlet">portlet</a>", and the portal combines them into a single Web page. Portlets may be hosted locally on the portal server or remotely on a separate server.\n</p><p>Portal technology defines a complete event model covering reads and updates. A request for an aggregate page on a portal is translated into individual read operations on all the portlets that form the page ("<code>render</code>" operations on local, <a href="/wiki/JSR_168" class="mw-redirect" title="JSR 168">JSR 168</a> portlets or "<code>getMarkup</code>" operations on remote, <a href="/wiki/Web_Services_for_Remote_Portlets" title="Web Services for Remote Portlets">WSRP</a> portlets). If a submit button is pressed on any portlet on a portal page, it is translated into an update operation on that portlet alone (<code>processAction</code> on a local portlet or <code>performBlockingInteraction</code> on a remote, WSRP portlet). The update is then immediately followed by a read on <i>all</i> portlets on the page.\n</p><p>Portal technology is about server-side, <a href="/wiki/Multitier_architecture" title="Multitier architecture">presentation-tier</a> aggregation. It cannot be used to drive more robust forms of application integration such as <a href="/wiki/Two-phase_commit_protocol" title="Two-phase commit protocol">two-phase commit</a>.\n</p><p>Mashups differ from portals in the following respects:\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th></th>\n<th>Portal</th>\n<th>Mashup\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Classification\n</th>\n<td>Older technology, extension of traditional Web server model using well-defined approach\n</td>\n<td>Uses newer, loosely defined "Web 2.0" techniques\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Philosophy/approach\n</th>\n<td>Approaches aggregation by splitting role of Web server into two phases: markup generation and aggregation of markup fragments\n</td>\n<td>Uses APIs provided by different content sites to aggregate and reuse the content in another way\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Content dependencies\n</th>\n<td>Aggregates presentation-oriented markup fragments (HTML, WML, VoiceXML, etc.)\n</td>\n<td>Can operate on pure XML content and also on presentation-oriented content (e.g., HTML)\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Location dependencies\n</th>\n<td>Traditionally, content aggregation takes place on the server\n</td>\n<td>Content aggregation can take place either on the server or on the client\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Aggregation style\n</th>\n<td>"<a href="/wiki/Salad_bar" title="Salad bar">Salad bar</a>" style: Aggregated content is presented \'side-by-side\' without overlaps\n</td>\n<td>"<a href="/wiki/Melting_pot" title="Melting pot">Melting pot</a>" style - Individual content may be combined in any manner, resulting in arbitrarily structured hybrid content\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Event model\n</th>\n<td>Read and update event models are defined through a specific portlet API\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Create,_read,_update_and_delete" title="Create, read, update and delete">CRUD</a> operations are based on <a href="/wiki/Representational_State_Transfer" class="mw-redirect" title="Representational State Transfer">REST</a> architectural principles, but no formal API exists\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<th>Relevant standards\n</th>\n<td>Portlet behavior is governed by standards <a href="/wiki/JSR_168" class="mw-redirect" title="JSR 168">JSR 168</a>, <a href="/wiki/JSR_286" class="mw-redirect" title="JSR 286">JSR 286</a> and <a href="/wiki/Web_Services_for_Remote_Portlets" title="Web Services for Remote Portlets">WSRP</a>, although portal page layout and portal functionality are undefined and vendor-specific\n</td>\n<td>Base standards are XML interchanged as <a href="/wiki/REST" class="mw-redirect" title="REST">REST</a> or Web Services. <a href="/wiki/RSS" title="RSS">RSS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atom_(standard)" class="mw-redirect" title="Atom (standard)">Atom</a> are commonly used. More specific mashup standards such as <a href="/wiki/EMML" class="mw-redirect" title="EMML">EMML</a> are emerging.\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The portal model has been around longer and has had greater investment and product research. Portal technology is therefore more standardized and mature. Over time, increasing maturity and standardization of mashup technology will likely make it more popular than portal technology because it is more closely associated with Web 2.0 and lately <a href="/wiki/Service-oriented_architecture" title="Service-oriented architecture">Service-oriented Architectures</a> (SOA).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  New versions of portal products are expected to eventually add mashup support while still supporting legacy portlet applications. Mashup technologies, in contrast, are not expected to provide support for portal standards.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_mashups">Business mashups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Business mashups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Mashup uses are expanding in the business environment. Business mashups are useful for integrating business and data services, as business mashups technologies provide the ability to develop new integrated services quickly, to combine internal services with external or personalized information, and to make these services tangible to the business user through user-friendly Web browser interfaces.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Business mashups differ from consumer mashups in the level of integration with business computing environments, security and access control features, governance, and the sophistication of the programming tools (mashup editors) used.  Another difference between business mashups and consumer mashups is a growing trend of using business mashups in commercial <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">software as a service</a> (SaaS) offering.\n</p><p>Many of the providers of business mashups technologies have added <a href="/wiki/Service-oriented_architecture" title="Service-oriented architecture">SOA</a> features.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architectural_aspects_of_mashups">Architectural aspects of mashups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mashup_(web_application_hybrid)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Architectural aspects of mashups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The architecture of a mashup is divided into three layers:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Presentation / <a href="/wiki/User_interaction" class="mw-redirect" title="User interaction">user interaction</a>: this is the <a href="/wiki/User_interface" title="User interface">user interface</a> of mashups. The technologies used are <a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">HTML</a>/<a href="/wiki/XHTML" title="XHTML">XHTML</a>, <a href="/wiki/CSS" title="CSS">CSS</a>, <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ajax_(programming)" title="Ajax (programming)">Asynchronous JavaScript and Xml (Ajax)</a>.</li>\n<li>Web Services: the product\'s functionality can be accessed using API services. The technologies used are <a href="/wiki/XMLHTTPRequest" class="mw-redirect" title="XMLHTTPRequest">XMLHTTPRequest</a>, <a href="/wiki/XML-RPC" title="XML-RPC">XML-RPC</a>, <a href="/wiki/JSON-RPC" title="JSON-RPC">JSON-RPC</a>, <a href="/wiki/SOAP" title="SOAP">SOAP</a>, <a href="/wiki/REST" class="mw-redirect" title="REST">REST</a>.</li>\n<li>Data: handling the data like sending, storing and receiving. The technologies used are <a href="/wiki/XML" title="XML">XML</a>, <a href="/wiki/JSON" title="JSON">JSON</a>, <a href="/wiki/KML" class="mw-redirect" title="KML">KML</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>Architecturally, there are two styles of mashups: Web-based and server-based. Whereas Web-based mashups typically use the user\'s <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a> to combine and reformat the data, server-based mashups analyze and reformat the data on a remote <a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">server</a> and transmit the data to the user\'s browser in its final form.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Mashups appear to be a variation of a <a href="/wiki/Fa%C3%A7ade_pattern" class="mw-redirect" title="Fa\xc3\xa7ade pattern">fa\xc3\xa7ade pattern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  That is: a software engineering design pattern that provides a simplified interface to a larger body of code (in this case the code to aggregate the different feeds with different <a href="/wiki/API" title="API">APIs</a>).\n</p><p>Mashups can be used with software provided as a service (<a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">SaaS</a>).\n</p><p>After several years of standards development, mainstream businesses are starting to adopt <a href="/wiki/Service-oriented_architecture" title="Service-oriented architecture">service-oriented architectures</a> (SOA) to integrate disparate data by making them available as discrete Web services.  Web services provide open, standardized <a href="/wiki/Protocol_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Protocol (computing)">protocols</a> to provide a unified means of accessing information from a diverse set of platforms (<a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming languages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Application_software" title="Application software">applications</a>). These Web services can be reused to provide completely new services and applications within and across organizations, providing business flexibility.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
