<i>Stockholm Skavsta flygplats</i>), or <b>Nyk\xc3\xb6ping Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>NYO</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stockholm_Skavsta_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>An airbase during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the airport was used as a <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dermanland_Air_Force_Wing" class="mw-redirect" title="S\xc3\xb6dermanland Air Force Wing">military airport</a> until 1980, when it was taken out of service.\n</p><p>The council of <a href="/wiki/Nyk%C3%B6ping_Municipality" title="Nyk\xc3\xb6ping Municipality">Nyk\xc3\xb6ping Municipality</a>, where the airport is located, decided in 1984 to take over its control and resume its activities. Civilian passenger air traffic started in September 1984, mainly to Arlanda which at the time had almost all air traffic in Stockholm. In 1998 (after deregulation allowing airlines and airports to compete with each other to a higher degree) the Nyk\xc3\xb6ping municipal council put 90% of the capital of the airport up for sale, with the objective of strengthening its commercial management and enabling investments for its expansion. They acquired this parcel of shares and began the transformation of Skavsta, which has become the second airport of Stockholm and the favoured option for inhabitants who live in the area south of the Swedish capital.<sup id="cite_ref-information-sv_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-information-sv-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airport has a capacity of 2.5 million passengers per annum and is designed for expansion in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-information_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-information-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is owned by ADC &amp; HAS, the same company that owns <a href="/wiki/Belfast_International_Airport" title="Belfast International Airport">Belfast International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Sanford_International_Airport" title="Orlando Sanford International Airport">Orlando Sanford International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Oduber_Quir%C3%B3s_International_Airport" title="Daniel Oduber Quir\xc3\xb3s International Airport">Daniel Oduber Quir\xc3\xb3s International Airport</a> (<a href="/wiki/Liberia,_Costa_Rica" title="Liberia, Costa Rica">Liberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mariscal_Sucre_International_Airport" title="Mariscal Sucre International Airport">Mariscal Sucre International Airport</a> (<a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Santamar%C3%ADa_International_Airport" title="Juan Santamar\xc3\xada International Airport">Juan Santamar\xc3\xada International Airport</a> (<a href="/wiki/San_Jos%C3%A9,_Costa_Rica" title="San Jos\xc3\xa9, Costa Rica">San Jos\xc3\xa9</a>, Costa Rica).\n</p><p>Because the airport is located 43 kilometres (27&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Harpsund" title="Harpsund">Harpsund</a>, the official leisure residence for the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Sweden" title="Prime Minister of Sweden">Prime Minister of Sweden</a>, to which foreign heads of governments sometimes are invited, Skavsta is sometimes used for <a href="/wiki/Air_transports_of_heads_of_state_and_government" title="Air transports of heads of state and government">flights for a head of government</a>, with associated police escort.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span long-term parking facilities are available. The terminal can be reached by foot from all parking areas.<sup id="cite_ref-toandfrom_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toandfrom-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The road distance from Stockholm is 108 kilometres (67&#160;mi).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coach_and_bus">Coach and bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stockholm_Skavsta_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coach and bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Flygbussarna" title="Flygbussarna">Flygbussarna</a> airport coaches depart hourly, travelling directly between Stockholm Skavsta Airport and the <a href="/wiki/Cityterminalen" title="Cityterminalen">City Terminal</a> in Stockholm (approx. 90 min travel time). There are also airport buses to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dert%C3%A4lje" title="S\xc3\xb6dert\xc3\xa4lje">S\xc3\xb6dert\xc3\xa4lje</a>, <a href="/wiki/Link%C3%B6ping" title="Link\xc3\xb6ping">Link\xc3\xb6ping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norrk%C3%B6ping" title="Norrk\xc3\xb6ping">Norrk\xc3\xb6ping</a>, and local stops in the southern parts of <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>. Local bus services are available to Nyk\xc3\xb6ping\'s city centre and its railway station.<sup id="cite_ref-toandfrom_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toandfrom-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Train">Train</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stockholm_Skavsta_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Train">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The railway station in Nyk\xc3\xb6ping is 7 kilometres (4&#160;mi) away. It is served by regional trains (operated by <a href="/wiki/SJ_AB" title="SJ AB">SJ</a>) on the Link\xc3\xb6ping\xe2\x80\x93Stockholm\xe2\x80\x93(<a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4vle" title="G\xc3\xa4vle">G\xc3\xa4vle</a>) route.<sup id="cite_ref-toandfrom_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toandfrom-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Local bus or taxi can be used to the airport.<sup id="cite_ref-toandfrom_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toandfrom-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stockholm_Skavsta_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 9 October 1974, a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_C-47" class="mw-redirect" title="Douglas C-47">Tp 79</a> s/n 79005 of the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Air_Force" title="Swedish Air Force">Swedish Air Force</a> crashed on approach to Nyk\xc3\xb6ping Airport.
