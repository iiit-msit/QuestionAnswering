R. \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>: <i lang="sk">Letisko M. R. \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nika</i>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Flughafen M. R. \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>BTS</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LZIB</b>), also called \xe2\x80\x93 especially in English \xe2\x80\x93 <b>Bratislava Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>: <i lang="sk">Letisko Bratislava</i>; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Flughafen Bratislava</i>) or <i>Bratislava-Ivanka</i>, located approximately 9&#160;km (5.6&#160;mi) northeast of the city center of <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a>, spanning over the area of three municipalities (Bratislava-Ru\xc5\xbeinov, Bratislava-Vraku\xc5\x88a and Ivanka pri Dunaji). It is the main <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>. Shortly after the independence of Slovakia in 1993, it was named after general <a href="/wiki/Milan_Rastislav_%C5%A0tef%C3%A1nik" title="Milan Rastislav \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik">Milan Rastislav \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik</a>, whose aircraft crashed near Bratislava in 1919. The airport is owned and run by <i>Letisko M. R. \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nika \xe2\x80\x93 Airport Bratislava, a.s. (BTS)</i>. As of September 2014 the company is fully owned by the Slovak Republic via the Ministry of Transport, Construction and Regional Development.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Bratislava is a base for the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Government_Flying_Service" title="Slovak Government Flying Service">Slovak Government Flying Service</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, <a href="/wiki/AirExplore" title="AirExplore">AirExplore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Smartwings_Slovakia" title="Smartwings Slovakia">Smartwings Slovakia</a>. During a brief period in 2011, the airport was also a secondary hub for <a href="/wiki/Czech_Airlines" title="Czech Airlines">Czech Airlines</a>. Two maintenance companies, <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Airlines" title="Austrian Airlines">Austrian Technik Bratislava</a> and East Air Company are also based at the airport. Air Livery has one painting bay for aircraft at the airport. The airport is category 4E for aircraft, and category 7 or 8 on request in terms of potential rescue.\n</p><p>Bratislava is also served by the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna International Airport</a> located 49 kilometres (30.4&#160;mi) west of the city centre. Conversely, Bratislava Airport may serve as a low-cost alternative for people from <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and the neighbouring areas.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bratislava Airport is located 9&#160;km (5.6&#160;mi) to the north-east of the city center, covering an area of 4.77&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (1.84&#160;sq&#160;mi). It is within a one-hour drive of <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> (Austria), <a href="/wiki/Brno" title="Brno">Brno</a> (Czech Republic) and <a href="/wiki/Gy%C5%91r" title="Gy\xc5\x91r">Gy\xc5\x91r</a> (Hungary), covering a <a href="/wiki/Catchment_area" title="Catchment area">catchment area</a> of four countries. The nearest large international airport is <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna International Airport</a> approx. 50 kilometres (31&#160;mi) to the west.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The first regular flight between <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> started in 1923, by the newly formed carrier <a href="/wiki/Czech_Airlines" title="Czech Airlines">Czechoslovak Airlines</a>. At that time the airport for Bratislava was in <a href="/wiki/Vajnory" title="Vajnory">Vajnory</a>, about 3&#160;km away from the current airport. That airport is now closed. Preparation for the current airport started in 1947 and construction began in 1948. Two runways were constructed (04/22, 1900 m and 13/31, 1500 m) and the airport opened in 1951.\n</p><p>The number of passengers served at Bratislava Airport decreased temporarily in the early 1990s due to competition from the nearby <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna International Airport</a> (which is only 55&#160;km (34&#160;mi) away from Bratislava Airport), but passenger numbers have been quickly increasing since, partly since Ryanair started traffic in 2004 marketing it as serving both Vienna and Bratislava. In 2005, the airport served 1,326,493 passengers; and in 2008, 2,218,545 passengers. Nevertheless, due to the economic downturn and the collapse of <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Airlines" title="Slovak Airlines">Slovak Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/SkyEurope" title="SkyEurope">SkyEurope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Slovakia" title="Air Slovakia">Air Slovakia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seagle_Air" title="Seagle Air">Seagle Air</a>, the number of passengers has declined to just over 1.4 million in 2012, increasing again after 2014, and in 2018, the airport recorded the highest number of passengers in its history (2,292,712). In January 2019 the only domestic route of Slovakia Bratislava\xe2\x80\x93Ko\xc5\xa1ice, and the Prague\xe2\x80\x93Bratislava route were closed down by Czech Airlines.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminals">Terminals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Terminals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg/220px-17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg/330px-17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg/440px-17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:17-06-01-Flughafen_Bratislava_RR71735.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Departures area</div></div></div>\n<p>The airport has one terminal serving arrivals and departures, completed in July 2012 and replacing the original Terminal A, built in 1970 and demolished in January 2011. Terminal B, built in 1994 and designated to serve the non-Schengen arrivals and departures and Terminal C, built in 2006, are both currently out of service.\n</p><p>The current terminal includes 29 check-in desks located on the ground floor of the departures terminal, one of them designated for oversized baggage. In the non-public zones of the waiting area targeted at departing passengers, there are 13 gates, 8 in the Schengen and 5 in the non-Schengen area.\n</p><p>The airport is also home to the General Aviation Terminal (GAT), where passengers on private, business and VIP flights are handled, as well as passengers of emergency flights and crew.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_facilities">Other facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A new <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control#Air_traffic_control_tower" title="Air traffic control">control tower</a> was added in the 1990s. The parking lot near the terminal has 970 places and is used for short- and long-term parking. The current capacity of the airport is over 5 million passengers per annum. The offices of the Slovak <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(Slovakia)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (Slovakia)">Civil Aviation Authority</a> are on the airport property.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runways">Runways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Runways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The current <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runways</a> enable the landing of virtually all types of aircraft used in the world today (except Airbus A380, Antonov An-225 and Boeing 747-8). The airport features two perpendicular runways (04/22 and 13/31), both of which underwent a complete reconstruction in the 1980s. Runway 13/31 is equipped for the <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization" title="International Civil Aviation Organization">ICAO</a> category IIIA approach and landing, while 04/22 is category I.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buses_and_coaches">Buses and coaches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Buses and coaches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> - Public transportation bus No. 61 connects the airport to the city centre and the <a href="/wiki/Bratislava_main_railway_station" title="Bratislava main railway station"> central railway station</a> during the day. Bus No. 96 operates from Petr\xc5\xbealka. At night the airport is served by bus N61 from the central railway station.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> - Blaguss/FlixBus<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and Slovak Lines (jointly with Postbus) operate bus lines (25 services a day \xe2\x80\x93 approximately once every 45 minutes) to Vienna which stop also at the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna International Airport</a>. The journey to Vienna city centre takes between 75 minutes and 90 minutes.</li>\n<li>Other destinations - Slovak Lines also operates to destinations around Slovakia.</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Bratislava Airport can be reached by private car from the city centre, which is 9&#160;km (5.6&#160;mi) away, or from <a href="/wiki/D1_motorway_(Slovakia)" title="D1 motorway (Slovakia)">D1</a> highway. There is also a taxi stand just near the entrance to the airport with Taxi Slovakia company (taxi of other companies can be called by telephone but rates for the airport are usually higher).\n</p><p>Long-term and short-term car parking is provided at the airport, in front of the terminal building. P1 is an outdoor car park free for up to 15 minutes. P2 is the other outdoor car park, secured by a barrier and a camera system, with 970 parking spaces. P2 parking costs 20 EUR for one day and 35 EUR for a week.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bratislava_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 4 May 1919, <a href="/wiki/Milan_Rastislav_%C5%A0tef%C3%A1nik#Death" title="Milan Rastislav \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik">M.R. \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik crashed on approach to Vajnory Airport</a>, the predecessor to M. R. \xc5\xa0tef\xc3\xa1nik Airport. Many rumors about his death exist.</li>\n<li>On 24 November 1966, an <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-18" title="Ilyushin Il-18">Il-18</a> on multi-leg <a href="/wiki/TABSO_Flight_101" title="TABSO Flight 101">TABSO Flight 101</a> from Sofia to East Berlin via Budapest and Prague crashed into the forested foothills of <a href="/wiki/Little_Carpathians" title="Little Carpathians">Little Carpathians</a> west of the airport shortly after take-off from Bratislava Airport, where it had been grounded due to bad weather in Prague. All 74 passengers and eight crew members died.</li>\n<li>On 28 July 1976, an Il-18 on <a href="/wiki/%C4%8CSA_Flight_001" title="\xc4\x8cSA Flight 001">\xc4\x8cSA Flight 001</a> from Prague crashed into the <a href="/wiki/Zlat%C3%A9_Piesky" title="Zlat\xc3\xa9 Piesky">Zlat\xc3\xa9 Piesky</a> lake just north-west of the airport while executing a <a href="/wiki/Go-around" title="Go-around">go-around</a>. 69 of 73 passengers and six crew members died in the crash. Two passengers later died in the hospital.</li>\n<li>On 7 February 1999, a Boeing 707 aircraft crashed on <a href="/wiki/Takeoff" title="Takeoff">takeoff</a> from BTS. No one was injured.</li>\n<li>On 6 June 1999, a <a href="/wiki/British_Aerospace_Hawk_200" title="British Aerospace Hawk 200">BAE Hawk 200</a> aircraft crashed during SIAD \'99 air show killing the pilot and one female spectator on the ground that was swept off the roof by explosion.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span
