Bhd.</b>  (<a href="/wiki/Doing_business_as" class="mw-redirect" title="Doing business as">doing business as</a> <b>Rayani Air</b>; <a href="/wiki/Jawi_script" class="mw-redirect" title="Jawi script">Jawi</a>: <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1011841111">.mw-parser-output .script-arabic,.mw-parser-output .script-Arab{font-family:"Scheherazade New",Scheherazade,Lateef,LateefGR,Amiri,"Noto Naskh Arabic","Droid Arabic Naskh",Harmattan,"Arabic Typesetting","Traditional Arabic","Simplified Arabic","Arabic Transparent","Times New Roman",Arial,"Sakkal Majalla","Microsoft Uighur",Calibri,"Microsoft Sans Serif","Segoe UI",serif,sans-serif;font-weight:normal}</style><span class="script-arabic script-Arab" dir="rtl" style="font-size: 125%;">\xd8\xb1\xd8\xa7\xd9\x8a\xd8\xa7\xd9\x86\xd9\x8a \xd8\xa3\xd9\x8a\xd8\xb1</span>&#8206;, <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>: <span lang="ta">\xe0\xae\xb0\xe0\xae\xaf\xe0\xae\xbe\xe0\xae\xa3\xe0\xae\xbf \xe0\xae\x8f\xe0\xae\xb0\xe0\xaf\x8d</span>) was a full-service airline in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Shah_Alam" title="Shah Alam">Shah Alam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Kuala Lumpur metropolitan area">Kuala Lumpur metropolitan area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was the first <a href="/wiki/Sharia" title="Sharia">Sharia</a>-compliant airline in Malaysia and the fourth such airline in the world, after <a href="/wiki/Royal_Brunei_Airlines" title="Royal Brunei Airlines">Royal Brunei Airlines</a> (RBA), <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabian_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Saudi Arabian Airlines">Saudi Arabian Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran_Air" title="Iran Air">Iran Air</a>. No alcoholic beverages or pork were served on board in accordance with <a href="/wiki/Islamic_dietary_laws" title="Islamic dietary laws">Islamic dietary laws</a> and prayers were recited before every flight. It was forced to cease operations after only five months due to management, safety and security issues, pilot strikes, and lack of funding.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rayani_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Rayani Air was founded by Ravi Alagendrran and his wife Karthiyani Govindan, both <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Indian" class="mw-redirect" title="Malaysian Indian">Malaysians of ethnic Indian descent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The name Rayani is said to be a combination of the first names of the founders.\n</p><p>Rayani Airlines was originally intended to be based at <a href="/wiki/Malacca_International_Airport" title="Malacca International Airport">Malacca International Airport</a>, with its inaugural flight expected to take place in August 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, the airline changed its base to Langkawi International Airport to attract tourists to the resort island.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was previously rumoured that Rayani Air would be launched as a <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carrier</a>; however, the airline officially positioned itself as a full-service carrier in December 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 20 December 2015, the airline\'s inaugural flight to Langkawi (RN600) took off smoothly.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airline was planning to co-operate with Royal Brunei Airlines to strengthen the Sharia-compliant airline concept within the aviation industry.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 9 April 2016, Rayani Air temporarily suspended all operations until further notice, citing \'restructuring exercises\' in an announcement made by its co-founder.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Malaysian Aviation Commission (Mavcom) announced in a statement on 13 June 2016 that Rayani Air\'s licence had been revoked, saying it had breached the conditions of its licence and lacked the financial and management capacity to continue operating as a commercial airline.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rayani_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Kuala Lumpur (Main Hub)</li>\n<li>Langkawi</li>\n<li>Kota Bharu</li>\n<li>Kuching</li>\n<li>Kota Kinabalu</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rayani_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Before ceasing operations, the airline\'s fleet consisted of two Boeing 737-400 aircraft, with the following on order: two more Boeing 737-400s, four Boeing 737-800s and two Boeing 777-200s.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rayani_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>On 9 February 2016, Rayani Air flight RN180 scheduled for <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_International_Airport_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Kuala Lumpur International Airport 2">Kuala Lumpur International Airport 2</a> was cancelled due to a broken cockpit windshield. The next day, the airline issued a press statement apologising for the flight cancellation, and attributed the shattered windshield to a previous night flight. Rayani Air said they were investigating to see whether the broken windshield was an act of sabotage, and would liaise with relevant authorities to deal with this.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<ul><li>In March 2016, Rayani Air faced several issues and numerous complaints, highlighted in several media outlets.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Passengers complained that the airline frequently rescheduled flights, often up to 13 hours later. The airline also frequently cancelled flights without advance notice and without compensation or assistance to affected passengers. It was reported that pilots refused to fly because of the poor condition of the aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Politician Lau Weng San criticised the airline for using handwritten boarding passes, posing a security threat to the aviation industry.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> This led the Minister of Transport, Datuk Seri <a href="/wiki/Liow_Tiong_Lai" title="Liow Tiong Lai">Liow Tiong Lai</a>, to state that the airline would be investigated and suspended if necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 11 April 2016, Rayani Air was officially suspended for 3 months.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Its licence was permanently revoked two months later.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
