The facility is located just north of the village <a href="/wiki/Paradeisi" class="mw-redirect" title="Paradeisi">Paradeisi</a>, about 14&#160;km southwest of the capital city, <a href="/wiki/Rhodes,_Greece" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhodes, Greece">Rodos</a>. id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhodes_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg/220px-Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg/330px-Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg/440px-Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1416" data-file-height="1064" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Flughafen_Rhodos_im_Terminal.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Check-in at Rhodes Airport</div></div></div>\n<p>Civil aviation on Rhodes started after the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, on the location of the nearby military <a href="/wiki/Rhodes_Maritsa_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhodes Maritsa Airport">Rhodes Maritsa Airport</a>.\n</p><p>This was the island\'s main airport until 1977, when the new Rhodes International Airport was opened. The need for a new facility was big, as the \'old\' Maritsa airport did not meet the needs for a modern civil airport. The new "Diagoras" airport was built in 1977. It was decided that, on this location, it would meet the needs of the island better.\n</p><p>Improvements have been made to the airport, like expansion of taxiways, platform and airport buildings. The most recent upgrade is a new passenger terminal, opened in 2005, to accommodate the growing number of charter flights and passengers. The airport\'s plants currently cover a total area of 60,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015, Rhodes Airport was closed for 14 hours after a \'<a href="/wiki/Sinkhole" title="Sinkhole">sinkhole</a>\' appeared in the runway. Flights were diverted to surrounding airports such as Kos and Heraklion as well as <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> and the runway was fixed and reopened shortly afterwards.\n</p><p>In December 2015, the privatisation of Rhodes International Airport and 13 other regional airports of Greece was finalised with the signing of the agreement between the <a href="/wiki/Fraport" title="Fraport">Fraport AG</a>/Copelouzos Group joint venture and the state privatisation fund.<sup id="cite_ref-tornosnews_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tornosnews-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> "We signed the deal today," the head of Greece\'s privatisation agency <a href="/wiki/HRADF" class="mw-redirect" title="HRADF">HRADF</a>, Stergios Pitsiorlas, told Reuters.<sup id="cite_ref-reuters_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to the agreement, the joint venture will operate the 14 airports (including Rhodes International Airport) for 40 years as of autumn 2016.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhodes_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhodes_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Rhodes Airport terminal is actually 2 separate buildings with a joined corridor in the middle. Departures are served both in ground (check-ins, baggage checks) and first floor (passenger screening, gates, shops) while arrivals are served only in ground floor\'s southern corner where passport control, customs and baggage reclaim operate. Airport does not have air bridges so boarding and disembarking require the use of buses.\n</p><p>Both terminals are and will be under renovations for the next couple of years from Fraport as a series of chronic problems need to be addressed. Toilets and air conditioning system were first to be fixed while more complicated works including new check in  facilities that will eliminate double checks for baggage as well interior remodeling will proceed gradually during low traffic winter months.\n</p><p>Airport also hosts all necessary services such as a police station, a medical clinic, airline and handlers offices, rent-a-car and tour operators kiosks, VIP halls as well recently refurbished duty free shops, cafes and restaurants.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rhodes_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport\'s single runway direction is 065/245 degrees (designated as 06/24), having a length of 3,305 meters and a width of 60 meters. There are two taxiways and four taxi links connecting the runway with the apron. The apron with the new pushback configuration can accommodate up to 20 ICAO reference category C or smaller airplanes, simultaneously. Three parking positions have MARS (Multiple Apron Ramp System) capability and can accommodate larger category D and E (such as B757-300, B787-900, B777-300, B747-400,A340-500 and A350) aircraft. There is a small general aviation apron with three dedicated positions for small propelled or jet aircraft.\n</p>\n<h2><span
