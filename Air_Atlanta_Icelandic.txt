title="Aircraft lease">ACMI</a> <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a> based in <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B3pavogur" title="K\xc3\xb3pavogur">K\xc3\xb3pavogur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It specialises in leasing aircraft on an ACMI (Aircraft, Crews, Maintenance, Insurance) and <a href="/wiki/Wet_lease" class="mw-redirect" title="Wet lease">wet lease</a> basis to airlines worldwide needing extra passenger and cargo capacity. It also operates charter services. tour</div></div></div>\n<p>The airline was established on 10 February 1986 by Captain Arngrimur Johannsson and his wife, Thora Gudmundsdottir. It started operations in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-FI_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Its first contract came when <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribbean_Airways&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caribbean Airways (page does not exist)">Caribbean Airways</a> wet-leased a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_707-320" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 707-320">Boeing 707-320</a> from them for its <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> to <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> flights. In 1988, the airline leased planes for <a href="/wiki/Air_Afrique" title="Air Afrique">Air Afrique</a> to be used during the <a href="/wiki/Hajj" title="Hajj">Hajj</a> pilgrimage trips. This would become an important part of the structure of Air Atlanta Icelandic later on. <a href="/wiki/Sudan_Airways" title="Sudan Airways">Sudan Airways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> were among the other airlines that benefited from leasing passenger airplanes from Air Atlanta Icelandic during the late 1980s, as well as <a href="/wiki/Finnair" title="Finnair">Finnair</a>, which leased a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a> from the Icelandic company.\n</p><p>It was in 1991 that Air Atlanta Icelandic was able to take passengers to the sky with its own airline operation. Its first flight was with a <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_L-1011" class="mw-redirect" title="Lockheed L-1011">Lockheed L-1011-500</a> plane. Later in 1992, Air Atlanta Icelandic participated in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> peacekeepers airlifting, flying the UN representatives from <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">former Yugoslavia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and France.\n</p><p>In 1993, the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> arrived, and <a href="/wiki/Saudia" title="Saudia">Saudia</a> became one of the first airlines to lease that plane from Air Atlanta Icelandic, also for <a href="/wiki/Hajj" title="Hajj">Hajj</a> flights. After signing a contract with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Samvinn_Travel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Samvinn Travel (page does not exist)">Samvinn Travel</a>, Air Atlanta Icelandic began operating domestic charter flights as well. Later in 1993, a flight from <a href="/wiki/Phnom_Penh" title="Phnom Penh">Phnom Penh</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a> was launched using a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737-200</a>.\n</p><p>By 1994, Air Atlanta Icelandic had obtained rights to operate service from many other countries, including the United States, from where it had a flight to <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, from where it was allowed to operate domestic charter flights. About that time, it began to offer flights within <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and began service to <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.\n</p><p>In 1996, a couple rented an Air Atlanta Icelandic <a href="/wiki/L-1011" class="mw-redirect" title="L-1011">Lockheed L-1011-500</a> to have a sky-wedding. The couple and its wedding guests were treated to a flight over the Arctic Circle, while the wedding was performed inside of the jet.\n</p><p>1997 saw the arrival of contracts with airlines such as <a href="/wiki/Britannia_Airways" title="Britannia Airways">Britannia Airways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iberia_(airline)" title="Iberia (airline)">Iberia</a>, which would use an Air Atlanta Icelandic plane for its routes from <a href="/wiki/Barajas_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Barajas International Airport">Barajas International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> to <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mart%C3%AD_International_Airport" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Mart\xc3\xad International Airport">Jos\xc3\xa9 Mart\xc3\xad International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a> and to other points in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. That year also saw the arrival of the airline\'s first Boeing 747SP plane, which would later be utilized by government officials, sports teams, <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, and others.\n</p><p>In 1998, Air Atlanta Icelandic leased planes for <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a>. In 1999, Magnus G. Thorstenn was named the company\'s new CEO. The airline became a fully wide bodied airplane airline in 1999 when it sold the last of its Boeing 737s. In 2000, <a href="/wiki/Air_India" title="Air India">Air India</a> joined the growing list of airlines that have leased airplanes from Air Atlanta Icelandic airlines.\n</p><p>In 2003, Air Atlanta Icelandic expanded into the United Kingdom with its subsidiary, <a href="/wiki/Air_Atlanta_Europe" title="Air Atlanta Europe">Air Atlanta Europe</a>, which operated <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747s</a> ad hoc, charter and for the Florida tour operator, Travel City Direct.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2004, the company acquired a 40.5% stake in the UK charter airline <a href="/wiki/Excel_Airways" class="mw-redirect" title="Excel Airways">Excel Airways</a>. That stake later increased to 76.9%. In January 2005, the Avion Group was formed, and Air Atlanta Icelandic and Islandsflug merged under the Air Atlanta Icelandic brand name.<sup id="cite_ref-FI_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2005, the Avion Group acquired <a href="/wiki/Eimskip" title="Eimskip">Eimskip</a>, a leading Icelandic sea transportation company, and Travel City Direct, a UK holiday company. In 2006, the Avion Group announced the purchase of the entire issued share capital of French charter airline <a href="/wiki/Star_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Star Airlines">Star Airlines</a>, the second largest charter airline in the French market. Star Airlines operated charter flights mainly to destinations in Africa, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean, in addition to regular services to Lebanon, Male, and cities in Mexico.\n</p><p>In June 2006, Air Atlanta Icelandic wet-leased one Boeing 747-200 to <a href="/wiki/Yangtze_River_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Yangtze River Express">Yangtze River Express</a> to operate cargo freight between Shanghai - Anchorage - Los Angeles.\n</p><p>In October 2006, the Avion Group changed its name to HF <a href="/wiki/Eimskipaf%C3%A9lag_%C3%8Dslands" class="mw-redirect" title="Eimskipaf\xc3\xa9lag \xc3\x8dslands">Eimskipaf\xc3\xa9lag \xc3\x8dslands</a>, and at the same time sold <a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Leisure_Group_Excel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="UK Leisure Group Excel (page does not exist)">UK Leisure Group Excel</a> as well as 51% of Avion Aircraft Trading.<sup id="cite_ref-FI_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A decision was also made to merge both Excel Airways and Air Atlanta Europe into one airline. This was completed by October 2006.\n</p><p>In mid-2007, the airline decided to shift its focus to <a href="/wiki/Air_Cargo" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Cargo">Air Cargo</a> operations. The airline planned to move to <a href="/wiki/Cargo_aircraft" title="Cargo aircraft">cargo aircraft</a> and laid off most of its <a href="/wiki/Passenger_aircraft" class="mw-redirect" title="Passenger aircraft">passenger aircraft</a> <a href="/wiki/Aircrew" title="Aircrew">aircrew</a>. Today, the company focuses on both passenger aircraft and cargo aircraft.\n</p><p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britflights&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Britflights (page does not exist)">Britflights</a> cancelled plans to operate aircraft from Air Atlanta Icelandic from the UK to Canada from June 2011, citing fuel uncertainties.<sup id="cite_ref-Britflights_-_About_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britflights_-_About-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Britflights_-_Where_we_fly_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britflights_-_Where_we_fly-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Air Atlanta Icelandic operated two Boeing 747-400s for <a href="/wiki/National_Airlines_(N8)" title="National Airlines (N8)">National Airlines (N8)</a> in the year 2011. One flew cargo charter flights and the other one was wet-leased to <a href="/wiki/Cargolux" title="Cargolux">Cargolux</a> for a period of time.\n</p><p>In December 2015, three former Air Atlanta Icelandic <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> registered TF-ARH, TF-ARM and TF-ARN were reported abandoned at <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur_International_Airport" title="Kuala Lumpur International Airport">Kuala Lumpur International Airport</a>. Air Atlanta Icelandic senior vice president of sales and marketing Baldvin M. Hermannsson said that the three aircraft had belonged to Air Atlanta, but were returned to their owner in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016 the airline provided a Boeing 747-428 (registration TF-AAK) to the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a> for the <a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Souls_World_Tour" title="The Book of Souls World Tour">Book of Souls World Tour</a>, which replaced a modified Astraeus Airlines 757 as "Ed Force One". On 12 March, the aircraft sustained damage to engines 1 and 2 while being towed for refueling at Santiago, Chile. The extent of the damage required the band and its crew to charter additional aircraft to continue touring through Argentina and Brazil while the aircraft awaited repairs at <a href="/wiki/Comodoro_Arturo_Merino_Ben%C3%ADtez_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Comodoro Arturo Merino Ben\xc3\xadtez International Airport">Santiago International Airport.</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Repairs involving replacing both port-side engines, cowlings, and reversers were completed by 20 March and the aircraft returned to the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Air_Atlanta_Icelandic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Air Atlanta Icelandic operates worldwide passenger and cargo flights as charters and ACMI services for various customers. For example, as of January 2012, the airline operated cargo flights on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Saudia" title="Saudia">Saudia</a> from their hub in <a href="/wiki/Jeddah" title="Jeddah">Jeddah</a> to <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hahn_Airport" title="Frankfurt\xe2\x80\x93Hahn Airport">Frankfurt (Hahn)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dhaka" title="Dhaka">Dhaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" Arabian Airlines">Saudi Arabian Airlines</a> as Flight 810 (TF-ARS) from <a href="/wiki/Prince_Mohammad_Bin_Abdulaziz_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Mohammad Bin Abdulaziz Airport">Prince Mohammad Bin Abdulaziz Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madinah" class="mw-redirect" title="Madinah">Madinah</a> made an unscheduled landing at <a href="/wiki/Zia_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" During the roll the tower controller reported that he saw a fire on the right hand wing. Upon vacating the runway, the crew received a fire indication for engine number three.
