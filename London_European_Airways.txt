class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>London European Airways</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> airline based at <a href="/wiki/Luton_Airport" title="Luton Airport">Luton Airport</a> that operated services from the United Kingdom to Amsterdam and Brussels in the late 1980s. It was taken over by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and operated as <b>Ryanair Europe</b>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_European_Airways&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The company was formed in 1984 and applied to operate a business class service between <a href="/wiki/Luton_Airport" title="Luton Airport">Luton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Airport_Schiphol" title="Amsterdam Airport Schiphol">Amsterdam</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Vickers_Viscount" title="Vickers Viscount">Vickers Viscount</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following the approval from the Department of Transport (DoT) to operate the route the twice-daily service started on 25 February 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-flight2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The approval was challenged by Luton-based airline <a href="/w/index.php?title=Euroflite&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Euroflite (page does not exist)">Euroflite</a>, the DoT then changed the approval to allow both operators on the Luton-Amsterdam route.<sup id="cite_ref-flight2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The airline was forced by this action to purchase Euroflite for \xc2\xa3300,000.<sup id="cite_ref-flight3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 17 February 1986, the airline suspended services following financial problems and the temporary removal of the operating licence.<sup id="cite_ref-flight3_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In November 1986 a \xc2\xa3630,000 financial rescue package was proposed by Cathal Ryan and other directors of the Irish-airline <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, they would then own 85% of the company.<sup id="cite_ref-flight3_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The proposal was accepted by the shareholders, the new owners decided that LEA and Ryanair would remain separate companies although Ryanair flights from Ireland would inter-line with the LEA services from Luton.<sup id="cite_ref-flight5_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight5-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airline was re-launched in April 1987 using a leased twin-engined <a href="/wiki/BAC_One-Eleven" title="BAC One-Eleven">BAC One-Eleven 500</a> jet airliner, with services to Amsterdam and Brussels starting on 22 May 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-flight5_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight5-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-flight7_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 1988, the airline was renamed <b>Ryanair Europe</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-flight8_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight8-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 1989, the airline ended its scheduled service to Brussels and started to concentrate on being a charter airline for Ryanair.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By 1991, the airline had reverted to the London European name and was operating five <a href="/wiki/BAC_One-Eleven" title="BAC One-Eleven">BAC One-Elevens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-flight9_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flight9-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> With the parent Ryanair making losses and move by them to fly from Stansted<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> London European Airways ceased to operate.\n</p>\n<h2><span
