2004 as <b>Amira Air</b> by investor Ronny Pecik as a provider of charter flights for executive travel, serving European and international destinations.\n</p><p>In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> took over Amira Air and renamed the company <b>Laudamotion</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Until February 2018, the airline operated chartered business flights with a fleet of business jets. The operation was moved to a new unit to make way for scheduled passenger flights.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 23 January 2018, the creditors\' committee of the insolvent airline <a href="/wiki/Niki_(airline)" title="Niki (airline)">Niki</a> agreed to sell the airline to Lauda Motion GmbH. It was announced that the Niki brand would be retired and all operations transferred to Laudamotion, which would operate 15 aircraft transferred from Niki and be ready to begin operations for the summer 2018 season.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 16 February 2018, <a href="/wiki/Condor_Flugdienst" class="mw-redirect" title="Condor Flugdienst">Condor</a> agreed to take over sales and marketing of three Laudamotion planes, as well as operational functions. Initial destinations will included flights from <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="D\xc3\xbcsseldorf">D\xc3\xbcsseldorf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a> to <a href="/wiki/Majorca" class="mw-redirect" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="M\xc3\xa1laga">M\xc3\xa1laga</a>, which could be booked through the Condor website and travel agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-relaunch_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-relaunch-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Takeover_by_Ryanair">Takeover by Ryanair</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauda_(airline)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Takeover by Ryanair">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 20 March 2018, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced it would acquire 24.9% of LaudaMotion, to rise to 75%, subject to approval by EU authorities. The deal was reported to be worth an initial \xe2\x82\xac50m plus \xe2\x82\xac50m for first year start up and operating costs. The intention was to reach profitability by the third year of operations with a fleet of 30 aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 29 March 2018, the airline announced it would open seven bases in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> in addition to the bases at <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zurich" class="mw-redirect" title="Zurich">Zurich</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-base_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-base-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, in May 2018 Laudamotion announced it would not go ahead with proposed routes to and from <a href="/wiki/Zurich_Airport" title="Zurich Airport">Zurich Airport</a> owing to unavailability of leased aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2018, <a href="/wiki/Condor_(airline)" title="Condor (airline)">Condor</a> announced it was ending its cooperation and sales agreement with Laudamotion earlier than planned. Condor stopped providing sales infrastructure and stopped selling Laudamotion flights at the end of April 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the same months, <a href="/wiki/Eurowings" title="Eurowings">Eurowings</a> also announced it would not extend its short-lived cooperation with Laudamotion. The wet-lease contract ended on 31 May 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-EWLM_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EWLM-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2018, Ryanair announced it had increased its stake in the airline to 75% and that it planned to double the fleet from 9 to 18 aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Laudamotion was scheduled to receive 18 Airbus A320 aircraft by summer 2019, which were to replace and expand its current fleet.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In January 2019, Ryanair revealed that it completed the purchase of 100% of Laudamotion from NL Holdings in late December 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-jan19_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jan19-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It also revealed further fleet expansion plans, increasing from 18 to 25 aircraft by end of 2019, and 40 by summer 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-jan19_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jan19-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Initially, the plan was for Laudamotion\'s fleet to remain exclusively Airbus, despite Ryanair only operating Boeing aircraft, and in March 2019, Laudamotion announced plans to purchase at least 100 (50+50 options) <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A321neo" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A321neo">Airbus A321neo</a> for future expansions.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> However, during an interview in May 2020 (amidst the <a href="/wiki/Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic_on_aviation" title="Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on aviation">COVID-19 crisis</a>) Ryanair CEO <a href="/wiki/Michael_O%27Leary_(businessman)" title="Michael O&#39;Leary (businessman)">Michael O\'Leary</a> said, \xe2\x80\x9cI think Lauda will have a fleet of about 30 Airbus aircraft - we would probably replace those Airbus with Boeing over the next couple of years\xe2\x80\x9d.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2019, the airline changed its <a href="/wiki/Brand_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Brand name">brand name</a> from Laudamotion to just <b>Lauda</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-touristikaktuell_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-touristikaktuell-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 2019, Lauda announced the opening of its fifth base, at <a href="/wiki/Zadar_Airport" title="Zadar Airport">Zadar Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> shifting over further capacity from parent Ryanair.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, the Zadar base was scrapped amidst the Coronavirus pandemic with a possible start delayed to 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2020, Lauda announced the planned closure of its largest base at <a href="/wiki/Vienna_International_Airport" title="Vienna International Airport">Vienna International Airport</a> on 29 May 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-ViennaClosing_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ViennaClosing-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The planned closure was a source of conflict between Lauda and Ryanair, the labour union and the Austrian government.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In June 2020, Ryanair decided that Lauda flights would be operated under its own <i>FR</i> code, downgrading Lauda from an airline to an inhouse <a href="/wiki/Wetlease" class="mw-redirect" title="Wetlease">wetlease</a> operator.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> This includes the Vienna base which now will remain open in that function with the exception of flights to <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a> which remains under the Lauda brand due to traffic rights.\n</p><p>At the same time, Ryanair cancelled all remaining <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320_family" title="Airbus A320 family">Airbus A320 family</a> orders for Lauda.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2020, Lauda announced they would shut down their base at <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart_Airport" title="Stuttgart Airport">Stuttgart Airport</a> by October 2020. Prior to this announcement, the base staff rejected a new labour agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In late July 2020, it was announced that Laudamotion GmbH would cease operations at the end of 2020, with the fleet being transferred to Malta-based <b>Lauda Europe</b>. The impact on operations is expected to be minimal. The new company will be the second Ryanair group entity to be based in Malta, alongside <a href="/wiki/Malta_Air" title="Malta Air">Malta Air</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2020, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> announced the closure of its base in D\xc3\xbcsseldorf as well - which has been operated on a wetlease basis by Lauda - by 24 October 2020, leaving Lauda without bases in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of 31 October 2020, Lauda ceased all operations with its assets being handed over to <a href="/wiki/Lauda_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Lauda Europe">Lauda Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-laudach-aviation_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laudach-aviation-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span
