K\xc3\xb6ln/Bonn \'Konrad Adenauer\'</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>CGN</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EDDK</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>\'s fourth-largest city <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, and also serves <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a>, former capital of <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>. With around 12.4 million passengers passing through it in 2017, it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_busiest_airports_in_Germany" title="List of the busiest airports in Germany">seventh-largest passenger airport in Germany</a> and the third-largest in terms of cargo operations. By traffic units, which combines cargo and passengers, the airport is in fifth position in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As of March 2015, Cologne Bonn Airport had services to 115 passenger destinations in 35 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is named after <a href="/wiki/Konrad_Adenauer" title="Konrad Adenauer">Konrad Adenauer</a>, a Cologne native and the first post-war Chancellor of West Germany.\n</p><p>The airport is located in the district of <a href="/wiki/Porz" title="Porz">Porz</a> and is surrounded by the <i>Wahner Heide</i> nature reserve. The airport is centrally located in the <a href="/wiki/Cologne_Bonn_Region" title="Cologne Bonn Region">Cologne Bonn Region</a> 12&#160;km (7.5&#160;mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Innenstadt,_Cologne" title="Innenstadt, Cologne">Cologne city centre</a> and 16&#160;km (9.9&#160;mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a>. Cologne Bonn Airport is one of the country\'s few 24-hour airports and serves as a hub for <a href="/wiki/Eurowings" title="Eurowings">Eurowings</a>, <a href="/wiki/FedEx_Express" title="FedEx Express">FedEx Express</a><sup id="cite_ref-cgnfedexhub_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cgnfedexhub-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/UPS_Airlines" title="UPS Airlines">UPS Airlines</a><sup id="cite_ref-cgnupshub_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cgnupshub-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as well as a focus city for several leisure and low-cost airlines. It is also a host of the German and European space agencies <a href="/wiki/German_Aerospace_Center" title="German Aerospace Center">DLR</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Astronaut_Centre" title="European Astronaut Centre">EAC</a>, part of <a href="/wiki/ESA" class="mw-redirect" title="ESA">ESA</a>, which train <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronauts</a> there for <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space explorations</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><div class="mediaContainer" style="width:220px"><audio id="mwe_player_0" controls="" preload="none" style="width:220px" class="kskin thumbimage" data-durationhint="4.693537414966" data-startoffset="0" data-mwtitle="CGN_Audiologo.ogg" data-mwprovider="wikimediacommons"><source src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2a/CGN_Audiologo.ogg" type="audio/ogg; class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Airport_K%C3%B6ln_Skyline.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Airport_K%C3%B6ln_Skyline.jpg/440px-Airport_K%C3%B6ln_Skyline.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Airport_K%C3%B6ln_Skyline.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Airport in front of Cologne\'s skyline</div></div></div>\n<p>In 1913, the first plane took off from the Wahner Heide military training area on an artillery reconnaissance flight. In 1939 an airfield was built for the German <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the British military took over and expanded the airport (as RAF Wahn). A 1,866 m runway was built in this period. In 1951 the airport was opened for civilian air traffic, superseding the former <a href="/wiki/Cologne_Butzweilerhof_Airport" title="Cologne Butzweilerhof Airport">Cologne Butzweilerhof Airport</a>.\n</p><p>During the 1950s and 1960s two additional runways and a new passenger terminal were constructed. On 1 November 1970 a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> took off for <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> from the airport for the first time.\n</p><p>Cologne Bonn Airport was chosen by <a href="/wiki/United_Parcel_Service" title="United Parcel Service">United Parcel Service</a> (UPS) in 1986 as the location for their European hub.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In the late 1990s the airport started an expansion program. Several new parking lots and a second terminal were built, and in 2004 a new long-distance railway station was opened.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_in_the_2000s">Development in the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cologne_Bonn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development in the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Coinciding with the start of several <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost airlines</a> in Germany, Cologne/Bonn opened new capacities. This enabled the airport to make competitive offers to the airlines. Consequently, <a href="/wiki/Germanwings" title="Germanwings">Germanwings</a> and <a href="/wiki/TUIfly" class="mw-redirect" title="TUIfly">TUIfly</a> started operations from Cologne/Bonn as their hub in the fall of 2002. As a result, the number of passengers in 2003 rose by 43% compared to 2002. These airlines were joined by <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">easyJet</a> in late 2003 and <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a> in June 2006.\n</p><p>Also, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Forces</a> began to use the airport as a staging area to move troops and supplies in support of humanitarian missions and possible anti-terrorism roles.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2006 the Brazilian airline BRA provided a twice a week connection to <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro%E2%80%93Gale%C3%A3o_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio de Janeiro\xe2\x80\x93Gale\xc3\xa3o International Airport">Rio de Janeiro\xe2\x80\x93Gale\xc3\xa3o</a>, which was discontinued in April 2007 due to problems with the airline. Also in 2006 a daily <a href="/wiki/Transatlantic_flight" title="Transatlantic flight">transatlantic flight</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Newark_Liberty_International_Airport" title="Newark Liberty International Airport">Newark Liberty International Airport</a> by <a href="/wiki/Continental_Airlines" title="Continental Airlines">Continental Airlines</a> was established, operating with a Boeing 757-200. This route was discontinued on 4 September 2008 due to a reduction in passenger numbers.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Low-cost carriers <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Air_Shuttle" title="Norwegian Air Shuttle">Norwegian Air Shuttle</a> began service to Cologne/Bonn in May 2012. In April 2014 Ryanair announced the opening of their fifth German base at Cologne/Bonn Airport for October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In December 2014, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> announced it would base <a href="/wiki/Eurowings" title="Eurowings">Eurowings</a>\' new long-haul operations at Cologne Bonn Airport with flights to <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> to start by the end of 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Condor_Flugdienst" class="mw-redirect" title="Condor Flugdienst">Condor</a> cancelled their service to <a href="/wiki/Varadero" title="Varadero">Varadero</a> after only one season due to the expected competition.<sup href="/w/index.php?title=Cologne_Bonn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Terminal 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The older Terminal 1 is a 1970s building that sports large surfaces of bare concrete in its design. It features a u-shaped main building with shops, restaurants, check-in and service facilities and a visitors deck on its roof as well as the star-shaped piers B and C with five aircraft stands each plus a central airside hall between them added in 2004 with joint security-check facilities, more shops and restaurants as well as three additional stands. All ten stands at both piers feature <a href="/wiki/Jet_bridge" title="Jet bridge">jet bridges</a> while the other three use walk-boarding. Also several bus-boarding stands are available at the apron. Terminal 1 is used by <a href="/wiki/Eurowings" title="Eurowings">Eurowings</a>, which occupy most of the landside check-in facilities, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Airlines" title="Austrian Airlines">Austrian Airlines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-airportmap_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airportmap-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Terminal 1 features its own direct connection to the railway station.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal_2">Terminal 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cologne_Bonn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Terminal 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Construction of Terminal 2 began in June 1997, and operations at the terminal commenced on 21 June 2000. It is located to the north of Terminal 1. Both feature separate check-in facilities but are connected through a landside walkway. As part of a plan-approval procedure the airport is currently seeking approval for building an airside connection between both terminals. Terminal 2 is a modern-style rectangular building made out of glass and steel which is equipped with eight stands with jet bridges as well as several stands for bus-boarding. It is used by several airlines such as <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran_Air" title="Iran Air">Iran Air</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-airportmap_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airportmap-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Terminal 2 is also directly connected to the airports\' railway station via the basement level. The terminal hosts an interdenominational prayer room on its base level.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_use">Military use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cologne_Bonn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Military use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The German Air Force\'s <a href="/wiki/Executive_Transport_Wing_(German_Air_Force)" title="Executive Transport Wing (German Air Force)">Executive Transport Wing</a> is based on the south-western side of the airport, adjacent to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wahn_Air_Force_Barracks&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wahn Air Force Barracks (page does not exist)">Wahn Air Force Barracks</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luftwaffenkaserne_(K%C3%B6ln)" class="extiw" title="de:Luftwaffenkaserne (K\xc3\xb6ln)">de</a>&#93;</span>. The military part of the airport is also used by the Canadian Forces as an operational support hub.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg/220px-Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg/330px-Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg/440px-Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1408" data-file-height="1056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bahnhof_Flughafen_K%C3%B6ln-Bonn.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Platforms at Cologne/Bonn Airport station</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Cologne/Bonn_Airport_station" title="Cologne/Bonn Airport station">Cologne/Bonn Airport station</a> is a 4-track railway station on a <a href="/wiki/Cologne_Airport_Loop" class="mw-redirect" title="Cologne Airport Loop">loop</a> off the <a href="/wiki/Cologne%E2%80%93Frankfurt_high-speed_rail_line" title="Cologne\xe2\x80\x93Frankfurt high-speed rail line">Cologne\xe2\x80\x93Frankfurt high-speed line</a> that connects Cologne Bonn Airport to long-distance trains at least once an hour per direction, most of them <a href="/wiki/Intercity-Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Intercity-Express">ICE</a> services. The station lies directly across both terminals under a large glass roof and features direct connections to the basement of Terminal 2 as well as the check-in area at Terminal 1-C. The <a href="/wiki/S-Bahn" class="mw-redirect" title="S-Bahn">S-Bahn</a> line <i>S 19</i> to <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCren" title="D\xc3\xbcren">D\xc3\xbcren</a> and regional train line <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_regional_railway_lines_in_North_Rhine-Westphalia#Lines_1\xe2\x80\x939" title="List of regional railway lines in North Rhine-Westphalia">RE 6</a></i> to <a href="/wiki/Minden_(Westfalen)_station" title="Minden (Westfalen) station">Minden (Westfalen) </a> connect the airport station with via <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ln_Hauptbahnhof" title="K\xc3\xb6ln Hauptbahnhof">Cologne Main Station</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_regional_railway_lines_in_North_Rhine-Westphalia#Lines_20\xe2\x80\x9329" title="List of regional railway lines in North Rhine-Westphalia">RB 27</a></i> between <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6nchengladbach_Hauptbahnhof" title="M\xc3\xb6nchengladbach Hauptbahnhof">M\xc3\xb6nchengladbach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Koblenz_Hauptbahnhof" title="Koblenz Hauptbahnhof">Koblenz</a> calls at the airport station as well. <sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cologne_Bonn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has its own exit (named <i>Flughafen</i>) on <a href="/wiki/Bundesautobahn_59" title="Bundesautobahn 59">motorway A59</a> which links it to the city centres of Cologne and Bonn as well as the <a href="/wiki/Ruhrgebiet" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruhrgebiet">Ruhrgebiet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-transport_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-transport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cologne_Bonn_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Local bus lines also connect the airport with Cologne (route <i>161</i>) and Bonn (route <i>SB60</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-transport_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-transport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On 28 October 2015, a new coach terminal opened and is used for remote bus services to other German cities and many other European countries.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
