information.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">July 2015</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>vertical seat</b> (<b>standing seat</b>, <b>stand-up seat</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Airline_seat" title="Airline seat">airline seat</a> configuration, created in 2003 by <a href="/wiki/Airbus" title="Airbus">Airbus</a>, canvassed to Asian carriers in 2006, and promoted by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Spring_Airlines" title="Spring Airlines">Spring Airlines</a> in 2010s. The configuration involves <a href="/wiki/Standing_passenger" title="Standing passenger">standing passengers</a> travelling while being braced in position.\n</p><p>The configuration and all designs have not yet been approved by regulatory agencies. There is no bar to such a configuration in <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> (US) regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-2006-04-25_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2006-04-25-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, according to <a href="/wiki/Transport_Canada" title="Transport Canada">Transport Canada</a> (the agency responsible for aviation safety in Canada), vertical seats do not conform to Canadian seating requirements.<sup id="cite_ref-TorStar-2010-07-02_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TorStar-2010-07-02-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Airbus" title="Airbus">Airbus</a> was quietly polling <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airlines</a> to see about the viability of a perch seat,<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-2006-04-25_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2006-04-25-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which it had created in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-TorStar-2010-07-02_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TorStar-2010-07-02-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> promulgated a vertical seat design for use in its aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph-2010-07-01_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-2010-07-01-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Both the <a href="/wiki/European_Aviation_Safety_Agency" title="European Aviation Safety Agency">European Aviation Safety Agency</a> and UK <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">Civil Aviation Authority</a> were skeptical as to the design being able to meet licensing requirements. The design was expected to start undergoing safety trials in the 2011-2012 time frame. It has already been rejected by <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-SMH-2010-07-02_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMH-2010-07-02-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> as it believes that the design would not meet the 16G regulatory requirement.<sup id="cite_ref-TorStar-2010-07-02_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TorStar-2010-07-02-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tigerair_Australia" title="Tigerair Australia">Tigerair Australia</a> announced that it would also pursue the Ryanair design,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> as has its parent, <a href="/wiki/Tigerair" title="Tigerair">Tigerair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BN-2010-07-05_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BN-2010-07-05-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to UK aviation law, passengers require a seatbelt on landing and takeoff, so the lack of a seat may preclude the design from being legal in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-NYDN-2010-07-03_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYDN-2010-07-03-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Ryanair design would have passengers perched on a narrow shelf, with their weight taken up by their legs, and their back against the seatback, strapped in, with additional shoulder restraints similar to rollercoaster overhead restraints.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The configuration is superficially similar to the <a href="/wiki/SkyRider_(seat)" title="SkyRider (seat)">SkyRider</a> <a href="/wiki/Saddle_seat" title="Saddle seat">saddle seat</a> design proposed by <a href="/wiki/Aviointeriors" title="Aviointeriors">Aviointeriors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NatPost-2010-09-15_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NatPost-2010-09-15-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015, Chinese airline Spring Airlines expressed interest in using the vertical seat concept for consumer flights.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, the Italian company Aviointeriors revealed its design for vertical seats.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
