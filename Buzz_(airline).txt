href="/w/index.php?title=Buzz_(airline)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">September 2009</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The airline was established in 1999 and started operations on 4 January 2000. It was launched by KLM as a sub-brand of <a href="/wiki/KLM_uk" title="KLM uk">KLM uk</a> to compete with other <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carriers</a> such as <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">EasyJet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Go_Fly" class="mw-redirect" title="Go Fly">Go Fly</a> and Ryanair by taking over many of the point-to-point routes of the former <a href="/wiki/AirUK" title="AirUK">AirUK</a>/KLM UK network. It started with 8 <a href="/wiki/BAe_146" class="mw-redirect" title="BAe 146">BAe 146</a>-300 aircraft transferred from the KLM uk brand which retained the Amsterdam feeder routes (transferred to <a href="/wiki/KLM_Cityhopper" title="KLM Cityhopper">KLM Cityhopper</a> in November 2002). This was shortly followed by two leased ex-<a href="/wiki/Deutsche_BA" class="mw-redirect" title="Deutsche BA">Deutsche BA</a> Boeing <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737#737-300" title="Boeing 737">737-300</a> aircraft. In late 2002, buzz leased from <a href="/wiki/ILFC" class="mw-redirect" title="ILFC">ILFC</a> another 6 <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737#737-300" title="Boeing 737">737-300s</a> previously operated by <a href="/wiki/Continental_Airlines" title="Continental Airlines">Continental Airlines</a> and the first 2 737s were returned to lessor Shananda in early 2003. Buzz also was the first low-cost airline trying to attract the high yield business travel segment, especially by promoting its flights to/from London Stansted to Frankfurt (international airport) and D\xc3\xbcsseldorf. Buzz had a local commercial manager in Germany (2000\xe2\x80\x932003) who established the first direct contacts to corporates, TM\'s (Travel Managers) and TMC\'s (Travel Management Companies). \n</p><p>On 31 January 2003 KLM announced its intention to sell Buzz to Ryanair, estimated to be in the region of \xe2\x82\xac20.1 million, and was completed on 10 April 2003 following approval from the UK <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Fair_Trading" title="Office of Fair Trading">Office of Fair Trading</a> (OFT). In addition to acquiring Buzz slots at <a href="/wiki/London_Stansted_Airport" title="London Stansted Airport">London Stansted Airport</a>, Ryanair acquired the leases of all 6 of the <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a> 737s and sub-leased 4 of the <a href="/wiki/BAe_146" class="mw-redirect" title="BAe 146">BAe 146s</a> from KLM.\n</p><p>In order to capitalise on the acquisition and save costs, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> restarted Buzz as a wholly owned subsidiary called <b>Buzz Stansted</b>, which retained the 10 aircraft on the UK register. Several of the original Buzz operations staff were transferred to the new organisation and an application was made for a new Air Operators Certificate (AOC). After route proving flights on 25 April 2003 an AOC was issued by the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">British Civil Aviation Authority</a> on 28 April 2003 with the first revenue sectors being operated the same day.\n</p><p>The aircraft fleet, the majority of which retained the Buzz livery, flew several of the original Buzz routes and some Ryanair routes that had been operated by 737-200s under the Ryanair call sign. However, the BAe 146 aircraft were returned to KLM in January 2004 and the 737s continued operating the remaining routes that were not dropped following the withdrawal of the 146s.\n</p><p>In September 2004, Ryanair decided to close Buzz Stansted and flying operations ceased on 31 October 2004. The 737 aircraft were returned to <a href="/wiki/ILFC" class="mw-redirect" title="ILFC">ILFC</a> and the Ryanair 737-800s took over from the Buzz aircraft. Ryanair also discontinued some of the original Buzz routes and redirected others to other airports as they were considered nearby to where existing Ryanair services operated.\n</p>\n<h3><span
