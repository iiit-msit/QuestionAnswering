class="external text" href="http://stobartair.com">stobartair<wbr />.com</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Stobart Air</b>, legally incorporated as <i>Stobart Air <a href="/wiki/Unlimited_company" title="Unlimited company">Unlimited Company</a></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Regional_airline" title="Regional airline">regional airline</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It operates scheduled services under the brands <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus_Regional" title="Aer Lingus Regional">Aer Lingus Regional</a>, <a href="/wiki/BA_CityFlyer" title="BA CityFlyer">BA CityFlyer</a> and <a href="/wiki/KLM_Cityhopper" title="KLM Cityhopper">KLM Cityhopper</a> on behalf of their respective owners. Stobart Air has operating bases at <a href="/wiki/Cork_Airport" title="Cork Airport">Cork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man_Airport" title="Isle of Man Airport">Isle of Man</a> for Aer Lingus Regional. The airline employs over 570 people.<sup id="cite_ref-aviationtribune.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aviationtribune.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Established as <b>Aer Arann</b> 1970, a major refinancing in 2014 was associated with a name change to <i>Stobart Air</i>. <b>Aer Arann</b> (styled as <b>Aer Arann Regional</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Regional_airline" title="Regional airline">regional airline</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The airline operated scheduled services on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus_Regional" title="Aer Lingus Regional">Aer Lingus Regional</a>. The airline had three bases in <a href="/wiki/Cork_Airport" title="Cork Airport">Cork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Airport" title="Shannon Airport">Shannon</a> which operated under the Aer Lingus Regional brand.\n</p><p>On 19 March 2014, Aer Arann announced that it would be changing its corporate name to Stobart Air by the end of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aer_Arann_Express_logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aer Arann\'s former logo, used between 1998 and 2003</div></div></div>\n<p>Aer Arann was established in 1970 by James Coen and Ralph Langan to provide an island-hopping air service between <a href="/wiki/Galway" title="Galway">Galway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Aran_Islands" title="Aran Islands">Aran Islands</a> off the west coast of Ireland. Operations, using a single <a href="/wiki/Britten-Norman_Islander" class="mw-redirect" title="Britten-Norman Islander">Britten-Norman Islander</a>, began in August 1970. This service has since been moved to the less distant <a href="/wiki/Connemara_Airport" title="Connemara Airport">Connemara Airport</a> and operates as "<a href="/wiki/Aer_Arann_Islands" title="Aer Arann Islands">Aer Arann Islands</a>". As of 2015 it still used Islander aircraft.\n</p><p>The turning point for the airline was in 1994 when <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1draig_%C3%93_C%C3%A9idigh" title="P\xc3\xa1draig \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh">P\xc3\xa1draig \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh</a> and Eugene O\'Kelly purchased the airline. \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh and O\'Kelly began to expand the airline\'s routes and fleet, launching scheduled services in 1998. Also in 1998, the Irish government awarded the airline the <a href="/wiki/Public_service_obligation" title="Public service obligation">Public service obligation</a> (PSO) route between <a href="/wiki/Donegal_Airport" title="Donegal Airport">Donegal Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin Airport</a> followed by the PSO route between <a href="/wiki/Sligo_Airport" title="Sligo Airport">Sligo Airport</a> and Dublin.\n</p><p>In 2002, service to the United Kingdom and <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> was introduced, followed by service to <a href="/wiki/Lorient" title="Lorient">Lorient</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nantes" title="Nantes">Nantes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a> in 2004.\n</p><p>In 2007, Aer Arann had a turnover of \xe2\x82\xac100 million and passenger numbers in excess of 1.15 million.\n</p><p>In 2008, in recognition of the company\'s marketing achievements, the Head of Sales and Marketing, Colin Lewis was awarded the All Ireland Marketing Leader of the Year Award by the Marketing Institute of Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2008, Aer Arann entered into a franchise agreement with <a href="/wiki/Nex_Aviation" title="Nex Aviation">Nex Aviation</a> and introduced service to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="M\xc3\xa1laga">M\xc3\xa1laga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faro,_Portugal" title="Faro, Portugal">Faro</a>, using <a href="/wiki/British_Aerospace_146" title="British Aerospace 146">British Aerospace 146</a> aircraft during the summer months. The Amsterdam service, which was operated by an ATR 72-500, ended on 14 January 2009.\n</p><p>In October 2008, the airline announced that it would be implementing a cost-saving programme, due to the downturn in passenger traffic, which would involve some focus on charter flights and leasing of aircraft and their crews to other airlines.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The airline stated that up to 100 redundancies would be needed, but this was later revised to 70 which would represent a 20% reduction of staff.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The airline\'s key routes were to remain unchanged.\n</p><p>In January 2010, Aer Arann and <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a> founded <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus_Regional" title="Aer Lingus Regional">Aer Lingus Regional</a> which effectively removed Aer Arann\'s hubs at Cork and Dublin under the new airline.\n</p><p>In July 2010, Aer Arann confirmed it would end flights between Cork-Belfast and Cork-Dublin from 31 August 2010. The company\'s press release stated, <i>"we sincerely regret having to suspend these services and the inconvenience that it will cause our loyal customers on both routes. Unfortunately however we have an aircraft leaving the fleet and this has an obvious but regrettable impact on our schedule.</i> Aer Arann had operated flights on the Cork to Dublin route since 2001 and began service from Cork to Belfast in March 2004 after the demise of the airline <a href="/wiki/JetMagic" title="JetMagic">JetMagic</a>. Aer Arann now no longer operates from Cork Airport in its own entity but continues to expand its presence at Cork under the Aer Lingus Regional brand.\n</p><p>On 26 August 2010 an <a href="/wiki/Examinership" title="Examinership">examiner</a> was appointed to Aer Arann. A full hearing of the company\'s application took place on 8 September and Aer Arann was in interim <a href="/wiki/Examinership" title="Examinership">examinership</a> under the protection of the Court. All Aer Arann flights continued to operate normally. It was understood by the high court in Dublin that Aer Arann would return to profitability in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2010, British logistics company <a href="/wiki/Stobart_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Stobart Group">Stobart Group</a> was named as the preferred buyer for the airline, also announcing that the airline would serve <a href="/wiki/London_Southend_Airport" title="London Southend Airport">London Southend Airport</a> from March 2011 to destinations in Ireland and possibly France and open a base there.<sup id="cite_ref-sbpost_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sbpost-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sstandard_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sstandard-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 10 November 2010, the deal was completed. Aer Arann is now owned by Everdeal Limited, which at the time became 67.5% owned by P\xc3\xa1draig \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh, Aer Arann\'s Chairman; 27.5% owned by Tim Kilroe, a UK-based businessman; and 5% owned by the <a href="/wiki/Stobart_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Stobart Group">Stobart Group</a>, through 35 preference shares of \xe2\x82\xac1 each acquired on 10 November 2010. Stobart Group entered into an option to increase its shareholding by a further 27.5% to 32.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-independent.ie_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independent.ie-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-stobartgroup.co.uk_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stobartgroup.co.uk-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 7 April 2011, Aer Arann confirmed it would end its PSO (Public Service Obligation) routes after the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Government">Irish Government</a> withdrew funding for the routes, which included Dublin to Galway, Knock and Sligo.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2011, Aer Arann announced it would suspend all services from Galway Airport, axed routes included Galway to Edinburgh, London-Luton, London-Southend, Manchester and Lorient. The company said the routes were "financially no longer viable". Galway Airport then confirmed it would have to make <a href="/wiki/Layoff" title="Layoff">redundant</a> 40 staff due to losing the airport\'s only operator.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 8 February 2012, Aer Arann\'s Chief Executive Paul Schutz resigned, and was replaced by Sean Brogan, the head of Stobart Ireland. This gave Stobart Group management control of the airline, and prompted speculation that it would exercise its option to increase its 5% stake in Everdeal Limited by a further 27.5% to 32.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-thesundaytimes.co.uk_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thesundaytimes.co.uk-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 2 March 2012, it was confirmed that Aer Arann was in final discussions to transfer all of its own operated flights to the Aer Lingus Regional brand. This would mean Aer Arann would operate on behalf of Aer Lingus Regional with the airline no longer operating routes as its own entity.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 14 March 2012, Aer Arann confirmed it would no longer exist in its own entity from 10 April 2012. All its services transferred to Aer Lingus Regional on 25 March, streamlining services to a single brand,<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with the one remaining route operated on an RE flight number, London City to Isle of Man, ceasing on 10 April.\n</p><p>On 6 November 2012, Aer Arann announced it was pulling out of Waterford, as its services had not performed to a "sustainable level". However, it did not rule out returning to Waterford in the future, "if the business case can be justified".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 12 December 2012, Stobart Group exercised its option to increase its 5% shareholding in Everdeal Limited by 27.5% to 32.5%, and acquired a further 12.5% of the company, taking its stake to 45%. This diluted Chairman P\xc3\xa1draig \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh\'s shareholding to 47.5% and Tim Kilroe\'s shareholding to 7.5%.<sup id="cite_ref-ainonline.com_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ainonline.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 25 January 2013, Aer Arann\'s Chairman P\xc3\xa1draig \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh resigned. Stobart Group was therefore expected to buy his 47.5% shareholding in Everdeal Limited by the beginning of February 2013. This would have enabled it to have a 92.5% controlling stake in the airline.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 30 April 2013, Aer Arann\'s ownership was restructured, under the ownership of Everdeal Holdings Limited, which is in turn 45% owned by the <a href="/wiki/Stobart_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Stobart Group">Stobart Group</a>; 42% owned by <a href="/wiki/Invesco" title="Invesco">Invesco</a>; 8% owned by Cenkos Securities; and 5% owned by P\xc3\xa1draig \xc3\x93 C\xc3\xa9idigh, Aer Arann\'s former Chairman. Stobart Group had an option to acquire complete control of the airline, by increasing its shareholding by a further 55% to 100%.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_20-0" class="reference"><a id="cite_ref-tca.ie_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tca.ie-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Stobart Air announced on 25 March 2014, it was diversifying away from operating exclusively for <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a> by enacting a five-year agreement with <a href="/wiki/Flybe" title="Flybe">Flybe</a> to operate six routes in Northern Europe and Benelux from 5 June 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-echo-news.co.uk_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-echo-news.co.uk-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-southendairport.com_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-southendairport.com-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> It was further announced in November 2014 that Stobart Air was also to operate two routes from <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> to <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orly_Airport" title="Orly Airport">Paris-Orly</a>, on behalf of <a href="/wiki/CityJet" title="CityJet">CityJet</a>, from 1 December 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The latter two routes have since (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>On 24 February 2017, Stobart Group completed 100% acquisition of Stobart Air, after purchasing the remaining 19% of Everdeal Holdings. Stobart Group also completed 100% ownership of Propius Holdings Ltd, the aircraft leasing firm through which many of the Stobart Air aircraft are sourced.<sup id="cite_ref-lse.co.uk_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lse.co.uk-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of April 2018, Stobart Air wet leased two E195s from Flybe. From the second half of 2018, Stobart Air\'s leasing firm Propius acquired three E195s from leasing firm GOAL. These aircraft were leased to Flybe until the end of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 11 January 2019, Stobart Aviation and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Atlantic" title="Virgin Atlantic">Virgin Atlantic</a> formed the <a href="/wiki/Connect_Airways" title="Connect Airways">Connect Airways</a> consortium to make a takeover bid for Flybe.<sup id="cite_ref-fg-20190111_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fg-20190111-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The consortium was also to take over Stobart Air, with the aim of creating an integrated carrier operating under a Virgin brand.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-20190111_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-20190111-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Stobart Air\'s wet lease operations for other airlines would continue unchanged.<sup id="cite_ref-insidermedia-20190111_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-insidermedia-20190111-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On 15 January 2019, Connect Airways announced an increased offer, which Flybe\'s board accepted.<sup id="cite_ref-atwonline-20190115_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atwonline-20190115-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The deal was completed on 21 February 2019, despite a last-minute rival bid.<sup id="cite_ref-cityam-20190221_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cityam-20190221-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 5 February 2019, it was announced that Stobart Air would wetlease one of its E195s to <a href="/wiki/KLM_Cityhopper" title="KLM Cityhopper">KLM Cityhopper</a> to operate four flights daily between <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Airport_Schiphol" title="Amsterdam Airport Schiphol">Amsterdam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Airport" title="Geneva Airport">Geneva</a> and four between Amsterdam and <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a>. The flights were executed between 25 February and 30 March.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>On 5 March 2020, Flybe entered administration due to ongoing difficulties compounded by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">coronavirus pandemic</a>; all Flybe flights operated by Flybe itself and by Stobart Air ceased.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\nOn 18 March 2020, Connect Airways in turn entered administration.<sup id="cite_ref-ch-av-20200318_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ch-av-20200318-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Stobart Air itself did not go into administration, and in mid-April Stobart Group announced that it was "reviewing all options" with the administrators, including the possibility of buying back Stobart Air and its sister company Propius Leasing, believing that the two companies have a viable future after the pandemic.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 27 April 2020, <a href="/wiki/Stobart_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Stobart Group">Stobart Group</a> bought back Stobart Air and Propius Leasing from the <a href="/wiki/Connect_Airways" title="Connect Airways">Connect Airways</a> bankruptcy administrators. Stobart Group initially paid \xe2\x82\xac344,000 to Connect Airways administrators EY, the deal is set to be worth over \xe2\x82\xac9.45m. Airlines</a> as the preferred option.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2021, Stobart Air was sold to <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man"
