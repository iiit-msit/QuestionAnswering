id="Founding_to_Irish_financial_crisis">Founding to Irish financial crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_Post&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Founding to Irish financial crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>The Sunday Business Post</i> was co-founded by four people: the economist and editor <a href="/wiki/Damien_Kiberd" title="Damien Kiberd">Damien Kiberd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aileen_O%27Toole" title="Aileen O&#39;Toole">Aileen O\'Toole</a> (former editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Business_%26_Finance" title="Business &amp; Finance">Business &amp; Finance</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Frank_Fitzgibbon" title="Frank Fitzgibbon">Frank Fitzgibbon</a> (editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times" title="The Sunday Times">The Sunday Times</a></i> Ireland) and <a href="/wiki/James_Morrissey" title="James Morrissey">James Morrissey</a> (spokesperson for <a href="/wiki/Denis_O%27Brien" title="Denis O&#39;Brien">Denis O\'Brien</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>SBP</i> was previously owned by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Crosbie_Holdings" title="Thomas Crosbie Holdings">Thomas Crosbie Holdings</a> (TCH). It was then owned by Key Capital, Paul Cooke and staff members (6% equity for staff).<sup id="cite_ref-rte.ie_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rte.ie-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was then owned by <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_Media" title="Sunrise Media">Sunrise Media</a>, the shareholders of which include Key Capital. It is now owned by Kilcullen Capital Partners.<sup id="cite_ref-kilcullenkapital.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kilcullenkapital.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The paper\'s first edition appeared on 26 November 1989. While TCH\'s other major newspaper titles, the <i><a href="/wiki/Irish_Examiner" title="Irish Examiner">Irish Examiner</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Evening_Echo" class="mw-redirect" title="Evening Echo">Evening Echo</a></i>, are based in <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a>, the <i>Post</i> is published in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>. The paper describes itself as "Ireland\'s Political, Economic and Financial Newspaper". It is a general newspaper with a strong emphasis on commerce, politics and financial markets. The newspaper does not have a sports section. Three supplements are published weekly with the newspaper: <i>The Market</i>, which concentrates on <a href="/wiki/Irish_Stock_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Stock Exchange">Irish Stock Exchange</a> and company news; <i>The Magazine</i>, an entertainment and features magazine; and a property supplement. A once-monthly <i>Computers in Business</i> magazine is also included.\n</p><p>Ted Harding succeeded Kiberd as editor of the paper in 2001 and edited the paper for three years, resigning in October 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Examinership_.282013.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Examinership_(2013)">Examinership (2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_Post&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Examinership (2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>An interim examiner was formally appointed to Post Publications Ltd, publisher of <i>The Sunday Business Post</i> newspaper, on 15 March 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/High_Court_(Ireland)" title="High Court (Ireland)">High Court</a> was told that there might be a voluntary redundancy scheme at the newspaper in late 2013 which would target 25 staff positions. This could coincide with pay cuts of 7% for employees and further possible changes as part of a financial restructuring plan for the company, the court was told.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was reported on 28 April 2013 that two companies were interested in acquiring the <i>SBP</i> from the examiner. <a href="/wiki/Landmark_Media_Investments" title="Landmark Media Investments">Landmark Media Investments</a>, which acquired some of the assets of Thomas Crosbie Holdings, was no longer interested in the title. It was also reported that the <i>SBP</i> required an investment of at least \xe2\x82\xac2m to acquire the paper and restructure it. Any new bidder would have to fund the cost of laying off some staff.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 8 May 2013, it was reported that <i>The Sunday Business Post</i> had started staff redundancies. Up to ten staff were to leave the company by 10 May 2013. Staff were also told about a third pay cut. Post Publications was also looking to reduce the amount paid to its landlord by \xe2\x82\xac1 million per year.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was reported on 12 May 2013 that bidders for <i>The Sunday Business Post</i> included Michael Brophy, a former chief executive of Independent News &amp; Media (Northern Ireland).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 15 May 2013, Post Publications was back in court. It reported that the examiner had failed to find a buyer in the first 70 days of the examinership. The examiner was given a final 30-day extension to find a buyer. If a buyer did not materialise, the examiner had to advise the court immediately.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Annual revenue fell from \xe2\x82\xac15.3 million to \xe2\x82\xac7.3 million in the five years to 2013. Circulation revenue fell from \xe2\x82\xac4.9 million to \xe2\x82\xac3.6 million in the last 5 years to 2013. The newspaper had 76 full-time employees and 123 freelance contributors and made a loss of \xe2\x82\xac1.2 million in the year to 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Paul_Cooke_and_Key_Capital_Ownership_.282013.E2.80.932016.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Paul_Cooke_and_Key_Capital_Ownership_(2013\xe2\x80\x932016)">Paul Cooke and Key Capital Ownership (2013\xe2\x80\x932016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_Post&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Paul Cooke and Key Capital Ownership (2013\xe2\x80\x932016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 8 June 2013, it emerged that Key Capital, Paul Cooke and staff (6% equity for staff) had acquired <i>The Sunday Business Post</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-rte.ie_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rte.ie-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Examinership ended on 19 June 2013. The new owners of the <i>SBP</i>, Brindisi Ltd, took out a \xe2\x82\xac350,000 loan to part-fund the purchase.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Several staff left the paper, including former chief executive Fiachra O\'Riordan, former senior assistant editor Kieron Wood and former deputy chief sub-editor Garvan Grant.\n</p><p>In the trading year for 2014, Post Publications Limited made a pre-tax loss of \xe2\x82\xac628,000. Post Publications Limited paid \xe2\x82\xac409,374 for redundancy payments and a new premises. Shareholders had pumped an additional \xe2\x82\xac300,000 into the company; staff numbers had reduced again. Post Publications employed an average of 61 people in the year to the end of June 2014. Both circulation and advertising revenue remain "under pressure", the directors noted.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2014, Post Publications Ltd., the publisher of the paper, announced that <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Taylor_(journalist)" title="Cliff Taylor (journalist)">Cliff Taylor</a> would leave the paper after ten years as editor. Pat Leahy, the paper\'s deputy editor and political editor, was the paper\'s acting editor until <a href="/wiki/Ian_Kehoe" title="Ian Kehoe">Ian Kehoe</a> was made editor.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Kilcullen_Capital_Ownership_.282016_.E2.80.93_present_day.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Kilcullen_Capital_Ownership_(2016_\xe2\x80\x93_present_day)">Kilcullen Capital Ownership (2016 \xe2\x80\x93 present day)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_Post&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kilcullen Capital Ownership (2016 \xe2\x80\x93 present day)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 13 July 2016, it was announced that Paul Cooke had sold his stake to Key Capital. Cooke also resigned as managing director.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The <i>SBP</i> became legally owned by Sunrise Media, and the main shareholder in Sunrise Media is Conor Killeen\'s Key Capital.<sup id="cite_ref-independent.ie_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independent.ie-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2017, it was reported that <i>The Sunday Business Post</i> was for sale.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In September 2018, it was announced that Kilcullen Kapital Partners (KKP) had acquired the <i>SBP</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-kilcullenkapital.com_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kilcullenkapital.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> KKP owned the <i>SBP</i> via a company called Encircle Business Post 365.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2018, it was announced that a three-to-four-month consultation was occurring at <i>The Sunday Business Post</i> that might lead to the print closure.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> At a later stage, it was announced that the paper version of the paper would not close.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2019, it was announced that Encircle Business Post 365, the company that owns the <i>SBP</i>, had acquired event management company iQuest from outgoing owner Michael Nolan.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2019, it was announced that the CEO of the Post Publications was leaving the company<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and it was reported that the <i>SBP</i> owner might float it on a stock exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2019, it was reported that Beach Point Capital have provided backing to the owners of the Sunday Business Post. Beach Point Capital have a charge over certain assets.
