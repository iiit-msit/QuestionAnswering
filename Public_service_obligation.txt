PSOs may operate in any field of public service, but <a href="/wiki/Postal_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Postal services">postal services</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_services" title="Social services">social services</a>, <a href="/wiki/Energy_law" title="Energy law">energy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transport_law" title="Transport law">transport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banking_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Banking law">banking</a> are identified as specific sectors where the concept is relevant.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_service_obligation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transport law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dash_8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Dash_8.jpg/220px-Dash_8.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Dash_8.jpg/330px-Dash_8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Dash_8.jpg/440px-Dash_8.jpg 2x" data-file-width="630" data-file-height="419" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dash_8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Two <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada" title="De Havilland Canada">DHC</a> <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada_Dash_8" title="De Havilland Canada Dash 8">Dash-8-100</a> aircraft of <a href="/wiki/Wider%C3%B8e" title="Wider\xc3\xb8e">Wider\xc3\xb8e</a> of Norway at <a href="/wiki/Trondheim_Airport,_V%C3%A6rnes" title="Trondheim Airport, V\xc3\xa6rnes">Trondheim Airport, V\xc3\xa6rnes</a>. Wider\xc3\xb8e makes a large portion of its revenue from PSO routes like these in Northern and Western Norway.</div></div></div>\n<p>In the context of EU transport law, a PSO is an arrangement in which a governing body or other authority offers <a href="/wiki/Subsidy" title="Subsidy">subsidies</a> in an <a href="/wiki/Auction" title="Auction">auction</a>, whereas the winning company will be obliged to operate a specified service of <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a> for a specified period of time for the given subsidy. This usually leads to a <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a> of the winning bidder, as competing services would not be viable without subsidies. That is done if there is not enough <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">revenue</a> for routes to be profitable in a <a href="/wiki/Free_market" title="Free market">free market</a>, but there is a socially desirable advantage in the transport being available.\n</p><p>The use of PSO can be applied to many <a href="/wiki/Mode_of_transport" title="Mode of transport">mode of transport</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Air_transport" class="mw-redirect" title="Air transport">air</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ship_transport" class="mw-redirect" title="Ship transport">sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Road_transport" title="Road transport">road</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">rail</a>. In many, cases the introduction of PSO has been a way to <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatize</a> government-owned transport. The infrastructure is often separated from the operation, and may be owned by the governing body or by a third party. The authority may also maintain the ownership of the vehicles, such as ferries or <a href="/wiki/Rolling_stock" title="Rolling stock">rolling stock</a>.\n</p><p>Traditionally, public transport has been operated by a company wholly owned by the state with monopoly, like a national railway company. Alternatively, private companies were granted <a href="/wiki/Privilege_(social_inequality)" class="mw-redirect" title="Privilege (social inequality)">privileges</a> (with or without subsidies) granting them a monopoly. In recent years, many markets have been deregulated, especially in Europe, by paying the lowest bidding operator to carry out the traffic at regular auctions.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Specification">Specification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_service_obligation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Public_service_obligation&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">June 2014</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The authority issuing the auction may be a <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Transport">ministry of transport</a>, <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_(administrative_division)" class="mw-redirect" title="State (administrative division)">state</a>, <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> or other regional or local authority, or it can be a <a href="/wiki/Transit_authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Transit authority">transit authority</a> or other ad hoc organization responsible for some state of transport in an area. To be the auctioning part the authority must have some vested interest in subsidizing the transport in addition to the authority to ban other interests from operating competing services on parts or all of the system.\n</p><p>The auctioneer will specify a number of terms of the service, such as <a href="/wiki/Headway" title="Headway">frequency</a>, size of vehicle, timing of service, the maximum permitted <a href="/wiki/Fare" title="Fare">fare</a> paid by some or all the available seats and/or other specifications related to service and quality. The auctioneer can specify either a net or gross contract. In the latter the operator bids for the full operating costs, and all revenue goes to the authority; with net contracts the operator will be granted all revenue, and will bid only for the necessary difference between fare revenue and that needed to make the desired profit. The use of gross contracts reduces the risk of the operator since they do not need to estimate the ridership and will normally give lower bids since there is a lower <a href="/wiki/Risk_premium" title="Risk premium">risk premium</a> in each bid. This option also makes it easier to create free transfer between operators and modes.\n</p><p>Reasons to apply net contracts are to give the operators an incentive to increase ridership, that there would be a high administrative costs with the authority administrating the fares, or that the auctioneer wants to reduce its own risk. Urban public transport with transit authorities is most commonly operated with gross contracts, while airlines and ferry routes are typically operated on net contracts.\n</p><p>Typical uses of PSO include <a href="/wiki/Regional_airline" title="Regional airline">regional airline</a> services in districts of for instance in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Norway" title="Northern Norway">Northern Norway</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, where the airlines serve marginally populated areas not profitable for the carriers without subsidies. Rail services can be unprofitable even on major routes, and government subsidies are sometimes offered through PSO on unprofitable routes, for instance this has been very popular in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Most cities subsidize their <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a>, and in places where the transport is provided by private companies, that may be awarded through PSO. Other common PSO services are ferries, such as the routes to <a href="/wiki/Gotland" title="Gotland">Gotland</a> in Sweden, or car ferries on the road network of Norway. Ferry routes serving <a href="/wiki/Islands_of_Hong_Kong" class="mw-redirect" title="Islands of Hong Kong">outlying islands</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> are also subsidised since recently, as a result of rising oil price and therefore the costs of operations.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Union">European Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_service_obligation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: European Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Veolia_GTW_front.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Veolia_GTW_front.JPG/220px-Veolia_GTW_front.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Veolia_GTW_front.JPG/330px-Veolia_GTW_front.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Veolia_GTW_front.JPG/440px-Veolia_GTW_front.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Veolia_GTW_front.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Veolia_Transport" title="Veolia Transport">Veolia</a> train in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. Veolia has expanded rapidly based on winning many PSO contracts for bus and rail services in Europe.</div></div></div>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, route PSOs are governed by Regulation (EC) No 1008/2008 and Council Regulation (EEC) 3577/92. They must be offered for <a href="/wiki/Call_for_bids" class="mw-redirect" title="Call for bids">tender</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Official_Journal_of_the_European_Union" title="Official Journal of the European Union">Official Journal of the European Union</a></i> and be open to any transport operator registered in an EU member state. There are limitations in the number of passengers which can be carried where the route can remain eligible for PSO. The winning tenderer usually receives a monopoly on the route, but may have to conform to one or more conditions of service, such as the type and size of vehicle, the timing of services, the maximum fare paid for a portion or all of the seats offered, membership of a common reservation system, quality of service measured by a maximum percentage of cancelled services, etc.\n</p><p>Examples of air transport PSOs currently in operation in Europe are routes from <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Airport" title="Dublin Airport">Dublin</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kerry_Airport" title="Kerry Airport">Kerry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donegal_Airport" title="Donegal Airport">Donegal</a>, routes between the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek Mainland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greek_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Greek Islands">Greek Islands</a>, routes from <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian mainland</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elba" title="Elba">Elba</a>, routes between the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French mainland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, certain domestic routes within <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, and routes to the <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Highlands_and_Islands" title="Highlands and Islands">Highlands and Islands</a>.\n</p><p>International routes are usually not supported by PSO. This is because of a political principle that people should pay for international travel themselves, not through tax money. This is especially evident in Scandinavia where there is very little international air traffic between cities other than through the capitals, where connections are commercially profitable. There are exceptions, like the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>-<a href="/wiki/Narvik" title="Narvik">Narvik</a> night trains.\n</p><p>The term is also used about electricity generation, such as wind power and biomass powerplants.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-redirect" title="Continental United States">continental United States</a> served by the Essential Air Service</div></div></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Essential_Air_Service" title="Essential Air Service">Essential Air Service</a> (EAS) is a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Government">U.S. government</a> public service obligation style program enacted to guarantee that small communities in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, which, prior to deregulation, were served by certificated <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airlines</a>, maintained commercial service. Its aim is to maintain a minimal level of scheduled air service to these communities that otherwise would not be profitable. This came in response to the <a href="/wiki/Airline_Deregulation_Act" title="Airline Deregulation Act">Airline Deregulation Act</a>, passed in 1978, which gave U.S. airlines almost total freedom to determine which markets to serve domestically and what fares to charge for that service.<sup id="cite_ref-DOT_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOT-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The program is codified at <a href="/wiki/Title_49_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 49 of the United States Code">49&#160;U.S.C.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/41731">\xc2\xa7\xc2\xa7&#160;41731</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/49/41748">41748</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Transportation" title="United States Department of Transportation">United States Department of Transportation</a> (DOT) subsidizes airlines to serve <a href="/wiki/Rural" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural">rural</a> communities across the country that otherwise would not receive any scheduled air service.<sup id="cite_ref-DOT_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOT-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As of November 1, 2013, the Essential Air Service subsidized 160 communities, of which 43 were in Alaska, whose guidelines for service are distinct from the rest of the country. The decision as to what degree of subsidized service a community requires is made based on identifying a specific <a href="/wiki/Airline_hub" title="Airline hub">hub</a> for the community and from there determining the number of trips, seats, and type of aircraft that are necessary to reach that hub.<sup id="cite_ref-DOT_3-2" class="reference"><a
