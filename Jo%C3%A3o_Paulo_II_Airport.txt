background</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Ponta Delgada - Jo\xc3\xa3o Paulo II Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>PDL</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LPPD</b>), named after <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a>, is an international <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> located on the island of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Miguel_Island" title="S\xc3\xa3o Miguel Island">S\xc3\xa3o Miguel</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> archipelago of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>. Situated 2&#160;km (1.2&#160;mi) west of the city centre of <a href="/wiki/Ponta_Delgada" title="Ponta Delgada">Ponta Delgada</a>, it is the primary (and busiest) airport in the Azores, as well as the fifth largest infrastructure managed by <a href="/wiki/ANA_Aeroportos_de_Portugal" title="ANA Aeroportos de Portugal">ANA Aeroportos de Portugal</a>. The terminal was finished in 1995; by 2005 the airport served a total of 873,500 passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It has scheduled domestic flights to all islands of the Azores, plus <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a> and the mainland, namely (<a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porto" title="Porto">Porto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faro,_Portugal" title="Faro, Portugal">Faro</a>). Jo\xc3\xa3o Paulo II Airport also accommodates international flights to and from Europe and North America. The airport is the major hub for the SATA Group of airlines, which includes both inter-island <a href="/wiki/SATA_Air_A%C3%A7ores" title="SATA Air A\xc3\xa7ores">SATA Air A\xc3\xa7ores</a> and international <a href="/wiki/Azores_Airlines" title="Azores Airlines">Azores Airlines</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and since April 2015 as a base for <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" terminal</div></div></div>\n<p>The airport was inaugurated on 24 August 1969 by President Admiral <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rico_Tom%C3%A1s" title="Am\xc3\xa9rico Tom\xc3\xa1s">Am\xc3\xa9rico Tom\xc3\xa1s</a>, after its construction was planned six years earlier, in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-Dias_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dias-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Transferred from Santana, at the time of its opening, the runway was 1,800 metres (5,900&#160;ft) long and was then referred to as the <i>Aeroporto da Nordela</i>, owing to its location at the extreme northwest of Ponta Delgada.<sup id="cite_ref-Dias_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dias-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was built in order to serve inter-island connections and the continent, using a single Boeing 737 from the national flag carrier (TAP33).<sup id="cite_ref-Dias_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dias-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Regular flights to Lisbon began two years later.<sup id="cite_ref-Dias_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dias-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 1995, at the inauguration of the airport terminal, the facility received the designation <i>Jo\xc3\xa3o Paulo II</i>, in honour of the visit of the Pope to the Azores in 1991.\n</p>\n<dl><dd><i>"This airport, is part of the integrated history and economic and social development of the Azores, in particular of the Micaelenses, and constitutes an relevant equipment at the service of air transport, not just of the passengers, but also the transport and mail. It is the infrastructure with the largest expression of air traffic in the autonomous region of the Azores, reaching in 2011 935,000 passengers."</i></dd></dl>\n<p>Along with the airports in Lisbon, Porto, Faro, Flores, Santa Maria, Horta and Beja, the airport\'s concessions to provide support to civil aviation was conceded to <a href="/wiki/ANA_Aeroportos_de_Portugal" title="ANA Aeroportos de Portugal">ANA Aeroportos de Portugal</a> on 18 December 1998, under provisions of decree 404/98.<sup id="cite_ref-ANA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> With this concession, ANA was also in charge of future planning, development and construction of future infrastructures.<sup id="cite_ref-ANA_5-1" class="reference"><a
