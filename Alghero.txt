pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[al\xcb\x88\xc9\xa1\xc9\x9b\xcb\x90ro]</a></span>), also known in the local <a href="/wiki/Algherese_dialect" title="Algherese dialect">Algherese dialect</a> as <b>L\'Alguer</b> (<small>Catalan pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Catalan" title="Help:IPA/Catalan">[l\xc9\x99l\xcb\x88\xc9\xa3e]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">Sardinian</a>: <i lang="sc">S\'Aligh\xc3\xa8ra</i>; <a insular <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Sassari" title="Province of Sassari">province of Sassari</a> in northwestern <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, next to the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>. Part of its population descends from <a href="/wiki/Catalans" title="Catalans">Catalan</a> conquerors from the end of the Middle Ages, when Sardinia was part of the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Crown of Aragon</a>. Hence, the <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan language</a> is co-official (a unique situation in Italy) and known as the <a href="/wiki/Alguer%C3%A8s" class="mw-redirect" title="Alguer\xc3\xa8s">Alguer\xc3\xa8s</a> dialect. The name Alghero comes from <i>Aleguerium</i>, which is a mediaeval <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> word meaning "stagnation of algae" (<i><a href="/wiki/Posidonia_oceanica" title="Posidonia oceanica">Posidonia oceanica</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Alghero is the third university center in the island, coming after <a href="/wiki/Cagliari" title="Cagliari">Cagliari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sassari" title="Sassari">Sassari</a>. It hosts the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Sassari" title="University of Sassari">Universit\xc3\xa0 degli Studi di Sassari</a>\xe2\x80\x99s Architecture and Design department. In 2012 it was the 10th most visited city by tourists in Italy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. The <a href="/wiki/Ozieri" title="Ozieri">Ozieri culture</a> was present here in the 4th millennium BC (<a href="/wiki/Necropolis_of_Anghelu_Ruju" title="Necropolis of Anghelu Ruju">Necropolis of Anghelu Ruju</a>), while the <a href="/wiki/Nuraghe" title="Nuraghe">Nuraghe</a> civilization settled in the area around 1,500 BC.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Phoenicians" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenicians">Phoenicians</a> arrived by the 8th century BC and the <a href="/wiki/Metalworking" title="Metalworking">metalworking</a> town of Sant\'Imbenia&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 in the area of later Alghero&#160;\xe2\x80\x93, with a mixed Phoenician and <a href="/wiki/Nuragic_civilization" title="Nuragic civilization">Nuragic</a> population, engaged in trade with the <a href="/wiki/Etruscans" class="mw-redirect" title="Etruscans">Etruscans</a> on the Italian mainland.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Due to its strategic position on the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, Alghero had been developed into a fortified port town by 1102, built by the <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoese</a> <a href="/wiki/Doria_(family)" title="Doria (family)">Doria</a> family. The Dorias ruled Alghero for centuries, apart from a brief period under the rule of <a href="/wiki/Pisa" title="Pisa">Pisa</a> between 1283 and 1284.  Alghero\'s population later grew because of the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalan</a> colonists. In the early 16th century Alghero received papal recognition as a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Alghero-Bosa" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Alghero-Bosa">bishopric</a> and the status of King\'s City (<i>ciutat de l\'Alguer</i>) and developed economically.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg/220px-Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg/330px-Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg/440px-Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Piazza_Sulis_Alghero.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sulis Tower</div></div></div>\n<p>Historically, the city was founded in the early twelfth century between 1102 and 1112, when the noble <a href="/wiki/Doria_(family)" title="Doria (family)">Doria family</a> of <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a> was allowed to build the first historical nucleus into an empty section of the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> of Nulauro in <a href="/wiki/Judiciary" title="Judiciary">Judicature</a> of Torres (<a href="/wiki/Sassari" title="Sassari">Sassari</a>). For two centuries it remained in the orbit of the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_republics" title="Maritime republics">Maritime Republics</a>, first and foremost the Genoese, apart from 1283\xe2\x80\x931284 when the <a href="/wiki/Pisa" title="Pisa">Pisans</a> were able to control it for a year. It is plausible that at this time the town shared, given its commercial and multi-ethnic nature, a language similar to the nascent <a href="/wiki/Sassarese_language" title="Sassarese language">Sassarese</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alghero_-_Chiesa_di_San_Francesco_(15).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" by force by the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Crown of Aragon</a>, at the behest of <a href="/wiki/Peter_IV_of_Aragon" title="Peter IV of Aragon">King Pere IV of Aragon</a> (r. 1336\xe2\x80\x931387), who later actively promoted colonisation of the town and the surrounding area, sending numerous families from different counties and provinces of the then Crown of Aragon, including <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Valencia" title="Kingdom of Valencia">Valencia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Majorca" title="Kingdom of Majorca">Majorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Catalonia" title="Principality of Catalonia">Catalonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">Aragon</a>. These were granted enticing privileges, and in fact, replaced the original population some of whom were sent to the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> and Majorca as slaves.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The dialects these families spoke in Alghero, were all very similar and derived from the same linguistic family. Over time it settled on its current form of Catalan, despite the subsequent decline of the Crown of Aragon.\n</p><p>The Aragonese were followed by the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Habsburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg">Habsburgs</a>, who ruled until 1702 and continued expanding the town.\n</p><p>In 1720 Alghero, along with the rest of Sardinia, was handed over to the <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a>-based <a href="/wiki/House_of_Savoy" title="House of Savoy">House of Savoy</a>, upon the arrival of which a policy of <a href="/wiki/Italianization" title="Italianization">Italianization</a> was commenced. In 1821, a famine led to a revolt by the population, which was bloodily suppressed. At the end of the same century, Alghero was de-militarised.\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">Fascist</a> era, part of the surrounding marshes were reclaimed and the suburbs of Fertilia and S.M. La Palma were founded. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> (1943), Alghero was bombed, and its historical centre suffered heavy damage. The presence of <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a> in the countryside was finally overcome in the 1950s.\n</p><p>Since then, Alghero has become a popular tourist resort.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Alghero is located on the northwestern coast of Sardinia, along the bay named after the city. In the north of the urban area, there is the Nurra plain; to the northwest, the karstic systems of Capo Caccia, Punta Giglio and Monte Doglia. The south is built mainly by mountains and the plateaus of <a href="/wiki/Villanova_Monteleone" title="Villanova Monteleone">Villanova Monteleone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bosa" title="Bosa">Bosa</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The climate at Alghero is mild due to the presence of the sea, which attenuates the temperatures especially during the summer. Summers are warm like in most parts of the Mediterranean. Winters are also tempered, with the thermometers showing negative Celsius temperatures just a few days per year.\n</p>\n<div style="width:82%;">\n<div>\n<table class="wikitable mw-collapsible" dialect of <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a> is <a href="/wiki/Algherese_dialect" title="Algherese dialect">spoken in Alghero</a>, introduced when Catalans settled in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Catalan was replaced as the official language of the Island by Spanish in the 17th century, then by Italian. The most recent linguistic research showed that 24.1% of the people have <a href="/wiki/Algherese" class="mw-redirect" title="Algherese">Algherese Catalan</a> as a mother tongue, which is habitually spoken by 18.5% and taught to the children by 8% of the population, whereas 88.2% have some understanding of the language.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Since 1997, Catalan has had official recognition and national and regional laws grant its right to be used in the city. Currently, there has been a revival of the arts in Algherese Catalan, with singers such as <a href="/wiki/Franca_Masu" title="Franca Masu">Franca Masu</a> performing original compositions in the language.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following a rural exodus from the surrounding villages towards the city, much of the population speaks or has some proficiency in <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_language" title="Sardinian language">Sardinian</a>, in addition to Italian and Catalan. Historically, the spread of Catalan was limited to the city and part of the coast, as the surrounding countryside has always been populated by Sardinian-speaking people.\n</p><p>Moreover, the ancient part of Alghero shows many characteristics of Catalan medieval architecture. The \xe2\x80\x98algueresos\xe2\x80\x99 (Alghero inhabitants) usually refer to their city as \xe2\x80\x98Barceloneta\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x93 \'little <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>\' \xe2\x80\x93 because of their ancestry and fraternity with the <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalan</a> capital. The cuisine is a blending of <a href="/wiki/Catalan_cuisine" title="Catalan cuisine">Catalan cuisine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_cuisine" class="mw-redirect" title="Sardinian cuisine">Sardinian cuisine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg/220px-Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg/330px-Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg/440px-Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sardinia_-_78_-_aga_-_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alghero Lungomare</div></div></div>\n<p>The many historical dominances that occurred in Alghero have created a rich variety of monuments, buildings and sights. Back from the Neolithic period from which many settlements remain, up to nowadays, in the last decades Alghero has become a touristic main point not only because of its coast and natural beauties but also because of a fairly well-preserved patrimony.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Archeological_sites">Archeological sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Archeological sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Several archeological sites out of the urban area: the <a href="/wiki/Necropolis_of_Anghelu_Ruju" title="Necropolis of Anghelu Ruju">Anghelu Ruju necropolis</a>, the Santu Pedru hill, the Villa Romana of Santa Imbenia or even the Purissima. Many nuraghi in some other points as Palmavera are also well preserved and open to visitors.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ramparts">Ramparts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ramparts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first ramparts system looks back to the 13th century and was imported from the <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genovese</a> system. In 1354 the city was occupied by Catalans, who restored and expanded the defensive system, back then in bad condition. Some features from the old walls were respected, but <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_the_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferdinand the Catholic">Ferdinand the Catholic</a>, who wanted to grant more protection to the city, built the majority of them in the 16th Century. Along the walls, 7 towers and 3 forts are found.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_architecture">Religious architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alghero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Religious architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg/220px-Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg/330px-Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg/440px-Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alghero_Dom_Kuppel1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sant Miquel\'s Dome</div></div></div>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Alghero_Cathedral" title="Alghero Cathedral">Alghero Cathedral</a> (<i>Cattedrale di Santa Maria Immacolata di Alghero</i>) is the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Alghero-Bosa" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Alghero-Bosa">Roman Catholic Diocese of Alghero-Bosa</a> (initially bishopric of Alghero). Construction started in 1570, and although it was opened in 1593, it was not until 1730 until it was finished and consecrated. The original church was in the Catalan-<a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> style, as can be seen in the five chapels of the <a href="/wiki/Presbytery_(architecture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Presbytery (architecture)">presbytery</a>, which also include the base of the bell tower. The nave and the two aisles are of late Renaissance style. In the 20th century a Neo-Classical <a href="/wiki/Narthex" title="Narthex">narthex</a> was added to the fa\xc3\xa7ade, radically altering its appearance.</li>\n<li>The church of St. Francis (1360, rebuilt in the late 16th century).
