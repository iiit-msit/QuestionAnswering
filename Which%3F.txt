rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.which.co.uk/">https://www.which.co.uk/</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><i><b>Which?</b></i> is a United Kingdom brand name that promotes <a href="/wiki/Informed_consumer" title="Informed consumer">informed</a> <a href="/wiki/Consumer_choice" title="Consumer choice">consumer choice</a> in the purchase of goods and services by testing products, highlighting inferior products or services, raising awareness of <a href="/wiki/Consumer_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer rights">consumer rights</a> and offering independent advice. The brand name is used by the <b>Consumers\' Association</b>, a <a href="/wiki/Registered_charity_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Registered charity in England">registered charity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_guarantee" title="Private company limited by guarantee">company limited by guarantee</a> that owns several businesses, including <i>Which? Financial Services Limited</i> (<i>Which? Mortgage Advisers</i>), <i>Which? Legal Limited</i> and <i>Which? Limited</i>, which publishes the <i>Which?</i> Papers.\n</p><p>The vast majority of the association\'s income comes from the profit it makes on its trading businesses, for instance subscriptions to <i>Which?</i> magazine, which are donated to the campaigning part of the organisation to fund advocacy activity and inform the public about consumer issues. <i>Which?</i> magazine maintains its independence by not accepting advertising, and the organisation receives no government funding.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Consumers\' Association is the largest <a href="/wiki/Consumer_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Consumer organisation">consumer organisation</a> in the UK, with over 573,000 subscribers to its magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Until 2006, the association used prize draws similar to those of <i><a href="/wiki/Reader%27s_Digest" title="Reader&#39;s Digest">Reader\'s Digest</a></i> to attract subscribers, but following criticism they were discontinued.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Association now attracts subscribers to its publications with free mini-guides and trial offers.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_background">History and background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The organisation was set up in 1957 \nas a response to the changing values and concerns of the post-war era, at a time of rapid changes in product markets and consumer behaviour \xe2\x80\x93 and a corresponding growth in sharp trading practices. To combat this, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Young_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Young (politician)">Michael Young</a>, research director for the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a>, proposed setting up a \'Consumer Advisory Service\' to be considered for the party\'s 1950 manifesto, only to be rejected by <a href="/wiki/Harold_Wilson" title="Harold Wilson">Harold Wilson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Encouraged by the efforts of Dorothy Bruchholz Goodman, of the United States, and Ray Goodman,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Young continued to push the idea and in October 1957 the first <i>Which?</i> magazine was published from a converted garage in Bethnal Green.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Its central aim was to improve the standard of goods and services available to the public in the UK. Under the editorship of <a href="/wiki/Eirlys_Roberts" title="Eirlys Roberts">Eirlys Roberts</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GObit_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GObit-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> the publication started as a small 32-page magazine that included reports on electric kettles, sunglasses, aspirin, cake-mixes, scouring powders, no-iron cottons and British cars.\n</p><p>By 1959, membership reached 150,000 and a membership services office in Hertford.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2007_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1960, <i>Which?</i> helped to found the International Organisation of Consumer Unions, later <a href="/wiki/Consumers_International" title="Consumers International">Consumers International</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 1962, coverage expanded with the launch of the first quarterly <i>Which? car</i> supplement, which becomes <i>Motoring Which?</i> in 1965, followed by <i>Money Which?</i> in 1968.\n</p><p>In 1970, <i>Which?</i> bought a set of buildings in <a href="/wiki/Harpenden" title="Harpenden">Harpenden</a>, Hertfordshire, to set up labs to test products including domestic appliances, DIY equipment, and consumer electronics. Previously, the work had been contracted out.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2007_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The 1970s saw <i>Which?</i> taking a greater campaigning role, and it began to adopt a more aggressive stance on issues such as lead poisoning and aircraft noise. In 1971, for example, <i>Which?</i> published the first league table on the tar and nicotine content of cigarettes.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2007_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1973, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Healy_(campaigner)" title="Maurice Healy (campaigner)">Maurice Healy</a> succeeded Eirlys Roberts as editor, and became editor-in-chief for all the Which? magazines, before he left in 1977 to join the <a href="/wiki/National_Consumer_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="National Consumer Council">National Consumer Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_obituary_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_obituary-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The organisation also dabbled in high street consumer advice centres during the 1970s. More than 120 such centres were operating in 1977-8, many of them funded by local councils. The centres closed as funding was withdrawn by the government in 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2007_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Which?</i> continued campaigning on consumer safety issues, with its car safety test results strengthening calls to legislate car manufacturers to fit seat belts in all new cars, helping to make it compulsory to <a href="/wiki/Clunk_Click_Every_Trip" title="Clunk Click Every Trip">Clunk Click Every Trip</a> in 1983. In 1992, <i>Which?</i> launched another satellite publication, this one aimed at youngsters aged 11\xe2\x80\x9315, called Check It Out!, but it failed to take off and closed in June 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2007_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1995 testing facilities were moved to <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">Milton Keynes</a> where they remained until 2002, when the majority of the <i>Which?</i> testing activities were contracted out. <i>Which?</i> Online, the first incarnation of the Which? website, was launched in 1996 which incorporated its own ISP, email and content in addition to the Which Online Forum - an area accessible to members which offered unfettered access to members of the senior management within Which? including the Directors.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The trading organisation was rebranded as <i>Which?</i> in 2004 and this is the brand promoted to the general public. However the charity the Consumers\' Association continues to own Which? Ltd and retains its own name.\n</p><p>Following the deregulation of the gas and electricity industry, <i>Which?</i> launched a free online energy comparison service called <i>Switch with Which?</i> in 2005, to compare energy tariffs. The service was awarded <a href="/wiki/Energywatch" class="mw-redirect" title="Energywatch">Energywatch</a> Confidence Code accreditation in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 2010 Superbrands index, <i>Which?</i> ranked fifth in the "Media \xe2\x80\x94 Newspapers and Magazines" category and 256th overall.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Product_testing">Product testing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Product testing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>Which?</i> carries out systematic testing of consumer products and <a href="/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">financial services</a>, the results of which are published in reports in <i>Which? magazine</i> and on the <i>Which?</i> website. Tests are carried out on goods, services, and suppliers. Testing covers <a href="/wiki/Reliability_engineering" title="Reliability engineering">reliability</a>, performance, safety, energy efficiency and value-for-money, as relevant in different cases. Top-rated products are awarded the <i>Which?</i> \'Best Buy\' status logo,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> an accreditation recognised by <a href="/wiki/Private_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Private industry">industry</a> and consumers. <i>Which?</i> first used the phrase in its second magazine edition in 1958, to describe Boots 365 talcum powder.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2007_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2007-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Conversely, the worst-performing products are labelled \'Don\'t Buys\'.\n</p><p><b><i>Which?</i> awards</b>\nThe Which? Awards is an annual event to \'celebrate the UK\'s best products and services, from cars and restaurants to supermarkets and financial services\'.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Award winners are selected by Which? staff, based on test findings and feedback from Which? members throughout the year.\n</p><p><b>Unusual test subjects</b>\nAs well as the usual household products,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <i>Which?</i> has tested things such as:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Bird seed for budgerigars in 1959</li>\n<li>Contraceptives in 1963<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Paper dresses in 1967</li>\n<li>Pets in 1977<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Most <i>Which?</i> publications are supplied only to subscribers, and are not on general sale. <i>Which?</i> magazine, started in 1957,<sup id="cite_ref-wh_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wh-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> is the organisation\xe2\x80\x99s principal publication. The magazine reports on consumer issues of the day, publishes product testing results and findings of original research and investigations.\n</p><p>The magazine also discusses trading which it believes goes against the interests of consumers. Examples of such discussions include an expos\xc3\xa9 into solar panel heating installation companies,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> an undercover enquiry with the <a href="/wiki/Action_on_Hearing_Loss" class="mw-redirect" title="Action on Hearing Loss">RNID</a> revealing serious problems at shops selling hearing aids<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and an investigation into electronics shop staffs\' knowledge of the products that they sell.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Other publications include <i>Which? Travel</i>, <i>Which? Car</i>, <i>Which? Money</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-wh_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wh-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <i>Which? Computing</i> and <i>Which? Gardening</i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a id="cite_ref-charitycomm_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-charitycomm-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Policy.2C_consultation_and_parliamentary_lobbying"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy,_consultation_and_parliamentary_lobbying">Policy, consultation and parliamentary lobbying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Policy, consultation and parliamentary lobbying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Which? regularly briefs and lobbies MPs, Peers, AMs, MLAs, MSPs and MEPs on national, European and international consumer issues. For example, Which? successfully lobbied to put an end to the \'65-day rule\' that allowed energy companies to wait 65 days before informing their customers of a price change.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish parliament">Scottish parliament</a> in 1999, in January 2002 Which? established an office in Edinburgh. Which? also works with the <a href="/wiki/European_Consumers%27_Bureau" class="mw-redirect" title="European Consumers&#39; Bureau">European Consumers\' Association</a> (<a href="/wiki/BEUC" class="mw-redirect" title="BEUC">BEUC</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> which brings together over forty consumer organisations from across Europe to represent common consumer interests within the EU institutions.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_powers">Legal powers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legal powers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Super-complaints">Super-complaints</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Super-complaints">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Consumers\' Association has the power under The <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Act_2002" title="Enterprise Act 2002">Enterprise Act 2002</a> to take action on behalf of consumers, including the ability to bring a <a href="/wiki/Super-complaint" title="Super-complaint">super-complaint</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Fair_Trading" title="Office of Fair Trading">Office of Fair Trading</a> (OFT).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Which? is one of eight organisations able to issue a super-complaint. The Consumers\' Association was among the first groups granted these new powers. A super-complaint can be made about any market that is not working properly for consumers. As consumers are not in the position to overcome the issue themselves, Which? can issue a super-complaint on consumers\' behalf. The OFT has 90 days in which to assess the complaint and decide what to do about it. It can reject the complaint in part or as a whole, it can launch a market investigation, take action under competition law or consumer law, or refer the market to the <a href="/wiki/Competition_and_Markets_Authority" title="Competition and Markets Authority">Competition and Markets Authority</a> for further investigation.\n</p><p>Which? made its first super complaint about <a href="/wiki/Private_medicine_in_the_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Private medicine in the UK">private dentistry</a> in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> It later made complaints about care homes, the Northern Ireland banking sector and <a href="/wiki/Credit_card_interest" title="Credit card interest">credit card interest</a> calculation methods. In March 2011 it made a super complaint<sup id="cite_ref-oft.gov.uk_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oft.gov.uk-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> about unfair debit and credit card payment surcharges made by retailers. The OFT upheld the super-complaint in June 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-which.co.uk_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-which.co.uk-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2016 Which? filed a super complaint against banks that routinely refuse to reimburse victims who have been scammed into transferring money into fraudsters\xe2\x80\x99 accounts.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>\nWhich? said banks should \xe2\x80\x9cshoulder more responsibility\xe2\x80\x9d for such fraud, much as they already reimburse customers who lose money through scams involving fraudulent account activity, or debit or credit cards.\nAccording to official industry data, such scams increased by 53% in a year: from 660,308 cases in the first half of 2015 to 1,007,094 in the first half of 2016. Which? said: \xe2\x80\x9cConsumers can only protect themselves so far. People cannot be expected to detect complex scams pressuring them to transfer money immediately, or lookalike bills from their solicitor or builder\xe2\x80\x9d that are copied from genuine bills but have had the bank account number and sort code changed.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2018 Which? was accused by members of its long-standing email service - which.net - of abandoning them by abruptly announcing cancellation of a service they\'d used for 22 years. One member stated \xe2\x80\x9cEven if this were a commercial company the way it\xe2\x80\x99s been done is appalling, but for it to be a charity that professes to protect consumers - it\'s a travesty and a betrayal of those members who trusted Which? in a way no one would trust a commercial entity."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_action_against_rogue_traders">Legal action against rogue traders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legal action against rogue traders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2005 Which? was granted legal powers to bring rogue traders to account for their actions under Part 8 of the <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Act_2002" title="Enterprise Act 2002">Enterprise Act 2002</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unfair_contract_terms">Unfair contract terms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Unfair contract terms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Which? has statutory powers under the <a href="/wiki/Unfair_Terms_in_Consumer_Contracts_Regulations_1999" title="Unfair Terms in Consumer Contracts Regulations 1999">Unfair Terms in Consumer Contracts Regulations 1999</a>. Which? can seek an <a href="/wiki/Injunction" title="Injunction">injunction</a> to restrain the use of an unfair contract term by a trader against consumers.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition_Appeals_Tribunal">Competition Appeals Tribunal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Competition Appeals Tribunal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Which? is one of the \'specified bodies\' who, under the Enterprise and Competition Acts, may bring proceedings before the Competition Appeals Tribunal (CAT) on behalf of two or more consumers for damages. Which? was granted specified body status on 1 October 2005 by the Ministers of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Trade_and_Industry_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department of Trade and Industry (United Kingdom)">Department of Trade and Industry</a>. In April 2007 Which? launched its first representative action on behalf of consumers unlawfully overcharged for football shirts due to price fixing.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisational_structure">Organisational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Which%3F&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Organisational structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Consumers\xe2\x80\x99 Association owns an operational company Which? Ltd who report to the Council of Trustees. The Board of Which? Ltd is made up primarily of co-opted members, members of Which? staff and some representative[s] from the Consumers\' Association Council.\n</p><p>The Consumers\' Association is subject to both the Companies Act 2006 and to charity requirements. Since 2012 it is governed by a Council of nine elected and up-to six co-opted members who can serve a maximum of 9 years at any one time.\n</p><p>Only Ordinary members of the Consumers\' Association can stand for the Council. Any subscriber can become a member of the Consumers\' Association by agreeing to guarantee the debts of the Association to a maximum of 50p. It also means they will receive Annual Accounts, AGM Minutes, and be able to vote on Resolutions that change the governing Articles.\n</p><p>None of the Council, or Board members is paid [other than the employees], although they are entitled to claim reasonable expenses. <a href="/wiki/Sam_Younger" title="Sam Younger">Sam Younger</a> became Chairman on 1 January 2020,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> replacing <a href="/wiki/Tim_Gardam" title="Tim Gardam">Tim Gardam</a> who had held the role since 2015.\n</p>\n<h2><span
