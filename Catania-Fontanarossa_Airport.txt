Bellini di Catania-Fontanarossa</i>) (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>CTA</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>LICC</b>), also known as <b>Vincenzo Bellini Airport</b>, is an international airport 2.3&#160;<a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">NM</a> (4.3&#160;km; 2.6&#160;mi) southwest<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a>, the second largest city on the Italian island of <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>. It is named after the opera composer <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Bellini" title="Vincenzo Bellini">Vincenzo Bellini</a>, who was born in Catania.\n</p><p>According to Assaeroporti, is the busiest airport in Sicily and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_busiest_airports_in_Italy" title="List of the busiest airports in Italy">fourth busiest in Italy</a> in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Major airlines such as <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a> and <a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a> offer services here and connect numerous European destinations such as Rome, <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, while low-cost airlines such as <a href="/wiki/EasyJet" title="EasyJet">easyJet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> offer flights to leisure destinations.\n</p><p>With nearly two million passengers carried in 2016, the Catania/Fontanarossa - Rome/Fiumicino route is Italy\'s busiest air route, and <a href="/wiki/List_of_busiest_passenger_air_routes#Europe" title="List of busiest passenger air routes">Europe\'s fourth busiest</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Catania Airport\'s history dates back to 1924, when it was the region\'s first airport. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> it was seized by the Allies during the <a href="/wiki/Sicily_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Sicily Campaign">Sicily Campaign</a> and used by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> as a military airfield. <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Air_Force" title="Twelfth Air Force">Twelfth Air Force</a> used the airport as a combat airfield, stationing the <a href="/wiki/340th_Bombardment_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="340th Bombardment Group">340th Bombardment Group</a>, which flew <a href="/wiki/B-25_Mitchell" class="mw-redirect" title="B-25 Mitchell">B-25 Mitchells</a> from 27 August to 19 November 1943. In addition, the HQ, <a href="/wiki/51st_Troop_Carrier_Wing_(World_War_II)" class="mw-redirect" title="51st Troop Carrier Wing (World War II)">51st Troop Carrier Wing</a> used the airport from 29 September 1943 to 29 June 1944. Various transport units used the airport for the rest of the war, afterward it was turned back over to civil authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By the late 1940s, it was clear that the airport was fast running out of space and it was deemed necessary to relocate and in 1950, the new bigger and improved Catania Airport opened for business.\n</p><p>After 20 years of unexpected growth and high passengers levels, in 1981 it was once again necessary to restructure the airport to cope with demand.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_since_the_2000s">Development since the 2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development since the 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In order to cope with the increasing passengers figures, a new terminal, equipped with 22 <a href="/wiki/Gate_(airport)" title="Gate (airport)">gates</a> and six loading bridges, opened on 8 May 2007 replacing the old facilities.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The current "investment programme" has ensured that Catania Fontanarossa Airport continues to look forward and plan for growth over the next ten years, implementing a whole new infrastructure and making many additions, including a panoramic restaurant, a new airside runway and further office space.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> started flying to Catania in 2013, initially announcing only one route to Catania while also starting operations to <a href="/wiki/Comiso_Airport" title="Comiso Airport">Comiso Airport</a>, a new airport which opened in 2013 and is located approximately 100&#160;km from Catania, near the city of <a href="/wiki/Ragusa,_Italy" class="mw-redirect" title="Ragusa, Italy">Ragusa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>To cope with the fast passengers growth two additional terminals were opened in 2018 (Terminal B and C). Terminal C is used exclusively by easyJet.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ground transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Train">Train</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Train">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A new train station, <b>Catania-Aeroporto Fontanarossa</b> served by regional train lines such as the <a href="/wiki/Messina%E2%80%93Syracuse_railway" title="Messina\xe2\x80\x93Syracuse railway">Messina-Syracuse railway</a>, the Catania-Palermo railway, as well as the Catania-Caltagirone railway. \nCatania-Aeroporto Fontanarossa rail station is part of Catania\'s suburban railway line. The station is situated between Bicocca and Catania-Acquicella stations.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\nA typical journey to and from <a href="/wiki/Catania_Centrale_railway_station" title="Catania Centrale railway station">Catania Central Station</a> will take less than 10 minutes, and approximately one hour to and from Syracuse or Taormina train stations.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Car">Car</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Car">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is located close to the <a href="/wiki/Autostrada_A19_(Italy)" title="Autostrada A19 (Italy)">A19</a> <a href="/wiki/Autostrada" class="mw-redirect" title="Autostrada">motorway</a>, which links Catania with <a href="/wiki/Palermo" title="Palermo">Palermo</a> and central Sicily, while the <a href="/wiki/European_route_E45" title="European route E45">European route E45</a> runs to <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_Sicily" title="Syracuse, Sicily">Syracuse</a> in the south.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catania%E2%80%93Fontanarossa_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Airport_bus" title="Airport bus">shuttle bus</a> service provides transport into Catania city centre and the Central Train Station, while scheduled bus services to other parts of the island<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> are also available direct from the airport.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
