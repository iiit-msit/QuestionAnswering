href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EPMO</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a>, formerly a military airbase. Originally opened by Germany in 1940 as an airbase after the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland" title="Invasion of Poland">invasion of Poland</a>, after the German capitulation in 1945 the airport was transferred over to Soviet/Polish control. The airbase closed in 2000 as it was deemed unsafe for any further operations, before eventually being overhauled and repurposed as a commercial airport.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is located 40&#160;km (25 miles) north of Warsaw\'s city centre in <a href="/wiki/Nowy_Dw%C3%B3r_Mazowiecki" title="Nowy Dw\xc3\xb3r Mazowiecki">Nowy Dw\xc3\xb3r Mazowiecki</a> and has a maximum capacity of approx. 2-2.3 million passengers a year. The airport is intended to be used by <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carriers</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. As of 2017, the airport is the fifth busiest airport in Poland,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> with 2,932,639 passengers currently exclusively served by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, however as of late the airport is slowly opening up to other airlines, such as the local Warsaw-based <a href="/wiki/Enter_Air" title="Enter Air">Enter Air</a>, however for now only operating seasonal services.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The main international airport of the city, however, is <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Chopin_Airport" title="Warsaw Chopin Airport">Warsaw Chopin Airport</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earlier_usage">Earlier usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Earlier usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Modlin_airbase_photographed_by_the_Discoverer_36_(Corona_9029)_satellite_(1961-12-12).png" class="image"><img alt="" December 1961</div></div></div>\n<p>Originally designed for military use in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Polish_Republic" title="Second Polish Republic">Second Polish Republic</a> in 1937, it was not opened by Polish authorities. Instead, it was made operational during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in 1940 as an airbase by the German <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> in occupied Poland. Postwar, between 1945 and 2000, it was used by Polish and Soviet air forces. In 2000, the Polish Ministry of National Defence declared the airfield closed.<sup id="cite_ref-history_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The airport\'s runway was in poor condition and lacked proper lighting and modern radio navigation aids such as an <a href="/wiki/Instrument_Landing_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrument Landing System">Instrument Landing System</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redevelopment">Redevelopment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Redevelopment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Subsequently, much of its original area was made available as capital in a joint management <a href="/wiki/Limited_liability" title="Limited liability">limited liability</a> company created to run the future airport, Port Lotniczy Mazowsze Warszawa-Modlin Sp. z o.o. The airport was converted for civilian use, primarily as a replacement for the now closed Etiuda terminal for low-cost carriers at <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Chopin_Airport" title="Warsaw Chopin Airport">Warsaw\'s main airport</a>, this idea emerged in the early 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-WVDec04_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WVDec04-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Numerous projected opening dates had slipped,<sup id="cite_ref-WVJan07_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WVJan07-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and business plans with extensive infrastructure improvements, including a new passenger terminal, had been proposed without any actual progress in the construction for some time. An environmental assessment was completed as well.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A schedule, announced in February 2008<sup id="cite_ref-GW26GFeb_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GW26GFeb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PMOnLine_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMOnLine-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> had the airport opening for business in early 2010. On 8 February 2010, the airport was registered officially as a civil airport by the Polish Aviation Authority (Urz\xc4\x85d Lotnictwa Cywilnego).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2009, it was announced<sup id="cite_ref-Polish_Market_2009-09-10_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Polish_Market_2009-09-10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> that tenders were being accepted and funding had been secured from the EU for an opening in 2011, in time for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012 Football Tournament</a>. Construction works finally began in October 2010 and were expected to be completed before Euro 2012;<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> however the deadline was not met and instead the airport began to operate in July 2012.\n</p><p>A new 5&#160;km rail spur branching off from the existing Warsaw\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Gdynia" title="Gdynia">Gdynia</a> line will be built with an underground station at the airport, theoretically providing a 30-minute commute to Warsaw centre.\n</p><p>Although the first aircraft was meant to depart Modlin on 16 July 2012, the airport was officially inaugurated the day before, and the first passenger flight from <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> arrived at the airport around 17:30.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Low-cost-airlines <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> started to use the airport as bases.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Struggle_and_growth">Struggle and growth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Struggle and growth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 22 December 2012, it was announced that the runway at the airport would be closed to larger aircraft such as the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a> and <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320">Airbus A320</a> indefinitely for safety reasons. Ryanair confirmed on the day of the runway closure it would divert all aircraft to <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Chopin_Airport" title="Warsaw Chopin Airport">Warsaw-Chopin Airport</a> until the runway was repaired. Wizz Air also confirmed that it would route its flights to Chopin Airport until Modlin re-opened. The official re-opening took place over six months later on 4 July 2013 after construction works to fix the runway had been completed. On 17 July 2013, <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a> announced it would not return to Modlin despite its re-opening, but stay at Warsaw-Chopin Airport instead. <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> returned to Modlin on 30 September 2013 and since added more routes to its initial schedule.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 19 September 2013, the Category I <a href="/wiki/Instrument_Landing_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrument Landing System">Instrument Landing System</a> was officially ready for use. At the same time tests began for the Category II <a href="/wiki/Instrument_Landing_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Instrument Landing System">Instrument Landing System</a> which was ready for use by 1 May 2014.\n</p><p>In October 2015, the airport welcomed its 5th millionth passenger overall.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In December of the same year, an <a href="/wiki/Airport_lounge" title="Airport lounge">airport lounge</a> was inaugurated.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since the inauguration of the new airport, its only current user Ryanair increased the network served from Modlin to 35 destinations by April 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Terminal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport features a new, one-storey passenger terminal building containing all departures and arrivals facilities as well as some shops. The apron features stands for 10 aircraft, as there are no jet-bridges, bus and walk boarding is used.<sup id="cite_ref-airportmap_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airportmap-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway">Runway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warsaw_Modlin_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Runway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport has one <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">asphalt</a> runway 2,500&#160;m long and 60&#160;m wide at an elevation of 104&#160;m<sup id="cite_ref-WAD_19-0" class="reference"><a class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airportmap-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport itself does not have a railway station, however, there are frequent shuttle bus services to the <a href="/wiki/Modlin_railway_station" title="Modlin railway station">railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Modlin,_Nowy_Dw%C3%B3r_Mazowiecki_County" title="Modlin, Nowy Dw\xc3\xb3r Mazowiecki href="/wiki/Warszawa_Centralna_railway_station" title="Warszawa Centralna railway station">Warsaw Central station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Chopin_Airport_railway_station" title="Warsaw Chopin Airport railway station">Warsaw Chopin Airport</a> while others terminate at <a href="/wiki/Warszawa_Zachodnia_station" title="Warszawa Zachodnia station">Warsaw West station</a>. In the other direction, most services terminate at Modlin while some continue to <a href="/wiki/Dzia%C5%82dowo_railway_station" title="Dzia\xc5\x82dowo railway station">Dzia\xc5\x82dowo</a>.<sup
