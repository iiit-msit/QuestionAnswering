href="/w/index.php?title=Kaunas_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Kaunas Airport started operations in 1988<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> when airport activities were moved from the historic <a href="/wiki/S._Darius_and_S._Gir%C4%97nas_Airport" title="S. Darius and S. Gir\xc4\x97nas Airport">S. Darius and S. Gir\xc4\x97nas Airport</a>, located in the central part of Kaunas city. In 1991, after Lithuania\'s independence, it gained the status of an <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> and in 1996 it became a member of <a href="/wiki/Airports_Council_International" title="Airports Council International">Airports Council International</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and began to take part in the activities of the "Lithuanian Airports" association.\n</p><p>Kaunas Airport was used by <a href="/wiki/Yakovlev_Yak-40" title="Yakovlev Yak-40">YAK-40</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yakovlev_Yak-42" title="Yakovlev Yak-42">YAK-42</a> of the local <a href="/wiki/Aeroflot" title="Aeroflot">Aeroflot</a> branch since 1988. The flight range was moderate and there were some scheduled flights from Kaunas to <a href="/wiki/Kyiv" title="Kyiv">Kyiv</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kharkiv" title="Kharkiv">Kharkiv</a>, Moscow, <a href="/wiki/Odessa" title="Odessa">Odessa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simferopol" title="Simferopol">Simferopol</a>, and <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0iauliai" title="\xc5\xa0iauliai">\xc5\xa0iauliai</a>. Regional airline <a href="/wiki/Air_Lithuania" title="Air Lithuania">Air Lithuania</a> based in Kaunas operated scheduled and charter flights from Kaunas to <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billund,_Denmark" title="Billund, Denmark">Billund</a>, Hamburg, <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malm\xc3\xb6">Malm\xc3\xb6</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> from 1993 till 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> started operating flights to Kaunas in 2004. In 2006 it announced establishment of its base in Kaunas. At the beginning of 2013, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> invested more than 3 million Euro in a new aircraft maintenance and repair hangar in Kaunas, which currently employs 220 people (2018 data). The airline is further expanding its aircraft maintenance activity at Kaunas Airport, with a planned further investment of 1.6 million Euro, which will allow the servicing of twice as many aircraft. The company intends to employ 40 additional aviation mechanics, and the employment strategy is based on the company\'s collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Kaunas_University_of_Technology" title="Kaunas University of Technology">Kaunas School of Mechanics at Kaunas University of Technology</a> in order to prepare necessary specialists and invite students for traineeships.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Air_Baltic" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Baltic">Air Baltic</a> operated Riga - Kaunas daily flights in 2006-2008 and 2009-2014, sometimes reaching up to 2 daily flights.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wizzair" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizzair">Wizzair</a> operated Kaunas-Warsaw-London Luton route for a short period of time in 2005. The airline returned to Kaunas in 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Two temporary terminals were in operation in 2017, when <a href="/wiki/Vilnius_Airport" title="Vilnius Airport">Vilnius Airport</a> was closed for reconstruction and flights transferred to Kaunas.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/LOT_Polish_Airlines" title="LOT Polish Airlines">LOT Polish Airlines</a> started operating six weekly flights to Kaunas International from Warsaw on 21 May 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span Ownership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport is owned and operated by the State-owned enterprise Kauno Aerouostas, and is fully governable to the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_and_Communications_(Lithuania)" title="Ministry of Transport and Communications (Lithuania)">Ministry of Transport and Communications</a>. In May 2013, the Government announced about the plans to merge <a href="/wiki/Vilnius_Airport" title="Vilnius Airport">Vilnius</a>, Kaunas and <a href="/wiki/Palanga_International_Airport" title="Palanga International Airport">Palanga</a> airports into one company and the plans were approved by the <a href="/wiki/Seimas" title="Seimas">Lithuanian parliament</a> in November 2013. The merger took place in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaunas_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> is the main passenger carrier at the airport, and has gradually expanded its network there since 2005 when the airline first landed at the airport. In 2010, Kaunas Airport became the first airline\'s base in Eastern Europe and this resulted a more-than-doubled-route network expansion at the airport. The airport reported 77% growth in passenger traffic that year and also won the EURO ANNIES 2011 prize awarded by a weekly aviation e-journal <a href="/wiki/Anna.aero" title="Anna.aero">anna.aero</a> as being the fastest-growing airport in Europe in the category of under one million passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catchment_area">Catchment area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a all passenger flow at the airport are travellers from the neighbouring countries <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" The 7,573-square-metre (81,520&#160;sq&#160;ft) building can handle 800,000 passengers per year and the maximum capacity has been already reached in 2010, three years after the opening. The simple linear terminal design allows further expansions both ways.\n</p><p>The ground level is designated arrival area and fully complies with <a href="/wiki/Schengen_Agreement" title="Schengen Agreement">Schengen regulations</a>. There are all essential facilities for arriving passengers, including <a href="/wiki/Bureau_de_change" title="Bureau de change">bureau de change</a> and car rental offices. The upper two levels are for departing passengers only. All 12 check-in desks are located on the first floor, where the passengers flow is separated to Schengen and non-Schengen departure zones through the security areas located on the first and second floors. The airport is not equipped with air bridges which suits the preference of airport\'s biggest client <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> of boarding and disembarking with steps.\n</p><p>The terminal facilities also include airline ticket offices, tourism agents, several shops, bars and cafes throughout the airport.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Runway_and_apron">Runway and apron</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaunas_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Runway and apron">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The runway of Kaunas Airport is 3,250m long and 45m wide, and is categorized with a 4E <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization" title="International Civil Aviation Organization">ICAO</a> reference code. This enables to handle aircraft with up to 45m wing span<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and 14m main gear wheel span, which includes planes the size of a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> or <a href="/wiki/Antonov_An-124" class="mw-redirect" title="Antonov An-124">Antonov An-124</a>. The runway is oriented along the dominant direction of western winds; it is also equipped with CAT II <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">ILS</a> equipment which allows Kaunas Airport to receive aircraft with minimum visibility meteorological conditions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Theoretical runway average capacity, when aircraft are landing or taking off, is 12 operations per hour.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> A new taxiway to improve the runway system was built in 2009. Further taxiway improvement works started in June 2013, expanding the southern part of the airport to construct a new 190m-long and 23.2m-broad runway by the end of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maintenance_facilities">Maintenance facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaunas_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Maintenance facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Ryanair operates its own maintenance hangar at Kaunas Airport, which launched operations in January 2013. It can accommodate one aircraft at a time and performs C-type maintenance checks. The same year Ryanair has set to double its <a href="/wiki/Maintenance,_repair,_and_operations" class="mw-redirect" title="Maintenance, repair, and operations">MRO</a> capabilities at the airport and began construction of the second hangar in Kaunas.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/FL_Technics" title="FL Technics">FL Technics</a> has announced its plans to invest almost 4 million US dollars into high-tech aircraft maintenance equipment at its newly launched MRO hangar in Kaunas. The latest equipment will support FL Technics MRO centre in servicing both narrow and wide body modern aircraft, including Boeing 747 and Boeing 787 Dreamliner. Following the full implementation, of the investment strategy, the new FL Technics base in Kaunas will create almost 300 new jobs, including over 200 places for aircraft mechanics, engineers and other aviation technical personnel.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and href="/wiki/A6_highway_(Lithuania)" title="A6 highway (Lithuania)">A6 highway</a>/<a href="/wiki/European_route_E262" title="European route E262">E262</a>, which connects to the other main <a and <a href="/wiki/European_route_E67" title="European route E67">Via Baltica (E67)</a>. Taxis take around 25 minutes to get to the city centre.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaunas_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Kaunas city centre is reachable by direct bus route no 29, operated daily by Kauno Autobusai. The express service 29E operates on the same route, but offers limited bus stops on the line. The direct intercity services to capital <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a> and Lithuanian coastal city <a href="/wiki/Klaipeda" class="mw-redirect" title="Klaipeda">Klaipeda</a> are also available from and to Kaunas Airport as well as regional low frequency bus links to and from <a href="/wiki/Anyk%C5%A1%C4%8Diai" title="Anyk\xc5\xa1\xc4\x8diai">Anyk\xc5\xa1\xc4\x8diai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bir%C5%BEai" title="Bir\xc5\xbeai">Bir\xc5\xbeai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ignalina" title="Ignalina">Ignalina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kupi%C5%A1kis" title="Kupi\xc5\xa1kis">Kupi\xc5\xa1kis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marijampol%C4%97" title="Marijampol\xc4\x97">Marijampol\xc4\x97</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mol%C4%97tai" title="Mol\xc4\x97tai">Mol\xc4\x97tai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Obeliai" title="Obeliai">Obeliai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roki%C5%A1kis" title="Roki\xc5\xa1kis">Roki\xc5\xa1kis</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0irvintos" title="\xc5\xa0irvintos">\xc5\xa0irvintos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Utena" title="Utena">Utena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visaginas" title="Visaginas">Visaginas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zarasai" title="Zarasai">Zarasai</a>.\n</p><p>The only international link to the airport is provided by Latvian operator Flybus, which runs the schedule to both <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a> city centre and <a href="/wiki/Riga_International_Airport" title="Riga International Airport">Riga International Airport</a>. The services are based on the timetable of departing and arriving flights at Kaunas Airport.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_facilities">Other facilities</span><span
