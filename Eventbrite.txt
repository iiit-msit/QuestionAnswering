category">Active</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Eventbrite</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Event_management" title="Event management">event management</a> and ticketing website. The service allows users to browse, create, and promote local events. The service charges a fee to event organizers in exchange for online ticketing services, unless the event is free.<sup id="cite_ref-XConomy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XConomy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Launched in 2006 and headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, Eventbrite opened their first international office in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> in 2012. The company has local offices in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cork_(city)" title="Cork (city)">Cork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mendoza,_Argentina" title="Mendoza, Argentina">Mendoza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="S\xc3\xa3o Paulo">S\xc3\xa3o Paulo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-XConomy_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XConomy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The company went public on the New York Stock Exchange on September 20, 2018 under the ticker symbol EB.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eventbrite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Eventbrite was founded in 2006 by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hartz" title="Kevin Hartz">Kevin Hartz</a> (Co-Founder and Executive Chairman) and <a href="/wiki/Julia_Hartz" title="Julia Hartz">Julia Hartz</a> (Co-Founder and CEO) and Renaud Visage (Co-Founder and CTO). The company was the first major player in this market in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Prior to his position at the company, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hartz" title="Kevin Hartz">Kevin Hartz</a> was involved with <a href="/wiki/PayPal" title="PayPal">PayPal</a> and was the Co-Founder and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Xoom_Corporation" title="Xoom Corporation">Xoom Corporation</a>, an international money transfer company. Julia Hartz, wife of Kevin, was raised in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz,_California" title="Santa Cruz, California">Santa Cruz, CA</a>. After studying broadcasting at Pepperdine University, she became a creative executive at <a href="/wiki/FX_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="FX Network">FX Network</a> in Los Angeles. Soon after the two became engaged, she moved to the Bay Area and helped co-found Eventbrite.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>On March 18, 2011 Eventbrite raised $50 million in Series E Financing led by <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Global" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiger Global">Tiger Global</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fast_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fast-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On April 22, 2013, Eventbrite raised another $60 million in growth capital financing led by Tiger Global, and including T. Rowe Price.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On March 13, 2014, Eventbrite raised a private equity round of $60 million,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and on September 1, 2017, the company raised $134 million in a Series G funding round. This brought their total funding to $334 million. Previous funding involved firms including <a href="/wiki/Sequoia_Capital" title="Sequoia Capital">Sequoia Capital</a>, <a href="/wiki/DAG_Ventures" title="DAG Ventures">DAG Ventures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tenaya_Capital" title="Tenaya Capital">Tenaya Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Crunchbase_Profile:_Eventbrite_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crunchbase_Profile:_Eventbrite-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tomiogeron_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tomiogeron-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016, Julia became the CEO of Eventbrite, while Kevin took the role of executive chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2017, Eventbrite purchased D.C.-based event tech startup Nvite for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On June 9, 2017 Eventbrite purchased <a href="/wiki/Ticketfly" title="Ticketfly">Ticketfly</a> from <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Pandora Radio">Pandora</a> for $200 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The acquisition was meant to strengthen Eventbrite\'s position in the live music market, but according to observers, executives were still struggling to integrate Ticketfly as of 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2018, Eventbrite acquired the Spanish ticketing service Ticketea, citing its events discovery platform and "robust ecosystem of third-party integrations" as being advantageous.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Later that month, Eventbrite was subjected to criticism over an update to its merchants\' agreement, which specified that the service had the right to attend and record footage of any aspect of an event for any purpose, and that event organizers were "responsible for obtaining, at your own cost, all third party permissions, clearances, and licenses necessary to secure Eventbrite the permissions and rights [to do so]." Following public backlash, Eventbrite chose to remove the passage entirely. The company stated that it wanted the option to "work with individual organizers to secure video and photos at their events for marketing and promotional purposes", but admitted that the clauses were too broadly-worded.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2018, <a href="/wiki/Picatic" title="Picatic">Picatic</a>, a Vancouver-based ticketing and event registration platform, was acquired by Eventbrite.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2020, during the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">coronavirus pandemic</a> which was causing a drastic drop in in-person events, Eventbrite laid off around 45% of its employees, which at that point numbered between 1,000 and 1,100.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Reportedly, online events had amounted to less than 10% of the company\'s revenue in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">Funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eventbrite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On March 18, 2011 Eventbrite raised $50 million in Series E Financing led by <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Global" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiger Global">Tiger Global</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fast_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fast-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On April 22, 2013, Eventbrite raised another $60 million in growth capital financing led by Tiger Global, and including T. Rowe Price.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> This brought their total funding to $140 million. Previous funding involved firms including <a href="/wiki/Sequoia_Capital" title="Sequoia Capital">Sequoia Capital</a>, <a href="/wiki/DAG_Ventures" title="DAG Ventures">DAG Ventures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tenaya_Capital" title="Tenaya Capital">Tenaya Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Crunchbase_Profile:_Eventbrite_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Crunchbase_Profile:_Eventbrite-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 23, 2018, the company filed for a $200 million IPO.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The company\'s biggest shareholder is <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Global" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiger Global">Tiger Global Management</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sequoia_Capital" title="Sequoia Capital">Sequoia Capital</a> and the Hartzs also owning significant shares.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Customer_satisfaction">Customer satisfaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
