href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EYVI</b>) (<a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>: <i lang="lt">Vilniaus oro uostas</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>. It is located 5.9&#160;km (3.7&#160;mi) south<sup id="cite_ref-AIP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AIP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> of the city. It is the largest of the three commercial airports in Lithuania by passenger traffic. With one runway and 5 million passengers a year.<sup id="cite_ref-ltou.lt_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ltou.lt-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Vilnius International Airport serves as a base for <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a>. The airport is managed by <a href="/wiki/State-owned_enterprise" title="State-owned enterprise">state-owned enterprise</a> Lithuanian Airports under the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Transport_and_Communications_(Lithuania)" title="Ministry of Transport and Communications (Lithuania)">Ministry of Transport and Communications</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airport began operations on 17 August 1932 as Wilno\xe2\x80\x93Porubanek, Porubanek was the name of the neighbouring village which today is part of the Kirtimai district of Vilnius. Before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, it operated the then-domestic route between Wilno (Vilnius) and <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a> as well as international route to <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>. Since 15 April 1939, it inaugurated a new route to Kovno (nowadays <a href="/wiki/Kaunas" title="Kaunas">Kaunas</a>). The airport was used as a military airfield during WWII. The airport resumed its activity as a civil airport as of 17 July 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Lithuanian Airlines (branded later as <a href="/wiki/FlyLAL" class="mw-redirect" title="FlyLAL">FlyLAL</a>) was established as the Lithuanian <a href="/wiki/Flag_carrier" title="Flag carrier">flag carrier</a> following independence in 1991 and inherited the Vilnius-based <a href="/wiki/Aeroflot" title="Aeroflot">Aeroflot</a> fleet of <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-134" title="Tupolev Tu-134">Tupolev Tu-134</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yakovlev_Yak-40" title="Yakovlev Yak-40">Yakovlev Yak-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yak-42" class="mw-redirect" title="Yak-42">Yak-42</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antonov_An-24" title="Antonov An-24">Antonov An-24</a>, <a href="/wiki/An-26" class="mw-redirect" title="An-26">An-26</a> aircraft, but rapidly replaced these Soviet-era aircraft types with modern <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boeing_757" title="Boeing 757">Boeing 757</a> jets and <a href="/wiki/Saab_340" title="Saab 340">Saab 340</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saab_2000" title="Saab 2000">Saab 2000</a> turboprops. Operations were suspended effective 17 January 2009 as a result of growing financial difficulties. With the collapse of flyLAL, the airport lost its scheduled services to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, Madrid and <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a>. flyLAL used to operate to <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, Frankfurt, London, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> and Paris in competition with <a href="/wiki/Aer_Lingus" title="Aer Lingus">Aer Lingus</a>, <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lufthansa" title="Lufthansa">Lufthansa</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">AirBaltic</a>, the national airline of <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a> and under <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Airlines" title="Scandinavian Airlines">Scandinavian Airlines</a> part-ownership, opened up a second base at <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a> in 2004 to complement its <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a> operation and became the largest carrier at Vilnius, using <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737</a> jets and <a href="/wiki/Fokker_50" title="Fokker 50">Fokker 50</a> turboprops. At one point, airBaltic operated to 19 destinations from Vilnius but, in 2009, the network covered only three destinations served by two aircraft based at Vilnius.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Vilnius Airport is the main hub for Grand Cru Airlines and a base for <a href="/wiki/Wizz_Air" title="Wizz Air">Wizz Air</a>. It used to be a main hub for Star1 Airlines until their end of operations in September 2010 and <a href="/wiki/Aurela" title="Aurela">Aurela</a> until Aurela had lost its flight license. It was the hub for <a href="/wiki/Small_Planet_Airlines" title="Small Planet Airlines">Small Planet Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aviavilsa" title="Aviavilsa">Aviavilsa</a> until both airlines folded. The airport was a secondary hub for <a href="/wiki/AirBaltic" title="AirBaltic">airBaltic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Air" title="Estonian Air">Estonian Air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skyways_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Skyways Express">Skyways Express</a> until they closed the bases in Vilnius.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>On 30 June 2013, <a href="/wiki/Air_Lituanica" title="Air Lituanica">Air Lituanica</a> also began its flights from the Vilnius Airport and established its base there serving several European cities. However, by 22 May 2015, the airline shut down all operations as well.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airport was closed for 35 days from 14 July 2017 to 17 August 2017 (inclusive) for runway reconstruction work, with all flights diverted to <a href="/wiki/Kaunas_Airport" title="Kaunas Airport">Kaunas Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminal">Terminal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The passenger throughput of the terminal increased, passenger service quality was improved and more stringent aviation security measures were implemented. The new area of the renovated passenger terminal now reaches 3,462&#160;m<sup>2</sup> (37,260&#160;sq&#160;ft). It is equipped with 6 passenger boarding bridges, modern passenger <a href="/wiki/Check-in" title="Check-in">check-in</a> equipment, new travel value and <a href="/wiki/Duty-free_shops" class="mw-redirect" title="Duty-free shops">duty-free shops</a> were opened as well as business lounge and VIP Lounge.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a Also, the Latvian company Flybus.lv operates service from Vilnius airport to Riga (via Panev\xc4\x97\xc5\xbeys and Bauska).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transportation">Public transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Public transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>City\'s buses operate from the airport. Also, the company Toks transports passengers from the bus station to Vilnius airport and back by <a href="/wiki/Microbus" class="mw-redirect" title="Microbus">microbuses</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aviation_services">Aviation services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Aviation services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Passenger handling, aircraft handling, into-plane fueling and de-icing/anti-icing services are handled by BGS and Litcargus.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vilnius_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2007_Dash_8_landing_gear_incidents" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Dash 8 landing gear incidents">Scandinavian Airlines Flight 2748</a>, operated with <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada_Dash_8" title="De Havilland Canada Dash 8">Dash-8-400</a> (LN-RDS) with 48 passengers and 4 crew members, took off from <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Airport" title="Copenhagen Airport">Copenhagen Airport</a> on 12 September 2007. It was heading to <a href="/wiki/Palanga_International_Airport" title="Palanga International Airport">Palanga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, but was diverted to Vilnius Airport (better suited for an emergency landing) when landing gear problems were discovered before landing. Upon touchdown, the right landing gear collapsed. All passengers and crew were evacuated safely. The local officials at the Vilnius International Airport noted that this was the most serious incident in recent years. This accident, along with the Aalborg accident just days earlier, caused all SAS Dash 8 Q400 planes to be grounded until the beginning of October.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
