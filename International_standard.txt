class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Standard developed by international standards organizations</div>\n<p><strong>International standards</strong> are <a href="/wiki/Technical_standards" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical standards">technical standards</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">international organizations</a> (<a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental organizations</a>), such as <a href="/wiki/Codex_Alimentarius" title="Codex Alimentarius">Codex Alimentarius</a> in food, the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> Guidelines in health, or <a href="/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union" title="International Telecommunication Union">ITU</a> Recommendations in <a href="/wiki/Information_and_communications_technology_in_Kosovo" title="Information and communications technology in Kosovo">ICT</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and being publicly funded, are freely available for consideration and use worldwide.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">Purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>International standards may be used either by direct application or by a process of modifying an international standard to suit local conditions. The adoption of international standards results in the creation of equivalent, national standards that are substantially the same as international standards in technical content, but may have (i) editorial differences as to appearance, use of symbols and measurement units, substitution of a point for a comma as the decimal marker, and (ii) differences resulting from conflicts in governmental <a href="/wiki/Regulation" title="Regulation">regulations</a> or industry-specific requirements caused by fundamental climatic, geographical, technological, or infrastructural factors, or the stringency of safety requirements that a given standard authority considers appropriate.\n</p><p>International standards are one way of overcoming technical barriers in international commerce caused by differences among technical regulations and standards developed independently and separately by each nation, national standards organization, or company. Technical barriers arise when different groups come together, each with a large user base, doing some well established thing that between them is mutually incompatible. Establishing international standards is one way of preventing or overcoming this problem.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standardization">Standardization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Standardization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:JFIScrewThread300.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/JFIScrewThread300.png/220px-JFIScrewThread300.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="266" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/94/JFIScrewThread300.png 1.5x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="363" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:JFIScrewThread300.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Graphic representation of formulae for the pitches of threads of screw bolts</div></div></div>\n<p>The implementation of standards in industry and commerce became highly important with the onset of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> and the need for high-precision <a href="/wiki/Machine_tool" title="Machine tool">machine tools</a> and <a href="/wiki/Interchangeable_parts" title="Interchangeable parts">interchangeable parts</a>. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Maudslay" title="Henry Maudslay">Henry Maudslay</a> developed the first industrially practical <a href="/wiki/Screw-cutting_lathe" title="Screw-cutting lathe">screw-cutting lathe</a> in 1800, which allowed for the standardisation of <a href="/wiki/Screw_thread" title="Screw thread">screw thread</a> sizes for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-Ping_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ping-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Maudslay&#x27;s work, as well as the contributions of other engineers, accomplished a modest amount of industry standardization; some companies&#x27; in-house standards spread a bit within their industries. <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Whitworth" title="Joseph Whitworth">Joseph Whitworth</a>&#x27;s screw thread measurements were adopted as the first (unofficial) national standard by companies around the country in <time>1841</time>. It came to be known as the <a href="/wiki/British_Standard_Whitworth" title="British Standard Whitworth">British Standard Whitworth</a>, and was widely adopted in other countries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By <time>the end of the 19th century</time> differences in standards between companies were making trade increasingly difficult and strained. The <a href="/wiki/BSI_Group" title="BSI Group">Engineering Standards Committee</a> was established in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in <time>1901</time> as the world&#x27;s first national standards body.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Robert_C_McWilliam_2001_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Robert_C_McWilliam_2001-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a>, similar national bodies were established in other countries. The <span lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Deutsches_Institut_f%C3%BCr_Normung" title="Deutsches Institut f\xc3\xbcr Normung">Deutsches Institut f\xc3\xbcr Normung</a></span> was set up in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> in <time>1917</time>, followed by its counterparts, the American <a href="/wiki/American_National_Standards_Institute" title="American National Standards Institute">National Standard Institute</a> and the French <a href="/wiki/AFNOR" title="AFNOR"><span lang="fr">Commission Permanente de Standardisation</span></a>, both in <time>1918</time>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ping_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ping-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_organizations">International organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_standard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:207px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Crompton.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/9/9a/Crompton.jpg" decoding="async" width="205" height="250" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="205" data-file-height="250" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Crompton.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/R._E._B._Crompton" title="R. E. B. Crompton">R. E. B. Crompton</a> drew up the first international standards body, the <a href="/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Commission" title="International Electrotechnical Commission">International Electrotechnical Commission</a>, in 1906.</div></div></div>\n<p>By <time>the mid to late 19th century</time>, efforts were being made to standardize electrical measurement. An important figure was <a href="/wiki/R._E._B._Crompton" title="R. E. B. Crompton">R. E. B. Crompton</a>, who became concerned by the large range of different standards and systems used by electrical engineering companies and scientists in <time>the early 20th century</time>. Many companies had entered the market in <time>the 1890s</time> and all chose their own settings for <a href="/wiki/Volt" title="Volt">voltage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frequency" title="Frequency">frequency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_current" title="Electric current">current</a> and even the symbols used on circuit diagrams. Adjacent buildings would have totally incompatible electrical systems simply because they had been fitted out by different companies. Crompton could see the lack of efficiency in this system and began to consider proposals for an international standard for electric engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In <time>1904</time>, Crompton represented Britain at the <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Purchase_Exposition" title="Louisiana Purchase Exposition">Louisiana Purchase Exposition</a> in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a> as part of a delegation by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_Engineers" class="mw-redirect" title="Institute of Electrical Engineers">Institute of Electrical Engineers</a>. He presented a paper on standardisation, which was so well received that he was asked to look into the formation of a commission to oversee the process.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> By <time>1906</time> his work was complete and he drew up a permanent constitution for the first international standards organization, the <a href="/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Commission" title="International Electrotechnical Commission">International Electrotechnical Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The body held its first meeting that year in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, with representatives from 14 countries. In honour of his contribution to electrical standardisation, Lord Kelvin was elected as the body&#x27;s first President.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg/220px-Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="85" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg/330px-Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg/440px-Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3200" data-file-height="1240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Memory_plaque_of_founding_ISA_in_Prague_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Memorial plaque of founding ISA in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_the_National_Standardizing_Associations" class="mw-redirect" title="International Federation of the National Standardizing Associations">International Federation of the National Standardizing Associations</a> (ISA) was founded in <time>1926</time> with a broader remit to enhance international cooperation for all technical standards and specifications. The body was suspended in <time>1942</time> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World <span class="nowrap">War II</span></a>.\n</p><p>After the war, ISA was approached by the recently formed United Nations Standards Coordinating Committee (UNSCC) with a proposal to form a new global standards body. In <time>October 1946</time>, ISA and UNSCC delegates from 25 countries met in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and agreed to join forces to create the new <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO); the new organization officially began operations in <time style="white-space:nowrap">February 1947</time>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
