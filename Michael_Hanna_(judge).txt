<span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Hanna_(judge)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Hanna originates from <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> and was educated at <a href="/wiki/St_MacNissi%27s_College" title="St MacNissi&#39;s College">St MacNissi\'s College</a>. His father was <a href="/wiki/Frank_Hanna" title="Frank Hanna">Frank Hanna</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Labour_Party" title="Northern Ireland Labour Party">Northern Ireland Labour Party</a> MP.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College_Dublin" title="Trinity College Dublin">Trinity College Dublin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Inns" title="King&#39;s Inns">King\'s Inns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IT_2004_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IT_2004-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a former auditor of the <a href="/wiki/College_Historical_Society" title="College Historical Society">College Historical Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He became a barrister in 1976 and a senior counsel in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-IT_2004_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IT_2004-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hanna\'s practice was focused on the Dublin and South-Eastern circuits, where he specialised in personal injury cases and the law of tort.<sup id="cite_ref-IT_2004_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IT_2004-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Criminal cases also formed part of his practice.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judicial_career">Judicial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Hanna_(judge)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Judicial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Hanna was appointed to the <a href="/wiki/High_Court_(Ireland)" title="High Court (Ireland)">High Court</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was appointed following recommendation by the Judicial Appointments Advisory Board.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He presided over a planning law case involving <a href="/wiki/Van_Morrison" title="Van Morrison">Van Morrison</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Rocca" title="Michelle Rocca">Michelle Rocca</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was the judge in a 2011 case taken by <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> against a German ticket sale website, in which he held that <a href="/wiki/Web_scraping" title="Web scraping">ticket scraping</a> was a breach of Ryanair\'s website terms. This was considered to be the first Irish case involving the enforceability of online <a href="/wiki/Terms_of_service" title="Terms of service">terms of service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He often acted as judge at the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Criminal_Appeal_(Ireland)" title="Court of Criminal Appeal (Ireland)">Court of Criminal Appeal</a>, prior to its disbandment following the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Appeal_(Ireland)" title="Court of Appeal (Ireland)">Court of Appeal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-II_2014_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-II_2014-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He frequently hears personal injuries cases in the High Court. He applied a test of "ordinary common sense" to assess evidence in a personal injuries case involving a woman who had slipped in a car park.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This test was endorsed in a judgment of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Irvine" title="Mary Irvine">Mary Irvine</a> in the Court of Appeal.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Hanna_(judge)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He became ill while in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in 2014 and was in an <a href="/wiki/Induced_coma" title="Induced coma">induced coma</a>. He was unable to hear cases for a period of time following.<sup id="cite_ref-II_2014_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-II_2014-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hanna is a singer of <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
