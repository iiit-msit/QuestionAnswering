Byrne Courthouse in Philadelphia" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/US_Eastern_Pennsylvania_District_Court.jpg/350px-US_Eastern_Pennsylvania_District_Court.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="322" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/US_Eastern_Pennsylvania_District_Court.jpg/525px-US_Eastern_Pennsylvania_District_Court.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/US_Eastern_Pennsylvania_District_Court.jpg/700px-US_Eastern_Pennsylvania_District_Court.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1616" data-file-height="1488" /></a></div>\n<p>The <b>United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania</b> (in <a href="/wiki/Case_citation" title="Case citation">case citations</a>, <b>E.D. Pa.</b>) is one of the original 13 federal judiciary districts created by the <a href="/wiki/Judiciary_Act_of_1789" title="Judiciary Act of 1789">Judiciary Act of 1789</a>. It originally sat in <a href="/wiki/Independence_Hall_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence Hall (United States)">Independence Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="United States District Court for the District of Pennsylvania">United States District Court for the District of Pennsylvania</a>, and is now located at the <a href="/wiki/James_A._Byrne_Courthouse" class="mw-redirect" title="James A. Byrne Courthouse">James Byrne Courthouse</a> at 601 Market Street in Philadelphia. There are Eastern District federal courtrooms in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster, Pennsylvania">Lancaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Pennsylvania" title="Reading, Pennsylvania">Reading</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Easton,_Pennsylvania" title="Easton, Pennsylvania">Easton</a>.\n</p><p>The Court\'s jurisdiction includes <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Berks_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Berks County, Pennsylvania">Berks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bucks_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Bucks County, Pennsylvania">Bucks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chester_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Chester County, Pennsylvania">Chester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Delaware</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster County, Pennsylvania">Lancaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Lehigh County, Pennsylvania">Lehigh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Montgomery County, Pennsylvania">Montgomery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northampton_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Northampton County, Pennsylvania">Northampton counties</a>. The district is a part of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Third_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit">Third Circuit</a>, and appeals are taken to that Circuit (except for <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> claims and claims against the U.S. government under the <a href="/wiki/Tucker_Act" title="Tucker Act">Tucker Act</a>, which are appealed to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Federal_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit">Federal Circuit</a>).\n</p><p>The current Chief Judge for the Eastern Pennsylvania District Court is Judge <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ramon_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Juan Ramon S\xc3\xa1nchez">Juan Ramon S\xc3\xa1nchez</a>.\n</p><p>The people in the district are represented by the United States Attorney for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania, currently Jennifer Arbittier Williams (as Acting U.S. Attorney).\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="United States District Court for the District of Pennsylvania">United States District Court for the District of Pennsylvania</a> was one of the original 13 courts established by the <a href="/wiki/Judiciary_Act_of_1789" title="Judiciary Act of 1789">Judiciary Act of 1789</a>, 1&#160;<a href="/wiki/United_States_Statutes_at_Large" title="United States Statutes at Large">Stat.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://legislink.org/us/stat-1-73">73</a>, on September 24, 1789.<sup id="cite_ref-S388_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S388-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fjcpa_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fjcpa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was subdivided on April 20, 1818, by 3&#160;<a href="/wiki/United_States_Statutes_at_Large" title="United States Statutes at Large">Stat.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://legislink.org/us/stat-3-462">462</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-S388_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S388-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fjcpa_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fjcpa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> into the Eastern and <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Western_District_of_Pennsylvania" title="United States District Court for the Western District of Pennsylvania">Western</a> Districts to be headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-S388_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S388-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Portions of these districts were subsequently subdivided into the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_Middle_District_of_Pennsylvania" title="United States District Court for the Middle District of Pennsylvania">Middle District</a> on March 2, 1901, by 31&#160;<a href="/wiki/United_States_Statutes_at_Large" title="United States Statutes at Large">Stat.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://legislink.org/us/stat-31-880">880</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fjcpa_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fjcpa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the time of its initial subdivision, presiding judge <a href="/wiki/Richard_Peters_(Continental_Congress)" title="Richard Peters (Continental Congress)">Richard Peters Jr.</a> was reassigned to only the Eastern District.\n</p>\n<h2><span judges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_District_Court_for_the_Eastern_District_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chief judges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Chief judges have administrative responsibilities with respect to their district court. Unlike the Supreme Court, where one justice is specifically nominated to be chief, the office of chief judge rotates among the district court judges. To be chief, a judge must have been in active service on the court for at least one year, be under the age of 65, and have not previously served as chief judge. A vacancy is filled by the judge highest in seniority among the group of qualified judges. The chief judge serves for a term of seven years or until age 70, whichever occurs first. The age restrictions are waived if no members of the court would otherwise be qualified for the position.\n</p><p>When the office was created in 1948, the chief judge was the longest-serving judge who had not elected to retire on what has since 1958 been known as <a href="/wiki/Senior_status" title="Senior status">senior status</a> or declined to serve as chief judge. After August 6, 1959, judges could not become or remain chief after turning 70 years old. The current rules have been in operation since October 1, 1982.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession_of_seats">Succession of seats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_District_Court_for_the_Eastern_District_of_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Succession of seats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div>\n\n&#32;\n<table class="multicol" role="presentation" style="border-collapse: collapse; padding: 0; border: 0; background:transparent; width:100%;"><tbody><tr>\n<td style="text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">\n<table class="wikitable" style="">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th colspan="4" align="center" style="background:#ffdead;" width="200">Seat 1\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="4" style="text-align:center;background:#ffdead;">Seat established on September 24, 1789 by 1&#160;Stat.&#160;73 for the District of Pennsylvania\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="4" style="text-align:center;background:#ffdead;">Seat reassigned to the Eastern District of Pennsylvania on April 20, 1818 by 3&#160;Stat.&#160;462\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Richard_Peters_(Continental_Congress)" title="Richard Peters (Continental Congress)">Peters, Jr.</a></td>\n<td>1818\xe2\x80\x931828\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Joseph_Hopkinson" title="Joseph Hopkinson">Hopkinson</a></td>\n<td>1828\xe2\x80\x931842\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Archibald_Randall" title="Archibald Randall">Randall</a></td>\n<td>1842\xe2\x80\x931846\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/John_K._Kane" title="John K.
